By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 19, 2015
Yahoo is closing its research and development center in China as its chief, Marissa Mayer, tries to placate shareholders who are pressuring the company to cut costs. The Beijing operation’s responsibilities will be shifted to other Yahoo offices, a statement released late Wednesday said. Yahoo, the Internet company, did not specify how many people would lose their jobs. With the closure, Yahoo will leave China, where it has been winding down since turning over its operations to the Alibaba Group in 2005.A version of this brief appears in print on March 20, 2015, on Page B2 of the New York edition with the headline: Yahoo Closing Research Center in China, Ending Effort There.
