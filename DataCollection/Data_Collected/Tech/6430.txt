Intel said on Monday that it had agreed to acquire the smaller chip maker Altera for $16.7 billion, Chad Bray writes.The deal comes after the two American companies held talks earlier this year. Further discussions had been delayed, however, when Altera rejected an earlier offer by Intel, people briefed on the talks have said.Under the terms of the transaction, Intel would pay $54 a share in cash for Altera.By buying Altera, which makes programmable chips that can be adapted for different uses, Intel would expand its product lines beyond standard chips for personal computers and corporate servers.
