We have reached Peak Amazon, or perhaps Prime Amazon, David Streitfeld reports.The e-commerce company beloved by Wall Street for its fast-growing ways did something completely out of character in the second quarter: It made a profit.It was only $92 million, practically a rounding error for Google or Apple. But it confirmed all the hopes and expectations of analysts and investors, who immediately pushed Amazon shares up in after-hours trading.Shares surged more than 20 percent in early trading on Friday, adding more than $46 billion to the company’s market value. The company’s market capitalization soared to more than $270 billion, overtaking that of Wal-Mart Stores, making this a deeply symbolic moment for e-commerce and the Internet.
