After nearly two years of suffering at the hands of upstart rivals and established industry giants, Samsung may finally be on the mend, Paul Mozur writes.Its new high-end Galaxy S6 smartphone has had strong advance sales. It has replaced Qualcomm as the maker of chips for its chief competitor, the iPhone from Apple. And a streamlined slate of product offerings could help it compete with the low-end devices dominating the market in China.The beleaguered South Korean manufacturer’s bottom line illustrates the potential turnaround, too: Samsung said on Tuesday that it expected its first-quarter earnings in 2015 to beat analysts’ forecasts.
