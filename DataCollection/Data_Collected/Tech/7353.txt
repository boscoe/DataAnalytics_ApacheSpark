Microsoft announced a major new round of layoffs on Wednesday, Nick Wingfield reports.The company, seeking to cut costs in a shifting technology landscape, said it would eliminate up to 7,800 jobs worldwide — more than 6 percent of its work force — with most of the cuts coming in its phone business. At the end of March, Microsoft had more than 118,000 employees.Satya Nadella, Microsoft’s chief executive, also announced that the company would restructure its phone business, taking an impairment charge of about $7.6 billion related to its acquisition of Nokia’s smartphone business. Microsoft has been stumbling in smartphones, a market in which it has continued to lose market share since acquiring Nokia’s handset business. Read more »
