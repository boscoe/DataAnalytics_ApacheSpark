Where will Google’s antitrust headaches end?It’s a question that likely went through the minds of executives at the company’s campus in Mountain View, Calif., on Monday after Russian antitrust authorities ruled that Google broke the country’s competition rules. As Mark Scott reported, Russia’s competition regulator has been investigating whether Google had unfairly bundled its own services, like digital maps, in its Android software.Google already faces antitrust charges in Europe. And India’s antitrust authority last month expressed concerns about the company’s dominance in search. Google denies wrongdoing in all of the cases. Even so, they are generating a regular drip of unflattering — and certainly for Google, unwanted — news.The news Google would rather be talking about: A new hire from the auto industry to lead the company’s self-driving car project.
