The Chinese e-commerce giant Alibaba said Thursday that its revenue grew 45 percent compared with last year, meeting expectations, but it announced a new chief executive in the wake of concerns about inefficiency, Paul Mozur writes.In a statement, the company said that its chief operating officer, Daniel Zhang, will replace Jonathan Lu as chief executive on Sunday. The top management shuffle comes more than a week after Alibaba’s founder, Jack Ma, gave a speech in Beijing announcing a hiring freeze and a companywide emphasis on efficiency.Mr. Lu will be stepping down as chief executive exactly two years after he took over from Mr. Ma. At the time of his ascent, he was portrayed as a utility man who would help the ballooning start-up manage its rapid growth. Mr. Lu will remain on the board. Read more »
