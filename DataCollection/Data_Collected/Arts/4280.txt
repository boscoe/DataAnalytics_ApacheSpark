LOS ANGELES – Universal Pictures whipped out release dates for its “Fifty Shades of Grey” sequels on Thursday: “Fifty Shades Darker” will arrive in theaters on Feb. 10, 2017, and “Fifty Shades Freed” has been slated for the same weekend a year later. Dakota Johnson and Jamie Dornan will reprise their characters in both movies.Universal did not name a director for either film, but the public announcement of release dates indicates that the studio may be advancing in its search. Sam Taylor-Johnson, who directed “Fifty Shades of Grey,” which is based on the best-selling bondage romance novel by E.L. James, said in March that she would not return for the sequels. Any director will need to contend with the domineering Ms. James, who was given an unusual degree of creative control by Universal.The screenwriter of “Fifty Shades of Grey,” Kelly Marcel, will also not return.  According to The Hollywood Reporter, Ms. James’s husband, Niall Leonard, who has writing credits on British television shows, will write the screenplays for the sequels, both of which will be based on books Ms. James has written.“Fifty Shades of Grey,” released in February, took in $568.8 million worldwide.A version of this article appears in print on 04/25/2015, on page C3 of the NewYork edition with the headline: Dates Are Set For ‘Fifty Shades’ Sequels.
