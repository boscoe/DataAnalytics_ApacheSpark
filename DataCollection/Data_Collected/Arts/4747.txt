The PEN American Center on Wednesday announced the winners of several of its 2015 PEN Literary Awards.The $10,000 John Kenneth Galbraith Award for Nonfiction went to Sheri Fink’s “Five Days at Memorial,” about fateful decisions made at a New Orleans hospital during and after Hurricane Katrina. Ms. Fink is a correspondent for The New York Times, and “Five Days at Memorial” had previously won the National Book Critics Circle Award and several other honors. The PEN/E.O. Wilson Literary Science Writing Award, which also comes with $10,000, was given to “War of the Whales” by Joshua Horwitz.John Branch, a reporter for The Times, won the $5,000 PEN/ESPN Award for Literary Sports Writing for “Boy on Ice: The Life and Death of Derek Boogaard.” Anna Whitelock won the $5,000 PEN/Jacqueline Bograd Weld Award for Biography for “The Queen’s Bed: An Intimate History of Elizabeth’s Court.”Three awards honored playwrights at different stages of their careers. The Master American Dramatist award went to Tina Howe, whose best-known works include “Coastal Disturbances” and “Pride’s Crossing.” The American Playwright in Midcareer award went to Anne Washburn, whose plays include “A Devil at Noon” and “Mr. Burns, a Post-Electric Play,” which deals with a post-apocalyptic future and one particular episode of “The Simpsons.” Jennifer Blackmer won the Emerging American Playwright award.Other awards, including the PEN/Robert W. Bingham Prize for Debut Fiction, will be presented at a ceremony on June 8. In all, the awards, fellowships and grants from the PEN American Center will confer $150,000 on writers, editors and translators.A version of this article appears in print on 05/14/2015, on page C3 of the NewYork edition with the headline: PEN Winners Announced.
