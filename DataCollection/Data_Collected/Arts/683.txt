Afterward, he’ll discuss the show with the actor Jon Hamm. At 7:30 p.m., 1395 Lexington Avenue, 212-415-5500, 92y.org. (Elise Czajkowski)‘Cuckooed’(through Nov. 21) Mr. Thomas is a politically motivated British comedian who describes his work as “a mix of stand-up, theater, journalism and the odd bout of performance art.” In this show, the activist comic recounts his discovery that a good friend was actually a spy for a major arms manufacturer. At various times, 59E59 Theaters, 59 East 59th Street, Manhattan, 212-279-4200, 59e59.org. (Czajkowski)★ Joe List(Wednesday and Thursday) Fresh off his first half-hour special on Comedy Central, Mr. List is known for his very funny, self-deprecating stories. Wednesday at 7:30 p.m. and Thursday at 9:30 p.m., Carolines, 1626 Broadway, at 49th Street, Manhattan, 212-757-4100, carolines.com. (Czajkowski)AdvertisementEugene Mirman Album Release Show(Friday) Mr. Mirman’s tendency toward absurdist projects continues with his new album, “I’m Sorry (You’re Welcome),” a nine-disc, 550-track recording that is a mix of stand-up, ambient music and sound effects (plus 45 minutes of crying). He is joined by H. Jon Benjamin and the poet Derrick Brown for this celebration. At 8:30 p.m., the Bell House, 149 Seventh Street, Gowanus, Brooklyn, 718-643-6510, thebellhouseny.com. Mr. Mooney, who wrote for Richard Pryor, “In Living Color” and “Chappelle’s Show,” has always been a forceful social critic on race issues. Mr. Gregory was the first African-American comedian to be called over to the couch on “The Tonight Show,” and over his 60-plus year career, he’s mixed social activism with biting comedy. At 7:30 p.m., B.B. King Blues Club & Grill, 237 West 42nd Street, Manhattan, 800-745-3000, bbkingblues.com. (Czajkowski)AdvertisementNew York Arab-American Comedy Festival(Thursday through Nov. 7) This annual event — now in its 12th year — showcases the talents of Arab-American comedians, actors, playwrights and filmmakers. Founded by Dean Obeidallah and Maysoon Zayid, the festival features dozens of comedians over five shows. It begins Thursday at 8 p.m. with “Arabs Got Talent,” hosted by Mr. Obeidallah, at Broadway Comedy Club, 318 West 53rd Street, Clinton, and continues through Nov. 7 at Gotham Comedy Club, 208 West 23rd Street, Chelsea. More information is at arabcomedy.com. (Czajkowski)★ ‘A Night of 100 One Liners’(Wednesday) In support of the comedian Erik Bergstrom, a master of dry one-liners who was recently diagnosed with Hodgkin’s lymphoma, 100 comedians will perform their favorite gags to raise money for Mr. Bergstrom’s treatment. At 8 p.m., the Creek and the Cave, 10-93 Jackson Avenue, Long Island City, Queens, 718-706-8783, creeklic.com. (Czajkowski)‘PS321 Fund-raiser: A Night of Comedytainment!’(Wednesday) Jon Glaser hosts this benefit for a Brooklyn elementary school, with a stacked lineup that includes Janeane Garofalo, John Hodgman, Wyatt Cenac, Andy Blitz, Scott Adsit, Eugene Mirman and Carl Newman. At 8 p.m., the Bell House, 149 Seventh Street, Gowanus, Brooklyn, 718-643-6510, thebellhouseny.com.
