A searchable guide to these and other shows is at nytimes.com/events.‘A Very Gay St. Paddy’s Day’  (Tuesday) Matteo Lane fronts this L.G.B.T.-friendly alternative to the St. Patrick’s Day parade, with an excellent lineup including Sabrina Jalees, Julio Torres and Emma Willmann. At 7:30 p.m., Carolines on Broadway, 1626 Broadway, at 49th Street, Manhattan, 212-757-4100, carolines.com. (Elise Czajkowski)‘Blaria Live!’  (Wednesday) Phoebe Robinson and Jessica Williams again welcome a faultless lineup to their monthly stand-up show, with Hasan Minhaj, Carmen Lynch and Eliot Glazer. At 8 p.m., Union Hall, 702 Union Street, at Fifth Avenue, Park Slope, Brooklyn, 718-638-4400, unionhallny.com. (Czajkowski)‘Brooklyn, Stand Up’  (Monday) This reliable monthly stand-up show is hosted by Kerry Coddett and DJ One; performers include Pat Brown, Reggie Conquest, Damien Lemon, Mark Normand, Seaton Smith and Gary Vider. At 7:30 p.m., Knitting Factory, 361 Metropolitan Avenue, at Havemeyer Street, Williamsburg, Brooklyn, 347-529-6696, bk.knittingfactory.com; free. (Czajkowski)Advertisement‘Des Bishop: Made in China’ (Friday through Mondays) Mr. Bishop moved to China with the intention of ultimately performing stand-up in Mandarin. In this show, he recounts the year he spent there, during which he worked as a host in a restaurant and appeared on a Chinese dating show. (Through March 29.) Fridays at 9:30 p.m., Saturdays at 5 and 9:30 p.m., Sundays at 5 p.m. and Mondays at 7:30 p.m., Barrow Street Theater, 27 Barrow Street, at Seventh Avenue South, West Village, 212-868-4444, barrowstreettheatre.com. (Czajkowski)AdvertisementHANK Comedy One Year Anniversary (Saturday) HANK, the online comedy duo of Dan Fox and Betsy Kenney, celebrates its one-year milestone with this live show, featuring new videos and stand-up from Jo Firestone, Carmen Christopher and Charles Gould. At 9:30 p.m., Videology, 308 Bedford Avenue, at South First Street, Williamsburg, Brooklyn, 718-782-3468, videology.info; free. (Czajkowski)‘Serious Matters’  (Thursday) Kevin McCaffrey’s monthly variety show mixes stand-up with lighthearted presentations on profound issues. This month’s comics include Nick Turner, Sean Donnelly and Liza Treygar, while the writers Brock Mahan and Josiah Madigan tackle the serious matter of “being yourself.” At 7:30 p.m., Union Hall, 702 Union Street, at Fifth Avenue, Park Slope, Brooklyn, 718-638-4400, unionhallny.com. (Czajkowski)‘Showtime with Anthony Apruzzese’ (Saturday) Anthony Apruzzese hosts this monthly late night show, alongside his house band, Eric Gersen & the Eric Gersen Nein. This month’s guests include Cipha Sounds and Joe Pera. At 11:30 p.m., Upright Citizens Brigade Theater East, 153 East Third Street, East Village, 212-366-9231, east.ucbtheatre.com. (Czajkowski)★ ‘The St. Patrick’s Day Drunken Throwdown: Part Deux’  (Tuesday) A selection of the Upright Citizens Brigade’s best improvisers will be drinking all day long, then taking the stage for a night of improv mayhem, with team names like “Team Drunked” and “Team Slorpy.” At 7:30 p.m., the Upright Citizens Brigade Theater, 307 West 26th Street, Chelsea, 212-366-9176, newyork.ucbtheatre.com.
