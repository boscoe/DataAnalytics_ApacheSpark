By KATHRYN SHATTUCKDEC. 10, 2015
His beard is flecked with gray now, his soul bedeviled by the ghost of Alice Morgan (who, alas, has run off with Noah Solloway in “The Affair”). And you can’t help wondering what’s lurking in his pretty head as he gazes into the beyond from the White Cliffs of Dover, where he has alighted during a self-imposed exile.But when D.C.I. John Luther regains his senses more than a year after abandoning us on Southwark Bridge and swaggers — as only Idris Elba can — into London, you’ll swear you feel the world right itself on its axis. Neil Cross, the creator of the series, goes for the jugular as “Luther” returns (Thursday, Dec. 17, at 9 p.m. Eastern on BBC America) for a hide-and-seek with a cannibalistic serial killer that pings the genius detective’s existential crisis. Knives are sharpened, blood is splattered and a new redhead (Rose Leslie) is toying with his mind. But Luther just keeps coming, like an Energizered big cat, no matter how crazy things get.
