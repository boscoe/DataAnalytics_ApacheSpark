DeVito. At 9:30 p.m., the Peoples Improv Theater, 123 East 24th Street, Manhattan, 212-563-7488, thepit-nyc.com. (Elise Czajkowski)★ ‘Bullseye Comedy Night’  (Saturday) Jesse Thorn, host of NPR’s pop-culture interview program “Bullseye,” hosts this exemplary night of stand-up comedy, with stand-up from Aisha Tyler, Maria Bamford, Aparna Nancherla and Ali Wong. At 7:30 p.m., Howard Gilman Opera House, Peter Jay Sharp Building, Brooklyn Academy of Music, 30 Lafayette Avenue, at Ashford Place, Fort Greene, 718-636-4100, bam.org. (Czajkowski)‘Clench Live’  (Wednesday) Charla Lauriston hosts this variety show inspired by her popular web series, “Clench & Release.” This month’s guests include Julio Torres, John Early, Gary Richardson, Kevin Iso and Doug Fischer. At 7:30 p.m., Union Hall, 702 Union Street, at Fifth Avenue, Park Slope, Brooklyn, 718-638-4400, unionhallny.com. (Czajkowski)AdvertisementAn Evening With Carol Burnett (Saturday) Ms. Burnett, the comedian and actor who charmed the nation on her long-running variety series, continues her tradition of “Laughter and Reflection” shows, in which she answers questions from audience members. At 8 p.m., St. George Theater, 35 Hyatt Street, at Central Avenue, Staten Island, 718-442-2900, stgeorgetheatre.com. (Czajkowski)★ ‘A Gay Show for All People!’  (Sunday) Aaron Jackson and Josh Sharp celebrate the one-year anniversary of their music-filled comedy show featuring, as they put it, “comics and queers and mostly people who are both.” The performers include Matteo Lane, Cole Escola, Amy Jo Jackson and Aparna Nancherla.
