A searchable guide to these and other shows is at nytimes.com/events.Cristela Alonzo (Tuesday) The star of the new ABC sitcom “Cristela,” about a young woman trying to get through law school, Ms. Alonzo is an eminently likable performer who is known for her sharp, clean stand-up. At 9:30 p.m., Gotham Comedy Club, 208 West 23rd Street, Chelsea, 212-367-9000, gothamcomedyclub.com. (Elise Czajkowski)‘Bad Bride’ (Thursday) The stand-up Giulia Rozzi’s one-woman show recounts her ill-conceived decision to marry young despite that fact that her “cold feet” never quite warmed up. At 8 p.m., Treehouse Theater, 154 West 29th Street, Chelsea, 212-244-1722, treehousetheaternyc.com. (Czajkowski)‘Benjamin Walker’s Find the Funny’ (Monday) The Broadway and film actor Benjamin Walker continues his long-running monthly variety show, which features a mix of comedians and musicians. At 9:30 p.m., Joe’s Pub, at the Public Theater, 425 Lafayette Street, at Astor Place, East Village, 212-967-7555, joespub.com. (Czajkowski)Advertisement★ ‘Dave Gorman’s PowerPoint Presentation’ (Friday and Saturday) Mr. Gorman specializes in high-concept comedic adventures, such as his breakout 2000 show, “Are You Dave Gorman?,” for which he traveled in search of others who shared his name. For “PowerPoint Presentation,” which concludes its two-week run at SubCulture on Saturday, this British comic has “formed a double act with a projector screen.” At 7 and 9 p.m., 45 Bleecker Street, near Lafayette Street, East Village, 212-533-5470, subculturenewyork.com.
