A searchable guide to these and other shows is at nytimes.com/events.Sandra Bernhard: ‘Feel the Bernhard’ (Saturday through Thursday) Ms. Bernhard returns for another end-of-the-year residency with an hour of sharp, politically-minded cabaret comedy that’s billed as “a revolution, Sandy style.” Saturday through Wednesday at 7:30 and 9:30 p.m.; Thursday at 9 and 11 p.m., Joe’s Pub, at the Public Theater, 425 Lafayette Street,East Village, 212-967-7555, joespub.com. (Elise Czajkowski)★ ‘Competitive Erotic Fan Fiction’ (Monday) Brian Cook hosts this quarterly storytelling show, which features comedians reading erotic fan fiction “based upon their own whims or audience suggestions.” Performers include Aparna Nancherla, Myq Kaplan, Ben Kronberg, Giulia Rozzi, Shane Torres, Lisa Curry and Lillian DeVane. At 8 p.m., Union Hall, 702 Union Street, at Fifth Avenue, Park Slope, Brooklyn, 718-638-4400, unionhallny.com. (Czajkowski)Chris Distefano (Sunday through Wednesday) Mr. Distefano, known for his turns on MTV’s “Guy Code” and “Girl Code,” is an energetic, engaging stand-up with a sharp take on modern love. Sunday through Tuesday at 9:30 p.m.; Wednesday at 7:30 p.m., Carolines on Broadway, 1626 Broadway, at West 49th Street, Manhattan, 212-757-4100, carolines.com. (Czajkowski)Advertisement‘Hard, Lonely & Vicious : New Year’s Eve!’ (Thursday) The special New Year’s Eve edition of Peggy O’Leary and Lucas Connolly’s regular show, which mixes drinking games and stand-up, includes performances by Nick Turner and Erik Bergstrom, among others. At 8 p.m., the Creek and the Cave, 10-93 Jackson Avenue, Long Island City, 718-706-8783, creeklic.com. (Czajkowski)‘Here’s What I Would Say with Jenn & Jay: New Year’s Eve Spectacular’ (Thursday) To ring in the new year, Jenn Welch and Jay Malsky host this “evening of comedy, song and unsolicited advice,” featuring live performances, a dance party, prizes and anything else that fits the mood. (Czajkowski)‘New Year’s Eve with Rainbow Dancers’ (Wednesday) One the eve of New Year’s Eve, the comedian Jo Firestone and a crew of amateur dancers will paint their faces in the colors of the rainbow and improvise synchronized dances to holiday and pop songs in a show described as “Blue Man Group meets community theater meets poppin’ dance club.” At 11 p.m., the Upright Citizens Brigade Theater, 307 West 26th Street, Chelsea, 212-366-9176, newyork.ucbtheatre.com. (Czajkowski)‘123 123 Dance: A 45 Minute Ode to Sia’ (Sunday) Allison Frasca and Tovah Silbermann will showcase their love for the singer Sia and her hit song “Chandelier” with a melodramatic 45-minute dance routine to the song, with help from their water boy, Brandon Scott Wolf. At 7 p.m., the Peoples Improv Theater, 123 East 24th Street, Manhattan, 212-563-7488, thepit-nyc.com. (Czajkowski)Advertisement★ ‘Two Beers In’ (Tuesday) Cody Lindquist and Charlie Todd, both longtime improvisers, host this political round table with a boozy twist — all panelists will drink two beers on stage before jumping into the conversation. Panelists include the journalist Pat Kiernan and the comedians Zhubin Parang and Neil Casey, with a special appearance by Anthony Atamanuik as Donald Trump. At 9:30 p.m., Upright Citizens Brigade Theater East, 153 East Third Street, East Village, 212-366-9231, east.ucbtheatre.com.
