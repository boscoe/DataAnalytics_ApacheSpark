By KATHRYN SHATTUCKNOV. 6, 2015
At last, Friday has arrived! Viewers with limited steam can veg out to knife-wielding munchkins on “MasterChef Junior” or to a contender for the year’s best comedy. Brain cells still intact? Rachel Maddow interviews Democratic candidates; the nation’s 41st president gives up some secrets; and Quentin Tarantino explains his comment about the police.MASTER OF NONE on Netflix. How to decide? Aziz Ansari riffs on his life as Dev, a New York actor faced with a thousand choices and 999 opportunities to get it wrong. James Poniewozik, writing in The New York Times, called it “the year’s best comedy straight out of the gate.” (Image: Mr. Ansari)PALIO (2015) on iTunes. “Rocky” on horseback: That’s what Mike Hale of The Times christened Cosima Spender’s hot-to-trot documentary about the cutthroat races on the piazza of Siena, Italy.What’s On TV FridayMR. TURNER (2014) 3:55 p.m. Eastern time, on Starz Cinema. Timothy Spall growls, sputters and spits as J. M. W. Turner (1775-1851), the man the critic John Ruskin called “the greatest painter of all time.” It’s “a mighty work of critical imagination, a loving, unsentimental portrait of a rare creative soul,” A. O. Scott wrote in The Times. (Image: Mr. Spall)AdvertisementMASTERCHEF JUNIOR 8 p.m. on Fox. Four seasons in, there’s still something delightfully compelling about watching a kitchen full of youngsters — who can barely peer over the counter — geek out to a roasted-fennel gratin, beef cheek ravioli or an Earl Grey tea tart with blood-orange coulis. And about seeing Gordon Ramsay get all gooey. This latest crop of pint-size culinary wizards begins with the basics: a signature burger. (Image: Mr. Ramsay with a contestant, Vivian)AdvertisementFIRST IN THE SOUTH DEMOCRATIC CANDIDATES FORUM 8 p.m. on MSNBC. Rachel Maddow talks with Hillary Rodham Clinton; former Gov. Martin O’Malley of Maryland; and Senator Bernie Sanders of Vermont, an independent, about the economy, policing and the state of the Democratic Party.CHITA RIVERA: A LOT OF LIVIN’ TO DO 9 p.m. on PBS. Ms. Rivera struts her stuff more than 60 years after first setting foot on Broadway.FOX NEWS REPORTING: DESTINY AND POWER — THE PRIVATE DIARIES OF GEORGE HERBERT WALKER BUSH 10 p.m. on Fox News. Brit Hume unveils never-before-heard recordings kept by Mr. Bush, the nation’s 41st president, as well as interviews with him and with his son former President George W. Bush; President Obama; and former Vice Presidents Dan Quayle and Dick Cheney. Jon Meacham also discusses his new biography of the elder Mr. Bush, out next week.20/20 10 p.m. on ABC. Angelika Graswald, who is accused of killing her fiancé, Vincent Viafore, while kayaking on the Hudson River in April, speaks with Elizabeth Vargas from prison.REAL TIME WITH BILL MAHER 10 p.m. on HBO. Keith Olbermann, who bailed on Mr. Maher back in September when his dog got sick, fulfills his commitment.
