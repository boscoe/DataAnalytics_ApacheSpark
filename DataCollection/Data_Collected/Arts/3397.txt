AdvertisementSupported byThe Week Ahead | ClassicalBy Anthony TommasiniKodo, the sensational Japanese drum ensemble, plays the Brooklyn Academy of Music as part of “Kodo One Earth Tour: Mystery,” a new work. This acclaimed troupe practices a tradition of massed drumming that traces back to the sixth century and often includes other instruments and even some singing. (7:30 p.m. Thursday-Saturday, Howard Gilman Opera House, 718-636-4100, bam.org.) Speaking of percussion instruments, the piano is featured in two enticing programs at Carnegie Hall. The French pianist Pierre-Laurent Aimard is joined by the Yugoslav-born Tamara Stefanovich in a concert devoted to the complete solo piano works of Pierre Boulez, along with his Structures, Book II, for two pianos. You can count on these artists to bring out the color, fantasy and daring in Mr. Boulez’s formidably complex music. (7:30 p.m. Monday, Zankel Hall, 212-247-7800, carnegiehall.org.)Finally, at Carnegie’s Stern Auditorium, the superb Polish pianist Piotr Anderszewski plays works by Beethoven, Bartok and Schumann, including Schumann’s towering Fantasy in C.
