American Ballet Theater’s spring season at the Metropolitan Opera House will include  a wealth of works by Alexei Ratmansky and feature Misty Copeland in lead roles in “Swan Lake,” “Firebird,” “Romeo and Juliet” and “La Fille Mal Gardée,” the company announced on Friday.The season, which will run from May 9 to July 2, will feature the world premiere of a new work by Mr. Ratmansky, the company’s artist in residence, set to Leonard Bernstein’s “Serenade After Plato’s Symposium.” It will also include the American premiere of the “The Golden Cockerel,” a ballet with music by Rimsky-Korsakov that Mr. Ratmansky made for the Royal Danish Ballet in 2012, with Veronika Part and Skylar Brandt leading the first cast.The company will also perform two different Ratmansky triple-bills: his “Shostakovich Trilogy” from 2013, and a program that will include his new Bernstein work, his “Seven Sonatas” from 2009 and his “Firebird” from 2012. And Mr. Ratmansky’s back-to-Petipa production of Tchaikovsky’s “The Sleeping Beauty,” which premiered this year, will return.Maria Kochetkova, who became a principal dancer with the company this summer, will lead the first night cast of “Le Corsaire.” Gillian Murphy, Marcelo Gomes and James Whiteside will dance the leads in the first performance of Frederick Ashton’s “Sylvia.” Polina Semionova and Mr. Gomes will lead the opening night cast of “Swan Lake.” Hee Seo and Cory Stearns will lead the opening night cast of Kenneth MacMillan’s “Romeo and Juliet.” And there will be several notable role debuts. Ms. Copeland and Stella Abrera, who both became principal dancers with the company over the summer, will dance the part of Lise in Ashton’s “La Fille Mal Gardée” for the first time, as will Isabella Boylston. Cassandra Trenary, a soloist, and Ms. Abrera will both debut the role of Princess Aurora in “The Sleeping Beauty.”
