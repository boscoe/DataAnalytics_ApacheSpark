By BRIAN SCHAEFERMAY 2, 2015
The Irish writer Colum McCann may be primarily a novelist, but he knows dance, too: His 2003 novel, “Dancer,” is a tasty fictionalized biography of Rudolf Nureyev and reveals a curiosity for, and attraction to, the artful ways of the body. Mr. McCann is credited with the libretto of “Writing Ground,” which receives its New York premiere this week when the San Francisco troupe Alonzo King Lines Ballet visits the Joyce (accompanied by a second New York premiere — “Concerto for Two Violins”). Mr. McCann traded prose for poetry to better suit dance’s ungraspability; his words will be printed in the program but aren’t spoken — the soundscape is a compilation of sacred chants from a rainbow of world religions. Since 1982, Mr. King’s company has practiced his distinct brand of balletic Neo-Classicism — fervent, fluid and bearing the marks of myriad cultures. Arriving from California, it’s liquid from a parched land.
