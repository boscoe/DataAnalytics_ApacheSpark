A searchable guide to these and other shows is at nytimes.com/events.★ ‘The Andy Kaufman Award’ (Sunday) This annual award celebrates quirky comedic talent “in the spirit” of Andy Kaufman; past winners include Reggie Watts and Kristen Schaal. This year, Elayne Boosler hosts, and the finalists are Alison Rich, Ana Fabrega, Ben Kronberg, Brett Davis, Jim Hickox, Jo Firestone, Julio Tores and Nathan Barnatt. At 7:30 p.m., Upright Citizens Brigade Theater East, 153 East Third Street, East Village, 212-366-9231, east.ucbtheatre.com; sold out, but there will be a standby line. (Elise Czajkowski)Kyle Dunnigan  (Friday) A writer for Comedy Central’s “Inside Amy Schumer,” Mr. Dunnigan recently won an Emmy for the song “Girl You Don’t Need Makeup” that appeared in one of the show’s sketches. On stage, his charismatic stand-up is a mix of storytelling, impressions and music. At 8 p.m., Highline Ballroom, 431 West 16th Street, Chelsea, 212-414-5994, highlineballroom.com. (Czajkowski)★ Cameron Esposito (Friday) Ms. Esposito is widely regarded as one of the rising stars on the stand-up scene for her politically progressive comedy and focus on modern LGBT issues — her 2014 album was titled “Same Sex Symbol.” She’ll be joined here by the comedian Rhea Butcher. At 8 p.m., Gramercy Theater, 127 East 23rd Street, Gramercy, 212-614-6932, venue.thegramercytheatre.com. (Czajkowski)Bobcat Goldthwait  (Friday and Saturday) Still known to many for the hyper-energetic stage persona he adopted in the 1980s, Mr. Goldthwait has re-emerged in recent years as a thoughtful, politically minded stand-up. Friday at 8:30 and 10:30 p.m., Saturday at 8, 10 and 11:45 p.m., Gotham Comedy Club, 208 West 23rd Street, Chelsea, gothamcomedyclub.com, 212-367-9000. (Czajkowski)‘Haunting Renditions: Live with Eliot Glazer’  (Wednesday) Mr. Glazer hosts this night of comically dark takes on sugary pop tunes. His guest is the comedian Michelle Collins, a co-host on “The View.” At 8:30 p.m., the Bell House, 149 Seventh Street, Gowanus, Brooklyn, 718-643-6510, thebellhouseny.com. (Czajkowski)‘Kapow-i GoGo Marathon!’  (Sunday) Madeleine Bundy stars as Kapow-i GoGo, a teenage girl on a cartoon-and-video-game-inspired adventure to save the world many times over. The three-part, nine-episode epic will be shown in one marathon session. At 1 p.m., Peoples Improv Theater, 123 East 24th Street, Manhattan, 212-563-7488, thepit-nyc.com. (Czajkowski)★ Josie Long: ‘Cara Josephine’  (Wednesday and Thursday, and through Oct. 25) A gem of the British comedy scene, Ms. Long is a stand-up and storyteller who dwells in earnestness and optimism. “Cara Josephine,” the best-reviewed comedy show at last year’s Edinburgh Fringe Festival, is a sweet and very funny hour about relationships, love and family. Wednesday and Thursday at 9 p.m., SoHo Playhouse, 15 Vandam Street, South Village, 212-691-1555, sohoplayhouse.com. (Czajkowski)★ New York Musical Improv Festival  (Thursday through Oct. 18) Now in its seventh year, this annual festival celebrates the comedic tightrope walk that is musical improv. Troupes from around the country, including Chicago’s much-loved Baby Wants Candy, perform over four nights. Shows begin at 7 p.m. on Thursday and next Friday, and at 5 p.m. on Oct. 17-18, Magnet Theater, 254 West 29th Street, Chelsea, 212-244-8824, nymif.com. (Czajkowski)‘The Talent Show’  (Tuesday) Elna Baker and Kevin Townley host this ever-changing variety show, with each edition based on a different idea. This month’s theme is “Mysteries of the Unknown,” and performers include Jon Ronson, Hanna Cheek, Maeve Higgins, Jason Kane, Georgia Stitt, Dave Hill, Joel Kim Booster and MC Frontalot. At 9:30 p.m., Joe’s Pub, 425 Lafayette Street, Astor Place, 212-539-8778, joespub.com. (Czajkowski)Advertisement
