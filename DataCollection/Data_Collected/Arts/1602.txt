Click here for a guide to performances of Handel’s “Messiah,” and here for other seasonal celebrations. Judson Memorial Church, Washington Square South at Thompson Street, Greenwich Village. The West Village Chorale rings in the holiday season with two concerts titled “Welcome Yule!” The program includes Vivaldi’s setting of “Magnificat,” as well as Britten, Saint-Saëns and the original version of “O Holy Night” — better known as “Cantique de Noel.” (Also Sunday at 5 p.m.) At 7 p.m., 212-517-1776, westvillagechorale.org.Trinity Church (Episcopal), Broadway at Wall Street, Lower Manhattan. The Trinity Youth Chorus performs its annual holiday concert, a blend of traditional songs and favorites from the canon of popular music. The choir will be joined by students from local schools and outreach programs. At 5 p.m., 212-602-0800, trinitywallstreet.org.Cathedral Church of St. John the Divine, 1047 Amsterdam Avenue, at 112th Street, Morningside Heights. Bach’s “Magnificat,” a lively setting of the Latin text, is featured in this annual concert, led here by Malcolm Merriweather. At 7 p.m., 212-316-7540, stjohndivine.org.Madison Avenue Presbyterian Church (Episcopal), Madison Avenue at East 73rd Street. The church’s 11th annual Carol Sing includes works by Britten, Saint-Saëns and John Rutter, with performances by the Choir of Madison Avenue Presbyterian Church and New York City Children’s Chorus. Andrew Henderson and Mary Huff conduct. At 4 p.m., 212-288-8920, mapc.com.St. Peter’s Chelsea, 346 West 20th Street. Chelsea Community Church, a nondenominational group that shares its space with St. Peter’s, presents its annual candlelight carol service, directed by Larry Long. The organist Christopher Houlihan and a string quartet will also perform in the program, which includes carols from the 15th to 21st centuries. At 6 p.m., 212-886-5463, chelseachurch.org.Corpus Christi Church (Roman Catholic), Broadway at West 121st Street, Morningside Heights. As part of the series Music Before 1800, this church and the period ensemble Juilliard415 present Bach’s buoyant “Magnificat,” as well as Corelli’s “Christmas Concerto.” At 4 p.m., 212-666-9266, mb1800.org.St. Bartholomew’s Church (Episcopal), Park Avenue at East 51st Street. Great Music at St. Bart’s presents “A Joyous Christmas Concert,” featuring the St. Bartholomew’s Choir and the Boy and Girl Choristers. The program includes the Christmas portion of Handel’s “Messiah,” as well as Britten’s “A Ceremony of Carols.” At 7:30 p.m., 212-378-0222, mmpaf.org.St. Patrick’s Cathedral (Roman Catholic), Fifth Avenue at 50th Street. “A City Singing at Christmas,” an annual concert hosted by the church, includes St. Patrick’s Cathedral Choir, the Young People’s Chorus of New York City, Queens College Choir and the organists Daniel Brondel and Michael Hey. At the end of the program, the church will be lit by candles while “Silent Night” is sung. At 7 p.m., 212-753-2261, saintpatrickscathedral.org.St. Thomas Church Fifth Avenue (Episcopal), Fifth Avenue at 53rd Street. The Boys of St. Thomas Choir, led by Stephen Buzard and with Bridget Kibbey on the harp, performs Britten’s “A Ceremony of Carols” and John Rutter’s “Dancing Day.” At 5:30 p.m., 212-757-7013, saintthomaschurch.org.Church of the Transfiguration (Episcopal), 1 East 29th Street, Manhattan. For the Christmas Candlelight Concert, the Transfiguration Choir of Men and Boys, the Girls Choir and Camerata will perform a staged production of Gian Carlo Menotti’s “Amahl and the Night Visitors.” The program also includes Britten’s “Ceremony of Carols.” Claudia Dumschat, organist and choirmaster, conducts. At 7:30 p.m., 212-684-4174, littlechurch.org.West End Presbyterian Church, Amsterdam Avenue at West 105th Street. This church’s annual Christmas concert features an all-vocal program by the church’s gospel choir, led by the music director John Bowen. At 5 p.m., 212-663-2900, westendchurchnyc.org.Middle Collegiate Church (Reformed Church in America), 112 Second Avenue, at Seventh Street, East Village. Tami Petty, the church’s music director, presents a mix of gospel and traditional music, including “Whatcha Gonna Call That Pretty Little Baby,” “Infant Holy” and Quincy Jones’s “A Soulful Celebration.” At 2:30 p.m., 212-477-0666, middlechurch.org.Church of the Intercession (Episcopal), Broadway at 155th Street, Washington Heights. This church’s tradition of a candlelight service with Clement Clarke Moore’s “A Visit From St. Nicholas” (commonly called “’Twas the Night Before Christmas”) features the Young People’s Chorus of New York City at Washington Heights, the Boys and Girls Choir of Harlem Alumni Ensemble, the Handbell Choir from the Church of the Good Shepherd in the Bronx and the Choir of the Church of the Intercession. Prelude at 3 p.m., service at 4, 212-283-6200, intercessionnyc.org.Church of the Ascension (Episcopal), Fifth Avenue at 10th Street, Greenwich Village. Dennis Keene, the organist and choirmaster, leads the Ascension Choir in a program that includes Bruckner, Rachmaninoff, Haydn and Ralph Vaughan Williams. At 9:30 p.m., 212-254-8620, ascensionnyc.org.Church of the Epiphany (Episcopal), York Avenue at East 74th Street, Manhattan. The Epiphany Choir and a string quartet, led by the organist and choirmaster Larry Long, offer a Christmas Eve service with a program that includes the centuries-old “Gloria in Excelsis Deo” by Hans Leo Hassler and Ralph Vaughan Williams’s “The Blessed Son of God.” At 10 p.m., prelude at 9:30 p.m., 212-737-2720, epiphanynyc.org.Church of St. Ignatius Loyola (Roman Catholic), 980 Park Avenue, at 84th Street. This church offers several Christmas Eve services start at 4 p.m. with the children’s choirs and Wallace Hall Choir performing Bach and carols. At 6 p.m., Bach is back on the program with Robert Reuter leading the Parish Community Choir, and at 8 p.m. the church’s choir and orchestra, led by K. Scott Warren, perform Bach’s “In Dulci Jubilo” along with arrangements of other classical songs. For midnight Mass, the choir and orchestra are back for a programthat includes Vivaldi and Mozart. 212-288-3588, stignatiusloyola.org.Church of St. Luke in the Fields (Episcopal), Hudson Street at Grove Street, West Village. First, a pageant: At 5 p.m., the church presents a program that includes John Rutter’s “Nativity Carol.” At 10 p.m., with a musical prelude at 9:30 p.m., the music director and organist David Shuler leads the Choir of St. Luke in the Fields for the festive choral Eucharist. 212-924-0562, stlukeinthefields.org.Church of St. Mary the Virgin (Episcopal), 145 West 46th Street, Manhattan. Services for this church begin at 4 p.m., with music for choir and organ, including works by Byrd and Holst; Simon Richard Walley is the organist and music director. A sung Mass begins at 5 p.m. with music by the 16th-century sacred music composer Giovanni Pierluigi da Palestrina. The choral and organ music returns at 10 p.m., followed at 11 by a Solemn Mass that includes music by Poulenc and a procession of the crèche. 212-869-5830, stmvirgin.org.Corpus Christi Church (Roman Catholic), 529 West 121st Street, Morningside Heights. The church’s choir, led by the music director and organist Louise Basbas, performs motets and other choral music dating to the 1500s and 1600s. The program also includes Bach’s “Vom Himmel hoch.” At 11:30 p.m., 212-666-9350, corpus-christi-nyc.org.Fifth Avenue Presbyterian Church, Fifth Avenue at West 55th Street. This candlelight service includes a mix of 20th-century Christmas songs and arrangements of traditional works, like John Rutter’s arrangement of “O Holy Night.” Ryan Jackson leads the choir and soloists, with Patrick Kreeger on organ. At 7:30 and 11 p.m. 212-247-0490, fapc.org.Madison Avenue Presbyterian Church, Madison Avenue at East 73rd Street. Andrew Henderson directs a program that includes excerpts from Saint-Saëns’s “Oratorio de Noël” and Stephen Jackson’s “Noël Nouvelet.” Instrumental pieces include excerpts from Marcel Tournier’s “Six Noëls pour Harpe” and Louis-Claude Daquin’s “Noël Suisse” for organ. At 10:30 p.m., prelude at 10:10 p.m., 212-288-8920, mapc.com.Parish of Cavalry-St. George’s (Episcopal), A family-friendly service in which Sunday school children perform the music will be at 4 p.m. at St. George’s Episcopal Church, 4 Rutherford Place, Manhattan. At 8 and 11 p.m. (with a prelude at 10:40 p.m.), Kamel Boutros leads the music for Christmas Eve services at the Cavalry Church, Park Avenue at East 21st Street. 646-723-4178, calvarystgeorges.org.St. James’s Church (Episcopal), Madison Avenue at East 71st Street. Services begin at 5 p.m. (choral prelude at 4:30 p.m.), with the Compostela and Canterbury Choirs performing carols and choral works like Peter Warlock’s “Benedicamus Domino.” At 10:30 p.m. (prelude at 10 p.m.), the Compostela Choir performs Mozart, selections from Handel’s “Messiah” and an arrangement of “O Holy Night.” 212-774-4200, stjames.org.St. Thomas Church (Episcopal), Fifth Avenue at 53rd Street. At 4 p.m., the church presents “A Festival of Nine Lessons and Carols,” a program that includes John Tavener. At 10:15 p.m., Scott McIntosh plays trumpet and Benjamin Sheen joins him on the organ for “Festival Music for Trumpet and Organ.” Shortly after, at 11 p.m., the Eucharist for the Nativity begins, with music by Handel and carols including works by John Rutter. At 4 p.m. 212-757-7013, saintthomaschurch.org.Trinity Church, Broadway at Wall Street, Lower Manhattan. The Trinity Orchestra and Choir of Trinity Wall Street perform a choral prelude and festive Eucharist, with a program that includes works by Britten and Poulenc, among others. The service will be immediately followed by a midnight Mass (starting at 11:45 p.m.), that includes Britten’s “Hymn to the Virgin.” At 9:30 p.m., 212-602-0800, trinitywallstreet.org.A sampling of holiday presentations at churches, cultural centers and concert halls in New York City, compiled by Joshua Barone.
