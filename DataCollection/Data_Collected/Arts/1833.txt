AdvertisementSupported byBy Kathryn ShattuckHarold and Kumar ring in the holiday on a high note, on an evening with something festive for just about every persuasion.A VERY HAROLD & KUMAR 3D CHRISTMAS (2011) 8 p.m. on MTV. Reunited by a mysterious package after a long estrangement, Harold and Kumar, those kings of recreational drug use played by John Cho and Kal Penn, search New York for the perfect Christmas tree after Kumar accidentally destroys the decorations of Harold’s father-in-law. Along for the ride: the baby of Harold’s new best friend. Neil Patrick Harris returns to sing, dance and talk dirty. “He, that stoned baby and a stunning riff on the tongue-stuck-to-a-pole scene in ‘A Christmas Story’ will, for fans of this franchise, make this a blissful holiday season indeed,” Neil Genzlinger wrote in The New York Times.THE SIMPSONS MINI-MARATHON 5 p.m. on FXX. A stocking’s worth of Christmas episodes — among them, “Marge Be Not Proud,” “Miracle on Evergreen Terrace,” “Grift of the Magi” and “Skinner’s Sense of Snow” — followed by “The Simpsons Movie” at 8.A CHRISTMAS CAROL (1951) 7 p.m. on FX Movie Channel. The Dickens classic, starring Alastair Sim as Scrooge, Mervyn Johns as Bob Cratchit and Glyn Dearman as Tiny Tim, and repeated throughout the evening.A CHRISTMAS STORY (1983) 7 p.m. on TNT; 8 p.m. on TBS. The annual 24-hour marathon of the tale of Ralphie, who wants only a Red Ryder air rifle for Christmas.THE BISHOP’S WIFE (1947) 8 p.m. on TCM. David Niven is Henry Brougham, an Episcopal bishop who prays for help in raising money to build a new cathedral. Cary Grant is Dudley, the dapper angel who hears his plea. But soon enough, Brougham starts to wonder if Dudley is out to replace him.IT’S A WONDERFUL LIFE (1946) 8 p.m. on NBC. Frank Capra’s enduring package of cheer, starring James Stewart as a suicidal businessman with a guardian angel.MAY PEACE PREVAIL ON EARTH: AN INTERFAITH CHRISTMAS SPECIAL 11:35 p.m. on CBS. Scripture readings, music and carols from St. Gregory’s of Nyssa Episcopal Church in San Francisco in a service that celebrates a world of traditions.THE CHRISTMAS EVE MASS FROM ST. PETER’S BASILICA 11:35 p.m. on NBC. Pope Francis celebrates midnight Mass at the Vatican.A VERY MURRAY CHRISTMAS on Netflix. Come holiday time it’s the small, perfectly imperfect gifts we often come back to. And we’re coming back to this one, starring Bill Murray, as a version of himself, whose cabaret act at the Carlyle Hotel goes bust when New York is shut down by the blizzard of the century. So he improvises, with the help of his pianist sidekick, Paul Shaffer, as well as Rashida Jones, Amy Poehler, Maya Rudolph, Chris Rock, David Johansen and — in the fantasy climax — Miley Cyrus and George Clooney. It’s a little boozy, a little woozy, but what’s a couple of cracked notes among friends?Advertisement
