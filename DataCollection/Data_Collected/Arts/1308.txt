A searchable guide to these and other shows is at nytimes.com/events.★ ‘Andy Kindler – Live and Even More Impersonal’ (Saturday) A stand-up known for criticizing fellow comedians or the state of comedy entirely, Mr. Kindler was recently described by Jason Zinoman in The New York Times as “a fierce ombudsman of comedy.” At 8 and 10 p.m., Union Hall, 702 Union Street, at Fifth Avenue, Park Slope, Brooklyn, 718-638-4400, unionhallny.com. (Elise Czajkowski)★ Dave Attell (Friday and Saturday) A revered figure in the stand-up community, Mr. Attell is a 30-year veteran known for his raunchy, unapologetic style and willingness to push the boundaries of good taste. At 7:30 and 10 p.m., Carolines, 1626 Broadway, at 49th Street, Manhattan, 212-757-4100, carolines.com. (Czajkowski)‘Giant Night of Comedy’ (Monday) Rashad Jennings of the New York Giants hosts this celebrity-studded fund-raiser, which benefits his literacy and community outreach charity. Along with appearances by several of Mr. Jennings’s teammates, the event includes performances by the comedians Pete Davidson of “Saturday Night Live,” Hasan Minhaj of “The Daily Show,” Jim Norton, Michael Bocchetti and John Melendez. At 8:30 p.m., Gotham Comedy Club, 208 West 23rd Street, Chelsea, 212-367-9000, gothamcomedyclub.com. (Czajkowski)Advertisement★ ‘Oh, Hello’ (Tuesday through Dec. 20) Nick Kroll and John Mulaney bring their memorable characters Gil Faizon (Mr. Kroll) and George St. Geegland (Mr. Mulaney), two Upper West Side gentleman with a fondness for Alan Alda and pranks involving tuna fish, to the stage. The duo have been part of the “Oh, Hello” show for more than a decade, having appeared in a web series before being prominently featured in Mr. Kroll’s Comedy Central sketch series, “Kroll Show.” Tuesdays through Saturdays at 9:30 p.m., Sundays at 7 p.m., Cherry Lane Theater, 38 Commerce Street, West Village, 866-811-4111, cherrylanetheatre.org. (Czajkowski)AdvertisementNick Kroll & John Mulaney (Sunday) Just before their sold-out Off Broadway run of “Oh, Hello” begins at the Cherry Lane Theater, Mr. Kroll (Comedy Central’s “Kroll Show”) and Mr. Mulaney — who just released his new stand-up special, “The Comeback Kid”, on Netflix — perform together for three unpredictable nights of comedy. At 11 p.m., Upright Citizens Brigade Theater, 307 West 26th Street, Chelsea, 212-366-9176, newyork.ucbtheatre.com; sold out, but a standby line will be available. (Czajkowski)‘Thanksgiving Hangover’ (Friday) Mike Kelton and a crew of improvisers will create an entire show based on an audience member’s Thanksgiving experience. Other performers include Lindsay Calleran, Sebastian Conelli, Sal Gentile, James III, Jackie Jennings, Casey Jost, Lisa Kleinman and Matt Mayer. At 9 p.m., Upright Citizens Brigade Theater East, 153 East Third Street, East Village, 212-366-9231, east.ucbtheatre.com. (Czajkowski)★ Nick Thune (Tuesday) Mr. Thune’s approach to comedy is unique, blending one distinct style – absurdist one-liners and accompanying music – with long-form, often deeply personal storytelling. The result is an always intriguing night of comedy. At 10:30 p.m., Union Hall, 702 Union Street, at Fifth Avenue, Park Slope, Brooklyn, 718-638-4400, unionhallny.com. (Czajkowski)‘Warm! Hugs!’ (Tuesday) In support of the 2015 New York Cares Coat Drive, this show will accept a winter coat (or a $10 donation) as admission to this excellent stand-up and storytelling show. The lineup includes Todd Barry, Ted Alexandro, Christian Finnegan and Stuckey & Murray. At 7:30 p.m., Le Poisson Rouge, 158 Bleecker Street, near Thompson Street, Greenwich Village, 212-505-3474, lepoissonrouge.com. If planning to donate a coat, R.S.V.P. to hello@hikercompany.com. (Czajkowski)Roy Wood Jr. (Friday and Saturday) Mr. Wood has been one of the breakout stars from the new cast members of “The Daily Show With Trevor Noah.” In his stand-up, he’s known for his ability to play to all audiences with an affable blend of political, social, and personal material. Friday at 8:30 and 10:30 p.m., Saturday at 8 and 10 p.m., Gotham Comedy Club, 208 West 23rd Street, Chelsea, 212-367-9000, gothamcomedyclub.com.
