A searchable guide to these and other shows is at nytimes.com/events.Katina Corrao(Tuesday) Ms. Corrao is a staple of the New York stand-up scene, and here she celebrates the release of her new album, “Hot Date.” Other performers include Shonali Bhowmik, Michelle Collins, John Early, Nick Turner, Sara Juliana Benincasa, Jon Friedman and Phoebe Robinson. At 7:30 p.m., the HiFi Bar, 169 Avenue A, at East 11th Street, East Village, 212-420-8392, thehifibar.com. (Elise Czajkowski)‘Fear Bazaar’(Thursday) The Upright Citizens Brigade’s East Village location hosts this annual immersive theater experience, drawing on the audience’s deepest fears in an event described as “a chamber of modern terrors and social anxieties.” Shows begin every two hours from 2 p.m. to midnight, 153 East Third Street, East Village, 212-366-9231, east.ucbtheatre.com. (Czajkowski)★ Josie Long: ‘Cara Josephine’(through Sunday) A gem of the British comedy scene, Ms. Long is a stand-up and storyteller who dwells in earnestness and optimism. “Cara Josephine,” the best-reviewed comedy show at last year’s Edinburgh Fringe Festival, is a sweet and very funny hour about relationships, love and family. Friday and Saturday at 9 p.m., Sunday at 3 p.m., SoHo Playhouse, 15 Vandam Street, South Village, 212-691-1555, sohoplayhouse.com. (Czajkowski)AdvertisementHasan Minhaj: ‘Homecoming King’Thursdays through Sundays, through Nov. 15) Mr. Minhaj, a correspondent on Comedy Central’s “The Daily Show,” presents this one-man performance about his experience as a first-generation immigrant and “his family’s quest to achieve the elusive American dream.” At various times, Cherry Lane Theater, 38 Commerce Street, West Village, 212-989-2020, cherrylanetheatre.org. (Czajkowski)‘Night Late’(Wednesday) Every month, a team of writers selects a performer to host one episode of a talk show designed entirely for his or her personality. This month’s comic is Naomi Ekperigin, who also recently wrote for Comedy Central’s “Broad City.” At 11 p.m., the Upright Citizens Brigade Theater, 307 West 26th Street, Chelsea, 212-366-9176, newyork.ucbtheatre.com. At 8 p.m., Mercury Lounge, 217 East Houston Street, at Ludlow Street, Lower East Side, 800-745-3000, mercuryloungenyc.com. (Czajkowski)David Spade in Conversation with Colin Quinn(Sunday) Mr. Spade, the actor and comedian known for his work on “Saturday Night Live” and “Just Shoot Me,” sits down with Mr. Quinn, a fellow “S.N.L.” alumnus, as part of the 92nd Street Y’s Funny People Series. The topic of discussion will be Mr. Spade’s new memoir, “Almost Interesting”; a book signing will follow. At 7:30 p.m. 92nd Street Y, 1395 Lexington Avenue, 212-415-5500, 92y.org. (Czajkowski)Advertisement‘You Don’t Know the Half’(Thursday) Two of the city’s finest rising comics, Janelle James and Kerry Coddett, each perform a half-hour of stand-up.
