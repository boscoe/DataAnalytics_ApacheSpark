Don’t let the parade pass you by! Macy’s kicks off its 89th extravaganza. Also on your Thanksgiving platter: serenades by Billy Joel and Arlo Guthrie, a train ride to nowhere, and Stellan Skarsgard as a cop who sees dead people.What’s On TVMACY’S THANKSGIVING DAY PARADE 9 a.m. on NBC. Even big kids might get a little lump in the throat as more than 8,000 balloons, bands and performers snake their way through the canyons of Manhattan. Matt Lauer, Savannah Guthrie and Al Roker host this 89th extravaganza. Stepping lively: Pat Benatar, Mariah Carey, Cirque du Soleil, Daughtry, Jennifer Nettles, Trey Songz, Panic! at the Disco and Questlove, with the Muppets of “Sesame Street” as well as the Broadway casts of “School of Rock,” “Fiddler on the Roof,” “The King and I” and “Something Rotten!,” and a sneak performance from NBC’s forthcoming “The Wiz Live!” (Image: SpongeBob SquarePants in 2014)A THANKSGIVING STATE OF MIND: THE BILLY JOEL MARATHON 6 a.m. on MSG. Twenty-four hours of the Piano Man, including seven concerts — from “The Old Grey Whistle Test” (1978) on the BBC to “Last Play at Shea” (2008) — and the documentary “Billy Joel: Home at the Garden,” which ventures behind the scenes of his record-setting Madison Square Garden performances. (Image: Mr. Joel at the Garden in December 2014)RAILROAD ALASKA: REAL TIME TRAIN RIDE 9 a.m. on Destination America. And you thought the Yule log was boring: five hours of snowy landscape captured by a camera mounted on the front of a train. Back in 1965, Arlo Guthrie threw out the trash after a Thanksgiving dinner and ended up arrested for littering and rejected from military service. The result was “Alice’s Restaurant,” an 18-minute musical monologue that became a platinum-selling record in 1967 and a feature film two years later. Here, Mr. Guthrie brings the work to the stage for the first time in a decade.AdvertisementBROADWAY AT THE WHITE HOUSE 8 p.m. on TLC. He sees dead people: Stellan Skarsgard stars as the troubled police inspector John Rivers, who just can’t get his former partner (Nicola Walker) out of his mind. So he taps into hallucinations — he calls them “manifests” — to heal his wounded psyche. Abi Morgan (“The Iron Lady,” “Suffragette”) wrote this BBC series.MYSTERY SCIENCE THEATER 3000 TURKEY DAY MARATHON Noon on MST3K. In this Shout!
