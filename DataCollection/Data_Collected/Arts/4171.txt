A searchable guide to these and other shows is at nytimes.com/events.★ Simon Amstell  (Friday and Saturday) Mr. Amstell, a British comedian and storyteller, is known for his insightful and fearlessly intimate shows. Now he returns to the States with his new hour, “To Be Free.” Friday and Saturday at 8 p.m., Theater 80, 80 St. Marks Place, near First Avenue, East Village, 212-388-0388, theatre80.net. (Elise Czajkowski)‘Rob Cantrell and Murderfist Save the Earth’  (Monday) Mr. Cantrell, a stand-up, teams up with the anarchic sketch troupe Murderfist for a salute to Earth Day and April 20’s other holiday, a celebration of marijuana. Other performers include Nick Vatterott and Michelle Wolf, alongside the dance music duo Unicorn Smack. At 8 p.m., Knitting Factory Brooklyn, 361 Metropolitan Avenue, at Havemeyer Street, Williamsburg, 347-529-6696, bk.knittingfactory.com. (Czajkowski)‘John Hamm’  (Wednesday) John Early and Hamm Samwich team up again for another night of music and comedy “in a shameless ploy for visibility.” At 8 p.m., Union Hall, 702 Union Street, at Fifth Avenue, Park Slope, Brooklyn, 718-638-4400, unionhallny.com. (Czajkowski)‘Kornfeld & Andrews’ (Sunday) Louis Kornfeld and Rick Andrews, veteran performers and instructors at the Magnet Theater, perform at their regular Sunday night long-form improv show. At 9 p.m., Magnet Theater, 254 West 29th Street, Chelsea, 212-244-8824, magnettheater.com. (Czajkowski)Advertisement★ Marcus Monroe  (Friday and Saturday) Mr. Monroe, a comedian and juggler, is one of the most enjoyable performers to watch in New York City. As part of the Week at the Creek series, he runs his hourlong show, “Famous,” about his attempt to become the world’s first famous juggler. At 7 p.m., the Creek and the Cave, 10-93 Jackson Avenue, Long Island City, Queens, 718-706-8783, creeklic.com; free. (Czajkowski)AdvertisementJoe Rogan  (Friday) Mr. Rogan has had an eclectic career, from his time as a cast member on “NewsRadio” to his gig hosting “Fear Factor” to his daily long-form video podcast, “The Joe Rogan Experience.” Onstage, he’s an intense stand-up who mixes observational comedy with sharp political views. Writers Showcase  (Thursday) Bryan Tucker, one of the head writers on “Saturday Night Live,” hosts this showcase of some of his fellow writers from the long-running show. At 8 p.m., Q.E.D., 27-16 23rd Avenue, at 28th Street, Astoria, Queens, 347-451-3873, qedastoria.com. (Czajkowski)★ ‘Adrienne Truscott’s Asking for It’ (Saturday) Ms. Truscott’s bold, award-winning show is an hourlong examination of rape jokes in the modern stand-up scene, which she performs naked from the waist down. At midnight, Joe’s Pub, at the Public Theater, 425 Lafayette Street, at Astor Place, East Village, 212-967-7555, joespub.com. (Czajkowski)‘The Terry Withers Mysteries’ (Thursday) In this twist on the improv show, the audience creates a crime scene for Mr. Withers, who then goes about trying to solve the murder mystery with the help of fellow improvisers and actors. At 9:30 p.m., Upright Citizens Brigade Theater, 307 West 26th Street, Chelsea, 212-366-9176, newyork.ucbtheatre.com.
