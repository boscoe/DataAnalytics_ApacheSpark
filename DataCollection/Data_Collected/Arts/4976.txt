A searchable guide to these and other shows is at nytimes.com/events.★ Ted Alexandro  (Monday through May 30) Mr. Alexandro has long been a gem of the New York City club scene, an intelligent, socially active stand-up with an appealing stage presence that succeeds with all sorts of audiences. Here, he performs an hour each night as part of the Week at the Creek series. At 7 p.m., the Creek and the Cave, 10-93 Jackson Avenue, near 49th Avenue Long Island City, Queens, 718-706-8783, creeklic.com.‘Comedy at the Knitting Factory’ (Sunday) Will Miles, Clark Jones and Kenny DeForest host this free and ever-reliable stand-up show; this week’s comics include Luis J. Gomez, Jabari Davis, Selena Coppock, Julio Torres, Grant Gordon and Robert Dean. At 9 p.m., Knitting Factory Brooklyn, 361 Metropolitan Avenue, at Havemeyer Street, Williamsburg, 347-529-6696, bk.knittingfactory.com.★ ‘Late Night Writer Review’  (Tuesday) Jon Fisch presents this look at writers and performers from New York-based late-night shows, with comedy from Jeff Maurer of “Last Week Tonight,” Travon Free of “The Daily Show,” Michelle Wolf of “Late Night,” Luke Cunningham of “The Tonight Show” and Sasheer Zamata of “Saturday Night Live.” At 7:30 p.m., Gotham Comedy Club, 208 West 23rd Street, Chelsea, 212-367-9000, gothamcomedyclub.com. This month’s stand-ups are Sasheer Zamata, Julio Torres, John Early, Michelle Biloon, Nick Rutherford and Nore Davis; the cartoonists are Dan Pinto, Gabe Pinto, Dimitri Drjuchin, Mike Wechsler, Chrissy Fellmeth, Brett Hall and Bryan Brinkman.
