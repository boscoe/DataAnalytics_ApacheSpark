By NATE CHINENDEC. 10, 2015
Luis Miguel is celebrated across several continents as an irresistibly suave balladeer — an expert delivery system for imploring boleros and rancheras románticas, strong of voice and single-minded in purpose. Born of Mexican heritage in Puerto Rico, he has been an arena headliner for long enough that his devoted fans have come to expect clockwork efficiency from him: tailored suits, soaring entreaties, a slick and potent performance.But a series of disruptions has troubled the air around Mr. Miguel, 45, in recent weeks. He disclosed problems with his hearing, related to tinnitus, and scrapped a string of concerts in Mexico City last month, citing a viral throat infection. All of which raises the stakes for his return to Madison Square Garden on Friday, Dec. 18 — along with the possibility that his tortured love song “Si Tú Te Atreves” could carry an undertone of redemption.
