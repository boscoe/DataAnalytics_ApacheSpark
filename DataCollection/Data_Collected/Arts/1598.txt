Click here for a guide to performances of Handel’s “Messiah,” and here for musical presentations at local churches. Harlem School of the Arts, 645 St. Nicholas Avenue, Hamilton Heights. In “Soul Nativity,” a timeless tale gets modern treatment as the school’s artistic director, Alfred Preisser, turns a Christmas pageant into a gospel extravaganza. Through Dec. 20; Fridays at 7 p.m., Saturdays at 3 and 7 p.m., Sundays at 3 p.m., 212-926-4100, hsanyc.org/soul-nativity.Brooklyn Center for the Performing Arts, Brooklyn College, 2900 Campus Road, Flatbush-Ditmas Park. The Vienna Boys Choir presents “Christmas in Vienna,” a program of Austrian folk songs, classics of the choral canon and holiday-themed favorites. At 8 p.m., 718-951-4500, brooklyncenter.org.Henry’s Restaurant, Broadway and West 105th Street. New York Festival of Song’s “A Goyishe Christmas to You!” is founded on the premise that Jewish composers have contributed a vast number of songs to the Yuletide canon. The program includes “I’ll Be Home for Christmas” and “White Christmas.” At 10 p.m., 212-866-0600, nyfos.org.AdvertisementCathedral Church of St. John the Divine, 1047 Amsterdam Avenue, at 112th Street, Morningside Heights. The Grammy Award-winning saxophonist Paul Winter presents his “Winter Solstice,” a program of music and dance that is a modern take on ancient rituals to celebrate the longest night of the year. At 8 p.m. (also next Friday at 8 p.m. and Dec. 19 at 2 and 7:30 p.m.), 212-316-7540, paulwinter.com.AdvertisementSymphony Space, Broadway at West 95th Street. The Irish Arts Center heads uptown for its annual presentation of “An Irish Christmas: A Musical Solstice Celebration,” an evening of storytelling, dance and music. The Jazz Conceptions Orchestra and this church’s parish choir, led by the music directors Kamel Boutros and Alex Nguyen, present “A Christmas Tapestry,” a blend of classical music and big-band jazz. At 7:30 and 9:30 p.m., 646-723-4178, olmstedsalon.com.Grace Rainey Rogers Auditorium, Metropolitan Museum of Art. This showing of Charles M. Schulz’s “A Charlie Brown Christmas” includes live accompaniment by the Rob Schwimmer Trio, as well as a singalong. At 11 a.m. and 1:30 p.m. (also at 1 and 3 p.m. on Dec. 20); 212-570-3949, metmuseum.org.Madison Avenue Presbyterian Church, Madison Avenue at East 73rd Street. In a program dedicated to the film composer James Horner, the Pipes of Christmas presents a holiday concert that blends drums and pipes with the brass-and-string sounds of Scotland, Ireland and Wales. At 2 and 7 p.m., pipesofchristmas.com.Judson Memorial Church, Washington Square South at Thompson Street, Greenwich Village. The West Village Chorale makes its way through Greenwich Village, starting at the Judson Memorial Church, for its annual Caroling Walk — a tradition that goes back more than 40 years. After the musical stroll, refreshments will be served at the church as the carols continue indoors. At 4:30 p.m., 212-517-1776, westvillagechorale.org.Church of the Intercession Broadway at 155th Street, Washington Heights. This annual candlelight service includes a reading of Clement Clarke Moore’s “A Visit From St. Nicholas,” (commonly called “’Twas the Night Before Christmas”), a visit to the author’s tomb and performances by choirs like the Young People’s Chorus of New York City at Washington Heights, the Boys and Girls Choir of Harlem Alumni Ensemble, the Handbell Choir from the Church of the Good Shepherd in the Bronx and the Choir of the Church of the Intercession.
