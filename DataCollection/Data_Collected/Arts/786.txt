By KATHRYN SHATTUCKNOV. 3, 2015
 TALK TO HER (2002) 6:05 p.m. E.S.T. on Starz Cinema. Talk to her; just don’t expect her to talk back: Two men (a nurse and a journalist) share an unusual friendship while they care for their girlfriends (a dancer and a matador) who are both comatose. But not all devotion is what it seems, and suddenly romance is tinged with horror. Like all great doomed affairs, Pedro Almodóvar’s tragic comedy “is full of lovely, sweet suffering,” Elvis Mitchell wrote in The New York Times. (Image: Leonor Watling, left, and Geraldine Chaplin)GRANDFATHERED 8 p.m. on Fox. Gerald can’t make it to Edie’s interview at the neighborhood’s most coveted preschool, so Jimmy goes instead. And suddenly one little girl has two dads. In “The Grinder,” at 8:30 p.m., Dean is about to quit love when an old flame re-enters his life. She looks a lot like Christina Applegate. And in “Scream Queens,” at 9 p.m., Dean Munsch collides with her ex-husband’s mistress, and the Kappas use a Ouija board to speak with Chanel No. 2.THE MUPPETS 8 p.m. on ABC. Miss Piggy helps Kermit find the perfect birthday gift for Denise. Kristin Chenoweth breaks up the band. In “Fresh Off the Boat,” at 9 p.m., Louis worries that he has brought shame to his family. (Image: Miss Piggy and Ms. Chenoweth)SKYFALL (2012) 8 p.m. on Syfy. Daniel Craig fills his suit nicely as Bond, James Bond, who is intent on outmaneuvering a former MI6 operative with a bloodthirst for Judi Dench’s M. The baddie this time: “an exceptional, wittily venal Javier Bardem,” who turns out to be “the most spectacular” of Sam Mendes’s special effects, Manohla Dargis wrote in The Times.
