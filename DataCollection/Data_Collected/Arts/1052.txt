By KATHRYN SHATTUCKNOV. 11, 2015
Sex, blood, dirty money. “Smuggled, stolen and forged art is a $10 billion business,” intones a voice more Bensonhurst than Beekman Place. “The big collectors are more than willing to look the other way.” That’s where Graham Connor (Christian Cooke) — a former soldier in Iraq turned antiquities thief who has maneuvered his way into a top Manhattan auction house — comes in. “I get them to look my way,” he says in “The Art of More,” a new series streaming Thursday, Nov. 19, on Crackle.The conspicuous consumers include Arthur Davenport (Cary Elwes) and Samuel Brukner (Dennis Quaid, an executive producer), neither of whom harbor many moral qualms about the ugly side of beautiful objects. Kate Bosworth plays the preternaturally calm competitor with her own secrets. The ripped-from-the-headlines merchandise: a Ferrari owned by Steve McQueen, $10 million worth of gold coins dug up in a couple’s garden and relics from Capt. Robert Falcon Scott’s doomed expedition to the South Pole.
