LOS ANGELES — Big-budget, visual effects-driven summer movies will be packed cheek by jowl for four months starting Friday, with the release of “Avengers: Age of Ultron.” So it was the box office calm before the storm over the weekend, with no major movies arriving to challenge “Furious 7,” which was the No. 1 movie in North America for the fourth week in a row.The Times critic A. O. Scott reviews “Furious 7.”“Furious 7” (Universal Pictures) took in an estimated $18.3 million over the weekend, for a new domestic total of $320.5 million, according to Rentrak, which compiles box office data. “Paul Blart: Mall Cop 2” (Sony Pictures) was steady in second place, selling about $15.5 million in tickets, for a two-week total of $43.9 million.“The Age of Adaline,” the weekend’s only new wide-release film, generated roughly $13.4 million from 2,991 theaters. A period romance starring Blake Lively, who is mostly known for her television work, “The Age of Adaline” cost Lionsgate and Lakeshore Entertainment about $30 million to make.Of note: “Little Boy” (Open Road), a light period drama aimed at religious audiences, took in an insubstantial $2.8 million from 1,045 theaters.
