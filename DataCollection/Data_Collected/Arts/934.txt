LOS ANGELES — Netflix will provide financing for the Korean director Bong Joon-ho’s adventure film “Okja,” which will be co-produced by Plan B Entertainment, Mr. Bong said in a statement. The film, about the travels of a girl and a friendly beast, is expected to cost about $50 million, and will feature Jake Gyllenhaal, Tilda Swinton, Bill Nighy and others. For Netflix, the investment is a nod toward its ambitions in Asia. It is starting out in South Korea next year. The streaming service is also financing “Crouching Tiger Hidden Dragon 2″ and financed the Weinstein Company’s “Marco Polo” television series, which was shot in Malaysia. Plan B, of which Brad Pitt is a founder, is known for feature films like “World War Z” and “12 Years a Slave.” Mr. Bong is best known for “Snowpiercer,” a post-apocalyptic film from 2014 that took in more than $86 million at the worldwide box office.  “For Okja, I needed a bigger budget than I had with ‘Snowpiercer’ and total creative freedom,” Mr. Bong said in a statement. “Netflix provided me with both.”A version of this article appears in print on 11/11/2015, on page C3 of the NewYork edition with the headline: Netflix, Eyeing Asia,Is to Finance Korean Film.
