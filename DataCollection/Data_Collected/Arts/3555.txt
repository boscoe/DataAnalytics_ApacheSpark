A searchable guide to these and other shows is at nytimes.com/events.‘ComedyJuice’ (Tuesday) This reliable stand-up show presents a first-rate lineup, with Todd Barry, Christian Finnegan, Dan St. Germain, Dustin Ybarra and Carmen Lynch. At 9:30 p.m., Gotham Comedy Club, 208 West 23rd Street, Chelsea, 212-367-9000, gothamcomedyclub.com. (Elise Czajkowski)‘Des Bishop: Made in China’ (Fridays through Sundays and Tuesday through Thursday) Mr. Bishop moved to China with the intention of ultimately performing stand-up in Mandarin. In this show, he recounts the year he spent there, during which he worked as a host in a restaurant and appeared on a Chinese dating show. (Through March 29.) At various times, Barrow Street Theater, 27 Barrow Street, at Seventh Avenue South, West Village, 212-868-4444, barrowstreettheatre.com. (Czajkowski)Fortune Feimster (Thursday through March 29) Ms. Feimster, a Southern comedian best known as a regular panelist on “Chelsea Lately,” headlines at Carolines on Broadway next week. Thursday and March 29 at 7:30 p.m., next Friday and Saturday at 7:30 and 10 p.m., 1626 Broadway, at 49th Street, Manhattan, 212-757-4100, carolines.com. (Czajkowski)Advertisement★ ‘Hot Tub Hijacks Night Train’ (Monday) Kurt Braunohler and Kristen Schaal, hosts of the long-running variety show “Hot Tub,” return to New York to reclaim Littlefield’s Monday night comedy spot for an evening. Eugene Mirman, Aamer Rahman, Alingon Mitra, Phoebe Robinson, Maeve Higgins, Matt Wayne and Michael Palascak are among the performers. At 8 p.m., Littlefield, 622 Degraw Street, between Third and Fourth Avenues, Gowanus, Brooklyn, littlefieldnyc.com.
