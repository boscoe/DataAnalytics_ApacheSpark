Mark Ronson’s “Uptown Funk” has dominated the Billboard singles chart since the beginning of 2015, and this week it notches a remarkable 13th time at No. 1. That is the longest run for any song since 2009, when Black Eyed Peas’ “I Gotta Feeling” was tops for 14 weeks straight.In its most recent week, “Uptown Funk” — featuring Bruno Mars on vocals — was downloaded 165,000 times and streamed 16.2 million times, according to Nielsen Music. Since its release in November, the song has racked up almost 5.1 million downloads and 252 million audio and video streams.On this week’s album chart, Kendrick Lamar’s “To Pimp a Butterfly” (TDE/Aftermath/Interscope) is No. 1 for a second time, with 107,000 sales and 18 million streams. Two recent soundtracks held strong: “Empire” (Columbia), the companion to the hit Fox drama, is No. 2, while “Fifty Shades of Grey” (Republic) rose one spot to No. 3.Sam Smith’s “In the Lonely Hour” (Capitol) rose five spots this week to No. 4, Ms. Swift’s “1989” (Big Machine) holds at No. 5, and Ed Sheeran’s “x” (Atlantic) is in sixth place. “Mr. Wonderful” (Vice/Atlantic), the major-label debut by the rapper Action Bronson, opened at No. 7 with 44,000 sales and 3.8 million streams.
