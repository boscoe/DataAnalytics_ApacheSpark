Every summer, they rise: moist, many-tentacled, confused, chaotic, pervasive. They pop up in cities across the country – around the world! – and sprawl out largely unchecked, imposing their will upon tens of thousands of people, who find themselves helpless in their presence.We’re talking about festivals, of course.They are a rite of summer, a reminder of the changing season and of the changing sound of young America (and sometimes older America). But how much festival is too much festival? In recent weeks, Jon Pareles visited the South by Southwest Music Conference and Festival in Austin, Tex., and Ben Ratliff found communion at both the Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival in Indio, Calif., and Big Ears, in Knoxville, Tenn.On this week’s Popcast, I talked to them about the blessings and vicissitudes of festival-going and whether, given how these events often can be primarily about something other than music, whether it’s much use for a music critic to go to them at all.Listen above, download the MP3 or subscribe in iTunes.
