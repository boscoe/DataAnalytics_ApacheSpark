A searchable guide to these and other shows is at nytimes.com/events.★ Dave Attell (Wednesday, next Friday and Nov. 28) A revered figure in the stand-up community, Mr. Attell is a 30-year veteran known for his raunchy, unapologetic style and willingness to push the boundaries of good taste. At 7:30 and 10 p.m., Carolines, 1626 Broadway, at 49th Street, Manhattan, 212-757-4100, carolines.com. (Elise Czajkowski)Barry Crimmins (Saturday through Wednesday) Mr. Crimmins is a political satirist and activist who has been performing comedy for more than 35 years. The subject of Bobcat Goldthwait’s recent documentary “Call Me Lucky,” Mr. Crimmins performs as part of the “Week at the Creek” series. At 7 p.m., the Creek and the Cave, 10-93 Jackson Avenue, Long Island City, Queens, 718-706-8783, creeklic.com. (Czajkowski)‘Cuckooed’ (through Saturday) Mark Thomas is a politically motivated British comedian who describes his work as “a mix of stand-up, theater, journalism and the odd bout of performance art.” In this show, the activist-comic recounts his discovery that a good friend was actually a spy for a major arms manufacturer. Friday at 8:15 p.m., Saturday at 2:15 and 8:15 p.m., 59E59 Theaters, 59 East 59th Street, Manhattan, 212-279-4200, 59e59.org. (Czajkowski)★ ‘Employee of the Month’ (Friday) Catie Lazarus hosts this long-running live talk show, where she interviews a range of people about their current and former jobs. This month’s guests include the comedian David Cross, the actress Greta Gerwig and the singer Sadie Dupuis. At 9:30 p.m., Joe’s Pub, at the Public Theater, 425 Lafayette Street, at Astor Place, East Village, 212-967-7555, joespub.com. (Czajkowski)Advertisement‘Felines of New York’ (Sunday) Jim Tews celebrates the release of his new blog-turned-book “Felines of New York” with a night of cat-related humor from Todd Hanson, Maeve Higgins, Mike Polk, Doogie Horner, Mrs. Smith and Team Submarine. At 8:30 p.m., Littlefield, 622 Degraw Street, Gowanus, Brooklyn, littlefieldnyc.com. (Czajkowski)Nick Kroll & John Mulaney (Sunday, Tuesday and Nov. 29) Only a few weeks before their sold-out Off Broadway run of “Oh, Hello” begins at the Cherry Lane Theater, Mr. Kroll (Comedy Central’s “Kroll Show”) and Mr. Mulaney — who just released his new stand-up special, “The Comeback Kid”, on Netflix — perform together for three unpredictable nights of comedy. At 11 p.m., Upright Citizens Brigade Theater, 307 West 26th Street, Chelsea, 212-366-9176, newyork.ucbtheatre.com; sold out, but a standby line will be available. (Czajkowski)Advertisement★ Solocom 2015 (Friday through Sunday) Now in its third year, this annual festival features more than 100 solo comedy shows, each running 15 to 60 minutes. The shows range from stand-up, storytelling and improv to cabaret, music and clowning. Prominent performers include Ali Farahnakian, Adam Wade, Keisha Zollar and Cory Kahaney. At various times, Peoples Improv Theater, 123 East 24th Street, Manhattan, 212-563-7488, thepit-nyc.com.
