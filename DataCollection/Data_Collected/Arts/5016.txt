By CORINNA da FONSECA-WOLLHEIMMAY 22, 2015
With his low-key, affable charm, the Austrian conductor Manfred Honeck rarely draws attention to himself. But the stellar work he has done with the Pittsburgh Symphony and a few highly successful guest stints with the New York Philharmonic have made him one of the contenders to succeed Alan Gilbert in New York when he vacates the job of music director. Players and audiences can test the chemistry this week at a series of concerts beginning on Thursday, featuring the magnificent violinist Augustin Hadelich in Mozart’s Violin Concerto No. 5 “Turkish,” Brahms’s Fourth Symphony and the overture to Strauss’s “Die Fledermaus.” (Avery Fisher Hall Thursday at 7.30 p.m., Friday at 2 p.m. and Saturday at 8 p.m.; nyphil.org)On Friday, the American Symphony Orchestra under the direction of Leon Botstein honors the centenary of George Perle with a Carnegie Hall program comprising Perle’s “Transcendental Modulations” and “Adagio for Orchestra,” alongside works by Copland, Foss and Schuman.
