By NEIL GENZLINGERFEB. 28, 2015
The Oscars the other night sure seemed glamorous, didn’t they? The gowns, the applause, the flashbulbs. Well, on Thursday night comes a reality check: a documentary on Showtime called “That Gal ... Who Was in That Thing: That Guy 2.”In it, eight actresses who have had some success but are not household names talk, often bluntly, about show business and what they have faced and continue to face as they try to make a career of it. The women, who include Paget Brewster of “Criminal Minds” and Cristine Rose of “Heroes,” discuss age, breast size, race and more in relating the highs and lows of being famous-ish.The film is a follow-up to a 2012 documentary about male character actors. What is striking is that even though they are not top-of-the-bill stars, these women are still part of a tiny minority: actresses who have accumulated a decent number of credits and made at least some money at their craft.
