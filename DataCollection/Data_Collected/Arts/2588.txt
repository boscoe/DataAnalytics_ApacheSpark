A searchable guide to these and other shows is at nytimes.com/events.★ Hannibal Buress (Sunday) Widely regarded as one of the next superstars of comedy, Mr. Buress is a clever storyteller with a laid-back but opinionated style. This weekend he brings his national “Comedy Camisado” tour to his home borough, Brooklyn. At 8 p.m., Howard Gilman Opera House, Brooklyn Academy of Music, 30 Lafayette Avenue, at Ashland Place, Fort Greene, 718-636-4100, bam.org; sold out. (Elise Czajkowski)Cheap Date Comedy Show (Tuesday) Sharron Paul and Calvin Cole host this monthly stand-up show, which offers free snacks as well as top-notch comedy. This month’s comics are Jeffrey Joseph, Ted Alexandro, Janelle James and Jon Laster. At 8 p.m., Union Hall, 702 Union Street, at Fifth Avenue, Park Slope, Brooklyn, 718-638-4400, unionhallny.com. (Czajkowski)Naomi Ekperigin (Monday) A writer for Comedy Central’s “Broad City” and a promising stand-up, Ms. Ekperigin headlines as part of the Upright Citizens Brigade’s Stand-Up Spotlight. She’ll receive support from Ilana Glazer, one of the stars of “Broad City.” At 6:30 p.m., Upright Citizens Brigade Theater, 307 West 26th Street, Chelsea, 212-366-9176, newyork.ucbtheatre.com. (Czajkowski)Advertisement★ ‘Rebecca and Evan: Your Love, Our Musical’ (Monday, Tuesday and Thursday through Feb. 14) The musical improvisers Rebecca Vigil and Evan Kaufman interview a couple in the audience about their relationship, then spin an impromptu musical about the couple’s love story. At 8 p.m., except Feb. 14, when the show starts at 7 p.m., Peoples Improv Theater, 123 East 24th Street, Manhattan, 212-563-7488, thepit-nyc.com.
