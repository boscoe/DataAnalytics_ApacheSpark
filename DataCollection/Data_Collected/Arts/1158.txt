Andris Nelsons will conduct the Boston Symphony Orchestra in performances of Mahler, Beethoven and the first two acts of Verdi’s “Aida,” with the soprano Kristine Opolais, who is married to him, in the title role. A battery of famous pianists will descend on the Berkshires. And George Benjamin’s new song cycle, “Dream of the Song,” will get its United States premiere.Those are among the highlights of the 2016 season at Tanglewood, which will run from June 25 through Sept. 3 in Lenox, Mass. This year’s season will feature Renée Fleming, singing both Strauss’s “Four Last Songs,” with Christoph von Dohnányi conducting the Boston Symphony, and Berg’s “Lyric Suite,” with the Emerson String Quartet; Joshua Bell performing Saint-Saëns’ Violin Concerto No. 3; and pianists including Emanuel Ax, Jonathan Biss, Yefim Bronfman, Jeremy Denk, Paul Lewis, Garrick Ohlsson, Daniil Trifonov and Yuja Wang.The 2016 Festival of Contemporary Music, which will run from July 21 through July 25, is this year under the direction of Steven Stucky, the American composer.Mr. Nelsons, who is in his second season as the music director of the Boston Symphony, will lead five programs, including an all-Brahms concert with the Tanglewood Music Center Orchestra, and the premiere of George Tsontakis’s “Sonnets,” a concerto for English horn and orchestra.
