Nick Di Paolo  (Saturday) Mr. Di Paolo has been a fixture of the New York comedy scene for decades, renowned for his fearlessly honest stage presence and politically aggressive material. He’s supported here by Dan Soder. At 8 p.m., Gramercy Theater, 127 East 23rd Street, Manhattan, 212-777-6800, venue.thegramercytheatre.com. (Elise Czajkowski)★ Harrison Greenbaum  (Wednesday) The 2010 winner of the Andy Kaufman Award for “originality, creativity and spirit,” Mr. Greenbaum is a charismatic young performer and rising star on the New York comedy scene. At 8 p.m., Carolines, 1626 Broadway, at 49th Street, Manhattan, 212-757-4100, carolines.com. (Czajkowski)★ Josie Long: ‘Cara Josephine’  (Saturday, Sunday,Tuesday and through Oct. 25) A gem of the British comedy scene, Ms. Long is a stand-up and storyteller who dwells in earnestness and optimism. “Cara Josephine,” perhaps the best-reviewed comedy show at last year’s Edinburgh Fringe Festival, is a sweet and very funny hour about relationships, love and family. Saturday at 5 p.m., Sunday, Tuesday and Thursday at 9 p.m., Wednesday at 7 p.m., SoHo Playhouse, 15 Vandam Street, South Village, 212-691-1555, sohoplayhouse.com. (Czajkowski)Hasan Minhaj: ‘Homecoming King’  (through Nov. 15) Mr. Minhaj, a correspondent on Comedy Central’s “The Daily Show,” presents this one-man performance about his experience as a first-generation immigrant and “his family’s quest to achieve the elusive American dream.” Thursdays through Sundays at various times, Cherry Lane Theater, 38 Commerce Street, West Village, 212-989-2020, cherrylanetheatre.org. (Czajkowski)★ New York Musical Improv Festival  (through Sunday) This annual festival — now in its seventh year — celebrates the comedic tightrope walk that is musical improv with troupes from around the country, including Chicago’s much-loved Baby Wants Candy. Shows begin Friday at 7 p.m. and Saturday and Sunday at 5 p.m., Magnet Theater, 254 West 29th Street, Chelsea, 212-244-8824, nymif.com. (Czajkowski)‘Night Train’  (Monday) David Cross guest-hosts this always excellent weekly stand-up show. Performers include Adam Cayton-Holland, Rob Haze, Anthony DeVito, Raj Sivaraman, Josie Long and Jenny Zigrino. At 8 p.m., Littlefield, 622 Degraw Street, Gowanus, Brooklyn, littlefieldnyc.com. (Czajkowski)Rosie O’Donnell  (Friday and Saturday) Though most famous for her years as a television host, Ms. O’Donnell’s career started on the comedy club circuit in the 1980s, and she’s never given up stand-up. Now she headlines four shows this weekend at Gotham Comedy Club. At 7:30 and 9:30 p.m., 208 West 23rd Street, Chelsea, 212-367-9000, gothamcomedyclub.com. (Czajkowski)‘Set List’  (Wednesday) This international hit show features comedians improvising jokes based on outrageous suggestions that they will see for the first time on stage. This month’s performers include Sean Patton, Matteo Lane and Mark Normand. At 8 p.m., Union Hall, 702 Union Street, Park Slope, Brooklyn, 718-638-4400, unionhallny.com. (Czajkowski)‘2 Dope Queens’  (Tuesday) Phoebe Robinson and Jessica Williams host this renamed version of their long running “Blaria” show. Performers include Seaton Smith, Matteo Lane and Jacqueline Novak. At 8 p.m., Union Hall, 702 Union Street, Park Slope, Brooklyn, 718-638-4400, unionhallny.com. (Czajkowski)‘Uptown Showdown’  (Tuesday) This reoccurring show presents two teams of comedians debating the least pressing issues of the day. This month, Alex Edelman, Christian Finnegan, Phoebe Robinson, Dan Soder and Kevin Townley square off on “Old New York vs. New New York.” At 8 p.m., Symphony Space, 2537 Broadway, at 95th Street, 212-864-5400, symphonyspace.org.
