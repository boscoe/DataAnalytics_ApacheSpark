Click here for holiday presentations at local churches and here for other seasonal celebrations. Our Lady Queen of Martyrs Church, Ascan Avenue at Queens Boulevard, Forest Hills, Queens. David Close leads the Sacred Music Society of Our Lady Queen of Martyrs in performing an excerpt from “Messiah,” followed by traditional Christmas carols, like Leroy Anderson’s holiday earworm “Sleigh Ride.” At 4 p.m., ourladyqueenofmartyrs.org, 718-268-6251.Good Shepherd Roman Catholic Church, Avenue S at Brown Street, Marine Park, Brooklyn. The Good Shepherd Chancel Choir and Orchestra, led by the conductor and music director Michael Fontana, performs this free concert of Handel’s “Messiah.” At 6 p.m., 718-998-2800, goodshepherdrcc.org.Hall of Fame Playhouse, Bronx Community College, 181st Street at University Avenue, the Bronx. Soloists and chorus members of the Bronx Opera Company, led by the conductor Michael Spierman and accompanied by the Orchestra of the Bronx and 23 narrators from the public, perform Handel’s “Messiah.” At 3 p.m., 718-289-5318, bronxopera.org.David Geffen Hall, Lincoln Center. Jane Glover conducts the New York Philharmonic in this season’s “Messiah” with the Westminster Symphonic Choir, directed by Joe Miller. Among the soloists is the tenor Paul Appleby, a smooth-voiced darling of recent seasons at the Metropolitan Opera. At 7:30 p.m. (also Wednesday, Thursday and Saturday at 7:30 p.m., next Friday at 11 a.m.), 212-875-5656, nyphil.org.Trinity Church, Broadway at Wall Street, financial district. The Choir of Trinity Wall Street and the Trinity Baroque Orchestra open a series of three performances of Handel’s “Messiah,” led by the conductor Julian Wachner. At 7:30 p.m. (also at 5 p.m. on Dec. 26), 212-866-0468, gemsny.org; sold out.Alice Tully Hall, Lincoln Center. The conductor Julian Wachner, leading the Choir of Trinity Wall Street and the Trinity Baroque Orchestra, takes Handel’s “Messiah” from Lower Manhattan to Lincoln Center for the second leg of his three-performance series. At 7:30 p.m., 212-721-6500, lincolncenter.org.Queensborough Community College, Performing Arts Center, 56th Avenue and Springfield Boulevard, Bayside. David Close leads the Oratorio Society of Queens in this performance, which also includes traditional Christmas favorites and Hanukkah tunes. The audience is welcome to sing along to “O Come, All Ye Faithful” with the chorus. At 4 p.m., 718-279-3006, queensoratorio.org.New Jersey Performing Arts Center, 1 Center Street, Newark. Jacques Lacombe conducts this performance of Handel’s “Messiah,” with the Montclair State University Singers and the New Jersey Symphony Chamber Orchestra. At 3 p.m., 888-466-5722, njpac.org.Carnegie Hall Mozart made his own arrangement of Handel’s “Messiah” 48 years after its premiere. The Oratorio Society of New York, under the baton of Kent Tritle, leads this performance of Handel through the eyes of Mozart. At 8 p.m. 212-400-7255, oratoriosocietyofny.org.Advertisement
