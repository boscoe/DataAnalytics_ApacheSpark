LOS ANGELES – Luke Skywalker is finally becoming one with the iPad generation: The “Star Wars” movies – for the first time — are being made available for purchase through digital stores like iTunes, Google Play and Amazon.Disney and 20th Century Fox said late Monday that the six movies – “Star Wars,” “Return of the Jedi,” “The Empire Strikes Back,” “The Phantom Menace,” “Attack of the Clones” and “Revenge of the Sith” — would arrive online worldwide on April 10. Rentals will not be offered. Prices will vary depending on the retailer.“Viewers will have the Rebel forces at their beck and call across their favorite devices anywhere – sunbathing at the beach, relaxing in their living room with friends, or waiting to board a flight to Endor (or elsewhere),” the studios said in a statement.Disney and Fox also promised “never-before-seen” bonus features. The announcement comes 10 days before Disney’s Lucasfilm unit kicks off a major “Star Wars” fan convention in Anaheim, Calif., and eight months before the release of the first new “Star Wars” movie in a decade. “Star Wars: Episode VII – The
Force Awakens” is scheduled to arrive in theaters on Dec. 18.A version of this article appears in print on 04/08/2015, on page C3 of the NewYork edition with the headline: Digital Stores to Offer ‘Star Wars’ Films.
