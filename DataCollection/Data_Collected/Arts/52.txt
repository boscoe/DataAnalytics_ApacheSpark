A searchable guide to these and other shows is at nytimes.com/events.Des Bishop: ‘Made in China’  (through Saturday) Mr. Bishop, a native of Queens, made a name for himself as a comedian in Ireland before moving to China in 2013 with the intention of ultimately performing stand-up in Mandarin. In this show, presented as part of the annual First Irish Theater Festival, he recounts the time he spent in Asia, during which he worked as a host in a busy restaurant and appeared on a Chinese dating show. At 7:30 p.m., Lucky Jack’s, 129 Orchard Street, at Delancey Street, Lower East Side, 866-811-4111, 1stirish.org. (Elise Czajkowski)★ Bo Burnham  (Monday, Wednesday and Thursday) Over the last decade, Mr. Burnham has evolved from a cutesy musical comic into one of the most creatively adventurous comedians in the country, blending original music, storytelling and poetry in elaborately theatrical shows. These performances are part of his “Make Happy” tour. At 8 p.m., PlayStation Theater, 1515 Broadway, Manhattan, 212-930-1940, playstationtheater.com. (Czajkowski)Gad Elmaleh  (Friday through Sunday, Tuesday and Wednesday) Frequently referred to as the “Jerry Seinfeld of France,” the Moroccan-born Mr. Elmaleh came to prominence in France with a series of one-man comedic shows before finding fame on television and film. Friday through Sunday at 7 p.m., Tuesday and Wednesday at 9:30 p.m., Joe’s Pub, at the Public Theater, 425 Lafayette Street, East Village, 212-539-8778, joespub.com. At 8 p.m., the HiFi Bar, 169 Avenue A, at East 11th Street, East Village, 212-420-8392, thehifibar.com. (Czajkowski)‘Inside Joke’  (Thursday) Ophira Eisenberg, a comedian, storyteller and NPR host, performs this show about her impending motherhood. Subtitled “An Unexpected Night of Stand-up Comedy, performed by Ophira Eisenberg (at 36 weeks),” the show explores Ms. Eisenberg’s past disinterest in having kids and her self-described “clueless, unprepared” current status. At 8:30 p.m., Union Hall, 702 Union Street, Park Slope, Brooklyn, 718-638-4400, unionhallny.com. (Czajkowski)‘Kapow-i GoGo Marathon!’  (Sunday and Oct. 11) Madeleine Bundy stars as Kapow-i GoGo, a teenage girl on a cartoon-and-video-game-inspired adventure to save the world many times over. (Czajkowski)‘Meet Me in the Bathroom and Tell Me All Your Secrets’ (Friday) The comedian Dave Hill and his band, Valley Lodge, host this regular night of “comedy, music and gunplay”. (Czajkowski)‘Running Late with Scott Rogowsky’  (Tuesday) Mr. Rogowsky will offer a special edition of his regular live talk show in honor of New York Super Week, a new pop culture festival from the organizers of New York Comic Con.
