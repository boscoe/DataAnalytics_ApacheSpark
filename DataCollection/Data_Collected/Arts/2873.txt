A searchable guide to these and other shows is at nytimes.com/events.★ Kurt Braunohler (Wednesday) Mr. Braunohler, a charismatic comedian with a flair for the absurd, is touring to promote his new Comedy Central web series, “Roustabout,” which documents his Jet Ski trip down the Mississippi, from Chicago to New Orleans, to raise money to send goats and chickens to needy families in Africa. At 9 p.m., the Bell House, 149 Seventh Street, Gowanus, Brooklyn, 718-643-6510, thebellhouseny.com. (Elise Czajkowski)‘Kapow-i GoGo’: Parts 1 and 2 (Friday, Saturday, Monday, Tuesday and next Friday) The first part of this “epic geek comedy” trilogy, “Kapow-i GoGo Goo!,” contains three episodes and follows the 14-year-old Kapow-i GoGo (Madeleine Bundy) on a video game-inspired series of challenges to fight assorted bad guys and become the world’s greatest fighter. The three episodes that make up the second part, “Kapow-i GoGo Z,” revisit Ms. Bundy’s Kapow-i GoGo, now a sulky 18-year-old, as she once again tries to save the world from an assortment of video-game inspired villains. “Kapow-i GoGo Goo!”: Friday and Saturday at various times; “Kapow-i GoGo Z”: Monday, Tuesday and next Friday at various times; Peoples Improv Theater, 123 East 24th Street, Manhattan, 212-563-7488, thepit-nyc.com. On the heels of his latest Comedy Central hour, “I Liked His Old Stuff Better,” he headlines the Bowery Ballroom. At 8 p.m., 6 Delancey Street, near the Bowery, Lower East Side, 800-745-3000, boweryballroom.com.
