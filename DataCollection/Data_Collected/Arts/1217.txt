AdvertisementSupported byBy Anthony TommasiniTwo student orchestras, featuring young players at very different stages of development, will appear this week at Carnegie Hall.On Sunday, Nov. 22, the always impressive New York Youth Symphony, founded in 1963, made up of students from the New York area ages 12 to 22, opens its season with a varied program conducted by Joshua Gersen, the ensemble’s dynamic music director. There will be the premiere of commissioned work by a young composer: Gabriella Smith’s “Lost Coast,” on a program featuring Strauss’s “Till Eulenspiegel’s Merry Pranks,” Ravel’s “Mother Goose” Suite, and Gershwin’s “An American in Paris.” (2 p.m., carnegiehall.org.)On Tuesday, Nov. 24, the Juilliard Orchestra takes the stage at 8 p.m. at Carnegie under Alan Gilbert, the music director of the New York Philharmonic, who also heads the conducting program at Juilliard. On a given day, this orchestra can sound like a major international ensemble. The program offers Schumann’s “Manfred” Overture, Berg’s Three Lyric Pieces and Mr. Gilbert’s orchestral arrangement of excerpts from Wagner’s “Ring.”Advertisement
