By NEIL GENZLINGERFEB. 14, 2015
The History channel remains a grab bag where you can find quirky reality shows like “Down East Dickering,” decent mini-series like last year’s “Houdini,” and assorted schedule-fillers about extraterrestrials and people who live in swamps. Amid this hodgepodge, “Vikings,” a scripted series that begins its third season on Thursday night, has emerged as an anchor.It’s a well-acted, relatively lavish epic about Vikings, their lusts and their power struggles, a PG-13 “Game of Thrones” with a smattering of actual history. It focuses on Ragnar Lothbrok (Travis Fimmel), who is on quite a personal journey. He started the series as a farmer whose interest in exploring put him at odds with the local leader, who preferred the tried-and-true routes for pillaging. Now, as Season 3 begins, Ragnar is a king.The series is also a family’s story whose characters include one of the toughest women in fantasy TV, Lagertha (Katheryn Winnick).
