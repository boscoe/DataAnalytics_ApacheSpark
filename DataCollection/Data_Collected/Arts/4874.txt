By ANDREW R. CHOWMAY 18, 2015
The Joyce Theater’s upcoming season will span many styles and eras, ranging from a reimagining of “Swan Lake” to a performance from the contemporary dance ensemble BalletBoyz.Dada Masilo, a Johannesburg-born choreographer, will set “Swan Lake” in Africa to examine gender issues and homophobia (Feb. 2-7).Twyla Tharp will celebrate 50 years since the founding of her dance company (Nov. 17-22). Daniil Simkin, a principal dancer for the American Ballet Theater, will bring several members to perform the multichoreographer piece “Intensio” (Jan. 5-10.) The all-male BalletBoyz will perform Christopher Wheeldon’s “Mesmerics” (Feb. 23-28.)Jazz will provide the inspiration for several performances: The composers featured include John Zorn (for Twyla Tharp), Ethan Iverson (for Dance Hegin-botham, Oct. 10-11) and Robert Glasper (for Abraham.In.Motion, Nov. 10-15).More details are at Joyce.org.A version of this article appears in print on May 18, 2015, on Page C3 of the New York edition with the headline: Joyce Theater Announces Fall/Winter Season.
