 sampling of seasonal celebrations in New York, including drama, readings and processions, compiled by Jonathan Wolfe. Free, unless otherwise noted. (Churches accept donations.) Palm SundayCALVARY-ST. GEORGE’S CHURCH (Episcopal) This parish offers two processions, each ending with musical services. The first, at 9:30 a.m., is at St. George’s Church (4 Rutherford Place, near East 17th Street, Manhattan), where Alex Nguyen will direct brass, organ and percussion musicians. At 10:45 a.m., a procession with palms will start in Gramercy Park and make its way to Calvary Church (Park Avenue South at 21st Street), where Kamel Boutros will direct a chamber string orchestra alongside brass and organ musicians. 646-723-4178, calvarystgeorges.org.ST. PAUL’S CHAPEL (Episcopal), Broadway and Fulton Street, and Trinity Church (Episcopal), Broadway at Wall Street, Lower Manhattan. A procession at 10:30 a.m. begins with a blessing of the palms at St. Paul’s Chapel and ends with a mass at Trinity Church at 11:15 a.m. The Choir of Trinity Wall Street, the Family Choir and the Trinity Youth Chorus will perform Gibbons, Leighton and Bach, and the mass will be webcast at trinitywallstreet.org. 212-602-0800.TRINITY CHURCH (Episcopal) Broadway at Wall Street, Lower Manhattan. Children and families can prepare for Holy Week at this interactive, storytelling event. At 11:15 a.m., 212-602-0800, trinitywallstreet.org.AdvertisementWednesdayTRINITY CHURCH (Episcopal), Broadway at Wall Street, Lower Manhattan. Candles will slowly be extinguished during this hourlong Tenebrae service. The Choir of Trinity Wall Street performs musical selections from Tallis’s “Lamentations of Jeremiah,” Bairstow’s “The Lamentation” and Allegri’s “Miserere Mei, Deus.” At 6 p.m., 212-602-0800, trinitywallstreet.org.AdvertisementMaundy ThursdayCATHEDRAL CHURCH OF ST. JOHN THE DIVINE (Episcopal) 1047 Amsterdam Avenue, at 112th Street, Morningside Heights. A midnight reception will follow a reading of Dante’s “Inferno,” from 9 p.m. until midnight, by Marilyn Nelson, the cathedral’s poet in residence; the poets Annie Finch and Cynthia Zarin; and others. 212-316-7540, stjohndivine.org.IMMANUEL LUTHERAN CHURCH, 122 East 88th Street, Manhattan. This lunchtime concert, sponsored by the Gothic Early Music Scene, features sacred music from Stradella and other composers in the artistic circles of Bernini and Queen Christina in 17th-century Rome. The early-music ensemble Artek; Eric Brenner and Ryland Angel, countertenors; Gwendolyn Toth, director. This celebration begins in darkness as participants light candles to illuminate the church while listening to scriptural readings and chanted psalms. A service led by the Bishop of New York with music by the Cathedral Choir follows.
