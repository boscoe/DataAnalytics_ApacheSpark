The Beinecke Rare Book and Manuscript Library at Yale has acquired the literary archive of Paula Vogel, whose play “How I Learned to Drive” won the Pulitzer Prize for drama in 1998. Ms. Vogel, who has taught at the Yale School of Drama since 2008 and is a playwright in residence at Yale Repertory Theater, is the first female playwright to be included in the library’s Yale Collection of American Literature, a repository that includes the papers of Eugene O’Neill, Thornton Wilder. A. R. Gurney and John Guare.Ms. Vogel’s archive includes drafts of most of her plays, teaching files and drafts of work by her students. At Brown University, where she was director of the graduate playwriting program for 24 years, she taught Sarah Ruhl, Nilo Cruz, Lynn Nottage and Quiara Alegría Hudes.Ms. Vogel’s plays include “Baltimore Waltz” (1992), winner of the Obie Award for best play, “Desdemona” (1979), “The Oldest Profession” (1981), “And Baby Makes Seven” (1984), “Hot ’n’ Throbbing” (1994) and “The Mineola Twins” (1996).About 200 computer disks and five computers in the Vogel archive contain photographs, documents, and email correspondence with theater critics, scholars and writers, including Ms. Ruhl, Bert O. States, David Savran and Amy Bloom, as well as numerous theater companies that have produced her work.
