A searchable guide to these and other shows is at nytimes.com/events.Bill Burr (Saturday) A working comedian for decades, Mr. Burr’s star has risen in the past few years; audiences are drawn to his aggressive, unapologetic style and Everyman politics. Here, he transfers his club style to Madison Square Garden for his new show, “Does Anybody Remember Laughter?” (Part of the New York Comedy Festival.) At 8 p.m., 800-745-3000, nycomedyfestival.com. (Elise Czajkowski)★ ‘Comedy Central Live in Brooklyn’ (Friday) Comedy Central shows off its impressive array of comedic talent with this show, which features performances from Hannibal Buress, Nathan Fielder, Big Jay Oakerson, Jeff Ross and Roy Wood Jr., as well as John Mulaney and Nick Kroll as George and Gil from their “Oh, Hello” sketches. (Part of the New York Comedy Festival.) At 8 p.m., Kings Theater, 1027 Flatbush Avenue, near Tilden Avenue, Brooklyn, 800-745-3000, nycomedyfestival.com. (Czajkowski)‘Cuckooed’ (through Nov. 21) Mr. Thomas is a politically motivated British comedian who describes his work as “a mix of stand-up, theater, journalism and the odd bout of performance art.” In this show, the activist comic recounts his discovery that a good friend was actually a spy for a major arms manufacturer. At various times, 59E59 Theaters, 59 East 59th Street, Manhattan, 212-279-4200, 59e59.org. (Czajkowski)AdvertisementAlex Edelman (Saturday) In 2014, Mr. Edelman won the Best Newcomer Prize at the Edinburgh Festival Fringe for his show “Millennial,” making him one of only three Americans to win the prize. His new show, “Everything Handed to You,” is a more personal hour of stand-up and storytelling. (Part of the New York Comedy Festival.) At 7:30 p.m., Union Hall, 702 Union Street, at Fifth Avenue, Park Slope, Brooklyn, 718-638-4400, unionhallny.com, nycomedyfestival.com. (Czajkowski)Advertisement‘An Evening with Jerry Seinfeld and Amy Schumer’ (Monday) Two of the most sought-after stand-ups today team up for this benefit show that celebrates 15 years of Baby Buggy, an organization that provides for families in need. At 7 p.m., Beacon Theater, 2124 Broadway, at 74th Street, 800-745-3000, beacontheatre.com. (Czajkowski)‘Funny People: Alan Zweibel in Conversation With Martin Short’ (Monday) Mr. Zweibel and Mr. Short, both prolific comedians and writers, will discuss their new books and reflect on their decadeslong careers. At 8 p.m., 92nd Street Y, 1395 Lexington Avenue, 212-415-5500, 92y.org. (Czajkowski)The Grawlix (Friday) Adam Cayton-Holland, Ben Roy and Andrew Orvedah are Denver-based stand-ups who perform together under the Grawlix moniker. The trio’s television show, “Those Who Can’t,” is scheduled to premiere on TruTV early next year. (Part of the New York Comedy Festival.) At 10 p.m., The Bell House, 149 Seventh Street, Gowanus, Brooklyn, 718-643-6510, thebellhouseny.com; nycomedyfestival.com. Kempsas, are veteran improvisers who have performed as a duo for nearly a decade. They occasionally reunite for a night of two-person, long-form improv, as they will here as part of the New York Comedy Festival. (Czajkowski)Norm Macdonald (through Sunday) Mr. Macdonald’s rambling presentation and quirky style have won him fans since his days as the host of Weekend Update on “Saturday Night Live.” (Part of the New York Comedy Festival.) Friday and Saturday at 7:30 and 10 p.m., Sunday at 8 p.m., 1626 Broadway, at 49th Street, Manhattan, 212-757-4100, carolines.com. (Czajkowski)Hasan Minhaj: ‘Homecoming King’ (through Sunday) Mr. Minhaj, a correspondent on Comedy Central’s “The Daily Show,” presents this one-man performance about his experience as a first-generation immigrant and “his family’s quest to achieve the elusive American dream.” At various times, Cherry Lane Theater, 38 Commerce Street, West Village, 212-989-2020, cherrylanetheatre.org. (Czajkowski)Paul Mooney and Dick Gregory (Thursday) Mr. Mooney and Mr. Gregory, two of the most influential living stand-ups, split the stage for the evening (rescheduled from earlier this month; tickets from that performance will be honored). Mr. Mooney, who wrote for Richard Pryor, “In Living Color,” and “Chappelle’s Show,” has always been a forceful social critic on race issues. Mr. Gregory was the first African-American comedian to be called over to the couch on “The Tonight Show,” and over his 60-plus year career, he’s mixed social activism with biting comedy. At 7:30 p.m., B.B. King Blues Club & Grill, 237 West 42nd Street, Manhattan, 800-745-3000, bbkingblues.com. (Czajkowski)★ Trevor Noah (Saturday) Mr. Noah became a star on the global stand-up scene by turning his difficult experience as a biracial child in apartheid-era South Africa into charming and very funny one-man shows. Now that he’s taken over as host of “The Daily Show,” he’s incorporating more topical and political comedy into his stand-up act. (Part of the New York Comedy Festival.) At 7 and 9:45 p.m., Town Hall, 123 West 43rd Street, Manhattan, 800-982-2787, nycomedyfestival.com. (Czajkowski)★ ‘NY’s Funniest Finals’ (Sunday) Carolines on Broadway hosts this annual competition, with the final round featuring 10 up-and-coming comedians vying for the title of New York’s Funniest. Michael Che, a previous winner and current “Saturday Night Live” cast member, will host the event, part of the New York Comedy Festival. At 4 p.m., Carolines, 1626 Broadway, at 49th Street, Manhattan, 212-757-4100, nycomedyfestival.com. (Czajkowski)Advertisement‘The Talent Show’ (Monday) Elna Baker and Kevin Townley host this monthly variety show, which changes formats each time. November’s theme is “Speech & Debate”; performers include Julie Klausner, Jean Grae, Stuckey & Murray, Naomi Ekperigin, John “Lypsinka” Epperson, Mark Oppenheimer, Bobby Tisdale, Arian Moayed and Meg Griffiths. At 9:30 p.m., Joe’s Pub, at the Public Theater, 425 Lafayette Street, at Astor Place, East Village, 212-967-7555, joespub.com.
