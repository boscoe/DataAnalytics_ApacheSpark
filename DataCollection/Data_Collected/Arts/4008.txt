A somewhat racy observational stand-up, he headlines the weekend at Carolines. Friday and Saturday at 7:30 and 10 p.m.; Sunday at 7:30 p.m.; Carolines on Broadway, 1626 Broadway, at 49th Street, 212-757-4100, carolines.com. (Czajkowski)‘Cocoon Central Dance Team: Snowy Bing Bongs Across the North Star Combat Zone’ (Monday and Tuesday) Cocoon Central Dance Team is a patently absurd comedy dance team that enlivens every show it’s on. At 7 p.m., the Peoples Improv Theater, 123 East 24th Street, Manhattan, 212-563-7488, thepit-nyc.com. (Czajkowski)★ Marcus Monroe  (Wednesday through April 18) Mr. Monroe, a comedian and juggler, is one of the most enjoyable performers to watch in New York City. As part of the Week at the Creek series, he runs his hourlong show, “Famous,” about his attempt to become the world’s first famous juggler. At 7 p.m., the Creek and the Cave, 10-93 Jackson Avenue, Long Island City, Queens, 718-706-8783, creeklic.com; free. (Czajkowski)‘Tiny Spectacular’ (Saturday) Magnet Theater continues its 10th-anniversary celebrations with this all-star improv show; scheduled performers include Ed Herbstman, Rachel Dratch, Jon Glaser and Scott Adsit. (Czajkowski)★ ‘Adrienne Truscott’s Asking For It’ (Saturday) Ms. Truscott’s bold, award-winning show is an hourlong examination of rape jokes in the modern stand-up scene, which she performs naked from the waist down.
