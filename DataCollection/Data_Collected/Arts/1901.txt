Tonya Pinkins, who plays the title role in Bertolt Brecht’s “Mother Courage and Her Children” at Classic Stage Company, will leave the production on Jan. 5, a publicist for the theater said on Tuesday.The Jan. 7 opening will be postponed while a replacement is sought.Ms. Pinkins, a  Tony Award winner for her work in “Jelly’s Last Jam,” is a distinguished actress of both plays and musicals. In an email, she wrote that she had drafted a statement detailing her reasons for leaving the show, but that her lawyer had discouraged her from sharing it. “I’m not even sure I want to tell it,” she wrote.Brian Kulick, the production’s director and Classic Stage’s artistic director, declined a request for an interview on Tuesday.“Mother Courage,” which began previews on Dec. 9, is Brecht’s 1939 epic drama about a canteen woman’s adventures during the Thirty Years’ War of the 17th century. It’s a plum role for an actress; among those who’ve played it are Fiona Shaw and Meryl Streep. The Classic Stage  production includes new music by the “Spring Awakening” composer Duncan Sheik.
