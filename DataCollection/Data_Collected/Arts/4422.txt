The jazz musician Esperanza Spalding, the theater star Taylor Mac and the country singer Jason Isbell are among the headliners of the 37th annual Celebrate Brooklyn Festival in Prospect Park. The festival, held at the Bandshell and organized by the arts organization BRIC, will take place June 3 through Aug. 12 and feature music, film, dance and an art installation.The festival kicks off with a free show by the R&B singer Chaka Khan on June 3. Other free shows include Ms. Spalding (June 12), the folk singer Lucinda Williams (June 25), the jazz pianist Jason Moran (June 27), the blues musician Rhiannon Giddens (July 18) and Mr. Isbell (July 24).Taylor Mac, the creator and star of “A 24-Decade History of Popular Music,” will perform an abridged version of that show on Aug. 1. Charles Isherwood in The New York Times called the show a “playful and thoroughly winning tour through American pop history.”On July 30 the Orchestra of St. Luke’s will team with the artist Clifford Ross for the world premiere of a multimedia piece called “The Musical World of Harmonium Mountain.”There will also be screenings of the silent movie “The Son of the Sheik” (July 17), accompanied by live music, and the film “Paris is Burning” (June 26).More information is at bricartsmedia.org.A version of this article appears in print on 04/30/2015, on page C3 of the NewYork edition with the headline: Celebrate Brooklyn!
