The Roundabout Theater Company is increasing security  at “Thérèse Raquin” after an audience member shouted at the movie star Keira Knightley, who is making her Broadway debut in the show, and hurled flowers at the stage during a performance.The incident occurred Thursday night, at the first preview of the play, which is a new adaptation by Helen Edmundson of the novel by Émile Zola. A man in the balcony of the Studio 54 theater shouted at Ms. Knightley, and then threw flowers as he was removed by security, according to a spokesman for the show.The New York City police said they were notified of the disturbance at 8:13 p.m. and that the officers responding decided to take the man to a hospital for mental evaluation. They said the man, whose name they did not release, was not arrested nor charged with a crime.Matt Polk, a spokesman for Roundabout, the nonprofit theater company that is producing “Thérèse Raquin” on Broadway, said in an e-mail: “Roundabout takes the safety of their actors and audiences very seriously and extra security will be added beginning tonight. For obvious reasons, we cannot comment on the exact nature of the additional security measures.”Al Baker contributed reporting.A version of this article appears in print on 10/03/2015, on page C3 of the NewYork edition with the headline: Outburst Disrupts Knightley Show.
