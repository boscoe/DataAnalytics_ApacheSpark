A searchable guide to these and other shows is at nytimes.com/events.‘Comedic Reform: A Variety Show Benefit in Support of Police Reform’ (Thursday) Keisha Zollar, one of the Upright Citizen Brigade’s finest improvisers, hosts this fund-raiser for the Malcolm X Grassroots Movement, which is seeking to reform New York’s policing practices. Other performers include Abbi Crutchfield, Jamie Kilstein, Aparna Nancherla, Sue Smith and Shonali Bhowmik. At 7 p.m., Shea Stadium BK, 20 Meadow Street, between Waterbury and Bogart Streets, Williamsburg, Brooklyn, 800-838-3006, brownpapertickets.com/event/1206001. (Elise Czajkowski)An Evening With Cori Gonzalez-Macuer (Sunday) Mr. Gonzalez-Macuer, an award-winning New Zealand comedian and star of the coming film “What We Do in the Shadows,” headlines this night at Union Hall. At 10 p.m., 702 Union Street, at Fifth Avenue, Park Slope, Brooklyn, 718-638-4400, unionhallny.com. (Czajkowski)‘Kapow-i GoGo Gooo!’ (Tuesday through Feb. 21) The first part of this “epic geek comedy” trilogy contains three episodes and follows the 14-year-old Kapow-i GoGo (Madeleine Bundy) on a video game-inspired series of challenges to fight assorted bad guys and become the world’s greatest fighter. (Parts two and three will be performed in subsequent weeks.) Episode 1 is at 7 p.m., Episode 2 is at 8 p.m., and Episode 3 is at 9 p.m., the Peoples Improv Theater, 123 East 24th Street, Manhattan, 212-563-7488, thepit-nyc.com. (Czajkowski)Advertisement★ ‘Rebecca and Evan: Your Love, Our Musical’  (Friday and Saturday) The musical improvisers Rebecca Vigil and Evan Kaufman interview a couple in the audience about their relationship, then spin an impromptu musical about their love story. Friday at 8 p.m., Saturday at 7 p.m., Peoples Improv Theater, 123 East 24th Street, Manhattan, 212-563-7488, thepit-nyc.com. (Czajkowski)Advertisement★ ‘The Rejection Show Valentine’s Day Heartbreak Haven’ (Saturday) Jon Friedman hosts this storytelling show dedicated to breakups and blues. The hefty lineup includes Adam Wade, Giulia Rozzi, Phoebe Robinson, Michelle Wolf, Aparna Nancherla, Elna Baker, Josh Gondelman, Kevin Allison, the Reformed Whores and many others. At 8 p.m., Littlefield, 622 Degraw Street, between Third and Fourth Avenues, Gowanus, Brooklyn, littlefieldnyc.com.
