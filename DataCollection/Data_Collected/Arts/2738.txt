A searchable guide to these and other shows is at nytimes.com/events.The Baylor Project  (Friday through Sunday) Led by the singer Jean Baylor and the drummer Marcus Baylor, also partners in marriage, this group specializes in uplift, exploring the overlap between jazz, gospel and soul. The ensemble’s ranks include Keyon Harrold on trumpet, Keith Loftis on saxophones and Dezron Douglas on bass; on piano Friday and Sunday is a special guest, Cyrus Chestnut. (The pianist on Saturday will be Sullivan Fortner.) At 7, 9 and 10:30 p.m., Smoke, 2751 Broadway, at 106th Street, 212-864-6662, smokejazz.com. (Nate Chinen)Matt Brewer Quintet  (Thursday) Matt Brewer has established a foothold as one of the stalwart young bassists on the progressive jazz scene, in bands led by the alto saxophonists Greg Osby and Steve Lehman. Here, drawing from his forthcoming debut album, he leads a quintet with Mr. Lehman, the tenor saxophonist Kyle Wilson, the guitarist Lage Lund and the drummer Craig Weinrib. At 8:30 p.m., Cornelia Street Café, 29 Cornelia Street, Greenwich Village, 212-989-9319, corneliastreetcafe.com. (Chinen)Billy Buss  (Saturday) Mr. Buss is an accomplished and extensively trained trumpeter who was first runner-up in the most recent Thelonious Monk International Jazz Competition, a prestigious proving ground for younger musicians. As such, he appears here on the Monk-in-Motion series, with his own band, which features another former competition finalist, the saxophonist Godwin Louis. At 7:30 p.m., TriBeCa Performing Arts Center, Borough of Manhattan Community College, 199 Chambers Street, near Greenwich Street, 212-220-1460, tribecapac.org. (Chinen)Billy Childs All-Star Jazz Quartet  (Tuesday through Feb. 21) The pianist Billy Childs has found inspiration both in classical music and the pop repertory; his most recent album is “Map to the Treasure: Reimagining Laura Nyro.” He appears with Steve Wilson on saxophones, Hans Glawischnig on bass and Ari Hoenig on drums. At 8:30 and 11 p.m., Birdland, 315 West 44th Street, Clinton, 212-581-3080, birdlandjazz.com. (Chinen)Gerald Clayton-Dayna Stephens Duo  (Friday) Mr. Clayton, a pianist of smartly soulful exposition, and Mr. Stephens, a tenor saxophonist with no shortage of ideas, have been associates for years; in this engagement they’ll also be equal partners in conversation, working without any additional support. At 8 and 10 p.m., Jazz at Kitano, 66 Park Avenue, at 38th Street, 212-885-7119, kitano.com. (Chinen)Andrew Drury  (Tuesday) Mr. Drury is a drummer and composer who has pitched his tent along the hinterlands of the avant-garde. This concert will feature material from both of his new albums, including “The Drum,” which consists entirely of solo improvisations on a floor tom. The other album, “Content Provider,” involves an ensemble of fellow searchers: the saxophonists Ingrid Laubrock and Briggan Krauss and the guitarist Brandon Seabrook. They’ll be joined by a few guests, including the violinist and violist Jason Kao Hwang. At 8 p.m., Roulette, 509 Atlantic Avenue, near Third Avenue, Boerum Hill, Brooklyn, 917-267-0363, roulette.org. (Chinen)★ Kurt Elling with Gary Versace and John Webber  (Wednesday) A nimble and peripatetic jazz singer, Mr. Elling has always had his romantic side, which makes him a natural for any Valentine’s week appearance. This one is extra-special for its intimacy: he’ll have Mr. Versace, an astute pianist, and Mr. Webber, a strong bassist, at Mezzrow, an elegant new grotto for duos and drummerless trios. At 9 and 10:30 p.m., Mezzrow, 163 West 10th Street, near Seventh Avenue, Greenwich Village, 646-476-4346, mezzrow.com. (Chinen)Champion Fulton  (Wednesday) Ms. Fulton is a suave jazz stylist who has occasionally turned her gaze to the past, as she will here with “Letters to Dinah Washington,” a repertory tribute to the inimitable artist hailed as Queen of the Blues. The radio personality and songbook authority Jonathan Schwartz will serve as host. At 7 p.m., the Greene Space, 44 Charlton Street, at Varick Street, SoHo, 866-811-4111, thegreenespace.org. (Chinen)Sara Gazarek  (Friday and Saturday) A jazz singer of sharp instincts and a strong connection to her musicians, Ms. Gazarek has made a good name for herself in Los Angeles, where she developed her art and still resides. This weekend she wraps up a five-night engagement with Josh Nelson on piano, Matt Aronoff on bass and Zach Harmon on drums. At 9 and 11 p.m., Birdland, 315 West 44th Street, Clinton, 212-581-3080, birdlandjazz.com. (Chinen)Highlights in Jazz  (Thursday) The tradition-minded Highlights in Jazz series breaks in its 43rd season with the singer Catherine Russell and her band, along with a band of repeat guests: the trumpeter Bria Skonberg, the saxophonist and clarinetist Dan Levinson, the pianist Gordon Webster and the bassist and vocalist Nicki Parrott. At 8 p.m., TriBeCa Performing Arts Center, Borough of Manhattan Community College, 199 Chambers Street, near Greenwich Street, 212-220-1460, tribecapac.org. (Chinen)★ Joe Lovano Nonet  (Tuesday through Feb. 22) Joe Lovano, a tenor saxophonist with broad experience in large ensembles, has led this bop-flavored nonet — stocked with peers like the trumpeter Tim Hagans and the saxophonist Steve Slagle — on and off for more than a decade. As ever, it’s a band meant to be heard in this room. At 8:30 and 10:30 p.m., Village Vanguard, 178 Seventh Avenue South, at 11th Street, West Village, 212-255-4037, villagevanguard.com. (Chinen)★ Russell Malone Quartet  (Tuesday and Wednesday) Russell Malone, one of the savviest and most sure-footed jazz guitarists of our time, celebrates the release of his fine new album, “Love Looks Good on You.” Joining him for this two-night stand, as on the album, are the pianist Rick Germanson, the bassist Gerald Cannon and the drummer Willie Jones III. At 7:30 and 10 p.m., Jazz Standard, 116 East 27th Street, Manhattan, 212-576-2232, jazzstandard.net. (Chinen)Matt Mitchell Quartet  (Friday and Saturday) Matt Mitchell, a proficient and probing pianist, has had a hand in some of the better bands of recent vintage, like Tim Berne’s Snakeoil and the Dave Douglas Quintet. He’s wrapping up a four-night residency with his own quartet, which recently recorded an album; his band mates are the saxophonist and clarinetist Chris Speed, the bassist Chris Tordini and the drummer Dan Weiss. At 9 p.m., Seeds, 617 Vanderbilt Avenue, at St. Marks Avenue, Prospect Heights, Brooklyn, seedsbrooklyn.org. (Chinen)T.S. Monk Sextet  (Wednesday and Thursday) T.S. Monk, the son of Thelonious Monk, is a hard-bop drummer of bounce and persistence, and a bandleader of conviction. He doesn’t take many spins around these parts, which lends some shine to this stand with the trumpeter Josh Evans, the saxophonists Patience Higgins and Willie Williams, the pianist Helen Sung and the bassist Kenny Davis. At 7:30 and 9:30 p.m., Dizzy’s Club Coca-Cola, Frederick P. Rose Hall, Jazz at Lincoln Center, 60th Street and Broadway, 212-258-9595, jazz.org. (Chinen)★ Gregory Porter  (Saturday) Mr. Porter is a jazz singer of thrilling presence, a booming baritone with a gift for earthy refinement and soaring uplift. Last year around this time, he made his Town Hall debut on the heels of a win at the Grammys (for best jazz vocal album). Appearing on Valentine’s Day, he should have no problem owning the room. At 8 p.m., Town Hall, 123 West 43rd Street, Manhattan, 800-982-2787, the-townhall-nyc.org. (Chinen)★ Dianne Reeves  (Friday and Saturday) An authoritative and extravagantly gifted jazz singer, Ms. Reeves makes a virtue of her open-mindedness, mingling standards with bossa nova staples and original pop-gospel exhortations. Her most recent album, “Beautiful Life,” includes simmering covers of tunes by Marvin Gaye, Bob Marley and Fleetwood Mac — material more than suitable for this Valentine’s Day concert run, which is fast becoming a tradition. At 8 p.m., Rose Theater, Frederick P. Rose Hall, Jazz at Lincoln Center, 60th Street and Broadway, 212-721-6500, jazz.org. (Chinen)Kermit Ruffins and the Barbecue Swingers  (Tuesday through Feb. 22) The tradition of New Orleans-bred trumpeter-entertainers has a robust representative in Mr. Ruffins, who’ll spend Mardi Gras week here, with his working band. At 8 and 10:30 p.m., Blue Note, 131 West Third Street, Greenwich Village, 212-475-8592, bluenote.net. (Chinen)Tierney Sutton Band  (Thursday through Feb. 22) A jazz singer of cool poise and effervescent style, Tierney Sutton draws from her romantically inclined new album, “Paris Sessions,” in this engagement. She has a strong and unforced bond with her band, featuring Christian Jacob on piano, Kevin Axt on bass and Ray Brinker on drums. At 7:30 and 10 p.m., Jazz Standard, 116 East 27th Street, Manhattan, 212-576-2232, jazzstandard.net. (Chinen)Manuel Valera and the Groove Square  (Friday) A perceptive pianist and composer originally from Cuba, Mr. Valera seeks a contemporary perspective with the Groove Square, featuring the saxophonist John Ellis, the guitarist Nir Felder, the bassist John Benitez and the drummer E.J. Strickland. At 8 and 10 p.m., the Jazz Gallery, 1160 Broadway, fifth floor, at West 27th Street, 646-494-3625, jazzgallery.org. (Chinen)★ John Zorn at the Vanguard  (through Sunday) The composer, alto saxophonist and ringleader John Zorn made his first Village Vanguard incursion last fall, with results encouraging enough to demand this sequel. He’ll close out this repeat appearance in strong form; highlights include a set with the percussionist Milford Graves and the alto saxophonist Steve Coleman, at 8:30 p.m. on Friday; his original Masada quartet, both sets on Saturday; and Bar Kokhba, a chamber-pop Masada ensemble, both sets on Sunday. (A special Sunday matinee, at 3 p.m., will feature the pianist Steve Gosling, the bassist Greg Cohen and the drummer Tyshawn Sorey interpreting an elaborate classical piano piece titled “In the Hall of Mirrors.”) At 8:30 and 10:30 p.m., Village Vanguard, 178 Seventh Avenue South, at 11th Street, West Village, 212-255-4037, villagevanguard.com.
