A searchable guide to these and other shows is at nytimes.com/events.★ Comedy Benefit Show for the Families of Second Avenue (Monday) The Upright Citizen Brigade’s finest improvisers are raffling off the chance to collaborate with various teams and performers at this show. The money goes toward LES Ready!, which is helping families affected by the explosion on March 26. At 9:30 p.m., UCB Theater East, 153 East Third Street, East Village, 212-366-9231, east.ucbtheatre.com; sold out, standby line available. (Elise Czajkowski)Comedy at the Grocery  (Monday) This faithful monthly show celebrates its fourth anniversary with a first-rate show, hosted by Nick Poyner and Evyatar Gour. At 8 p.m., Arlene’s Grocery, 95 Stanton Street, near Orchard Street, Lower East Side, 212-358-1633, arlenesgrocery.net. (Czajkowski)★ ‘Fancy Meeting You Here’  (Friday) Carl Arnheiter and Dave Hill lead this special comedic tour of the Metropolitan Museum of Art, with comedians giving their own take on famous masterpieces. Comics this week include Aparna Nancherla and Liam McEneaney. At 7 p.m., fancymeetingyouhere.org. (Czajkowski)Advertisement‘Female Gaze’  (Wednesday) More than a dozen comedians take part in this parody of daytime talk shows, with each of 14 “co-hosts” bringing her own strong personality and point of view to the issues of the day. At 9:30 p.m., the Upright Citizens Brigade Theater, 307 West 26th Street, Chelsea, 212-366-9176, newyork.ucbtheatre.com. (Czajkowski)Advertisement‘Forty Minutes in the Style of Wes Anderson: Popular Tales Retold’ (Tuesday) The Cinco de Mayo edition of this monthly show sees improvisers retell well-known stories in the style of the indie film director Wes Anderson. At 8 p.m., Knitting Factory, 361 Metropolitan Avenue, at Havemeyer Street, Williamsburg, Brooklyn, 347-529-6696, bk.knittingfactory.com; free. (Czajkowski)‘Greg Proops Is the Smartest Man in the World’  (Thursday) Mr. Proops, a 30-year veteran of stand-up, hosts this “salubrious soliloquy” of a podcast, in which he explores current events and any other topics that interest him. At 8 p.m., the Bell House, 149 Seventh Street, Gowanus, Brooklyn, 718-643-6510, thebellhouseny.com. (Czajkowski)Chris Hardwick  (Saturday) Mr. Hardwick, host of Comedy Central’s Internet-centric game show "@Midnight” and curator of the Nerdist comedy conglomerate, brings his peppy “Funcomfortable Tour” to New York City. At 8 and 10:30 p.m., Best Buy Theater, 515 Broadway at 44th Street, Manhattan, 212-930-1950, bestbuytheater.com. (Czajkowski)‘Kids in the Hall’ (Friday) All five original members of the influential Canadian sketch troupe reunite for a night of live sketch comedy. At 7:30 and 10:30 p.m., Town Hall, 123 West 43rd Street, Manhattan, 800-982-2787, thetownhall.org; sold out. (Czajkowski)★ Joe Machi (Saturday and Sunday) Mr. Machi, a finalist on the most recent season of MTV’s stand-up reality competition “Last Comic Standing,” is a distinct comedic presence who incorporates both the offbeat presentation of performance art with the undeniable punchlines of a working club comic. Saturday at 7:30 and 10 p.m., Sunday at 8 p.m., Carolines on Broadway, 1626 Broadway, at 49th Street, Manhattan, 212-757-4100, carolines.com.
