Aston Villa defender Alan Hutton scored an own goal.United won at Everton, 3-0, with Morgan Schneiderlin, Ander Herrera and Wayne Rooney scoring in an emphatic response to a 3-0 loss to Arsenal on Oct. 4.United is third with 19 points, two behind Manchester City, which routed Bournemouth, 5-1. Raheem Sterling scored a hat trick in the first half and Wilfried Bony netted twice, meaning the injured striker Sergio Aguero was not missed.Earlier Saturday, Jürgen Klopp’s first match as Liverpool’s manager ended in a 0-0 draw against Tottenham.Arsenal scored three times in 12 second-half minutes to win, 3-0, against Watford. Southampton surrendered a two-goal lead to draw, 2-2, at home to Leicester; Sunderland lost, 1-0, at West Brom in Sam Allardyce’s first game in charge of the Black Cats; and Crystal Palace was beaten, 3-1, by West Ham.NEYMAR NETS FOUR Neymar scored four goals and set up another as Barcelona won at home, 5-2, over Rayo Vallecano in the Spanish league, while Cristiano Ronaldo led Real Madrid with a goal and an assist in a 3-0 victory over visiting Levante.The results left the Spanish powerhouses atop the league standings with 18 points from eight matches, with Madrid ahead on goal difference. Villarreal is 2 points behind but can regain the lead when it hosts Celta Vigo on Sunday.Neymar converted penalty kicks in the 22nd and 32nd minutes after being brought down twice inside the penalty area, then added two more from close range in the 69th and 70th. Luis Suárez netted Barcelona’s fifth in the 77th minute.Also Saturday, Eibar held visiting Sevilla to a 1-1 draw; Valencia beat Málaga, 3-0; and Espanyol won, 3-1, at Real Betis.MÜLLER’S GOAL GOOD ENOUGH Thomas Müller’s first-half strike was enough for Bayern Munich to win, 1-0, at Werder Bremen and become the first team to start the German league with nine victories.Müller scored his ninth goal of the season in the 24th minute when he ran past the static Bremen defense for Thiago Alcantara’s clever pass, then eluded the goalkeeper with his first touch and slotted inside the post with his next.Bayern restored its seven-point lead over Borussia Dortmund with its 999th Bundesliga victory.Also Saturday, Max Meyer scored in injury time for Schalke to beat 10-man Hertha Berlin, 2-1, and consolidate third place; Max Kruse scored a hat trick as Wolfsburg defeated Hoffenheim, 4-2; Bayer Leverkusen drew, 0-0, at Hamburger S.V.; and promoted Darmstadt won, 2-0, at Augsburg.Borussia Mönchengladbach routed Eintracht Frankfurt, 5-1, away to continue its resurgence under the interim coach Andre Schubert.IBRAHIMOVIC LEADS P.S.G. Zlatan Ibrahimovic scored twice as Paris Saint-Germain won, 2-0, at Bastia in the French league to keep its confidence high ahead of its Champions League meeting with Real Madrid.P.S.G. didn’t have a shot on target in the first half, but Ibrahimovic, who had an earlier goal disallowed for offside, struck in the 72nd and 83rd minutes as the visitors avenged last year’s 4-2 defeat in Corsica.P.S.G., the league leader, moved provisionally eight points ahead of Angers and Caen, which traveled to Toulouse and Reims later.DE ROSSI’S GOAL LIFTS ROMA Daniele De Rossi marked his 500th game for Roma with a goal as he helped his team beat Empoli, 3-1, in Italy’s Serie A.Two set pieces in three second-half minutes undid Empoli as Miralem Pjanic scored with a free kick before sending in a corner for De Rossi to double Roma’s lead.Mohamed Salah sealed the result 10 minutes later.Marcel Buchel netted a consolation goal for Empoli with his first score for the club.Roma, which has scored 12 goals in its last three league matches, moved provisionally into second ahead of Sunday’s Derby d’Italia between Inter Milan and Juventus.A.C.
