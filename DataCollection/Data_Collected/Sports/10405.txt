AdvertisementSupported bySports Briefing | TennisBy The Associated PressEugenie Bouchard of Canada was upset in her opening match at the Miami Open in Key Biscayne, Fla., by the qualifier Tatjana Maria of Germany, 6-0, 7-6 (4). In the opening match, fourth-seeded Caroline Wozniacki of Denmark rallied to knock off Kaia Kanepi of Estonia, 4-6, 6-1, 6-3. The seven-time champion Serena Williams of the United States won her opening match, beating Monica Niculescu of Romania, 6-3, 6-1. The match was Williams’s first since she withdrew before the semifinals at Indian Wells, Calif., with a right knee injury. Williams pulled away after a slow start. In men’s play, Jo-Wilfried Tsonga of France made a successful 2015 debut by beating the American Tim Smyczek, 6-4, 3-6, 6-3. Tsonga, seeded 11th, sat out the start of this season with a right arm injury. ■ Serena and Venus Williams said they planned to play for the United States’ Fed Cup team next month against Italy. The best-of-five match in Brindisi, Italy, will be held April 18-19. The United States and Italy are vying to return to the tournament’s top tier.“We have some of the best players in the world,” Serena Williams said. “We should be in the World Group A and competing for the title.” Advertisement
