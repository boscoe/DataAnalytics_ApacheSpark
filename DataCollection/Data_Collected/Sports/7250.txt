By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJAN. 30, 2015
The Atlanta Hawks overcame 37 points from Portland’s LaMarcus Aldridge to beat the visiting Trail Blazers, 105-99, on Friday and extend their franchise-record winning streak to 18 games.Portland, which has lost four of five, led after each of the first three quarters, but could not stretch its lead to more than 6 points.The Hawks have not lost in 2015. Their last defeat was to Milwaukee on Dec. 26. Millsap scored 21 points, Al Horford had 17 points and Kyle Korver added 16.CAVALIERS 101, KINGS 90 Kevin Love and Kyrie Irving combined for 44 points, and LeBron James scored 19 in his return from a one-game absence, leading host Cleveland past Sacramento for its ninth straight win. Love led Cleveland with 23 points, and Irving had 21. James had missed Wednesday’s game with a sprained right wrist.AdvertisementThe Kings’ DeMarcus Cousins, who earlier in the day was selected to replace the injured Los Angeles Lakers star Kobe Bryant on the Western Conference’s All-Star team, had 21 points and 13 rebounds.JAZZ 110, WARRIORS 100 Gordon Hayward had 26 points and 15 rebounds as host Utah upset Golden State, the league’s highest-scoring team. Stephen Curry had his ninth 30-point game of the season, finishing with 32 for the Warriors.AdvertisementPELICANS 108, CLIPPERS 103 Eric Gordon scored a season-high 28 points, and host New Orleans ended Los Angeles’s six-game winning streak. With Anthony Davis sitting out with a sore left groin, the Pelicans benefited from exceptional shooting from behind the 3-point line, hitting 13 of their 25 attempts.MAVERICKS 93, HEAT 72 Charlie Villanueva scored 20 points, and Dallas went on a 37-2 run in the second half to rout host Miami. Dallas was down by 16 points in the third quarter and down 9 with about three minutes to go in the third before turning the game around.
