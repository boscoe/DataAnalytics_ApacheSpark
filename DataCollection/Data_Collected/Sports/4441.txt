By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSDEC. 10, 2015
Mark Emmert, the N.C.A.A.’s president, said it was time for universities to take a look at the bowl system and determine the purpose of some postseason games.Three teams with losing records will be among the 80 teams playing in a record 40 bowl games over the next month. The N.C.A.A. football oversight committee was forced to tweak a safety net program to allow 5-7 teams to be bowl-eligible based on their Academic Progress Rates. This off-season, a task force will study the bowl system.A version of this brief appears in print on December 10, 2015, on Page B14 of the New York edition with the headline: Emmert Backs Bowl Review.
