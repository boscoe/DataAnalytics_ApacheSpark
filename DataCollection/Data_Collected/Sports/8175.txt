By KEN BELSONFEB. 18, 2015
Joe DeLamielluere, one the biggest critics of the settlement in a suit that accused the N.F.L. of failing to warn former players about the dangers of concussions, has reversed himself and joined the agreement, according to a court filing on Tuesday. DeLamielluere, a former offensive lineman, joined nine others who initially opted out but will now take part in the settlement, which provides up to $5 million for a limited number of severe neurological conditions. In all, 211 retired players, or about 1 percent of the total, opted out of the deal, which lawyers for the N.F.L. and the former players revised Friday to meet some concerns of Anita B.
