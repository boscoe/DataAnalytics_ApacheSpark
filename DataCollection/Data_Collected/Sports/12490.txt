By REUTERSMAY 6, 2015
Manny Pacquiao was sued by two people who said they paid to watch him fight Floyd Mayweather Jr. but felt defrauded by Pacquiao’s failure to disclose a shoulder injury. Stephane Vanel and Kami Rahbaran filed the lawsuit in federal court in Nevada. It seeks status as a class action on behalf of ticket buyers, pay-per-view television viewers and people who gambled on the fight. Top Rank, a company that represents Pacquiao, was also named as a defendant in the suit.Pacquiao lost on a unanimous decision to Mayweather in a heavily hyped welterweight showdown in Las Vegas on Saturday. About an hour after the contest ended, Pacquiao’s promoter, Bob Arum, said the boxer had been hampered by an old injury to his right shoulder. Neither Pacquiao nor his team appeared to have informed the Nevada Athletic Commission about the shoulder problem until a couple of hours before the fight.A version of this brief appears in print on May 6, 2015, on Page B12 of the New York edition with the headline: Suit Claims Pacquiao Defrauded Fans.
