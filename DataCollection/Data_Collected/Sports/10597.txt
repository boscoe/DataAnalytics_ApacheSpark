RoundupBy The Associated PressChris Kreider scored with 3 minutes 46 seconds left in the third period, and Henrik Lundqvist and the visiting Rangers rallied to beat the Winnipeg Jets, 3-2, on Tuesday night.Lundqvist, playing for the second time since missing 25 games with a neck injury, stopped 32 shots and earned his first win since his return.Kreider beat defensemen Adam Pardy and Dustin Byfuglien near the blue line and sent a backhand deke past the sprawling goaltender Ondrej Pavelec to break a 2-2 tie.The playoff-bound Rangers, who also got goals from Mats Zuccarello and Derick Brassard, ended a two-game skid and moved to 1 point ahead of Montreal atop the Eastern Conference.Jim Slater and Lee Stempniak scored for Winnipeg. Pavelec made 21 saves in the loss.Slater scored his fourth goal of the season and second in three games. He kicked a loose puck to his stick at the side of the net and beat Lundqvist at 3:16 of the first period to put the Jets ahead, 1-0.Zuccarello tied it six and a half minutes later off a rebound after a Rick Nash shot for his 15th goal.Winnipeg outshot the Rangers, 16-9, in the first period.Stempniak, acquired in a trade with the Rangers on March 1, capitalized on a pass from Mathieu Perreault to restore Winnipeg’s lead in the second.Perreault was skating along the boards past the face-off circle and suddenly sent a pass back and out to Stempniak, who beat Lundqvist high on the glove side at 1:24 to make it 2-1.The Rangers tied it again when Brassard benefited from a screened Pavelec to score his 17th goal of the season at 10:13 on the power play.The teams tied, 7-7, on shots in the second period.BLUE JACKETS 3, DEVILS 2 Jack Johnson’s backhander 56 seconds into overtime lifted host Columbus over the Devils, extending its winning streak to seven games.It was the Devils’ sixth straight loss. They have scored seven goals during the skid.Mike Cammalleri scored a power-play goal in the first period and added a short-handed tally with 2:29 left in regulation for the Devils. Cory Schneider had 28 saves.CAPITALS 4, HURRICANES 2 Alex Ovechkin scored his N.H.L.-leading 50th goal, reaching that mark for the sixth time and tying a team record with the 472nd of his career, and added an assist as host Washington beat Carolina.CANUCKS 5, PREDATORS 4 Nick Bonino and Radim Vrbata scored in the shootout, and visiting Vancouver defeated Nashville to avoid a sweep.SENATORS 2, RED WINGS 1 Mark Stone scored the final goal in the shootout, and visiting Ottawa edged Detroit.BRUINS 3, PANTHERS 2 Milan Lucic scored the tiebreaking goal with 1:09 remaining as Boston rallied to defeat visiting Florida, giving the Bruins 2 valuable points as they try to hang on to the final playoff spot in the Eastern Conference.MAPLE LEAFS 3, LIGHTNING 1 Nazem Kadri had a goal and assist, and host Toronto played the role of spoiler in a win over Tampa Bay.Advertisement
