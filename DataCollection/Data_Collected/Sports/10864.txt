By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSAPRIL 6, 2015
Wisconsin’s win over previously undefeated Kentucky drew the most viewers for an N.C.A.A. semifinal in 19 years.The Badgers’ 71-64 victory Saturday night averaged 22.6 million viewers across TBS, TNT and truTV, according to Turner Sports and CBS. That was up 39 percent from last season’s matchup between the same teams.■ Kentucky guard Andrew Harrison apologized for directing an obscenity and a racial slur at Wisconsin’s Frank Kaminsky during a postgame news conference after the Wildcats’ loss.Harrison’s comment came as a reporter asked Kentucky’s Karl-Anthony Towns about defending against the 7-foot Kaminsky.Harrison muttered the comment with his hand in front of his mouth, but a microphone picked it up.A version of this brief appears in print on April 6, 2015, on Page D10 of the New York edition with the headline: Wisconsin’s Win a TV Hit.
