The Flyers, who won their seventh straight game, will face the winner of Monday’s game between Louisville and South Florida in the Albany Region. Kelley Austria made four free throws in the final 21 seconds and scored 17 points over all for Dayton, which hit 11 3-pointers to help overcome 24 turnovers. Makayla Epps scored 22 of her 29 points in the second half to lead Kentucky (24-10). TEXAS 73, CALIFORNIA 70 Imani McGee-Stafford had 20 points and 11 rebounds while creating a daunting mismatch inside, and fifth-seeded Texas advanced to the program’s first round of 16 in 11 years with a win against No. 4-seeded California in Berkeley, Calif. A year after losing a tough second-round game on Maryland’s home floor, the Longhorns had no problem taking on the Golden Bears (24-10) with another feisty home crowd against them. The Longhorns (24-10) advanced to the Albany Regional and a potential date with the two-time defending champion Connecticut.OKLAHOMA CITY REGIONNOTRE DAME 79, DEPAUL 67 Michaela Mabrey scored 19 points, hitting five 3-pointers, and Taya Reimer and Brianna Turner added 14 points each as No. 1-seeded Notre Dame beat DePaul, the No. 9 seed, in South Bend, Ind. Notre Dame (33-2) held DePaul (27-8) to 36 percent shooting to win its 19th straight game and advance to the round of 16 for the sixth straight season.Women’s N.C.A.A. bracket.BAYLOR 73, ARKANSAS 44 Nina Davis scored 21 points, Niya Johnson had 12 assists to tie Baylor’s career record, and the Lady Bears advanced to the round of 16 for the seventh year in a row with a win over No. 10-seeded Arkansas (18-14) in Waco, Tex. The Lady Bears (32-3), a No. 2 seed, took control with a 30-7 run to finish the first half. Baylor will play Iowa on Friday in Oklahoma City.IOWA 88, MIAMI 70 Bethany Doolittle scored 22 points, Ally Disterhoft had 15, and Iowa beat 11th-seeded Miami in Iowa City to earn its first trip to the round of 16 since 1996. Doolittle also had 11 rebounds for the third-seeded Hawkeyes (26-7). Miami finished at 20-13.SPOKANE REGIONGONZAGA 76, OREGON STATE 64 Emma Wolfram scored a career-high 17 points, and 11th-seeded Gonzaga held off a furious late rally by the third-seeded Oregon State Beavers in Corvallis, Ore. Gonzaga (26-7) will face the winner of Monday’s game in Knoxville, Tenn., between second-seeded Tennessee (28-5) and No. 10-seeded Pittsburgh (20-11). Fourth-seeded Duke (23-10) will play the Princeton-Maryland winner on Saturday in the Spokane Regional semifinals. Aleighsa Welch added 13 points and 10 rebounds for the Gamecocks (32-2). Syracuse trailed by 28 at halftime and never got closer than 20 points.
