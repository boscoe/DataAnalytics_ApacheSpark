By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSFEB. 26, 2015
John Coates, the International Olympic Committee vice president who almost a year ago called Rio de Janeiro’s preparations for the 2016 Olympics the worst in memory, now says much has changed. “I’m a lot more relaxed,” said Coates, who gave some of the credit to Gilbert Felli, a former Olympics official who has been in Rio as a troubleshooter.■ Nevada gambling regulators voted to allow the state’s sports books to offer bets on the Olympics.A version of this brief appears in print on February 27, 2015, on Page B15 of the New York edition with the headline: Feeling Better About Rio.
