By REUTERSJAN. 13, 2015
The West Indies star Chris Gayle accused his nation’s cricket board of victimization after the all-rounders Dwayne Bravo and Kieron Pollard were left out of the country’s World Cup squad. Gayle said he was flabbergasted by the dismissals, which he felt had little to do with performance. Bravo and Pollard led a player revolt in India that led to the abandonment of the West Indies tour last October after a pay dispute.A version of this brief appears in print on January 13, 2015, on Page B12 of the New York edition with the headline: Criticism After World Cup Omissions.
