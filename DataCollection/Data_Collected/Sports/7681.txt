By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSFEB. 7, 2015
Chelsea extended its Premier League lead to 7 points by winning at Aston Villa, 2-1, on Saturday while Manchester City was held to a 1-1 draw by visiting Hull.Eden Hazard and Branislav Ivanovic scored for Chelsea, and Jores Okore headed one in for Villa.It was José Mourinho’s first Premier League victory against Villa at its home stadium as Chelsea’s manager.“Obviously, it is a very important victory for us,” Mourinho said. “There is a long way to go; the Premier League is the Premier League, and every game is difficult. It is the only league in the world where the teams from the bottom, they can play the teams from the top and not just make it difficult, they can win matches and points.”City, the defending champion, needed a free kick in injury time from midfielder James Milner to salvage a point against Hull, which took the lead with a David Meyler goal in the first half.Advertisement“It is more difficult to reverse 7 points than 5 points, but Chelsea can also lose games,” City Manager Manuel Pellegrini said. “More importantly, for our team it is important to recover our performances.”AdvertisementElsewhere, Tottenham came from behind to beat its north London rival, Arsenal, 2-1 with two goals from striker Harry Kane.Mesut Ozil lashed a volley past Hugo Lloris after 11 minutes to give Arsenal the lead, but Arsenal immediately sat back and invited pressure from Tottenham.Kane converted a rebound in the 56th minute after Arsenal goalkeeper David Ospina made a brilliant save to keep out Mousa Dembele’s header, then nodded in Nabil Bentaleb’s cross four minutes from the end.Liverpool could not break down a stubborn Everton defense at Goodison Park in a scoreless draw.REAL MADRID IS ROUTED Atlético Madrid thrashed its crosstown rival, Real Madrid, 4-0, throwing open the Spanish league title race.Atlético, the defending league champion, got a quick start at Vicente Calderón Stadium as Tiago Cardoso and the substitute Saúl Ñíguez scored early, and then Antoine Griezmann and Mario Mandzukic sealed Atlético’s victory, its biggest over Madrid since 1987.Please verify you're not a robot by clicking the box.Invalid email address.
