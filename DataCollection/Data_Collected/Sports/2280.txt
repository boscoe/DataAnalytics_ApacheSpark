By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSNOV. 3, 2015
Real Madrid and Manchester City became the first teams to reach the second round of the Champions League, with Madrid beating previously unbeaten Paris St.-Germain, 1-0, at home in Group A and City winning at Sevilla, 3-1.City leads Group D with 9 points, 1 ahead of Juventus, which eliminated host Borussia Mönchengladbach from contention with a 1-1 draw.Wayne Rooney ended Manchester United’s 404-minute goal drought, clinching a 1-0 home victory over CSKA Moscow that sent United to the top of Group B.Benfica took advantage of Atlético Madrid’s scoreless draw at Astana, moving atop Group C by beating Galatasaray, 2-1, at home.■ The president of the German soccer federation, Wolfgang Niersbach, and two former high-ranking officials, Theo Zwanziger and Horst R. Schmidt, are the targets of a tax-evasion inquiry over a payment to FIFA in connection with the 2006 World Cup, the authorities said. Police officers and tax inspectors searched the premises of the federation and three private homes, seizing written and electronic documents, according to a Frankfurt prosecutor, who called it “a particularly serious case” that could result in a sentence of six months to 10 years in prison.■ Espanyol, an indebted Spanish club, said the Rastar Group, a Chinese company, had bought more than 50 percent of its shares and would invest in the team.A version of this brief appears in print on November 4, 2015, on Page B12 of the New York edition with the headline: Madrid and Manchester City Advance.
