By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSNOV. 2, 2015
Patrick Kane and Jonathan Toews each had a goal and an assist to lead the Chicago Blackhawks to a 4-2 victory over visiting Los Angeles, ending the Kings’ winning streak at seven. Kane scored to tie it at 2-2, then set up Teuvo Teravainen’s tiebreaking goal in the third as Chicago scored three goals in the final period.The Blackhawks’ Corey Crawford made 33 saves, and the Kings’ Jonathan Quick stopped 22 shots.■ Joffrey Lupul had two goals and an assist, James Reimer stopped 43 shots, and the Toronto Maple Leafs beat the Dallas Stars, 4-1, for their first home win.A version of this brief appears in print on November 3, 2015, on Page B16 of the New York edition with the headline: Blackhawks Defeat Kings.
