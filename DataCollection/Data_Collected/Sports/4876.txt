By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSDEC. 17, 2015
Dwyane Wade scored 10 of his 28 points in the fourth quarter, and Hassan Whiteside had 20 points and 13 rebounds as the Miami Heat beat the Nets, 104-98, on Wednesday night at Barclays Center.Goran Dragic added 16 points for Miami, which has won three straight. The Heat have also won five in a row against the Nets and 11 of their last 13 meetings on the road.“Dwyane had a real pep in his step,” Heat Coach Erik Spoelstra said. “This is what he’s built his career on. He had the full pie chart going.”Brook Lopez scored 25 points, and Jarrett Jack had 22 points and 10 assists for the Nets, who lost the final three games of their six-game homestand.AdvertisementThe Heat took an 11-point lead on a Wade basket with 5 minutes 14 seconds left in the third quarter, but the Nets cut the gap to 86-85 on a 3-point play by Donald Sloan with 7:31 left in the game.AdvertisementThe Heat then went on an 11-2 run, capped by another Wade basket with 4:09 remaining.The Nets again trimmed their deficit, to 101-98, as Jack made three free throws with 1:01 left. However, Wade countered with a floater with 43 seconds remaining.“You never know,” Wade said. “Last game I got so many great shots in Atlanta and couldn’t knock them down. I told my teammates if I get those same shots versus Brooklyn, they’re going in.”Thaddeus Young missed a reverse layup on the Nets’ next possession after Wade’s basket. Wade got the rebound, and the Nets did not commit a foul.“I told them to foul,” Nets Coach Lionel Hollins said. “I mean, come on.
