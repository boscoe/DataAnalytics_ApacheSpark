By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJAN. 4, 2015
Matt Hendricks scored twice on Sunday night as the host Edmonton Oilers beat the Islanders, 5-2, ending the Islanders’ winning streak at three games.Jordan Eberle, Derek Roy and Matt Fraser also scored for the Oilers, who won for just the third time in their last 25 games. Edmonton, however, improved to 2-0-2 in its last four games since the interim head coach Todd Nelson took sole possession of the Oilers’ bench, with General Manager Craig MacTavish moving back upstairs.Brock Nelson scored his 15th goal and Johnny Boychuk added his fourth goal for the Islanders, who are 7-2-1 in their last 10 games and 26-12-1 over all. They are tied with the Pittsburgh Penguins atop the Metropolitan Division.John Tavares assisted on both Islanders goals.CAPITALS 4, PANTHERS 3 Alex Ovechkin had a goal and an assist for host Washington against Florida.AdvertisementBrooks Laich and Karl Alzner added second-period goals for Washington, which has won 10 straight home games against the Panthers.AdvertisementThe Capitals improved to 10-1-3 in their last 14 games and avoided a letdown after their 3-2 win against Chicago in the Winter Classic on New Year’s Day.Braden Holtby made 29 saves for Washington.Nick Bjugstad had a goal and an assist for Florida, which also got goals from Vincent Trocheck and Dylan Olsen.The Panthers’ Roberto Luongo, who was involved in a scary first-period collision with Ovechkin, stopped 18 of 21 shots in the first two periods before giving way to Al Montoya.The Panthers fell to 1-1 during their six-game trip and have lost three of four.
