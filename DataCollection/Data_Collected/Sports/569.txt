RoundupBy The Associated PressKyle Turris scored twice, and the Senators won a coach’s challenge to overturn a third-period Sabres goal and hang on for a 3-1 victory on Thursday night.Buffalo forward Evander Kane scored on a wraparound that would have tied the score at 2-2. Ottawa Coach Dave Cameron challenged the goal, and a review showed that forward Zemgus Girgensons was offside as Buffalo entered the zone.The coach’s challenge was introduced as a rule this season. It came 83 seconds after Jack Eichel, the Sabres’ 18-year-old rookie center and future face of the franchise, scored a power-play goal, rousing Buffalo’s sellout crowd. BLUES 3, OILERS 1 The St. Louis rookie Robby Fabbri scored the tiebreaking goal midway through the third period to help defeat Edmonton and spoil the debut of Connor McDavid, the top overall pick and Fabbri’s childhood friend. Vladimir Tarasenko had the tying goal for the host Blues near the midpoint of the second period, and Troy Brouwer added an empty-netter. LIGHTNING 3, FLYERS 2 Jason Garrison scored his second goal of the game in overtime, and Tampa Bay edged visiting Philadelphia.Garrison’s goal came on a breakaway in the league’s first regular-season three-on-three overtime.PREDATORS 2, HURRICANES 1 Craig Smith had a power-play goal as Nashville scored twice in the first period and held off Carolina.Viktor Arvidsson scored the first goal of his career, and Seth Jones had two assists for the host Predators. Pekka Rinne lost a shutout as Eric Staal scored with 1:40 left. JETS 6, BRUINS 2 Chris Thorburn and Nicolas Petan scored in the third after Boston cut its deficit to one on a David Pastrnak goal to help Winnipeg secure a road win.
