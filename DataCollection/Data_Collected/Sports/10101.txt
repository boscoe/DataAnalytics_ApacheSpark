Kaleena Mosqueda-Lewis scored 23 points to lead top-seeded Connecticut to a 91-55 rout of Rutgers in Storrs, Conn., on Coach Geno Auriemma’s 61st birthday Monday night, putting the Huskies into the round of 16 of the women’s N.C.A.A. tournament’s for the 22nd straight year.Moriah Jefferson added 19 points, and Morgan Tuck had 16 points, 7 rebounds and 7 assists for the Huskies (34-1), who won their 33rd consecutive game and their 79th in 80 tries. They will face fifth-seeded Texas in the Albany Region’s semifinals.Tyler Scaife had 16 points for eighth-seeded Rutgers (23-10), which was in its first N.C.A.A. tournament since 2012.UConn had a 20-point lead at halftime and ran away from Rutgers after the Scarlet Knights lost guard Briyona Canty to a knee injury less than two minutes into the second half.AdvertisementLOUISVILLE 60, S.
