AdvertisementSupported bySports Briefing | BaseballBy The Associated PressIchiro Suzuki signed a one-year contract Tuesday to remain with the Miami Marlins, giving him an opportunity to reach 3,000 hits in the majors.Suzuki, who will turn 42 on Oct. 22, is tied for 33rd on the career hits list with 2,935. He had 91 hits in 153 games for the Marlins this season but batted a career-low .229, dropping his career average to .314. His slugging percentage this year was .279, the lowest among all major-league players with more than 300 at-bats. Injuries to other outfielders — including the slugger Giancarlo Stanton — made Suzuki an everyday player. Advertisement
