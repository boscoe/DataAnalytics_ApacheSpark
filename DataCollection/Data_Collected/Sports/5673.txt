By BEN SHPIGELJAN. 1, 2015
Make Doug Marrone the Jets’ seventh confirmed coaching candidate. The team plans to speak next week with Marrone, who opted out as coach of the Buffalo Bills on Wednesday, but no interview has been scheduled yet, said a person with knowledge of the team’s thinking.The Jets have already met with their assistant head coach, Anthony Lynn. They are scheduled to speak this weekend in Seattle with two members of the Seahawks’ staff, the defensive coordinator Dan Quinn and the assistant head coach Tom Cable, and at some point next week with the San Diego offensive coordinator Frank Reich. The Baltimore offensive coordinator Gary Kubiak and the Arizona defensive coordinator Todd Bowles also remain possibilities.A potential candidate for general manager dropped out of contention Thursday when the Baltimore assistant general manager Eric DeCosta declined the Jets’ interview request.A version of this brief appears in print on January 2, 2015, on Page B10 of the New York edition with the headline: Marrone, Ex-Bills Coach, Is Among Seven Candidates for Jets.
