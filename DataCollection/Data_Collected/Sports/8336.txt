By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSFEB. 20, 2015
A matchup of the Eastern Conference’s top teams turned into a rout as Toronto opened the second half of the season with a 105-80 win over the host Atlanta Hawks on Friday night.Lou Williams had 26 points and made 7 of 10 3-pointers, and DeMar DeRozan had 21 points for Toronto. The Raptors outscored the Hawks by 28-13 in the third quarter, breaking open what had been a close game and establishing a 23-point lead.Atlanta still has a five-and-a-half-game lead over Toronto in the Eastern Conference standings, but the Raptors won the season series between them, 3-1.The Hawks have lost four of seven since a team-record 19-game winning streak.Paul Millsap, Kyle Korver and Jeff Teague had only 11 points each for Atlanta.AdvertisementCAVALIERS 127, WIZARDS 89 Despite first-half foul trouble, LeBron James finished with 28 points in only 25 minutes, and Kyrie Irving scored 25, helping surging Cleveland overwhelm host Washington.The 38-point margin was Washington’s largest in a loss this season.TIMBERWOLVES 111, SUNS 109 Kevin Martin scored 28 points and Andrew Wiggins added 20, leading host Minnesota over Phoenix.Ricky Rubio had 10 points, 14 assists and 8 rebounds for the Timberwolves, who were playing their final home game before the return of Kevin Garnett.Markieff Morris scored 31 points for the Suns, who have lost six of seven and have fallen behind Oklahoma City in the race for the final Western Conference playoff spot.BUCKS 89, NUGGETS 81 Khris Middleton scored 15 points, John Henson added 14, and Milwaukee beat visiting Denver.AdvertisementHenson had four baskets during a 20-7 run over the last seven minutes of the third quarter.Michael Carter-Williams, who was acquired on Thursday from Philadelphia as part of a three-team deal that sent Brandon Knight to Phoenix, did not play because of a sprained right big toe.Please verify you're not a robot by clicking the box.Invalid email address.
