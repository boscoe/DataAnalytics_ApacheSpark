By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJAN. 20, 2015
D’Vauntes Smith-Rivera and the freshman reserve Isaac Copeland each scored 17 points as host Georgetown (13-5, 5-2 Big East) shocked No. 4 Villanova (17-2, 4-2), 78-58, holding the Wildcats to 33 percent shooting from the field.■ Mike Krzyzewski notched his 999th career victory as fifth-ranked Duke (16-2, 4-2 Atlantic Coast Conference) beat visiting Pittsburgh (13-6, 3-3) by 79-65. Krzyzewski, who started his head-coaching career at Army, improved to 999-308 over 40 years. His first chance to become the first men’s coach in N.C.A.A. Division I to reach 1,000 victories will be Sunday against St. John’s at Madison Square Garden.■ Kelly Oubre Jr. scored 19 points, and No. 11 Kansas (15-3, 4-1 Big 12) held on for an 85-78 home victory over No. 19 Oklahoma (12-6, 3-3) after blowing a 20-point first-half lead.  (AP)■ In women’s play, Jewell Loyd had 33 points, and No. 6 Notre Dame (17-2) beat fifth-ranked Tennessee, 88-77, ending an 11-game winning streak for the visiting Lady Volunteers (15-3).A version of this brief appears in print on January 20, 2015, on Page B11 of the New York edition with the headline: Georgetown Topples Villanova.
