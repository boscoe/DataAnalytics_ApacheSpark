By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSFEB. 12, 2015
Allisha Gray scored 13 of her 22 points in the final six minutes to lead No. 17 North Carolina to a comeback win, 71-63, over No. 7 Florida State on Thursday. Gray added 14 rebounds for the host Tar Heels (20-5, 7-4 Atlantic Coast Conference) and took over in the final minutes. The loss by the Seminoles (22-3, 9-2) ended a nine-game winning streak.FLORIDA 51, GEORGIA 48 Cassie Peoples hit two 3-pointers in the final four minutes to help Florida beat No. 24 Georgia for its first win in Athens since 2004. Peoples finished with 11 points for Florida (12-12, 4-7 Southeastern Conference). Erika Ford had 14 off the bench for Georgia (17-8, 5-7).KENTUCKY 92, MISS. STATE 90  Makayla Epps capped a career night with a putback basket as time expired in the second overtime to give No. 10 Kentucky a wild win over No. 13 Mississippi State at home. Epps scored 42 points for the Wildcats (19-5, 8-3 SEC). Victoria Vivians scored a career-high 39 points for the Bulldogs (23-4, 9-4).MIAMI 85, SYRACUSE 71 Suriya McGuire was 7 of 11 from the field, and visiting Miami upset No. 23 Syracuse. Miami (17-7, 7-4 A.C.C.) took the lead for good just over five minutes into the first half. Alexis Peterson led Syracuse (17-8, 7-5) with 21 points.AdvertisementSOUTH CAROLINA 86, L.S.U. 62 A’ja Wilson scored a career-high 26 points, and top-ranked South Carolina routed visiting Louisiana State. Wilson’s 12 steals and 18 fast-break points broke open a close game for the Gamecocks (23-1, 11-0 SEC). L.S.U. (14-10, 8-4) had won four straight and seven of nine coming into the game.TENNESSEE 69, MISSISSIPPI 49 Isabelle Harrison had 21 points and 14 rebounds to lead No. 6 Tennessee past host Mississippi. Tennessee (21-3, 11-0 SEC) scored a few seconds after the opening tip and never trailed. The Lady Vols have won 17 of 18 games dating to Dec. 3. Mississippi (14-10, 4-7) has lost six straight.IOWA 69, NEBRASKA 61 Ally Disterhoft had 14 points and 7 rebounds, and No. 14 Iowa overcame No. 22 Nebraska with a late surge. Kali Peschel added 13 points for the visiting Hawkeyes (20-4, 11-2 Big Ten), who won their third straight game.
