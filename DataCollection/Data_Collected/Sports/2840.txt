By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSNOV. 12, 2015
Scotty Stirling, a former National Basketball Association vice president for operations and a longtime team executive and scout, died on Wednesday in Sacramento. He was 86.The N.B.A. announced his death.A former sportswriter for The Oakland Tribune, Mr. Stirling, whose given name was Gordon, became the Knicks’ general manager in 1986, taking over for Dave DeBusschere, who was fired with the team’s record at 11-22.Mr. Stirling was fired the next year, after the Knicks ended the season at 24-58, and spent the next 27 years with the Sacramento Kings, including 24 seasons as scouting director.Earlier, he had been general manager of the 1967 American Football League champion Oakland Raiders and the 1968-69 American Basketball Association champion Oakland Oaks.AdvertisementHe was assistant to the president of the Golden State Warriors from 1976 to 1982 and spent three years as the N.B.A.’s vice president for basketball operations.An Army veteran, he graduated from the University of San Francisco and earned a master’s degree from Adelphi University.AdvertisementHe is survived by his wife, Pam; three sons, Gordon, Kevin and Donald, a member of the Utah Jazz organization; a sister, Helen Boak; a brother, Archie; six grandchildren; and three great-grandchildren.A version of this article appears in print on November 13, 2015, on Page B15 of the New York edition with the headline: Scotty Stirling, 86, Former N.B.A. and Team Executive .
