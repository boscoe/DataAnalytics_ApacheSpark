By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJAN. 26, 2015
Scott Dixon drove a gritty and long final leg to lead Chip Ganassi Racing to a record sixth victory in the Rolex 24 at Daytona International Speedway in Florida.Dixon, a three-time IndyCar champion, relieved Nascar’s reigning rookie of the year, Kyle Larson, with 3 hours 32 minutes remaining in the twice-around-the-clock endurance race.The team of Dixon, Larson, the Daytona 500 winner Jamie McMurray and the Indianapolis 500 winner Tony Kanaan beat the defending race winner, Action Express Racing, by 1.33 seconds.A version of this brief appears in print on January 26, 2015, on Page D6 of the New York edition with the headline: Dixon Keys Win at Rolex 24.
