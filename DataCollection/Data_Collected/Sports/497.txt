By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSOCT. 8, 2015
South Carolina is moving its home game against Louisiana State on Saturday to Baton Rouge because of flooding around the university. South Carolina officials wanted to keep the game in Columbia but said that law enforcement personnel were needed elsewhere in the area to help clean up from the flooding.■ Eastern Kentucky moved Thursday night’s Ohio Valley Conference game against Tennessee Tech north to Georgetown College after a security threat led university officials to cancel classes for the rest of this week.■ The Rutgers football team reinstated wide receiver Leonte Carroo, a day after criminal charges against him were dropped. Coach Kyle Flood said Carroo had agreed to undisclosed conditions and “accepted the responsibility” that came with his return to the team.■ Tennessee wide receiver Alton Howard was dismissed from the team for a violation of unspecified team rules. Howard had 54 catches for 618 yards last season, but he had only one reception for 8 yards this year while playing in two games.■ The former Michigan offensive tackle Logan Tuley-Tillman was charged with three felonies, nearly a month after he was kicked off the team. Tuley-Tillman was arraigned on one count of using a computer to commit a crime and two counts of capturing/distributing an image of an unclothed person.A version of this brief appears in print on October 8, 2015, on Page B16 of the New York edition with the headline: Game Moved After Floods.
