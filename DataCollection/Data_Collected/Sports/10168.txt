By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 25, 2015
Two former Toledo basketball players were given probation for their role in shaving points for gamblers who bet on games a decade ago. Sammy Villegas and Anton Currie were sentenced in Detroit federal court.■ Arrest warrants were issued for two dismissed Dayton basketball players in a series of burglaries on campus. The Montgomery County prosecutor Mat Heck Jr. said that Jalen Robinson, 21, and Devin Scott, 20, broke into dormitory rooms on Dec. 14.■ An Oklahoma man pleaded guilty to second-degree murder in the 2013 shooting death of a college baseball player from Australia. Michael DeWayne Jones, 19, was sentenced to life in prison for his role in the killing of Christopher Lane, who was jogging in the city of Duncan.■ Arizona State fired Herb Sendek after nine seasons as men’s basketball coach. Sendek went 159-137 at Arizona State, with two N.C.A.A. tournament appearances.■ Ben Howland, the former U.C.L.A. coach, was introduced as Mississippi State’s men’s basketball coach.■ Jordan Bowling had 18 points and 7 rebounds, and Dalton (Ga.) State College cruised to a 71-53 victory over Westmont (Calif.) College in the N.A.I.A. basketball national championship game in Kansas City, Mo.A version of this brief appears in print on March 25, 2015, on Page B14 of the New York edition with the headline: Two Sentenced in Gambling Scandal.
