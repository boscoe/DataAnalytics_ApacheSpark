By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSFEB. 23, 2015
The lightweight Terence Crawford, who won three fights in 2014, was voted fighter of the year by the Boxing Writers Association of America. Crawford, an American, won the World Boxing Organization’s version of the 135-pound title with a unanimous decision over Ricky Burns in Scotland, successfully defended it against Yuriorkis Gamboa of Cuba and added a win over Ray Beltrán of Mexico to cap his year. In the voting, Crawford edged out the light-heavyweight champion Sergey Kovalev of Russia, who also won three fights in 2014, including a victory over Bernard Hopkins of the United States.A version of this brief appears in print on February 24, 2015, on Page B14 of the New York edition with the headline: American Named Fighter of the Year.
