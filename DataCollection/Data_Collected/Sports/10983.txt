By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSAPRIL 8, 2015
Sam Holtz, a sixth grader from Hawthorn Woods, Ill., a Chicago suburb, completed a near-perfect bracket, missing only six of 67 games in the N.C.A.A. men’s basketball tournament and finishing in a tie for first in ESPN’s contest. Holtz said ESPN officials had told him that was he ineligible to claim the top prize — a $20,000 gift card and a trip to the Maui Invitational basketball tournament — because he is 12 years old. ESPN requires participants to be at least 18. ESPN awards the prize through a random drawing of the brackets that were among the top 1 percent in the contest — about 115,700 this year.
