By MARC TRACYOCT. 12, 2015
South Carolina Coach Steve Spurrier told his players Monday he was retiring, effective immediately, according to multiple news media reports citing anonymous sources.A spokesman for South Carolina did not immediately respond to a request for comment. The Gamecocks, who fell to Louisiana State last week by 45-24, are 2-4 (0-4 in the Southeastern Conference) and host Vanderbilt on Saturday.Spurrier, the winner of the 1966 Heisman Trophy as a quarterback for Florida, has been a head coach for three Division I teams for a combined 27 seasons. He helped revive the Gamecocks’ moribund program upon taking over in 2005, compiling a record of 86-49.Spurrier, 70, also coached Duke from 1987 to 1989, but he is best known for his time at Florida, where he delivered the Gators their first national title, in 1996, and finished with an overall record of 122-27-1. The only coach with more SEC wins is the storied Alabama coach Bear Bryant. Spurrier’s teams have finished in the top 10 of the Associated Press poll 11 times.He also coached the N.F.L.’s Washington Redskins in 2002 and 2003, going 12-20.A version of this article appears in print on October 13, 2015, on Page B14 of the New York edition with the headline: Spurrier Retiring, Effective Immediately, Reports Say.
