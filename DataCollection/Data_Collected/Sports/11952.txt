By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSAPRIL 27, 2015
Kurt Busch outran his Stewart-Haas Racing teammate Kevin Harvick over the final dozen laps on Sunday to win the rain-delayed Toyota Owners 400, the Nascar Sprint Cup race at Richmond International Raceway in Virginia, and end a 35-race winless streak.Busch, who missed the first three races of the season while serving a suspension because of a domestic-violence case, led 291 laps in a race that had only two green flag lead changes — when Busch passed Joey Logano after 94 laps to take the lead and when Jamie McMurray passed Busch after 262 laps. Busch, who was not charged by the Delaware attorney general and was reinstated by Nascar last month, regained the lead under caution and never gave it up.■ Josef Newgarden held off a hard-charging Graham Rahal in the Indy Grand Prix of Alabama in Birmingham, for his first IndyCar Series victory.
