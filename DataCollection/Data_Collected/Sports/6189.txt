AdvertisementSupported bySports Briefing | BaseballBy David WaldsteinThe Yankees are close to naming three new coaches, including a head hitting coach and an assistant who will share the duties, a first for the Yankees. Jeff Pentland will be the principal hitting instructor, according to a person familiar with the matter, and Alan Cockrell will be the assistant. Both have extensive major league experience. The YES network first reported the hires, which were made to replace Kevin Long, who was fired after the season.The team is also planning to hire Joe Espada, a former third-base coach for the Marlins, as infield coach, replacing Mick Kelleher.
