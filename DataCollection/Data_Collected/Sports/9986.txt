By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 21, 2015
COMMERCE CITY, Colo. —  Josh Saunders had seven saves and recorded his second consecutive shutout, and New York City F.C. tied Colorado, 0-0, on Saturday despite losing a player to a red card late in the game.Saunders guarded the near post in the eighth minute to knock away a shot by Gabriel Torres from the corner of the 6-yard box.Saunders again held his ground in the 63rd minute, punching another Torres shot over the crossbar.Shay Facey was sent off after receiving his second yellow card in the 82nd minute for colliding with Colorado’s Juan Ramirez just outside the penalty area.N.Y.C.F.C. (1-0-2) had its best scoring chance in the 53rd minute, but Colorado goalkeeper Clint Irwin pushed away David Villa’s shot.The game was the home opener for Colorado (0-0-2), which has not won a match since its 3-0 victory over Chivas USA on July 25, 2014.A version of this article appears in print on March 22, 2015, on Page SP9 of the New York edition with the headline: Despite a Late Red Card, City F.C.
