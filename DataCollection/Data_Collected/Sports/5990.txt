AdvertisementSupported byN.B.A. RoundupBy The Associated PressAvery Bradley scored 21 points, helping the Boston Celtics end a three-game losing streak with an 89-81 victory over the Nets on Wednesday night at Barclays Center.Tyler Zeller added 18 points for the Celtics, who won for just the second time in nine games by outplaying the Nets over the final three quarters after a slow start. Boston trailed by 11 in the first half but gradually pulled away after halftime despite shooting under 40 percent from the field for the game.Joe Johnson scored 17 points for the Nets, who dropped their third in a row and fell to 16-19. Mason Plumlee had 16 points and 12 rebounds, but he shot just 6 for 11 at the free-throw line.ROCKETS 105, CAVALIERS 93  James Harden scored 21 points, Dwight Howard had 17 points and 19 rebounds, and Houston roared past host Cleveland in the fourth quarter. The Cavaliers played their sixth straight game without the injured LeBron James.The Rockets broke away with a 15-5 run in the fourth and handed the Cavaliers their seventh loss in nine games. Corey Brewer made two 3-pointers and scored 8 consecutive points in Houston’s spree.Kyrie Irving scored a season-high 38 points for the Cavaliers, who lost their fourth straight at home. Kevin Love added 17 points and 16 rebounds. J. R. Smith did not score in 19 minutes in his Cleveland debut. During the game, the Cavaliers pulled off their second big trade in three days, acquiring the 7-foot-1-inch center Timofey Mozgov from Denver in exchange for two first-round draft picks. PISTONS 108, MAVERICKS 95  D. J. Augustin scored 17 of his 26 points in the fourth quarter, and host Detroit earned its seventh consecutive victory. The Pistons (12-23) have the N.BA.’s longest current winning streak.Greg Monroe had 27 points and 18 rebounds for Detroit, and Andre Drummond had a game-high 19 rebounds.Dallas had won six in a row. Dirk Nowitzki scored 19 points for the Mavericks.BUCKS 97, 76ERS 77  Brandon Knight and Khris Middleton each scored 18 points as Milwaukee won at Philadelphia. The Bucks shot 53.9 percent from the field in their fifth consecutive road win.K. J. McDaniels scored 14 points for the Sixers.HORNETS 98, PELICANS 94  Kemba Walker scored 31 points, including a game-winning 11-foot jumper off the glass with 1.4 seconds left, to lift Charlotte over visiting New Orleans.Anthony Davis had 32 points and 12 rebounds for the Pelicans.HAWKS 96, GRIZZLIES 86  Jeff Teague scored 25 points, and host Atlanta beat Memphis to continue the Hawks’ strong play against Western Conference teams. The Hawks, who have won six straight over all, have a nine-game winning streak against teams from the West. JAZZ 97, BULLS 77  Derrick Favors had 20 points and 11 rebounds, and visiting Utah used a strong defensive effort to beat Chicago.
