By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSAPRIL 13, 2015
Fifth-seeded Angelique Kerber of Germany rallied past the American Madison Keys, 6-2, 4-6, 7-5, to win the Family Circle Cup in Charleston, S.C. Kerber trailed by 4-1 in the final set but still earned her fourth career WTA title, her first on clay.Sania Mirza — who won the doubles title with Martina Hingis, defeating Casey Dellacqua and Darija Jurak, 6-0, 6-4 — became the first woman from India to reach the top of the WTA rankings in singles or doubles.■ Jack Sock won the U.S. Men’s Clay Court Championships in Houston for his first ATP World Tour title, beating eighth-seeded Sam Querrey, 7-6 (7), 7-6 (2).■ Eighth-seeded Anna Karolina Schmiedlova of Slovakia beat third-seeded Camila Giorgi of Italy, 6-4, 6-3, in the final of the Katowice Open in Poland, clinching her first WTA title.■ Second-seeded Martin Klizan defeated Daniel Gimeno-Traver, 6-2, 6-2, in the final of the Grand Prix Hassan II in Casablanca, Morocco, to claim his third ATP Tour title.A version of this brief appears in print on April 13, 2015, on Page D4 of the New York edition with the headline: Kerber Rallies to Win in South Carolina.
