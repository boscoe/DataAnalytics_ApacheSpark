By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSDEC. 6, 2015
Jeff Green’s dunk on an alley-oop inbound pass from Courtney Lee with half a second left gave the Memphis Grizzlies a 95-93 victory at home over the Phoenix Suns on Sunday.“They just made a play,” Phoenix Coach Jeff Hornacek said.The Lee-to-Green connection helped Memphis avoid squandering a 93-87 lead with 1 minute 30 seconds to play, giving the Grizzlies their ninth win in their last 12 games.The Suns’ last chance to tie the game came on Eric Bledsoe’s shot from the right corner as the horn sounded. The 2-point attempt was long, sending Phoenix to its fourth straight loss despite a late rally that pulled the Suns even.Marc Gasol led Memphis with 22 points. The inbound pass from Lee capped a fourth quarter in which he scored 13 of his 18 points. Mike Conley had 12 points and Mario Chalmers finished with 11. Green and Zach Randolph scored 10 points apiece.AdvertisementBledsoe led the Suns with 23 points and 6 assists and also had 7 rebounds. Brandon Knight scored 17 points, and Mirza Teletovic had 14. Alex Len had 12, and Jon Leuer finished with 10 points and 11 rebounds.AdvertisementPhoenix erased Memphis’s 6-point lead with 90 seconds left on two baskets inside by Len and another by P. J. Tucker.Len tied the score at 93-93 when he tipped in a missed free throw by Leuer with 11.9 seconds remaining. During the Grizzlies’ next possession, Len was initially called for goaltending on Conley’s drive with 2.1 seconds left.
