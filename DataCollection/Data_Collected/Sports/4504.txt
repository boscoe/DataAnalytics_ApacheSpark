By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSDEC. 11, 2015
Alabama running back Derrick Henry was the biggest star of college football’s awards night, winning the Maxwell Award, given to the college player of the year, and the Doak Walker Award, given to the nation’s top running back. The awards were presented Thursday at the College Football Hall of Fame in Atlanta. Clemson’s Deshaun Watson won the Davey O’Brien Award as the nation’s top quarterback.Stanford’s Joshua Garnett won the Outland Trophy, given to the top interior lineman. Tyler Matakevich, a linebacker out of Temple, won the Chuck Bednarik Award, given to the defensive player of the year. Iowa’s Desmond King, a cornerback, won the Jim Thorpe Award as the best defensive back. Baylor’s Corey Coleman won the Biletnikoff Award, given to the top receiver. Utah’s Tom Hackett won the Ray Guy Award, given to the top punter.
