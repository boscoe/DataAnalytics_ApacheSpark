By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 5, 2015
The Minnesota Vikings and the Buffalo Bills agreed on a trade that will send quarterback Matt Cassel to the Bills and fetch the Vikings two undisclosed draft picks. The trade indicated that the Vikings are confident in Teddy Bridgewater, the first-round draft pick who took over as the starter in the third game of last season after Cassel broke his left foot. Cassel spent two years with the Vikings. He had 14 touchdown passes and 14 turnovers in 12 games. Buffalo entered the winter with E. J. Manuel as the only quarterback on the roster after Kyle Orton announced his retirement.■ The Jets signed Ronald Talley, a former Arizona Cardinals defensive end.
