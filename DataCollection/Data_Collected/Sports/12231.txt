AdvertisementSupported byBy The Associated PressMike Phillips, who formed the so-called Twin Towers with Rick Robey on the Kentucky basketball team that won the 1978 N.C.A.A. title, died on Saturday at his home in Madisonville, Ky. He was 59.Barnett-Strother Funeral Home confirmed Phillips’s death. The Hopkins County coroner’s office told WFIE-TV that he died after a fall. Phillips, 6 feet 10 inches tall, played alongside the 6-11 Robey on the Kentucky team, coached by Joe B. Hall, that went 30-2 in 1978 and beat Duke in the N.C.A.A. final in St. Louis behind Jack Givens’s 41 points. It was Kentucky’s first championship in 20 years. Two years earlier, Phillips had helped Kentucky win the National Invitation Tournament.Phillips averaged more than 10 points a game during the 1978 season and finished his Kentucky career 25th on the program’s scoring list.After college he was drafted by the New Jersey Nets, but he never played in the National Basketball Association. He played professionally in Spain for several seasons. Robey played eight seasons in the N.B.A. and was a member of the Boston Celtics’ 1981 championship team.Phillips, who was born on March 24, 1956, was a star at Manchester High School in New Franklin, Ohio.He is survived by his wife, Candice, and a son, Michael, the funeral home said.Advertisement
