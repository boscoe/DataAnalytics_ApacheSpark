By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSDEC. 20, 2015
The freshman Mikaela Foecke had 19 kills, Amber Rolfzen added 10, and Nebraska won its fourth N.C.A.A. volleyball championship with a three-set sweep of Texas in Omaha.Cheered on by a tournament-record crowd announced at 17,561 at CenturyLink Center, the Cornhuskers (32-4) ended a five-match losing streak against the Longhorns (30-3).Kelsey Fien punctuated Nebraska’s big night when she slammed a set from Kelly Hunter. Fien went to her knees as her teammates swarmed the court.Foecke was named the most outstanding player of the Final Four. Nebraska also won national titles in 1995, 2000 and 2006.Yasmeen Bedart-Ghani led the Longhorns with 11 kills, and Amy Neal added 10.A version of this brief appears in print on December 20, 2015, on Page SP4 of the New York edition with the headline: Nebraska Tops Texas for Title.
