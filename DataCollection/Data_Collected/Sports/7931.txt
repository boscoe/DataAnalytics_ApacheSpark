By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSFEB. 13, 2015
The Sacramento Kings said they had reached an agreement in principle with George Karl to become head coach. Karl, who has the sixth-most wins of any coach in N.B.A. history, is set to be Sacramento’s third coach this season. The Kings fired Michael Malone in December after an 11-13 start in his second season. Malone was replaced by Tyrone Corbin, who was also dismissed but will remain with the Kings as an adviser.Karl, 63, last coached during the 2012-13 season, when he won the coach of the year award with the Denver Nuggets before being fired after a first-round playoff loss to the Golden State Warriors.A version of this brief appears in print on February 13, 2015, on Page B14 of the New York edition with the headline: Karl Is Set to Coach the Kings.
