AdvertisementSupported bySports Briefing | BaseballBy The Associated PressThe Toronto Blue Jays signed the two-time Cy Young winner Johan Santana to a minor league deal and invited him to spring training. Santana last pitched in the majors in 2012 with the Mets. He has undergone two operations on his left shoulder in recent years.Santana was in Baltimore’s minor league system last year and tore his left Achilles tendon in June. He’s made just one appearance since then, retiring six straight batters in January in the Venezuelan Winter League.Santana, a four-time All-Star, won American League Cy Young Awards with Minnesota in 2004 and 2006. He pitched the only no-hitter in Mets’ history and is 139-78 lifetime with a 3.20 E.R.A.Advertisement
