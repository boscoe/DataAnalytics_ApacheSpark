AdvertisementSupported bySports Briefing | TennisBy The Associated PressRafael Nadal, the defending champion, lost his first match of the season at the Qatar Open on Tuesday. The second-seeded Nadal, showing desire but displaying a rusty game, was upset by a German qualifier, Michael Berrer, 1-6, 6-3, 6-4 in the first round. The match was Nadal’s first since he lost in the Basel quarterfinals to 17-year-old Borna Coric in October. He spent much of the autumn with appendicitis and underwent an appendectomy on Nov. 3. ■ Serena Williams slumped to a 6-2, 6-1 loss to Eugenie Bouchard of Canada in the Hopman Cup in Perth, Australia. The Canadians clinched the Group A match against the United States when Vasek Pospisil beat John Isner, 6-3, 7-6 (4), in men’s singles. Williams appeared lethargic and later complained of fatigue.
