By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 4, 2015
ATHENS, Ga. —  No. 1 Kentucky overcame a 9-point deficit in the second half on Tuesday night to keep its perfect season intact, defeating Georgia, 72-64.The Wildcats (30-0, 17-0 Southeastern Conference) had won their previous five games by an average of 24 points.They were really challenged by Georgia (19-10, 10-7), which had a sellout crowd roaring when Yante Maten tipped in a miss of his own shot to put the Bulldogs ahead by 56-47 with nine minutes remaining.But Kentucky showed it knows how to win the close games, too. The Wildcats began pounding the ball inside to the 6-foot-11 freshman Karl-Anthony Towns, who managed to stay in the game until the closing seconds after picking up his fourth foul.
