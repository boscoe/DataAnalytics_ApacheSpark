By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJAN. 10, 2015
Brandon Jennings scored all 18 of his points in the second half, leading the Detroit Pistons past the Nets, 98-93, on Saturday night in Auburn Hills, Mich.The Nets have lost five straight and have won only one game in 2015. Joe Johnson led the team with 17 points, and Brook Lopez had 11 points and 15 rebounds.Detroit, which has won eight of nine games since releasing Josh Smith, got 16 points and 17 rebounds from Greg Monroe, and 14 points and 9 rebounds from Andre Drummond.The teams combined to go 10 for 33 from 3-point range in the first half, which ended at 47-47. Johnson and Kyle Singler both went 3 for 4 on 3-pointers in the half while the rest of their teammates struggled.AdvertisementJennings, who did not score in the first half, scored 13 points in the third, but the Pistons still needed a buzzer-beating dunk by Drummond to take a 74-69 lead into the fourth quarter.AdvertisementWith both teams continuing to shoot from beyond the arc with little success, the game remained close until the Pistons turned a pair of turnovers into fast-break baskets. Jennings then hit a 3-pointer to make the score 91-84 with 2 minutes 30 seconds to play.Mason Plumlee was called for a charge at the other end, and Greg Monroe’s layup gave Detroit a 9-point lead, its biggest of the game. After that, the only excitement came from a late 3-pointer by Lopez — the first of his 369-game career.76ERS 93, PACERS 92 Michael Carter-Williams hit a running layup with 9.2 seconds left to lift host Philadelphia over Indiana.Tony Wroten had 20 points to lead Philadelphia, which won its second straight game for just the second time this season and earned its third victory in the last four games. The 76ers, who won for the second time at home, are 7-13 since beginning the season 0-17.David West scored 28 points for the Pacers, but he came up short on a 13-footer at the buzzer that would have given Indiana the win.CLIPPERS 120, MAVERICKS 100 Blake Griffin scored 22 points, Chris Paul had 17 points and 13 assists, and Los Angeles beat visiting Dallas for its sixth win in eight games and its second in a row.Jamal Crawford added 19 points, Matt Barnes had 18 points, J. J. Redick 16, and DeAndre Jordan had 13 points and 15 rebounds in the Clippers’ eighth win in their last 11 games against the Mavericks.AdvertisementDirk Nowitzki scored 25 points for Dallas, whose three-game road winning streak ended.
