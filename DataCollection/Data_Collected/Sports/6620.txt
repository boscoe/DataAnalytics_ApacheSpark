By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJAN. 20, 2015
Algeria came from behind to beat South Africa, 3-1, at the Africa Cup of Nations in Mongomo, Equatorial Guinea, avoiding a big upset. Faouzi Ghoulam and Islam Slimani scored for Algeria, which fell behind but survived a missed penalty kick by South Africa.In the day’s other game, Senegal came from behind to beat Ghana, 2-1, on a last-minute goal by a substitute, Moussa Sow.■ The substitute Reza Ghoochannejhad scored an injury-time goal to lift Iran to a 1-0 win over the United Arab Emirates and the top spot in Group C at the Asian Cup in Australia.■ Toronto F.C. of Major League Soccer has a deal to acquire the Italian midfielder Sebastian Giovinco, 27, from Juventus in July.A version of this brief appears in print on January 20, 2015, on Page B11 of the New York edition with the headline: Algeria Avoids an Upset.
