By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJAN. 11, 2015
Chris Bosh scored 34 points, Hassan Whiteside had career highs of 23 points and 16 rebounds off the bench, and the visiting Miami Heat beat the Los Angeles Clippers, 104-90, on Sunday, ending a three-game road skid.Dwyane Wade added 17 points on 5-of-15 shooting and had 10 assists.The Clippers had no answer for the 7-foot Whiteside, in his third year out of Marshall. His offensive display ran the gamut and included tip-ins, dunks and jumpers. About the only thing Whiteside did not do was try a 3-pointer. He was 10 of 13 from the field and 3 of 4 from the line in 28 minutes.Blake Griffin scored 26 points and Chris Paul had 23 points and 9 assists for the Clippers. They had won two straight and six of their previous eight.Paul, Matt Barnes, DeAndre Jordan and Jamal Crawford were whistled for technical fouls, and the team spent much of the game barking at the referees.AdvertisementThe Clippers took a 19-6 lead, helped by a 3-pointer from J. J. Redick, who finished with 14 points, and two by Paul. From there, it was all Miami.AdvertisementWhiteside replaced Chris Andersen halfway through the first and propelled the Heat on a 24-10 spurt, including a 13-0 run when Jordan and Paul were charged with technical fouls, to give Miami a 30-29 lead. Whiteside was 5 of 6 from the field and had six rebounds in six minutes in the quarter.Miami ran off the first 8 points of the second quarter, extending its lead to 38-29.
