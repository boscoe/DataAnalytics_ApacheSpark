By ALLAN KREDAJAN. 11, 2015
Yale picked up where it left off against Harvard, scoring twice in the first period on the way to a 4-1 victory at Madison Square Garden on Saturday.Harvard (10-2-2) entered the game ranked third in the nation with a seven-game winning streak, but it fell to Yale (9-4-2) for the second time this season. Yale also won last year’s contest at the Garden against Harvard, 5-1. Harvard was 0-4-1 against Yale last season.Chris Izmirlian and Charles Orzetti gave Yale a 2-0 lead in the opening period as the Bulldogs outshot Harvard by 16-5.Matt Killian made the score 3-0 at 2 minutes 32 seconds of the second period before Jimmy Vesey, Harvard’s leading scorer, put the Crimson on the board two minutes later. Yale’s Mitch Witek completed the scoring at the three-minute mark of the third.“We don’t focus on rankings; we just want to play our team game,” Izmirlian said.Yale outshot its rival by 42-21 in the game, the 246th meeting between the schools. Alex Lyon made 20 saves for Yale. Steve Michalek made a number of strong stops for Harvard but could not stem Yale’s offensive onslaught.Harvard still holds a 140-87-19 advantage over Yale, but that was little solace to Coach Ted Donato.“We looked tentative,” said Donato, who played for Harvard’s 1989 N.C.A.A. championship team. “Our team wasn’t in sync.
