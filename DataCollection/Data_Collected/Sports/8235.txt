By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSFEB. 19, 2015
Lance Armstrong pleaded guilty to careless driving for hitting two parked cars with his sport utility vehicle in December in Aspen, Colo., a prosecutor said. Court records show Armstrong paid $238.50 to cover court fees and a $150 traffic fine. Armstrong’s girlfriend, Anna Hansen, initially told the police that she was driving. Hansen eventually told detectives that Armstrong was driving but that the couple had decided to let her take the blame.■ Host France won gold in the men’s team sprint competition at the track cycling world championships in St.-Quentin-en-Yvelines after New Zealand was disqualified in the final. Race officials ruled that New Zealand’s first relay began a fraction of a second too early.
