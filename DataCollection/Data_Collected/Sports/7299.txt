By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJAN. 31, 2015
Al Horford scored 23 points, including a tying basket late in the game after Philadelphia had taken its first lead, and the Atlanta Hawks recovered after squandering a 21-point lead at home to beat the 76ers, 91-85, on Saturday night and stretch their franchise-record winning streak to 19 games.The Hawks led by 21 in the first half, but the 76ers came back to take their first lead, 83-81, on a dunk by Nerlens Noel with less than three minutes remaining.Horford scored to pull the Hawks even, and Dennis Schroder’s 3-pointer with 1 minute 35 seconds left gave Atlanta an 86-83 lead. After a miss by Philadelphia’s K. J. McDaniels, Horford’s jumper gave the Hawks a 5-point advantage.With the loss, Philadelphia was denied its first three-game winning streak of the season. Noel had 11 points and 10 rebounds.AdvertisementThe Hawks pulled away early in the game, which was a matchup of the N.B.A.’s best defense and worst offense. Entering the game, Atlanta’s defense led the league, allowing 96.3 points per game.
