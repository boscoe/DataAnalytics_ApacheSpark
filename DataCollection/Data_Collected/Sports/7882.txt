By REUTERSFEB. 11, 2015
Organizers of the Iditarod sled-dog race in Alaska have opted for an alternate route this year, citing unsafe conditions and a lack of snow in critical areas of the 1,000-mile trail. The change will move the official start of the race to Fairbanks, some 300 miles north of the traditional location in Willow. “We did a lot of work on trail parts that were bad, but there just isn’t enough snow to get over the mountains,” the Iditarod board director Andy Baker said.A version of this brief appears in print on February 12, 2015, on Page B14 of the New York edition with the headline: Iditarod Shifts to Safer Route.
