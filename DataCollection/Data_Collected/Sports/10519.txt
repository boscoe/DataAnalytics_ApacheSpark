By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 31, 2015
Cincinnati’s men’s basketball coach, Mick Cronin, who missed most of the season because of a problem with a blood vessel in the back of his brain, was cleared to resume coaching. Recent tests found that the problem, which was treated with medication, had been resolved.■ The Wisconsin senior Frank Kaminsky was a unanimous choice for The Associated Press’s all-American men’s basketball team, and the Duke freshman Jahlil Okafor received all but one first-team vote. Notre Dame’s Jerian Grant, Kentucky’s Willie Cauley-Stein and Ohio State’s D’Angelo Russell rounded out the first team.■ New York’s attorney general, Eric Schneiderman, announced a settlement with the National Junior College Athletic Association to eliminate a national rule that had limited sports eligibility mostly to students who attended at least three years of high school in the United States.
