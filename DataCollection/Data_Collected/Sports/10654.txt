By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSAPRIL 1, 2015
Speaking directly about the topic for the first time since gambling-related messages tied to his account appeared on Twitter last week, Marlins pitcher Jarred Cosart said Wednesday, “I never have, never will bet on baseball.” The messages appeared to show Cosart seeking advice from a gambling expert, but did not appear to involve baseball bets. Cosart said last week that he was cooperating with baseball’s inquiry and had deleted his Twitter account.■ Christian Vazquez, expected to do much of the catching for the Red Sox this season, will have Tommy John surgery.A version of this brief appears in print on April 2, 2015, on Page B14 of the New York edition with the headline: Marlin Rejects Gambling Speculation.
