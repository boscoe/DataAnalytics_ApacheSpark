By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJAN. 23, 2015
Blake Griffin scored 22 of his 24 points in the first half, when the Clippers led by 33, and host Los Angeles went on to rout the Nets, 123-84, on Thursday night.J. J. Redick and Jamal Crawford each added 17 points, DeAndre Jordan had 14 points and 12 rebounds, and Chris Paul had a season-high 17 assists for the Clippers (29-14), who have won nine of their last 12 games and three in a row.With Kevin Garnett resting, the Nets (18-25) were led by Mason Plumlee with 16 points. Darius Morris and Jerome Jordan each scored 11 points, and Jordan had 11 rebounds. The Nets have lost nine of 11.The Clippers put the game away in the first half, when they shot 66 percent from the field and DeAndre Jordan had already secured his double-double. They were especially dominant in the second quarter, outscoring the Nets by 36-14.In the quarter, Jordan had back-to-back dunks, powering over Brook Lopez on the second one, and Paul hit a 3-pointer.
