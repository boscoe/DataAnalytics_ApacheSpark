By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSDEC. 11, 2015
Reliever Jonathan Broxton is staying with the St. Louis Cardinals, agreeing to a $7.5 million, two-year contract. The right-handed Broxton, 31, gets $3.75 million in each of the next two seasons under the agreement announced Thursday, which includes a full no-trade provision. Broxton, a two-time All-Star, was acquired from Milwaukee in August for the minor league outfielder Malik Collymore and went 3-3 with a 2.66 E.R.A. in 26 games with the Cardinals, with 26 strikeouts in 232/3 innings.
