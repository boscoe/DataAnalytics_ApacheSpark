By AGENCE FRANCE-PRESSEJAN. 4, 2015
Kraigg Brathwaite of the West Indies earned a footnote in cricket history when he opened with a seven in the third test against South Africa at Newlands in Cape Town.The batsmen ran three and gained four extra runs because of a bad throw. It was the first time in test history that a batsman’s first runs were a seven, the South African statistician Andrew Samson said.A version of this brief appears in print on January 5, 2015, on Page D6 of the New York edition with the headline: Batsman Achieves Rare Feat.
