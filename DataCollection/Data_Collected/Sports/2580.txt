By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSNOV. 9, 2015
Russell Knox became the first player to win a World Golf Championship in his debut, closing with a four-under-par 68 for a two-stroke victory over Kevin Kisner in the HSBC Champions in Shanghai.Li Haotong, the 20-year-old from China who felt like a rock star all week, faded quickly with a bogey-and-double-bogey start. But he saved par on the 18th for a 72 to tie for seventh, the highest finish ever by a Chinese player in a PGA Tour event.■ Billy Andrade won the season-ending Charles Schwab Cup Championship in Scottsdale, Ariz., beating the Champions Tour points winner Bernhard Langer with a birdie on the first hole of a playoff.  (AP)■ Sun-Ju Ahn won the Toto Japan Classic in Shima for her first LPGA Tour victory and 20th Japan LPGA title, beating Ji-Hee Lee and Angela Stanford with a birdie on the first hole of a playoff.
