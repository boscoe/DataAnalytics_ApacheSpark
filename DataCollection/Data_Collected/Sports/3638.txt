By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSNOV. 26, 2015
The free-agent catcher Alex Avila and the Chicago White Sox agreed to a $2.5 million, one-year contract. Avila is a .242 hitter with 66 home runs and 282 R.B.I. over seven major league seasons, all with the Detroit Tigers. He has thrown out 29.5 percent of base runners. Avila, 29, was an All-Star in 2011 when he set career highs with a .295 average, 19 homers and 82 R.B.I.■ The Oakland Athletics acquired the infielder Jed Lowrie from the Houston Astros in exchange for the minor league right-hander Brendan McCurry. Lowrie, 31, hit .222 with nine home runs and 30 R.B.I. in 69 games for Houston last season, starting 45 at third and 16 at shortstop. Lowrie has a .257 career average with 65 homers and 314 R.B.I.
