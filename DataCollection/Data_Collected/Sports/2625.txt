By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSNOV. 9, 2015
Andrew Wiggins tied a career high with 33 points for the Minnesota Timberwolves, who blew a 34-point lead but bounced back to win, 117-107, in Atlanta, ending the Hawks’ seven-game winning streak.A Paul Millsap shot with 3 minutes 26 seconds left gave Atlanta its first lead at 107-106. But Wiggins scored the next 7 points to prevent what would have been one of the biggest collapses in N.B.A. history.■ Nikola Mirotic had 20 points and 10 rebounds to lead the visiting Chicago Bulls past the Philadelphia 76ers, 111-88. Joakim Noah had been listed as a starter instead of Mirotic, but he ended up sitting out with a sore knee.Philadelphia (0-7) has lost 17 straight, dating to March 27 — the fourth-longest skid in team history. Three of those lengthy losing streaks have come since the start of the 2013-14 season.
