By REUTERSFEB. 15, 2015
Usain Bolt of Jamaica, a six-time Olympic gold medalist, will retire after the 2017 world championships in London, he told The Daily Mail, a British newspaper. Bolt, 28, had previously hinted that the 2016 Olympics might be his last competition, but he will continue until 2017, and focus on the 100 meters, because of sponsorship commitments.A version of this brief appears in print on February 15, 2015, on Page SP12 of the New York edition with the headline: Bolt Says He’ll Retire in 2017.
