By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 1, 2015
BOSTON — Casimir Loxsom broke his own American indoor record in the 600 meters Sunday, winning the USA Indoor Track & Field Championships in 1 minute 15.33 seconds.“The title means a lot more than the time to me,” Loxsom said. “I’ve never won a U.S. title before. I always seem to find a way to second place out of a potential victory.”In the women’s pole vault, the N.C.A.A. indoor record-holder Demi Payne of Stephen F. Austin overcame a shaky warm-up to win at 14 feet 11 inches. The defending champion, Mary Saxer, finished second at 14-9.Shannon Rowbury won her second title in the meet, taking the two-mile in 9:43.94. She won the mile Saturday.Alysia Montano, who was safely out front when her main rival, Ajee Wilson, crashed to the track after being tripped midway through the 600, won in 1:26.59. It was Montano’s first major competition since she gave birth to her first child in August.AdvertisementMichelle Carter won her third consecutive indoor title in the shot put with a mark of 63-9 ¾, best in the world this year.AdvertisementChaunte Lowe, the 2012 world indoor champion in the high jump, also won her third United States indoor title, jumping 6-2 to beat Amy Acuff, 39, who is beginning a comeback in a quest to make her sixth Olympic team in 2016.
