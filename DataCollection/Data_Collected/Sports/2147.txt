By BENJAMIN HOFFMANNOV. 1, 2015
Colts (3-4) at Panthers (6-0)8:30 p.m. Eastern, ESPNLine: Panthers by 6 ½Carolina showed last week that it can win even when Cam Newton plays poorly — an important development for a team trying to prove it is a true contender. Indianapolis, meanwhile, has struggled even with Andrew Luck back from a shoulder injury, calling into question its own status as a contender.While much will be made of those two quarterbacks and their vital roles, this game should serve as a chance for the Panthers to show off just how improved their defense is this season. Luke Kuechly leads a squad that was a top-10 defense in terms of yards allowed per game entering the weekend. The Panthers have also held opponents to 18.3 points a game, making things easy on the Carolina offense during the team’s undefeated start.The Panthers’ passing game figures to rebound this week, with Newton’s three-interception performance against Philadelphia seeming like an aberration. But no one knows what to expect from the Colts. There are reports that Luck may have an undisclosed rib injury, which could explain his problems since his return.Pick: PanthersA version of this article appears in print on November 2, 2015, on Page D2 of the New York edition with the headline: Monday’s Matchup.
