By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJAN. 27, 2015
Olympic organizers in Rio de Janeiro said they had no “Plan B” for the sailing competitions at the Games to be held there in 2016, despite a recent admission by a top environmental official that it would be impossible to meet pledges to clean up Guanabara Bay, where the events are to be staged. Athletes have described the bay as an “open sewer” and have raised concerns about hepatitis and other illnesses, as well as the possibility of catastrophic collisions with floating detritus.■ The International Olympic Committee dismissed a suggestion by an official in Saudi Arabia that the nation could co-host the Olympics with neighboring Bahrain by having male and female athletes compete in separate countries. Thomas Bach, the I.O.C.’s president, said Saudi Arabia would be ineligible to bid for the Olympics unless it complied with rules barring discrimination against women in sports.A version of this brief appears in print on January 28, 2015, on Page B13 of the New York edition with the headline: Rio Is Stuck With Filthy Bay.
