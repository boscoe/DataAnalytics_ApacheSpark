By BRETT MICHAEL DYKESOCT. 11, 2015
Steelers (2-2) at Chargers (2-2)8:25 p.m., ESPNLine: Chargers by 3 ½Each year, it seems, people write off San Diego quarterback Philip Rivers, saying that this will be the year he begins to fall off, and each year he and his odd shot-put throwing motion prove his doubters wrong. This season is no exception. Despite playing behind a badly depleted offensive line, Rivers remains one of the game’s best readers of a defense. After last week’s game at Cleveland, Browns defenders told reporters that they felt as if Rivers knew exactly what they were planning on the field.“There were a lot of situations where he was kind of seeing what we were doing and making adjustments,” Browns linebacker Paul Kruger said.Rivers will have another weapon this week with the return of Antonio Gates, who served a four-game suspension for performance-enhancing drugs.Pittsburgh is hoping Michael Vick improves after having some time to prepare as the starting quarterback, a role he was thrust into when Ben Roethlisberger hurt his knee in Week 3.Pick: ChargersA version of this article appears in print on October 12, 2015, on Page D6 of the New York edition with the headline: Monday’s Matchup.
