Willie Cauley-Stein scored 15 points, and top-ranked Kentucky stayed unbeaten with another tough road win Tuesday night, prevailing, 71-69, over Louisiana State when the Tigers missed a potentially game-winning 3-point attempt at the buzzer.Devin Booker added 14, and Andrew Harrison had 13 for the Wildcats (24-0, 11-0 Southeastern Conference), who can tie a program record for the best start to a season if they beat South Carolina at home Saturday. Karl-Anthony Towns had 12 points and 13 rebounds.“I’m not worried about losing” at this point of the season, Kentucky Coach John Calipari said. “This is about us getting better.”Jarell Martin had 21 points and 11 rebounds for L.S.U. (17-7, 6-5), while Keith Hornsby scored 17 points.WISCONSIN 65, NEBRASKA 55 Sam Dekker matched his career high with 21 points, and No. 5 Wisconsin (22-2, 10-1 Big Ten) built a big lead before holding off a late comeback bid by host Nebraska (13-11, 5-7).KANSAS 73, TEXAS TECH 51 Wayne Selden Jr. scored 16 points, and No. 8 Kansas (20-4, 9-2 Big 12) shot well from 3-point range, winning at Texas Tech (12-13, 2-10).
