By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJAN. 29, 2015
Rodney Stuckey scored 22 points, Roy Hibbert had 18 points and 10 rebounds, and the Indiana Pacers routed the visiting Knicks, 103-82, on Thursday.The struggling Pacers (17-31) picked up their most lopsided home victory of the season with the win, which was only their second in 10 games.Carmelo Anthony scored 18 points, 12 in the first nine minutes, and Lou Amundson added 17 for the Knicks (9-38), who managed just 33 second-half points. With the loss, the Knicks fell behind Philadelphia (9-37) for the worst record in the Eastern Conference.Indiana shifted the game’s momentum midway through the second quarter with a rally to take a 54-49 halftime lead.AdvertisementThe Pacers opened the second half on a 10-2 run that extended the lead to double digits. Indiana led by as many as 34, and at times, the game was downright ugly.
