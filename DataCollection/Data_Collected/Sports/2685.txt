By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSNOV. 10, 2015
Carmelo Anthony scored 25 points and Lance Thomas had 17 off the bench as the Knicks edged Toronto, 111-109, on Tuesday night, handing the Raptors their first home loss of the season.DeMar DeRozan had a season-high 29 points for Toronto, and Kyle Lowry had 23 points.The victory was the Knicks’ third on the road this season after just seven wins away from Madison Square Garden last season.The Knicks were 11 of 23 (48 percent) from 3-point range, including 3 of 3 by Thomas, while the Raptors connected on only 23.5 percent.“He helped us win this game tonight,” Knicks Coach Derek Fisher said of Thomas, while acknowledging a flawed performance by his team.Advertisement"We were fortunate to get out with the win giving up 60 points in the paint and fouling as much as we did,” Fisher said. “Those are areas that we’re really trying to improve in a major way.”In a game that featured 13 lead changes, the Knicks went ahead by 13 points less than a minute into the third quarter.
