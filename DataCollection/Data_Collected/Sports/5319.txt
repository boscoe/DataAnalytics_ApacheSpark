AdvertisementSupported bySports Briefing | Fantasy SportsBy ReutersDraftKings and FanDuel, the leading daily fantasy sports companies, filed lawsuits in Illinois on Thursday asking a judge to declare their games legal there. On Wednesday, the state’s attorney general declared that daily fantasy sports constituted illegal gambling under the state’s laws. DraftKings and FanDuel said they would continue to operate in Illinois pending the outcome of their lawsuits.DraftKings filed its lawsuit in Cook County Circuit Court in Chicago, and FanDuel brought its action in Sangamon County in conjunction with Head2Head Sports L.L.C., a fantasy sports company whose games are seasonal, not daily. FanDuel and Head2Head asked in their lawsuit for a declaration that both types of games are legal. Advertisement
