By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSFEB. 7, 2015
Seattle Seahawks linebacker Bruce Irvin has been fined $10,000 by the N.F.L. for his role in a scuffle with 18 seconds left in the Super Bowl. Three other players received smaller fines.Seahawks defensive end Michael Bennett and New England Patriots tight ends Rob Gronkowski and Michael Hoomanawanui were each fined $8,628. Hoomanawanui was fined for unnecessary roughness, and the others were fined for striking an opponent late.The shoving began when Patriots quarterback Tom Brady took a knee after cornerback Malcolm Butler’s interception with 20 seconds left in the Patriots’ 28-24 victory. Irvin was ejected and later apologized for his actions.A version of this brief appears in print on February 7, 2015, on Page D4 of the New York edition with the headline: Players Fined for Scuffle.
