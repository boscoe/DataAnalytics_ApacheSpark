By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSOCT. 7, 2015
A judge dismissed simple assault charges against Rutgers wide receiver Leonte Carroo.The charges were filed last month after authorities in New Jersey said Carroo had attacked a woman after a game and slammed her onto concrete. The woman’s hip, hands, elbow and head were injured.A Piscataway municipal court judge dismissed the charges after the woman said she did not want to testify. Earlier, another judge had lifted a temporary restraining order filed against Carroo because the woman said she did not think it was necessary.The team suspended Carroo when the charges were filed. But Carroo’s lawyer said he planned to petition the university to lift the suspension.The interim coach, Norries Wilson, declined to comment.■ South Carolina Athletic Director Ray Tanner said he was not “100 percent sure” that Saturday’s football game against No. 7 L.S.U. would be played at Williams-Brice Stadium in Columbia because of the devastating floods that hit the state over the weekend.Tanner said a decision would be made within 36 hours whether the game could go on.A version of this brief appears in print on October 7, 2015, on Page B16 of the New York edition with the headline: Charges Are Dropped in Rutgers Receiver’s Case.
