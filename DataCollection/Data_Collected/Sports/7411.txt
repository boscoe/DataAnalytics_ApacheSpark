By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSFEB. 2, 2015
Jarrett Jack made a tiebreaking jumper with 1.3 seconds left, and the Nets used a late rally to beat the visiting Los Angeles Clippers, 102-100, on Monday night.The Nets ended a seven-game home losing streak by erasing a 9-point deficit over the final 1 minute 35 seconds, getting two 3-pointers from Alan Anderson, one a 4-point play. Joe Johnson added a 3-pointer, and Deron Williams had another in his return after an 11-game absence.But after Chris Paul’s layup for the Clippers tied the score at 100 with 8.6 seconds to go, Jack dribbled right and lofted a jumper over the 6-foot-11 center DeAndre Jordan.PELICANS 115, HAWKS 100 Atlanta’s 19-game winning streak, a franchise record, was ended by host New Orleans. Anthony Davis had 29 points and 13 rebounds to help the Pelicans win for the sixth time in seven games.
