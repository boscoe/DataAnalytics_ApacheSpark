No,” Duke Coach Mike Krzyzewski said. “Does that mean we’re soft? No.”N.C. State (12-5, 3-1 Atlantic Coast Conference) made 10 of its 16 attempts from 3-point range and ran constant double teams at Duke’s Jahlil Okafor, a preseason All-American. Okafor had 23 points and 12 rebounds, but the Blue Devils shot 37 percent.It was the second time in three seasons that an unbeaten Duke team had lost in Raleigh. The Blue Devils (14-1, 2-1) were No. 1 and 15-0 when it happened two years ago.
