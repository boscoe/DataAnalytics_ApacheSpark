By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 7, 2015
LeBron James tied Cleveland’s career assists record and scored 17 points Saturday night as the Cavaliers beat the visiting Phoenix Suns, 89-79.James tied Mark Price at 4,206 assists in the third quarter. James was 6 for 16 from the field and had 8 assists and 6 rebounds to help the Cavaliers win their 13th straight at home and move into a tie for second with Chicago in the Eastern Conference.Timofey Mozgov led the Cavaliers with 19 points. Tristan Thompson had 15 points and 12 rebounds, and Kevin Love finished with 13 points and 10 rebounds.Markieff Morris scored 16 points to lead the Suns, who fell to two games behind eighth-place Oklahoma City in the Western Conference playoff race.AdvertisementThe Cavaliers built a 32-point lead in the third quarter, but the Suns cut it to 17 early in the fourth — prompting James’s return. After the Suns pulled to within 84-74, Thompson made two free throws and James hit a 3-pointer to seal the win.AdvertisementThe home winning streak is Cleveland’s longest since it won 16 straight from Feb. 11 to April 12, 2009.P.
