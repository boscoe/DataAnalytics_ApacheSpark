AdvertisementSupported byRoundupBy The Associated PressManchester City’s hopes of defending its Premier League title sustained another blow on Saturday as the club sustained its first loss to Burnley in 41 years.George Boyd scored the lone goal in a 1-0 home victory for Burnley, meeting a clearance from City’s captain, Vincent Kompany, with a half-volley that beat goalkeeper Joe Hart in the 61st minute.City, which has lost two of its last three league games, remained 5 points behind Chelsea with nine games left on its schedule. Chelsea has played two fewer games than City; it hosts Southampton on Sunday. “Maybe the chance of the title is now far,” City Manager Manuel Pellegrini said, “but we have to continue fighting for our position.”Arsenal eased to its fifth straight league victory as Olivier Giroud scored once and set up an Aaron Ramsey goal in a 3-0 home victory over West Ham. The result left third-place Arsenal a point behind City.Elsewhere, Aston Villa ended its road-game scoring woes, with all four goals in a 4-0 win at Sunderland coming in the first half. Crystal Palace beat visiting Queens Park Rangers, 3-1, and host West Bromwich Albion overcame Stoke, 1-0. Leicester drew, 0-0, with visiting Hull.BAYERN CRUISES TO A WIN Thomas Müller scored one goal and set up two more as Bayern Munich extended its Bundesliga lead to 14 points with a 4-0 win at Werder Bremen.Elsewhere, Joël Matip scored in the last minute to salvage a 2-2 draw for Schalke at Hertha Berlin, and Borussia Dortmund drew, 0-0, with visiting Cologne. MESSI POWERS BARCELONA Lionel Messi moved ahead of Cristiano Ronaldo for the scoring lead in the Spanish league, recording his 31st and 32nd league goals of the season in Barcelona’s 2-0 victory at Eibar.Barcelona, the league leader, moved to 4 points ahead of Real Madrid, which was to host Levante on Sunday.Atlético Madrid, which drew, 0-0, at Espanyol, remained in fourth place, a point behind Valencia, which moved into third with a 2-0 home win over Deportivo La Coruña on Friday.Advertisement
