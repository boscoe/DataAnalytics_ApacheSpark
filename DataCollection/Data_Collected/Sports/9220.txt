By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 10, 2015
DENVER —  Kenneth Faried had 19 points and 11 rebounds, and the Denver Nuggets routed the Knicks, 106-78, on Monday night.Wilson Chandler and Will Barton scored 17 points apiece for the Nuggets, who enjoyed their largest victory margin this season.The Knicks lost their fourth straight and fell to 12-50, the N.B.A.’s first 50-loss team this season. Alexey Shved had 19 points for the Knicks, who have seven wins in 2015.The lead grew to 34 in the fourth, the largest of the season for the Nuggets, who tied a season low with eight turnovers. They did not have a turnover in the second or third quarter.AdvertisementThe matchup between two of the worst teams in the league was close late in the second quarter, but the Nuggets then went on a run to take control. Faried had a dunk, and Randy Foye hit a jumper before Andrea Bargnani drained a 3-pointer to pull the Knicks to 43-40 behind with 3 minutes 13 seconds left in the half.Denver scored the next 8 points to go up by 11 and then ended the half with 4 points in the last 2.1 seconds. Joffrey Lauvergne hit a layup and was fouled. He missed the free throw, but Darrell Arthur tipped in the rebound to make it 55-40 at intermission.AdvertisementThe Nuggets quickly went ahead, 65-45, on Ty Lawson’s 3-pointer early in the third.
