By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSFEB. 5, 2015
With riot officers protecting the players from incensed rival fans and using tear gas to disperse troublemakers, Ghana advanced to the Africa Cup of Nations final with a 3-0 win over host Equatorial Guinea. The referee Eric Otogo-Castane halted the match in the 82nd minute. It resumed after about a 40-minute delay and finished soon after.The riot police were called in to protect Ghana’s players and fans after Ghana scored two late goals in the first half. After Ghana scored a third goal in the second half and water bottles were again being thrown, the match was halted and the team’s fans were escorted out of the stadium for their own safety. Ghana will play Ivory Coast in the final.A version of this brief appears in print on February 6, 2015, on Page B13 of the New York edition with the headline: Ghana Advances to Final in Africa.
