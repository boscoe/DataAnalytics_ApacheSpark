AdvertisementSupported bySports Briefing | BaseballBy Billy WitzThe Yankees hired Mike Harkey as their bullpen coach, completing their coaching staff for next season. Harkey, 49, who served in the same role for the Yankees from 2008 to 2013, was fired as the Arizona Diamondbacks’ pitching coach after the season. He replaces Gary Tuck, whom the Yankees fired last month.The Yankees had previously announced that Alan Cockrell would be promoted to hitting coach, replacing the fired Jeff Pentland. Cockrell, who was Pentland’s assistant last year, will have Marcus Thames as his assistant. Thames was the hitting coach at Class AAA Scranton/Wilkes-Barre last season. The first-base coach, Tony Pena, will also coach the catchers, a duty handled last season by Tuck. Larry Rothschild, Rob Thomson and Joe Espada return as pitching coach, bench coach and third-base coach.■ Willie Mays and Yogi Berra are among the 17 people who will be honored Nov. 24 with the United States’ highest civilian award, the Presidential Medal of Freedom.
