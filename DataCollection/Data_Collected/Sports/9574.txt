AdvertisementSupported byBy The Associated PressTemple, Old Dominion, Colorado State and Richmond will have to settle for a No. 1 seed in the N.I.T. Those teams, the first four left out of the N.C.A.A. tournament, were automatically given the top seeds for the first time in the N.I.T. field, which was announced Sunday.Temple (23-10), which finished third in the American Athletic Conference, was passed over in favor of teams like Dayton and U.C.L.A. after losing to Southern Methodist in the conference tournament. The Owls will now try to make a run in the N.I.T., starting with a home game Wednesday against Bucknell.The A.A.C. landed just two teams in the N.C.A.A. tournament — the league champion, S.M.U., and Cincinnati — while teams like Tulsa (22-10) and last year’s national champion, Connecticut (20-14), were relegated to the N.I.T.UConn is just the sixth defending national champion not to make the N.C.A.A. field since it was expanded to 64 teams in 1985. The Huskies will host Arizona State (17-15) on Wednesday. Other notable major programs in the N.I.T. field include Pittsburgh, Stanford, Texas A&M, Miami, Illinois and Alabama, which plans on competing despite dismissing Coach Anthony Grant earlier Sunday.Advertisement
