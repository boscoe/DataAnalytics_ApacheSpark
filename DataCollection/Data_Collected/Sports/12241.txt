AdvertisementSupported byThe Washington Capitals’ Joel Ward scored with 1.3 seconds left on Thursday night to win Game 1 against the Rangers, his third career game-winning goal in the playoffs. He most memorably netted the winner in overtime of Game 7 against the Boston Bruins in 2012. According to Elias Sports Bureau, Thursday was the third time a winning goal had been scored in the final two seconds of an N.H.L. playoff game. APRIL 11, 1964 Maple Leafs forward Bob Pulford scored with two seconds remaining to give Toronto a 3-2 victory in Game 1 of the Stanley Cup finals against the Detroit Red Wings. Toronto took the series in seven games, claiming the Cup for the third year in a row. The Leafs have won one Stanley Cup since. Pulford, who was inducted into the Hockey Hall of Fame as a player in 1991, later coached the Los Angeles Kings and the Chicago Blackhawks, for whom he was also a longtime executive.APRIL 21, 2009 The Devils were leading the Carolina Hurricanes, two games to one, in a first-round playoff series and had rallied from a 3-0 deficit in Game 4 to tie the score 8 minutes 46 seconds into the third period. But Carolina’s Jussi Jokinen scored with two-tenths of a second left, deflecting a shot from Dennis Seidenberg. The Hurricanes went on to win the series in seven games, scoring two goals in the final two minutes of Game 7 to advance. Jokinen had seven postseason goals, including three game-winners, as Carolina reached the Eastern Conference finals. Advertisement
