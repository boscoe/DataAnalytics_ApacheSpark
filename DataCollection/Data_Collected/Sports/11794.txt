By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSAPRIL 24, 2015
President Obama honored the New England Patriots for winning their fourth Super Bowl in 14 years despite a “huge distraction” over underinflated footballs in their A.F.C. championship game.Obama recognized the Patriots during a White House ceremony on the South Lawn.“That whole story got blown a little out of proportion,” Obama said as the crowd chuckled.Obama joked that he wished the city of Boston would share some its sports titles with his hometown Chicago. “I’m hoping that I get to see the Bears before I leave here,” he said.■ The Jets exercised their fifth-year option for 2016 on Quinton Coples, an outside linebacker who had a career-high six and a half sacks last season.A version of this brief appears in print on April 24, 2015, on Page B14 of the New York edition with the headline: Obama Honors Champion Patriots.
