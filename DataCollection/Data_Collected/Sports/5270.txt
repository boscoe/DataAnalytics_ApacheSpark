By BENJAMIN HOFFMANDEC. 23, 2015
8:25 p.m. Eastern, NFL NetworkLine: Raiders by 6Much of fans’ attention in this matchup will focus on things that have little to do with the outcome. Both teams are trying to relocate to Los Angeles for next season, and this will also be the last home game for Charles Woodson, a Hall of Fame-caliber defensive back who announced Monday that this would be his final season.The Chargers and the Raiders are not all that different, but they are headed in drastically different directions. They both score points in bunches but have struggled to find consistency on defense. The difference is that Oakland is stocked with young players who seem on the verge of something better — Derek Carr, Amari Cooper and Khalil Mack all look like franchise cornerstones — while San Diego is mostly relying on the aging arm of Philip Rivers, with the rest of the team no longer generating the enthusiasm it once did.San Diego’s offense has been painfully one-dimensional, averaging an N.F.L.-low 84 rushing yards a game.
