AdvertisementSupported bySports Briefing | Winter SportsBy The Associated PressTed-Jan Bloemen of Canada shattered the world record in the 10,000 meters in a World Cup speedskating event in Salt Lake City, finishing in 12 minutes 36.30 seconds. Bloemen broke the mark of 12:41.69 set by Sven Kramer of the Netherlands last week in Calgary, Alberta. The American Heather Bergsma broke the world record in the women’s 1,500 meters at the event. Bergsma finished in 1:50.85 to break the mark of 1:51.59 set by her teammate Brittany Bowe last week in Calgary. ■ Germany’s ski jumping team, which included the world champion Severin Freund, edged Slovenia to win a World Cup event in Klingenthal, Germany. Germany earned 1,021.9 points, beating the Slovenian team by 20.4 points. Austria was third (934.2 points) and Japan fourth (906.5). ■ Javier Fernandez of Spain, the reigning world champion, won the men’s gold medal at the Rostelecom Cup Grand Prix figure skating competition in Moscow. The Russian Adian Pitkeev picked up the silver, his first Grand Prix medal, and the American Ross Miner finished in third. Elena Radionova led a Russian sweep of the women’s medals, the pairs gold went to Ksenia Stolbova and Fedor Klimov of Russia, and the Canadians Kaitlyn Weaver and Andrew Poje topped the ice dancers.
