By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSNOV. 4, 2015
Al Horford scored 21 points, and Dennis Schroder added 20 points as the Atlanta Hawks beat the visiting Nets, 101-87, on Wednesday night for their fifth straight victory.Brook Lopez finished with 27 points, his season high, and Thaddeus Young had 14 points for the Nets, who dropped to 0-5.Jeff Teague had 16 points and 6 assists for the Hawks, and Paul Millsap added 12 points and 9 rebounds.After going 1 for 7 from the field with four turnovers in the first half, Schroder had a strong start to the third quarter, hitting three 3-pointers and two layups and assisting on two baskets by Horford.AdvertisementThe Hawks took the game’s first double-digit lead when Schroder hit two free throws to make the score 95-84 with 4 minutes 21 seconds remaining. Atlanta had begun the third quarter with a 2-point lead.AdvertisementLopez was 11 for 16 from the field. The Nets’ Joe Johnson was 1 for 10, going 0 for 3 on 3-point attempts, and finished with 5 points.WIZARDS 102, SPURS 99 Bradley Beal made a 3-pointer with 0.3 of a second left to lift Washington to a home victory over San Antonio.Beal finished with 25 points, and John Wall had 17 points and 13 assists for Washington. Wall had 13 points in the fourth quarter, and the two scored the Wizards’ last 15 points.Otto Porter added 19 points to help Washington win its second straight over San Antonio, dating to last season. The Spurs had previously won 17 straight against the Wizards.Kawhi Leonard led San Antonio with 23 points, and Tony Parker scored 11 of his 17 points in the fourth quarter. LaMarcus Aldridge had 10 points and 14 rebounds for the Spurs, who committed 21 turnovers and had a three-game winning streak end.PACERS 100, CELTICS 98 Paul George had 26 points and 10 rebounds, Jordan Hill added 16 points and 10 rebounds, and Indiana beat visiting Boston.Monta Ellis made two free throws with 13.7 seconds left to break a 98-98 tie. Toronto shot only 42 percent from the field but benefited from 19 turnovers by Oklahoma City.Russell Westbrook scored 20 of his 22 points in the second half and finished with 16 assists. Kevin Durant had 27 points, and Enes Kanter added 15 for the Thunder, who lost their second straight despite shooting 48 percent.
