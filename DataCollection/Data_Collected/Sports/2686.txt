By REUTERSNOV. 10, 2015
Lamine Diack, who stepped down as president of the International Association of Athletics Federations in August, has resigned as president of the International Athletics Foundation, the I.A.A.F. said Tuesday. The foundation is a Monaco-based charity best known for its work with the year-end world athletics gala in Monte Carlo. It also assists the I.A.A.F. in the development and promotion of track and field worldwide.Last week, Diack, 82, was placed under formal investigation in France. He is accused of receiving more than $1 million in bribes in 2011 to cover up positive doping tests of Russian athletes.A version of this brief appears in print on November 11, 2015, on Page B15 of the New York edition with the headline: Foundation Chief Resigns.
