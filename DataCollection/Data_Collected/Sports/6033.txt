CreditZacharias Abubeker for The New York Times        For generations of young Maasai, the tradition of lion hunting served as a rite of passage into adulthood. But as Africa’s lion population diminished, village leaders recognized a need for change.A partnership was formed in 2008 with Big Life Foundation, a wildlife conservation organization, to create a competition that would challenge the warriors’ abilities and promote an ecologically sustainable future.On Dec. 13, members of four villages converged on the Sidai Oleng Wildlife Sanctuary in southern Kenya to compete in the second Maasai Olympics.A version of this article appears in print on January 9, 2015, on Page B11 of the New York edition with the headline: The Games Warriors Play.
