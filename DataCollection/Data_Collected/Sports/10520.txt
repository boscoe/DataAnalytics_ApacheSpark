By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 31, 2015
The Russian Football Union appointed its first dedicated antiracism official in an effort to crack down on discrimination before the World Cup, to be held in Russia in 2018. It was not clear what powers had been given to the official, Alexei Tolkachev, who already deals with racism as the group’s head of safety and fan outreach. A report by two anti-discrimination organizations last month said more than 200 cases of discriminatory behavior had been linked to Russian soccer over two seasons.Also on Monday, UEFA, the governing body for European soccer, said it had opened disciplinary proceedings against Montenegro and Russia after their European Championship qualifier on Friday was called off because of crowd violence.■ Armenia Coach Bernard Challandes quit after his team failed to win any of its first four games in European Championship qualifying.A version of this brief appears in print on March 31, 2015, on Page B12 of the New York edition with the headline: Antiracism Official Named in Russia.
