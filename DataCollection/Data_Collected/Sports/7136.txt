By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJAN. 28, 2015
Jerian Grant had 23 points and 12 assists, and No. 8 Notre Dame beat No. 4 Duke, 77-73, on Wednesday. The win kept host Notre Dame (20-2, 8-1) in second place in the Atlantic Coast Conference. Duke (17-3, 4-3) is off to its worst start in league play since opening the 1995-96 season at 3-4. Jahlil Okafor had 22 points and 17 rebounds for Duke.■ Frank Mason III scored 16 points and had a couple of key shots in the go-ahead run, and No. 9 Kansas (17-3, 6-1 Big 12) held on for a 64-61 victory at Texas Christian (14-6, 1-6).■ Fred VanVleet scored 27 points to lead No. 12 Wichita State (19-2, 9-0 Missouri Valley) to a 58-47 win over visiting Loyola (13-8, 3-6).■ Paul Jesperson matched his season high with 13 points, and Seth Tuttle also had 13 to keep No. 18 Northern Iowa (19-2, 8-1) in the hunt for the Missouri Valley Conference title with a 59-52 win over Southern Illinois (9-13, 2-7).■ Kiah Stokes had the fourth triple-double in Connecticut’s history, helping the No. 2 Huskies (19-1, 9-0 American Athletic Conference) to an 87-32 rout of visiting East Carolina (13-8, 4-6) in a women’s game. Stokes, a 6-foot-3 center, had 10 points, 11 rebounds and 10 blocks.A version of this brief appears in print on January 29, 2015, on Page B13 of the New York edition with the headline: No. 8 Notre Dame Tops No. 4 Duke.
