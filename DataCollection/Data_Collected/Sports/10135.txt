The best-selling sports books in February 2015, according to the New York Times best-seller list. 1. UNBROKEN, by Laura Hillenbrand (Random House). An Olympic runner’s story of survival as a prisoner of the Japanese in World War II; now a movie.2. THE BOYS IN THE BOAT, by Daniel James Brown (Penguin). The University of Washington’s eight-oar crew and its quest for gold at the 1936 Berlin Olympics.3. H IS FOR HAWK, by Helen Macdonald (Grove/Atlantic Inc.). A British woman who is also a lifelong falconer raises a fierce goshawk, rekindling the spirit of this Arthurian sport even as she reflects and quotes from T. H. White’s book “The Goshawk.”4. ON MY OWN TWO FEET, by Amy Purdy with Michelle Burford (HarperCollins/William Morrow). The winner of a bronze medal in adaptive snowboarding in the 2014 United States Paralympic Games describes her comeback after childhood bacterial meningitis rendered her a double amputee with prosthetic legs.Advertisement5. POWER FORWARD, by Reggie Love (Simon & Schuster). The author graduated from Duke, where he played on the men’s basketball team as it won a national title in 2001 and later became a captain. He was a special assistant and personal aide to President Obama from 2007 to 2011.Advertisement6. WRESTLING FOR MY LIFE, by Shawn Michaels with David Thomas (Zondervan). The sports titles ranked here are selected by the editors from among all adult nonfiction books in all print and electronic formats, as reported to the New York Times best-seller lists for the previous month.
