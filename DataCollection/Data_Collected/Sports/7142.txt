By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJAN. 28, 2015
Kyrie Irving scored a career-high 55 points, breaking the Quicken Loans Arena record while LeBron James watched from the bench, and host Cleveland won its eighth straight game, defeating the Portland Trail Blazers, 99-94, on Wednesday night.Irving broke Allen Iverson’s single-game scoring record at the arena. His point total was also the highest in the N.B.A. this season.ROCKETS 99, MAVERICKS 94 Josh Smith scored 18 points, and James Harden added 17 as host Houston outlasted Dallas. It was the third straight win for the Rockets and the fourth loss in a row for the Mavericks.CLIPPERS 94, JAZZ 89 Jamal Crawford scored 21 points, and Los Angeles used a fourth-quarter rally to beat host Utah. The Clippers have won 13 straight games against the Jazz, a franchise record. Crawford knocked down four 3-pointers in the win.TIMBERWOLVES 110, CELTICS 98 Kevin Martin scored 21 points in his first game in more than two months, and Gorgui Dieng added 16 points and 14 rebounds to lift host Minnesota over Boston.AdvertisementRAPTORS 119, KINGS 102 Lou Williams scored 27 points, and host Toronto made 17 3-pointers, a season high, in a victory over Sacramento. Greivis Vasquez scored 18 and Jonas Valanciunas had 15 for the Raptors, who won their fourth straight.AdvertisementNUGGETS 93, PELICANS 85 Arron Afflalo scored 18 points as visiting Denver ended a seven-game losing streak with a win over New Orleans. Jusuf Nurkic scored 15 for Denver, and Kenneth Faried had 14.SPURS 95, HORNETS 86 Tony Parker scored 17 points, and host San Antonio overcame a lethargic third quarter to beat Charlotte.
