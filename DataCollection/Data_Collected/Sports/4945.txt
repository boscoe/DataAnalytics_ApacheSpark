By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSDEC. 18, 2015
The Olympian Bobby Lea was suspended for 16 months for doping after returning a positive test during the track cycling national championships this year. Shortly after the United States Anti-Doping Agency revealed the suspension, Lea said in a letter posted on his website that he had taken the prescription painkiller Percocet, which contains the banned substance oxycodone, to help him sleep.A version of this brief appears in print on December 18, 2015, on Page B12 of the New York edition with the headline: Lea Suspended for Doping.
