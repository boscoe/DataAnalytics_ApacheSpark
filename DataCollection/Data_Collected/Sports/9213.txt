By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 10, 2015
Mushers in the Iditarod began their 1,000-mile trek across Alaska on a new route after poor trail conditions led organizers to push the race’s start north. The race has usually begun in Willow, Alaska, but because of a lack of snow, officials decided to move the entire route around the Alaska Range to Fairbanks, 225 miles to the north.A version of this brief appears in print on March 10, 2015, on Page B13 of the New York edition with the headline: Relocated Iditarod Begins.
