By REUTERSOCT. 6, 2015
Chung Mong-joon of South Korea said he was facing a 15-year suspension by FIFA’s ethics committee, hampering his campaign for the FIFA presidency, but he denied wrongdoing and vowed to continue his bid to lead soccer’s world governing body.In Seoul, Chung read a nine-page statement addressing the accusations against him, which he dismissed as a ploy. Chung said the accusations stemmed from his support for South Korea’s 2022 World Cup bid, when he proposed the start of a Global Football Fund.■ The injured forward Giovani dos Santos will miss Mexico’s playoff game at the United States on Saturday, a match to determine which nation will represent its region at the 2017 Confederations Cup.
