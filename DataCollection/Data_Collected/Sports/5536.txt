By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSDEC. 30, 2015
Adrien Théaux won a World Cup downhill in Santa Caterina Valfurva, Italy, by 1.04 seconds, but Christof Innerhofer of Italy grabbed most of the attention by finishing fourth after skiing halfway down the course with a gate attached to him. About a minute into his run, Innerhofer got his left arm wrapped around a gate, and the panel and the long pole became caught on his equipment. After about 30 seconds, Innerhofer rid himself of the pole, but he carried the panel to the finish.■ Frida Hansdotter of Sweden held on to her first-run lead to win a women’s World Cup slalom in Lienz, Austria, making her the main candidate to take over the discipline title from the injured Mikaela Shiffrin.  (AP)■ Severin Freund became the first German winner on the ski-jumping Four Hills Tour in 13 years when his 137.5-meter jump in the second heat gave him the opening leg in Oberstdorf, Germany.A version of this brief appears in print on December 30, 2015, on Page B10 of the New York edition with the headline: Skier Drags Gate to the Line.
