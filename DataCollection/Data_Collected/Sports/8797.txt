By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 1, 2015
Jennifer O’Neill and Makayla Epps combined for 22 second-half points, and the No. 13 Kentucky women topped No. 2 South Carolina, 67-56, at home on Sunday in their regular-season finale, denying the Gamecocks an unbeaten season in Southeastern Conference play.The Wildcats (21-8, 10-6) shot just 32 percent from the field but made 27 of their 35 free-throw attempts. They left the Gamecocks (27-2, 15-1) tied with Tennessee atop the SEC, although South Carolina earned the top seed for this week’s conference tournament in Little Rock, Ark., by beating the Volunteers last week.VIRGINIA 75, LOUISVILLE 59 Faith Randolph scored 23 points, and Sarah Imovbioh had 22 points and 17 rebounds as host Virginia (17-12, 7-9 Atlantic Coast Conference) upset No. 8 Louisville in the final regular-season game for both teams. Louisville (24-5, 12-4) had already clinched the third seed in the A.C.C. tournament.NOTRE DAME 67, N.C. STATE 60 Jewell Loyd scored 16 points, and No. 4 Notre Dame survived a mistake-filled performance to win at North Carolina State. The Fighting Irish (28-2, 15-1) clinched the A.C.C. regular-season title outright. Len’Nique Brown-Hoskin had 16 points to lead the undermanned Wolfpack (16-13, 7-9), who have been down to seven scholarship players and nine players total for the last month because of injuries.AdvertisementMARYLAND 69, N’WESTERN 48 The reserve guard Brene Moseley scored 18 points to help No. 5 Maryland wrap up a perfect inaugural season in Big Ten play, winning at No. 25 Northwestern (22-7, 12-6). Maryland, which moved over from the A.C.C., went 18-0 against Big Ten opponents this season. The Seminoles (27-3, 14-2) had already secured the second seed, behind Notre Dame, in the A.C.C. tournament and will play in the quarterfinals Friday in Greensboro, N.C.
