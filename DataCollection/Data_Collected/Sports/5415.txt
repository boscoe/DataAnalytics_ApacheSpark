By BEN SHPIGELDEC. 26, 2015
1 p.m., CBSMATCHUP TO WATCH: Ryan Fitzpatrick vs. the Patriots’ defenseIn eight games against New England, Fitzpatrick is 1-7 with 17 interceptions. When told that he had thrown so many interceptions against the Patriots, Fitzpatrick quipped: “I think a lot of people have. They’re a pretty good team.” Indeed the Patriots are, and if Fitzpatrick is to retain any hope of playing in the postseason for the first time, he must shine in a game that, he said, means “everything for us.” Earlier in the season, the Jets leaned on their running game as Fitzpatrick continued to build a rapport with his receivers, but now the offense flows through him. That evolution ignited an offensive surge: The Jets have averaged 27.5 points in their four-game winning streak. It will very likely take as many points, if not more, to beat the Patriots.NUMBER TO WATCH: 10That is by how many points, combined, the Jets have lost their last three games against New England. Their 7-point defeat on Oct. 25 was the second-largest margin in the last seven installments of the rivalry.QUOTATION OF THE WEEK:‘Hopefully, he gets traded at some point.
