Nicolas Batum had 24 points and 8 assists, and the Charlotte Hornets held off the visiting Nets, 116-111, on Wednesday.The teams entered the fourth quarter tied at 84-84, but the Hornets began to pull away.The Nets cut the Hornets’ lead to 112-108 with two minutes remaining, but they ruined an opportunity to make it a one-possession game as Thaddeus Young, who had 27 points on 11-of-17 shooting, missed an easy follow shot.The Hornets kept giving the Nets chances to win the game.Jeremy Lin turned over the ball with 33 seconds left, but Brook Lopez missed a short jumper. Then Batum made an errant pass that sailed out of bounds. Again, the Nets failed to take advantage as Lopez was called for an illegal screen at midcourt, giving the Hornets possession.“It could be a little fatigue, but there are no excuses for missed opportunities,” Young said of the Nets’ miscues. “We had every chance to win that game and it slipped by us.”AdvertisementBrook Lopez added 23 points and Jarrett Jack and Joe Johnson each had 16 for the struggling Nets.“Nic Batum is such a good playmaker as well as a scorer,” Nets coach Lionel Hollins said. “He was making plays at the basket and when we had to help he got the ball back out to the 3-point line and they made them.”AdvertisementROCKETS 108, BLAZERS 103 James Harden scored 45 points, including all 9 for host Houston in overtime, and the Rockets won after firing Kevin McHale.Corey Brewer made a 3-pointer with less than a second left to force overtime, and Harden took over from there.The interim coach J.
