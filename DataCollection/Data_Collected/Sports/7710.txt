By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSFEB. 8, 2015
The South Korean rookie Sei Young Kim won the Bahamas L.P.G.A. Classic for her first L.P.G.A. Tour title, sinking an 8-foot birdie putt on the first hole of a playoff with her countrywoman Sun Young Yoo and Ariya Jutanugarn of Thailand.Kim, 22, shot a one-under-par 72 in the completion of the delayed third round and closed with a 68 to match Yoo and Jutanugarn at 14-under 278 on Atlantis Resort’s Ocean Club course on Paradise Island. Kim birdied the par-5 18th in regulation and then won the playoff. Yoo finished with rounds of 69 and 70, and Jutanugarn shot 70-69.■ Anirban Lahiri of India won the Malaysian Open in Kuala Lumpur with a four-under 68 in the final round, finishing a stroke ahead of Bernd Wiesberger of Austria. Lahiri, who finished at 16-under 272, rolled in a 40-foot birdie putt from off the green on the 17th hole as he overcame a five-shot deficit at the start of play.■ Paul Goydos closed with a birdie for a three-under 69 and a one-stroke victory over Gene Sauers in the Champions Tour’s Allianz Championship in Boca Raton, Fla.■ The former Stanford star Patrick Rodgers won the Web.com Tour’s Colombia Championship in Bogotá for his first professional title, beating Steve Marino with a birdie on the second hole of a playoff. Rodgers, 22, birdied the final hole of regulation for a six-under 65 and a 17-under 267 total. (AP)■ An engaged couple, Richard Green of Australia and Marianne Skarpnord of Norway, made golf history by winning the men’s and women’s titles at the Victoria Open in Melbourne, Australia, on the same course and on the same day.
