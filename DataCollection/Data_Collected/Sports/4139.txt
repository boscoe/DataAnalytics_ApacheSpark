By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSDEC. 5, 2015
Lindsey Vonn won the season-opening World Cup downhill Friday for her 16th victory at Lake Louise, Alberta, and her 34th career World Cup downhill victory. She finished in 1 minute 50.5 seconds. Cornelia Hütter was second in 1:51.08.■ Aksel Lund Svindal won his third straight World Cup race, holding off Kjetil Jansrud in a demanding downhill in Beaver Creek, Colo. Svindal finished in 1 minute 42.34 seconds to beat Jansrud by 0.30 of a second.■ Sara Takanashi won the season-opening event on the women’s ski jump World Cup in Lillehammer, Norway, overcoming a tremendous first jump by Maja Vtic. Takanashi had jumps of 95.5 and 97.5 meters for a total score of 251.7 points, beating Vtic by 11.5 points. Vtic jumped 101.5 meters in the first round but had lower style points than Takanashi, then managed just 93 meters in her second jump.A version of this brief appears in print on December 5, 2015, on Page D5 of the New York edition with the headline: Vonn Wins in Downhill.
