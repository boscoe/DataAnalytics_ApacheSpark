By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJAN. 24, 2015
Rio de Janeiro will not make good on its pledge of cutting by 80 percent the flow of sewage and garbage into Guanabara Bay, where the 2016 Olympics sailing and wind surfing competitions are to be held, the state’s top environmental official said.“I’m not worried about the water quality in those areas,” the official, André Correa, said. “I’m much more worried about floating garbage there.”A version of this brief appears in print on January 24, 2015, on Page D4 of the New York edition with the headline: Rio Set to Break Pledge.
