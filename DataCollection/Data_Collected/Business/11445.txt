By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJAN. 26, 2016
Stocks jumped on Tuesday as the price of oil made another abrupt reversal, this time rising almost 4 percent after having fallen sharply the day before.Energy stocks climbed along with the price of oil, and Chevron and Exxon Mobil made major gains.Strong fourth-quarter results from the beleaguered wireless provider Sprint gave telecom stocks a lift.Quarterly earnings also sent other stocks higher, including the Post-it Notes maker 3M; Procter & Gamble, which makes Crest toothpaste; and the luxury handbag maker Coach.The Dow Jones industrial average increased 282.01 points, or 1.8 percent, to 16,167.23, its best day since early December. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index rose 26.55 points, or 1.4 percent, to 1,903.63. The Nasdaq composite index added 49.18 points, or 1.1 percent, to 4,567.67.AdvertisementThe price of United States crude rose $1.11, or 3.7 percent, to close at $31.45 a barrel in New York. It fell almost 6 percent on Monday. Brent crude, a benchmark for international oils, rose $1.30, or 4.3 percent, to $31.80 a barrel in London. Despite the rebound, United States crude is down almost 18 percent this month.AdvertisementExxon Mobil shares were up $2.72, or 3.7 percent, to $76.70, and Chevron shares rose $3.23, or 4 percent, to $84.12.The shares of Weight Watchers International soared 20 percent after a new commercial starring Oprah Winfrey aired, in which she says she has lost 26 pounds using the weight-loss company’s plan.Ms. Winfrey bought a 10 percent stake in Weight Watchers in October, and since then the stock is up more than 90 percent.

Procter & Gamble reported a larger profit in the fourth quarter as it raised prices and cut costs. Shares of the maker of Pantene shampoo, Crest toothpaste and Charmin toilet paper added $1.96, or 2.6 percent, to $78.81.Coach reported a greater profit than analysts had expected, and its stock rose $2.98, or 9.8 percent, to $33.33. Even with that big gain, however, its shares are down 10 percent over the last 12 months.3M, which makes industrial coatings and ceramics, reported a greater profit and also beat analysts’ expectations. Shares rose $7.21, or 5.2 percent, to $144.78.Huntington Bancshares agreed to buy a competitor, FirstMerit, for $3.4 billion. The deal would create the largest bank in Ohio, and the companies would have about $100 billion in combined assets. The yield on the 10-year Treasury note slipped to 2 percent from 2.01 percent, and the yield on the two-year Treasury note dipped to 0.84 percent from 0.86 percent. In the last week the yields on those two bonds have gotten closer than they’ve been since June 2008, a sign that investors are concerned about economic growth.“Fear is the biggest driver,” said Guy LeBas, chief fixed-income strategist for Janney. Mr. LeBas said investors were also anticipating weaker inflation and thought the Federal Reserve would be more cautious about raising interest rates because the market has experienced so much turmoil this month.United States government bonds become more popular with investors when the economy looks shaky because the government is extremely likely to make good on its debt. Investors are willing to accept lower interest payments when they are concerned about safety.When yields on longer-term bonds like the 10-year bond fall toward the yield on short-term bonds, it signals that investor expectations for future growth have dimmed.The CAC 40 in France rose 1.1 percent, and the DAX in Germany went up 0.9 percent. The FTSE 100 in Britain gained 0.6 percent. Asian markets, however, were hammered by Monday’s slide in oil prices, which can signal weak demand. The Shanghai composite dropped 6.4 percent to finish at 2,749.78, the lowest since December 2014. The Nikkei 225 in Japan lost 2.4 percent to 16,708.90.AdvertisementGold rose $15.50 to $1,121.70 an ounce, and silver gained 31 cents, or 2.2 percent, to $14.564 an ounce. Copper increased 1.9 cents to $2.158 a pound.The price of gold has risen 5.8 percent this year. Only 13 stocks in the S&P 500 have made a bigger gain.In other energy trading, wholesale gasoline rose 1.7 cents to $1.047 a gallon, and heating oil gained 3.2 cents, or 3.5 percent, to 96.8 cents.
