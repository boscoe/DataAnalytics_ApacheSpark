AdvertisementSupported byBusiness BriefingBy The Associated PressConAgra Foods is spinning off its frozen potatoes business and unloading a second major division as it works to focus on its prominent, name-brand foods. The company said on Wednesday that it would split off its leading brands into a company called Conagra Brands. It will spin off its frozen potato business under the Lamb Weston name. The division is expected to be completed by the fall of 2016. Conagra Brands will include the names Marie Callender’s, Hunt’s, Rotel, Reddi-wip, Slim Jim, Chef Boyardee, Orville Redenbacher’s, P.F. Chang’s and Healthy Choice. It will also include several other businesses. Advertisement
