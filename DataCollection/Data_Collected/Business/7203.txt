By CHAD BRAYJUNE 30, 2015
Cenovus Energy, a Canadian oil company, said on Tuesday that it had agreed to sell its oil-and-gas royalty business to the Ontario Teachers’ Pension Plan for 3.3 billion Canadian dollars, or about $2.7 billion.The business, known as Heritage Royalty, owns royalties or mineral rights for about 4.8 million gross acres in Alberta, Saskatchewan and Manitoba. It had third-party royalty interest volumes of about 7,800 barrels of oil a day in the first quarter.“We believe this agreement captures significant value for Cenovus shareholders from our royalty and fee lands business,” Brian Ferguson, the Cenovus president and chief executive, said in a news release.“The proceeds from this sale will strengthen our balance sheet and provide us with greater resilience during these uncertain times as well as the flexibility to invest in organic projects with strong returns.”AdvertisementCenovus had been considering strategic alternatives for the business, including an initial public offering, before opting for the sale.The transaction is expected to close at the end of July.Based in Calgary, Cenovus produced more than 200,000 barrels of oil a day and posted revenue of $20.1 billion in 2014.
