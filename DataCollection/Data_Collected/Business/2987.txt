Horton, one of the largest homebuilders in the United States, reported a higher-than-expected 37 percent jump in quarterly revenue as it delivered more homes. The company said homes sold rose 29 percent to 7,973 in the quarter that ended Dec. 31, its first quarter. Net sales orders, an indicator of future revenue, rose 35 percent during the period. D.R. Horton was well positioned to capture demand in the all-important spring selling season, the company’s chairman, Donald Horton, said in a statement. The company’s net income rose to $142.5 million, or 39 cents a share, in the quarter, from $123.2 million, or 36 cents a share, a year earlier. Advertisement
