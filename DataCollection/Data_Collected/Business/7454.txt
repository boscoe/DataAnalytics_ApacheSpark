By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJULY 10, 2015
Wholesale stockpiles rose 0.8 percent in May, double the gain in April and the largest one-month rise since November, the Commerce Department reported Friday. Sales were up 0.3 percent in May after a 1.7 percent surge in April, which had been the biggest sales increase in more than a year. The strong increase in inventory building in May could signal that businesses were more confident about the future. A decision to accelerate inventory stocking would help bolster overall economic growth.
