By CHAD BRAYMARCH 20, 2015
LONDON — The holding company Camfin, the largest shareholder of the Italian tire maker Pirelli, is in talks to sell its stake in the company to China National Chemical Corporation in a deal that could pave the way for a full takeover of Pirelli, according to a person briefed on the discussions.In a statement on Friday, Camfin said that it was in talks with an “international industrial partner” to sell its 26.2 percent stake in Pirelli.The unnamed partner has offered to pay 15 euros a share for its Pirelli stake, which would value the tire maker at 7.1 billion euros, or about $7.6 billion, Camfin said.“As things presently stand, an agreement is yet to be reached,” Camfin said in a news release.A person briefed on the discussions confirmed that China National Chemical Corporation was the unnamed investor and the deal could be sealed as early as this weekend.AdvertisementOnce the deal for Camfin’s stake is completed, the unnamed investor would begin a bid to acquire the remaining outstanding shares of Pirelli, Camfin said.
