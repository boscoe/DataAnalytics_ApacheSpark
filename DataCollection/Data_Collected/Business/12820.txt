By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 28, 2016
Stocks got a little help from an international hotel deal and dueling superheroes on Monday, but they could only wobble to a split finish in a quiet day of trading.Shares of the Starwood and Marriott hotel chains climbed after a Chinese insurance company made another offer to buy Starwood. Stock in the media giant Time Warner rose after “Batman v Superman: Dawn of Justice” had a big opening weekend.Stocks flipped between small gains and losses for most of the day. Oil prices slipped, and energy companies took modest losses. Mining and metals companies traded slightly higher.Monday was the slowest trading day of the year for United States markets, and European exchanges were closed for Easter.

The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index increased 1.11 points to 2,037.05, ending a three-day losing streak.AdvertisementThe Dow Jones industrial average rose 19.66 points, or 0.1 percent, to 17,535.39. The Nasdaq composite index lost 6.72 points, or 0.1 percent, to 4,766.79.AdvertisementStocks have flagged over the last few days after a five-week rally.The bidding for Starwood may have reached an end as a group of investors led by China’s Anbang Insurance Group offered to buy the company for $14 billion. Marriott agreed to buy Starwood for $12.2 billion last year, and recently raised that offer to $13.5 billion.Starwood stock added $1.62, or 2 percent, to $83.75. It has risen 19 percent in the last two weeks. Marriott shares rose $2.70, or 3.9 percent, to $71.34.Time Warner shares climbed after “Batman v Superman” had one of the best opening weekends ever, despite negative reviews and some skepticism from fans. It grossed about $424 million worldwide, including $170 million in the United States. That was a good sign for Time Warner’s planned series of movies based on DC Comics characters. The stock rose $2.53, or 3.6 percent, to $72.54.

Also making gains were Netflix, whose shares rose $2.85, or 2.9 percent, to $101.21, and the retailer Dollar Tree, which added $2.32, or 3 percent, to $80.84.Those and other consumer stocks rose after the government said on Friday that the economy grew at a faster pace in the fourth quarter than earlier estimates showed. And on Monday, the Commerce Department said consumer spending continued to grow in February, though for the third straight month it rose by only a small amount.Consumers have saved a lot of money as gas and heating prices have plunged, said Michael J. Scanlon Jr., managing director and portfolio manager for John Hancock Asset Management. But he said people were not sure that prices would stay low, so they had not increased their spending. That has surprised experts.“The fact that spending is growing in line with wages is showing how much more responsible the U.S. consumer is today,” Mr. Scanlon said.AdvertisementBenchmark United States crude fell 7 cents, to $39.39 a barrel, in New York. Brent crude, used to price international oils, lost 17 cents, to $40.27 a barrel, in London. That weighed on energy companies. Hess shares lost $1.26, or 2.4 percent, to $50.84, and stock in Devon Energy fell 63 cents, or 2.4 percent, to $25.84.

The cosmetics giant Avon Products said it reached a deal with activist investors, including Barington Capital, that headed off a possible proxy fight. Avon stock rose 36 cents, or 8.4 percent, to $4.64.Pandora Media skidded after the streaming music company announced a management shake-up. A co-founder, Tim Westergren, will replace Brian P. McAndrews as chief executive. The stock tumbled $1.33, or 12.2 percent, to $9.60.In other energy trading, wholesale gasoline was little changed at $1.47 a gallon. Heating oil fell 2 cents to $1.18 a gallon. Natural gas rose 4 cents, or 2.3 percent, to $1.85 per 1,000 cubic feet.The price of gold fell $1.50 to $1,219.90 an ounce. Silver lost 1 cent to $15.19 an ounce. Copper rose 2 cents to $2.25 a pound.Bond prices rose. The yield on the 10-year Treasury note dipped to 1.88 percent from 1.91 percent. The euro inched up to $1.1198 from $1.1175 late Thursday.
