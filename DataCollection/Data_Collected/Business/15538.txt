By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSAUG. 26, 2016
Stocks on Wall Street ended the day mostly lower on Friday, giving up modest gains following a generally upbeat assessment of the economy from the Federal Reserve chairwoman, Janet L. Yellen.Phone companies and utilities, widely considered safe-play stocks, led the list of declining stocks, while health care and technology companies rose. Shares of AT&T and Verizon each fell about 1 percent.KEEPING SCORE The Dow Jones industrial average closed 0.3 percent lower and the Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index gave up 0.2 percent. The Nasdaq composite index gained 0.1 percent.YELLEN UPBEAT Ms. Yellen said in a speech that the Fed was moving toward raising interest rates, citing a solid job market and an improved outlook for the economy and inflation. But she stopped short of signaling any timetable for the next increase.AdvertisementBOND REACTION After initially rising after Ms. Yellen’s speech, United States government bond prices fell. The yield on the 10-year note, which moves in the opposite direction from the price, rose to 1.59 percent from 1.54 earlier Friday. The yield was 1.58 percent late Thursday.
