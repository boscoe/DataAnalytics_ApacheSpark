AdvertisementSupported byBusiness BriefingBy ReutersGeneral Motors received 75 claims last week for compensation for ignition-switch defects in its cars, bringing the overall total to 4,312, the official administering the compensation program said on Monday. The official, Kenneth R. Feinberg, has so far determined that 56 deaths, nine severe injuries and 78 other injuries were eligible for payment. Through Friday, G.M. had received 478 claims for death, 288 for catastrophic injuries and 3,546 for less serious injuries requiring hospitalization. The number of claims found eligible for compensation rose to 143 from 131 last week. The report said 513 claims had been deemed ineligible, while 1,388 were under review. Advertisement
