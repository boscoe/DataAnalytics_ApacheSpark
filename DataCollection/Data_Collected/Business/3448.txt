By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSFEB. 9, 2015
McDonald’s said sales dropped 12.6 percent in January at locations open at least 13 months in the division that includes Asia. That overshadowed a 0.4 percent rise in the United States and a 0.5 percent increase in Europe. The company, the world’s largest hamburger chain, said its performance in Asia was hurt by customer perception issues in Japan, where a human tooth, plastic pieces and other objects have been found in its food. In China, McDonald’s is trying to recover from the fallout from another food-safety scandal. The chief financial officer, Peter Bensen, said during a January conference call that it would take at least three more months for its business in China to return to normal.
