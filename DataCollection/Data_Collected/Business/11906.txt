By REUTERSFEB. 17, 2016
T-Mobile nearly tripled its profit in the fourth quarter as its Binge On video-streaming service and lower-priced plans helped it add more than two million subscribers. T-Mobile, the No. 3 United States wireless provider, said on Wednesday that net income jumped to $297 million, or 34 cents a share. Total revenue rose 1.1 percent compared with a year ago, to $8.25 billion. T-Mobile said it added a net 2.1 million customers, including 1.3 million new monthly or postpaid customers in the three months that ended Dec. 31. “We aren’t just winning customers. We’re keeping them, too,” said the chief, John J.
