By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJULY 29, 2015
The head of the International Monetary Fund, Christine Lagarde, said the world economy was recovering but fragile and faced “some downside risks.” In an online news conference on Wednesday, Ms. Lagarde described the United States as “a strong performer” and China as “resilient” despite a recent drop in Chinese stock prices. Ms. Lagarde shrugged off a recent sell-off in Chinese stocks, noting that China’s shares were still 80 percent higher than they were a year ago. “The Chinese economy is resilient and strong enough to withstand” volatility in financial markets, she said.
