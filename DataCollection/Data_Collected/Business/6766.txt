By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJUNE 11, 2015
Stocks rose for a second day after an encouraging report on retail sales suggested that Americans were finally feeling confident enough to spend more.The market climbed from the start of trading on Thursday, pulled back at midmorning on fears over a possible Greek default, but managed to hold on to modest gains across industries.Seven of the 10 sectors of the Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index rose, led by a 0.7 percent increase in utility stocks.
