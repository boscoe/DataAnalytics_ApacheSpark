By REUTERSFEB. 20, 2015
The Dow Jones industrial average and the Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index ended at record highs, without adjustment for inflation, on Friday, after Greek and eurozone finance ministers reached a deal to extend Greece’s financial rescue by four months. The Nasdaq notched an eighth consecutive day of gains.The agreement removes the immediate risk of Greece’s running out of money next month and being forced out of the single currency area.Investors awaited a deal all week. As talks progressed, the euro rebounded and the benchmark 10-year United States Treasury note retreated.In the United States, the Nasdaq matched an eight-session winning streak from a year ago and inched closer to its 5,132.52 intraday high, which was reached in March 2000 just before the dot-com bubble burst. The S.&P. 500 ended slightly higher for the week as well, its third consecutive week of gains.AdvertisementGreece’s deal should allow investors to concentrate on the fundamentals that should be driving the market, said Benjamin A. Pace, chief investment officer at HPM Partners in New York.Advertisement“You’re seeing a little bit better U.S. economic statistics than you’ve been seeing over the past three or four weeks,” Mr. Pace said. “The European statistics have gotten a lot better, too.”Surveys indicated on Friday that the eurozone’s private sector expanded in February at its fastest pace in seven months, though companies continued to cut prices, suggesting that low inflation remains a challenge for policy makers.The euro traded up slightly at $1.1375. Against the yen, the dollar pared losses to trade up 0.12 to 119.06.
