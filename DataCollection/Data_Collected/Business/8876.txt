AdvertisementSupported byBy Michael J. de la MercedThe NCR Corporation’s efforts to sell itself may not be done after all.The company, a 131-year-old maker of cash registers and other payment systems, is again putting itself on the auction block, a person briefed on the matter said on Friday. The move comes after talks to sell the company to the investment firm Thoma Bravo fell through this summer.Among those taking another look at NCR is the Blackstone Group, said this person, who spoke on condition of anonymity. Still, the restarted sales process is in early stages and no transaction is imminent.Resuming a possible sale of itself would be another attempt by the company to improve its returns for shareholders as it grapples with declining profits.Shares of NCR have fallen nearly 19 percent over the last 12 months.The company’s talks with Thoma Bravo fell apart over the price. NCR has since lowered its price expectations, the person briefed on the matter said.A spokesman for NCR wasn’t immediately available to comment on the new sales process, which was reported earlier by The New York Post. A spokesman for Blackstone declined to comment.Shares of NCR were up 3 percent in afternoon trading on Friday at $27.25. That values NCR at about $4.5 billion.Advertisement
