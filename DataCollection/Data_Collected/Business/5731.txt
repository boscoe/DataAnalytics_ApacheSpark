A decade after Hurricane Katrina, when many dislocated families living in contaminated trailers reported illnesses linked to elevated levels of formaldehyde, the E.P.A. is close to taking action to impose restrictions on the use of the chemical in wood products. But the furniture industry, with the help of some members of Congress, has been trying to get the proposed new rule scaled back. Here is a look at that effort.
