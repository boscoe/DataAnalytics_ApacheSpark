AdvertisementSupported byBy The Associated PressA rally in energy companies helped push the United States stock market higher for a second straight day on Tuesday. Investors also worked through corporate news and prepared for crucial United States economic data later this week.The Dow Jones industrial average rose 89.39 points, or 0.5 percent, to 17,918.15. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index rose 5.74 points, or 0.3 percent, to 2,109.79, and the Nasdaq rose 17.98 points, or 0.4 percent, to 5,145.13.Investors showed a wave of interest in the oil and gas companies, one of the most battered parts of the market. The energy sector of the S.&P. 500 rallied 2.5 percent. The advance was mostly on the back of a jump in the price of oil, which climbed 4 percent.Drilling and exploration companies were the biggest benefactors. Pioneer Natural Resources, Diamond Offshore and Anadarko Petroleum all rose more than 5 percent. Exxon Mobil was up 2 percent, and Chevron 3 percent.Investors found some good news in the monthly sales reports from the automakers. General Motors, Ford and others reported double-digit increases in sales from a year earlier, and some automakers are on pace to break annual sales records.“Even with the market volatility this summer, there aren’t the signs that consumers are hunkering down,” said David Kelly, chief global strategist at J. P. Morgan Funds.G.M. rose 21 cents, or 0.6 percent, to $35.78 and Ford rose 6 cents, or 0.4 percent, to $14.81.In other company news, shares of King Digital Entertainment, the publisher of the Candy Crush Saga video game, jumped $2.31, or 15 percent, to $17.85 after Activision Blizzard announced it would buy the company for $5.9 billion. Activision Blizzard rose $1.25, or 3.6 percent, to $35.82.Investors will be turning to economic data out later this week. The October jobs report comes out on Friday. If the survey comes in strong, it will raise market expectations that the Federal Reserve will raise interest rates at its December meeting. Economists surveyed by FactSet expect that United States employers added 185,000 jobs in October and that the unemployment rate remained steady at 5.1 percent.United States government bond prices fell. The yield on the 10-year Treasury note rose to 2.22 percent from 2.18 percent late Monday. In currencies, the euro fell to $1.0962 from $1.1015 and the dollar rose to 120.98 yen from 120.75 yen.In the metals market, gold fell $21.60, or 2 percent, to $1,114.20 an ounce; silver fell 17 cents, or 1 percent, to $15.24 an ounce; and copper rose a penny, to $2.33 a pound.Benchmark United States crude oil added $1.76, or 3.8 percent, to close at $47.90 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange. Brent crude, which is used to price international oils, rose $1.75, or 3.6 percent, to $50.54 a barrel in London.Wholesale gasoline jumped 7 cents, to $1.446 a gallon in New York; heating oil rose 5.9 cents, to $1.566 a gallon; and natural gas fell 0.3 cents, to $2.253 per 1,000 cubic feet.Advertisement
