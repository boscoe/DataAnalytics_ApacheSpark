By REUTERSAUG. 11, 2016
Wall Street stocks ended at record highs on Thursday as surging oil prices and strong earnings from the department stores Macy’s and Kohl’s buoyed investor sentiment.Crude oil jumped as much as 5 percent on comments from the Saudi oil minister about potential action to stabilize prices and the International Energy Agency’s forecast that crude oil markets would rebalance in the next few months.The Dow Jones industrial average climbed 0.64 percent to end at 18,613.52 points, and the Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index gained 0.47 percent to 2,185.79.The Nasdaq composite index added 0.46 percent to 5,228.40.A stock market rally since late June has pushed the S.&P. 500 up 7 percent in 2016, helped by better-than-expected quarterly earnings and low interest rates, but some investors are worried about high valuations.

Robust economic data also helped on Thursday, with a report showing the number of Americans applying for unemployment benefits fell to 266,000 from 269,000 the previous week.Valeant dropped 10.36 percent after The Wall Street Journal reported that federal prosecutors had opened a criminal investigation on the drug maker.The energy sector was the best-performing major group in the S.&P. index, rising 1.3 percent, buoyed by the rise in oil prices.Crude settled at $43.49 per barrel, up $1.78 or 4.3 percent, in New York, while Brent crude closed $1.99, or 4.5 percent, higher at $46.04. Both jumped more than 5 percent during the session.
                Over the last two and a half years, the oil industry experienced its deepest downturn since at least the 1990s.
