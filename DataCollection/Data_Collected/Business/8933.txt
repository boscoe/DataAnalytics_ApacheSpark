By RAVI SOMAIYASEPT. 14, 2015
Condé Nast announced an executive shake-up Monday, appointing its president, Robert A. Sauerberg Jr., as its chief executive, and moving Charles H. Townsend from C.E.O. to chairman of Condé Nast U.S.The company’s previous leader, S.I. Newhouse Jr., a famed figure in the magazine industry and part of the family that owns Condé Nast, will become chairman emeritus.Mr. Sauerberg will remain president, as well as becoming chief executive, and will be responsible for strategic and daily operations. Mr. Townsend will work closely with him, and with the company’s board, Condé Nast said in a statement. The changes will take effect in January 2016.“I am thrilled to accept the role of C.E.O. of Condé Nast, and honored to have the opportunity to continue to work with Chuck, the Condé Nast Board and the exceptional editors, publishers, engineers, developers and their teams during this most exciting time in our great company’s history,” Mr. Sauerberg said in a statement.Mr. Sauerberg takes the helm at a troubled time in the industry. Condé Nast’s magazines, which include Vogue, Vanity Fair and The New Yorker, are transitioning from highly lucrative print businesses to more complex digital ones.A version of this article appears in print on September 15, 2015, on Page B2 of the New York edition with the headline: Business Briefing; Condé Nast’s President Succeeds Chief in Shake-Up.
