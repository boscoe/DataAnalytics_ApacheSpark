AdvertisementSupported byBy Chad BrayLONDON — Pentair said on Monday that it had agreed to acquire the electrical fastenings company Erico Global for $1.8 billion in cash, including the repayment of debt.The deal is expected to broaden Pentair’s product offerings in its technical solutions business segment.“The addition of Erico’s complementary business will expand our presence in the commercial and industrial sectors,” Randall J. Hogan, the Pentair chairman and chief executive, said in a news release.The transaction is subject to regulatory approval and is expected to be completed by the end of the year.Erico, which is based in Solon, Ohio, manufactures electrical and fastening products for use in electrical and mechanical devices, including telecommunications, fire protection, and heating and air-conditioning equipment. The privately held company employs 1,200 people in 30 countries.Pentair, which is based in Worsley, England, said it expected to finance the deal through a combination of cash and debt.Pentair manufactures water, fluid and thermal control systems for a variety of industries, including the mining, agriculture, chemical and telecom sectors. It posted revenue of $7 billion in 2014 and employs about 30,000 people.Goldman Sachs and the law firm Foley & Lardner advised Pentair, while JPMorgan Chase and the law firm Jones Day advised Erico.Advertisement
