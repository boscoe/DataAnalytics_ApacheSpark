AdvertisementSupported byBy The Associated PressUnited States stocks rose across the board on Wednesday after big gains in Asia and Europe, a buoyant end to the worst quarter for the market in four years.With worries over a slowing Chinese economy, uncertainty over interest rates and a scary slide in commodity prices, stocks have been hit with one blow after another in the last three months. But on Wednesday, investors were in the mood to buy. Energy companies and raw material suppliers, the biggest losers in the quarter, each rose more than 2 percent.The buying began at the opening of trading and swept across all 10 sectors of the Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index. Among the big gainers, the fashion company Ralph Lauren jumped 14 percent after announcing a new chief executive would take over from Mr. Lauren, its founder.Tim Courtney, chief investment officer of Exencial Wealth Advisors, said it was only a matter of time before investors started buying, given recent declines.“I’ve been surprised we haven’t had rallies like the one we’re seeing now,” he said. After “so many negative days, you’re going to get a bounce back.”The S.&P. 500 jumped 35.94 points, or 1.9 percent, to 1,920.03. The index had fallen seven of the last 10 days, and is off 6.9 percent in the third quarter, the worst quarterly showing since 2011.The Dow Jones industrial average gained 235.57 points, or 1.5 percent, to 16,284.70. It fell 7.6 percent in the quarter. The Nasdaq composite index climbed 102.84 points, or 2.3 percent, to 4,620.17. The rally in the United States followed bigger gains overseas. Stock indexes in France, Germany, Britain and Japan climbed more than 2 percent.On Wednesday, investors even mustered enough courage to buy biotechnology companies, breaking an eight-day streak of declines for the sector. The Nasdaq biotechnology index, down 24 percent from a peak in July, rose 4.5 percent. It fell sharply last week after the Democratic presidential front-runner, Hillary Rodham Clinton, wrote in a Twitter post that drug prices were too high, then said she would use government drug-buying programs to slash prices if elected.Among stocks making big moves on Wednesday, Western Digital, the data storage company, surged $10.57, or 15 percent, to $79.44 after it agreed to a $3.8 billion investment from China’s Unisplendour. The Gap fell $1.72, or nearly 6 percent, to $28.50. The new chief of Ralph Lauren, Stefan Larsson, will leave his current job as global president of Gap’s lower-price Old Navy chain. Ralph Lauren rose $14.11, to $118.16.Chesapeake Energy rose 54 cents, or 8 percent, to $7.33 after announcing that it would cut 15 percent of its work force. Investors are waiting for jobs data, due on Friday, for clues about when the Federal Reserve will raise interest rates. Policy makers have said they will probably raise rates this year. On Wednesday, the payroll processor ADP reported that American employers added 200,000 jobs in September, up from 180,000 in August.United States crude fell 14 cents to close at $45.09 a barrel in New York. Oil ended the volatile month down 8 percent. Brent Crude, a benchmark for international oils used by many American refineries, rose 14 cents to close at $48.37 a barrel in London.In other futures trading on the Nymex, wholesale gasoline rose 2.6 cents to close at $1.389 a gallon, heating oil rose 1.5 cents to close at $1.513 a gallon and natural gas fell 6.2 cents to close at $2.524 per 1,000 cubic feet.Prices of United States government bonds were flat. The yield on the 10-year Treasury note slipped to 2.04 percent.Precious metal futures ended slightly lower, but copper surged. Gold slipped $11.60, to $1,115.20 an ounce; silver fell 6 cents, to $14.52 an ounce; and copper jumped 9 cents, to $2.34 a pound.Advertisement
