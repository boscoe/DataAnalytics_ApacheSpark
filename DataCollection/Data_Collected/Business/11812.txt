By REUTERSFEB. 11, 2016
Shares of the social media company Twitter hit a nominal low on Thursday a day after it said that user growth had stalled for the first time since the company went public in 2013. More than 20 brokerage firms cut price targets on the shares, which have fallen more than 35 percent in less than six weeks and more than 50 percent since Jack Dorsey returned as chief executive in July.
