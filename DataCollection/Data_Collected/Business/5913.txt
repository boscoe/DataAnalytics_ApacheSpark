By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMAY 8, 2015
Shares had their best day in two months Friday after encouraging news about the job market. The surge was enough to push two of the three major indexes to gains for the week.European markets also rose sharply after David Cameron’s Conservative Party won an outright majority in Britain’s Parliament, greatly reducing the threat of political uncertainty there.The Dow Jones industrial average gained 267.05 points, or 1.5 percent, to 18,191.11. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index added 28.10 points, or 1.4 percent, 2,116.10, its biggest percentage gain since March 16. The Nasdaq composite index rose 58 points, or 1.2 percent, to 5,003.55.Investors cheered news that American employers added 223,000 jobs in April, a solid gain suggesting that the economy may be recovering after a stumbling start to the year. The unemployment rate fell to 5.4 percent from 5.5 percent in March, the lowest rate since May 2008.AdvertisementThe jobs report is always closely watched, but April’s survey drew even more interest than usual. The revised March figures were abysmal, with employers creating only 85,000 jobs that month.Advertisement“I am even more convinced that the March report was an outlier,” Paul Christopher, an investment strategist with Wells Fargo Advisors, said. “We all know the first quarter was a tough quarter. The jobs numbers needed to hold up and they did.”Both the Dow and S.&P. 500 ended fractionally higher for the week. The Nasdaq composite index ended down less than 0.1 percent.The bond market took a more nuanced take on the employment report. The 10-year Treasury note rose 10/32 to 98 24/32, pushing the yield down to 2.14 percent from 2.18 percent Thursday. That rate had been as high as 2.30 percent just two days ago, representing a big move for that market.

Prices for European government bonds rose broadly, sending yields lower.The British pound advanced sharply against the dollar and the euro. “The U.K. general election result is a surprisingly market-friendly outcome,” said Vicky Redwood from Capital Economics in London.AdvertisementVisa was among the biggest gainers in the S.&P. 500 and the Dow. Shares in Visa rose in late-afternoon trading after Bloomberg News reported it was in talks to buy Visa Europe. Visa Europe was split off from Visa in 2007 when the United States-based Visa decided to become a publicly traded company. Visa Europe is owned by European banks, which is similar to how Visa was structured before it went public. The shares rose 4.3 percent to close at $69.47.The price of oil rose slightly Friday as the dollar weakened. Benchmark United States crude rose 45 cents to close at $59.39 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange, ending a volatile week nearly flat. After finishing last week at $59.15 a barrel, oil rose to its highest level in four months on Wednesday, then fell sharply on Thursday.
