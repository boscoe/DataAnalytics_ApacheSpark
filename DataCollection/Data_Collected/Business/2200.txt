By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSDEC. 28, 2015
Stocks closed modestly lower on Monday as a deepening slump in crude oil prices pulled down energy and mining shares on a lighter-than-usual day of trading.Chevron fell 1.8 percent, the most in the Dow Jones industrial average. Consol Energy sank 9 percent.After recovering a bit last week, United States crude oil fell 3 percent on Monday on reports that Iran intended to increase exports by 500,000 barrels per day after economic sanctions are removed. That would add to excess global supplies that have helped depress oil prices.Investors worry that falling oil prices could be a signal of further economic weakness ahead, said Paul Christopher, head global market strategist at Wells Fargo Investment Institute.

Chevron fell $1.679, to $90.37. Exxon Mobil lost 58 cents, or 0.7 percent, to $78.75. Consol Energy tumbled 78 cents, to $7.84, and Chesapeake Energy slid 38 cents, or 8.5 percent, to $4.06.

The mining company Freeport-McMoRan sank 9.6 percent after news that James R. Moffett, the company’s executive chairman and co-founder, was stepping down. Plunging commodity prices have led to mass layoffs across the entire industry. It was revealed recently that the activist investor Carl C. Icahn has taken a huge stake in the company. The stock shed 73 cents, to $6.84.Six of the 10 sectors in the S.&P. 500 index moved lower. Energy stocks fell the most, 2 percent. The sector is down 23.2 percent this year. Consumer discretionary stocks fared the best, gaining 0.3 percent. That sector is the best performer so far this year, up 9.1 percent.Trading volume was lighter than usual after the Christmas holiday weekend. That is likely to be the case this week as well, ahead of the New Year’s Day holiday, said David Schiegoleit, managing director of investments at the Private Client Reserve at U.S. Bank.“We could see heightened volatility given the low volume, but I don’t see anything fundamental happening between now and the end of the year that could drive prices in either direction,” Mr. Schiegoleit said.AdvertisementBenchmark United States crude shed $1.29, or 3.4 percent, to close at $36.81 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange. Brent crude, which is used to price international oils, lost $1.27, or 3.4 percent, to close at $36.62 a barrel in London.

Heating oil fell a cent to close at $1.09 a gallon, and natural gas rose 21 cents to close at $2.238 per 1,000 cubic feet.Major stock indexes in Europe also ended lower on Monday. In Japan, the Nikkei added 0.6 percent, while the Shanghai composite index lost 2.6 percent. In Hong Kong, the Hang Seng slipped 1 percent.
