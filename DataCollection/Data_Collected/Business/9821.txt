To the Editor:Re “$2,500: A Fair Price for ‘Hamilton’” (The Upshot, Economic View, Oct. 23):N. Gregory Mankiw’s piece on why he is thrilled, just thrilled, to pay $2,500 for a ticket to “Hamilton” requires the minting of a new term: “richsplaining.”He gets to see “Hamilton” and deigns to explain to the rest of us from his lofty and well-paid perch in Cambridge why we should all be in love with our dictated-by-the-immutable-laws-of-the-free-market oppression. It’s why people hate classical economics, classical economists, and Harvard classical economists in particular.Peter ScottoSouth Hadley, Mass., Oct. 23Letters for Sunday Business may be sent to sunbiz@nytimes.com.A version of this article appears in print on October 30, 2016, on Page BU6 of the New York edition with the headline: Letters.
