AdvertisementSupported byBy Ben SisarioThe Harry Fox Agency, a clearinghouse for songwriting rights that has been a mainstay of the music publishing business since 1927, is being sold to Sesac, another rights group, for slightly more than $20 million, according to several people briefed on the deal who spoke on condition of anonymity because they were not authorized to discuss it.Sesac and the National Music Publishers’ Association, a trade group that has long owned the Fox agency, announced the sale on Tuesday but declined to comment on the price.The deal, which must still be ratified by the members of the publishers’ association, is the latest change to the sometime byzantine structure of the music publishing business, which handles the copyrights for songwriting.Sesac — like Ascap and BMI, its much larger rivals — handles the performing rights of songs, while Harry Fox issues a different kind of license, known as “mechanical,” for the sale of CDs and downloads. Through the deal, Sesac would become the first major performing-rights group to handle both kinds of licenses, which may give it an advantage with online music services that often require a range of licenses to operate.Advertisement
