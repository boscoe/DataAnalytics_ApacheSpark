By CHAD BRAYAPRIL 21, 2015
The government of Slovakia is seeking to raise as much as $1.1 billion in an initial public offering of its 49 percent stake in Slovak Telekom, the company said Tuesday.Earlier this month, Slovak Telekom said the National Property Fund of the Slovak Republic, which handles the country’s privatizations, was planning to sell the government’s entire stake through an I.P.O., with listings on the Bratislava Stock Exchange and the London Stock Exchange.On Tuesday, Slovak Telekom said that it expected the offer of 42.3 million shares at 17.70 euros to 23.60 euros a share and $19 to $25.3 for each global depositary receipts, which will be traded in London.The offering would raise up to €1 billion, or about $1.1 billion, the company said.
