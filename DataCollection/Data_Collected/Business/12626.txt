By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 17, 2016
Another surge in stocks on Thursday, in the middle of a four-week rally, turned the Dow Jones industrial average positive for the year.The price of gold, silver and oil jumped, raising the shares of materials and energy companies. The dollar continued to weaken, lifting industrial companies on hopes they will be able to sell more of their products overseas.The Dow Jones rose 155.73 points, or 0.9 percent, to 17,481.49. It is now up 0.3 percent for the year, the first time it has been in the black for 2016.The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index gained 13.37 points, or 0.7 percent, to 2,040.59. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index remains down 0.2 percent for the year. A little more than a month ago, the Dow and the S.&P. 500 were both down 10 percent for the year.

The Nasdaq composite rose 11.01 points, or 0.2 percent, to 4,774.99, but remains down close to 5 percent this year.AdvertisementThe price of oil also crossed a threshold, closing above $40 a barrel for the first time since early December. The benchmark United States crude oil rose $1.74, or 4.5 percent, to close at $40.20 a barrel. Brent crude, the benchmark for international oils, gained $1.21, or 3 percent, to $41.54 a barrel in London.AdvertisementOil prices are now higher than they were at the end of 2015, but still far lower they have been for most of the last decade.The dollar, after years of strength, has weakened in recent days, in part because of the Federal Reserve’s decision to leave interest rates unchanged and slow the pace of future increases. Commodities are priced in dollars around the world, so a weaker dollar makes them more affordable in foreign markets and increases demand.“When the dollar strengthens, gold tends to sell off and vice versa,” said James Butterfill, head of research and investment strategy at ETF Securities. Also, he said, investors aren’t sure what monetary policy makers in Europe will do, and that kind of uncertainty usually sends metals prices higher.

The price of gold rose $35.20, or 2.9 percent, to $1,265 an ounce. Silver climbed 81 cents, or 5.3 percent, to $16.03 an ounce. Copper rose 6 cents, or 2.6 percent, to $2.29 a pound. Gold is at its highest price in about a year and silver and copper are around five-month highs.Mining companies and makers of chemicals, jets, farm equipment and heavy machinery all traded higher. General Electric rose 79 cents, or 2.6 percent, to $30.96, and Boeing gained $3.13, or 2.5 percent, to $130.70. The agribusiness giant Monsanto rose $2.21, or 2.4 percent, to $92.92.Health care stocks continued to slump as a Senate committee sharply questioned executives from Turing Pharmaceuticals, which drew criticism last year when it raised the price of a decades-old anti-infection drug by 5,000 percent.
