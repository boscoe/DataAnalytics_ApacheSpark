By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSSEPT. 21, 2015
Lennar’s results for its third fiscal quarter beat analysts’ estimates as it delivered more homes and new orders rose in an improving housing market. Its shares rose more than 2 percent in morning trading on Monday. Steady job growth coupled with low mortgage rates have increased home sales this year. Lennar, a leading homebuilder, earned $223.3 million, or 96 cents a share, for the period that ended on Aug. 31 — compared with $177.8 million, or 78 cents a share, a year earlier. The results topped Wall Street expectations. The average estimate of nine analysts surveyed by Zacks Investment Research was for earnings of 79 cents a share.
