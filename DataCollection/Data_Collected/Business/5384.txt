By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSAPRIL 21, 2015
Employers in 31 states cut jobs last month as weak economic growth weighed on hiring and a slowdown in oil and gas drilling caused big job losses in some states. The Labor Department said on Tuesday that unemployment rates rose or were unchanged in 27 states and fell in 23 states. The biggest job cuts occurred in states with large oil and gas drilling, led by Texas, which lost 25,400 jobs, and followed by Oklahoma, which cut 12,900. Nationwide, employers added just 126,000 jobs in March, the fewest in 15 months and snapping a yearlong streak of monthly gains above 200,000.A version of this brief appears in print on April 22, 2015, on Page B2 of the New York edition with the headline: Slowdown in Drilling Brings Some Job Cuts.
