By REUTERSSEPT. 16, 2015
The assistant chairman of China’s securities regulator, Zhang Yujun, is under investigation for suspected “serious violation of discipline,” the country’s graft watchdog said on Wednesday. Authorities have been working to restore confidence in the nation’s stock market after recent volatility and an unsuccessful government intervention. The graft watchdog gave no further details, but the terminology it used in its statement on its website is a euphemism for corruption. Mr. Zhang was not available for comment. He is the first official at the regulatory commission to come under investigation during the recent market turmoil.A version of this brief appears in print on September 17, 2015, on Page B2 of the New York edition with the headline: China Opens Inquiry Into Securities Regulator .
