AdvertisementSupported byBusiness BriefingBy ReutersShake Shack said on Thursday that sales at established restaurants jumped more than expected in the third quarter, sending its shares up 7 percent in extended trading. The chain said same-restaurant sales were up 17.1 percent, easily topping expectations. Those sales include the results from 16 domestic company-operated units open for 24 months or longer. The company has 41 company-operated shops in the United States, and 75 restaurants worldwide. The chain increased its 2015 revenue forecast to a range of $189 million to $190 million from $171 million to $174 million previously. Advertisement
