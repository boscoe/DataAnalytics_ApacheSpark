AdvertisementSupported byBusiness BriefingBy ReutersShares of the wearable fitness device maker Fitbit fell nearly 14 percent in after-hours tradingafter it estimated that its current-quarter profit would be below Wall Street expectations. The news overshadowed bigger-than-expected quarterly revenue and profit, which were buoyed by strong demand during the holiday shopping season. Fitbit said on Monday it expected an adjusted profit of zero to 2 cents per share for the quarter ending March 31, lagging expectations of 23 cents, according to Thomson Reuters I/B/E/S. Fitbit said it sold 8.2 million of its devices in the fourth quarter, ending Dec. 31, up 55 percent from a year earlier. Net income attributable to common stockholders rose to $64.2 million, or 26 cents a share, from $11.9 million. Revenue rose to $711.6 million from $370.2 million.Advertisement
