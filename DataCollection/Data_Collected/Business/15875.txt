AdvertisementSupported byBy The Associated PressStocks on Wall Street abruptly changed course again on Tuesday and took large losses.Investors worried about the possibility of a weaker global economy and tried to anticipate the Federal Reserve’s plans for interest rates. Energy companies fell with the price of oil after the International Energy Agency, a leading industry group, said demand for oil was down more than it previously thought.Stocks sank over the first few hours of trading and never regained their footing. The price of oil fell 3 percent after the agency’s remarks about oil demand. The group expects weaker growth because of a more pronounced slowdown in the global economy.The Dow Jones industrial average gave up 258.32 points, or 1.4 percent, closing at 18,066.75. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index fell 32.02 points, or 1.5 percent, to 2,127.02. The Nasdaq composite lost 56.63 points, or 1.1 percent, to close at 5,155.25.After two months of unusual market calm, stocks have whipsawed over the last few days. They plunged Friday and recovered about half those losses on Monday, only to drop lower on Tuesday. Confusion over the Fed’s intentions has been a major factor.Randy Frederick, vice president for trading and derivatives at Charles Schwab, said investors did not know what the Fed would do at its meeting next Tuesday and Wednesday and the market might remain volatile until then.The prospect of weaker oil demand led benchmark crude to fall $1.39, or 3 percent, to $44.90 a barrel in New York. Brent crude, the benchmark for international oil prices, slid $1.22, or 2.5 percent, to $47.10 a barrel in London.Exxon Mobil sank $2.08, or 2.4 percent, to $85.21 and Marathon Oil stumbled $1.13, or 7.3 percent, to $14.34.Anadarko Petroleum agreed to pay $2 billion to buy Freeport-McMoRan’s deepwater assets in the Gulf of Mexico. The activist investor Carl C. Icahn, who bought a stake in Freeport-McMoRan last year, has pushed the company to spin off its oil and gas business so it can focus on copper mining.Anadarko stock dipped 20 cents to $57.59. Freeport-McMoRan fell 93 cents, or 8.4 percent, to $10.15.Of the 30 stocks in the Dow average, only Apple traded higher. It rose $2.51, or 2.4 percent, to $107.95 after T-Mobile reported strong preorders for Apple’s newest iPhones, introduced last week.Bond yields jumped. The yield on the 10-year Treasury note rose to 1.73 percent from 1.67 percent. The yield on the 30-year Treasury bond also rose.Weight Watchers International slumped 68 cents, or 6.6 percent, to $9.68 after the weight loss company said its chief executive, James R. Chambers, would step down at month’s end. Weight Watchers more than doubled last October after it announced an alliance with Oprah Winfrey, who bought a 10 percent stake and joined the board. The stock has given up some of its gains lately.Gold dipped $2, to $1,319 an ounce. Silver fell 3 cents, to $18.98 an ounce. Copper held steady at $2.10 a pound.The dollar rose to 102.65 yen from 101.83 yen. The euro fell to $1.1211 from $1.1232.The German DAX lost 0.4 percent while the CAC-40 in France slipped 1.2 percent. The FTSE 100 index of leading British shares was down 0.5 percent.
