By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSFEB. 17, 2015
British officials said inflation slowed in January to an annual 0.3 percent — the lowest pace on record — in the face of falling energy prices and supermarket price wars. The Office for National Statistics said the rate was down from 0.5 percent in December. The Bank of England governor, Mark Carney, has said inflation could even turn negative but has also said he thought low inflation to be a short-term phenomenon that would raise consumer spending.
