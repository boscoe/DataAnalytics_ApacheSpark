AdvertisementSupported byBy Liz MoyerThe French industrial gas company Air Liquide said on Tuesday that it had agreed to buy Airgas for $10.3 billion in a deal that would swell the company’s presence in the United States.Airgas, based in Radnor, Pa., is one of the largest United States suppliers of industrial, medical and specialty gases, with 17,000 employees. It was founded in 1982 by Peter McCausland, who continues to be its executive chairman and owns 6.6 million or 9 percent of the company’s shares.The company had previously fended off a $5.9 billion hostile takeover by an American rival, Air Products and Chemicals. In February 2011, Air Products withdrew its offer of $70 a share in cash.This time around, the transaction was friendly and the agreed-upon offer was $143 a share in cash. Air Liquide is also assuming $2.8 billion of Airgas debt. The deal represents a total enterprise value of $13.4 billion, the companies said.Mr. McCausland and the boards of both companies have approved the merger, the companies said.Shares of Airgas jumped 29 percent Tuesday afternoon.Air Liquide, based in Paris, operates in 80 countries, supplying gases as well as technologies and services. It has its United States base in Houston and has 200 locations, including 140 industrial gas plants and more than 2,000 miles of pipelines.Airgas will become a wholly owned subsidiary of Air Liquide.Benoît Potier, Air Liquide’s chairman and chief executive, said in a statement that the deal “advances Air Liquide’s vision” as well as its geographic reach. It will increase Air Liquide’s gas and services sales by more than 30 percent, the companies said.Barclays and BNP Paribas advised Air Liquide and are underwriting the bridge financing. Goldman Sachs and Bank of America Merrill Lynch advised Airgas.Advertisement
