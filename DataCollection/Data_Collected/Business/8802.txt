By CHAD BRAYSEPT. 8, 2015
The Blackstone Group said on Tuesday that it had agreed to acquire Strategic Hotels and Resorts, an owner and investor in high-end properties, for about $6 billion, including debt.Strategic Hotels’ portfolio include the Four Seasons hotels and resorts in Silicon Valley, Washington and Jackson Hole, Wyo.; the Fairmont and Intercontinental hotels in Chicago; and the JW Marriott Essex House Hotel near Central Park in Manhattan.Under the terms of the deal, affiliates of a Blackstone real estate fund will pay $14.25 a share in cash for Strategic Hotels, a real estate investment trust.That represented a 13 percent premium to the company’s unaffected intraday trading price on July 23, before a media report discussing a potential transaction appeared, the companies said.AdvertisementStrategic Hotels confirmed in August that it was exploring possible strategic alternatives, including a potential sale.Please verify you're not a robot by clicking the box.Invalid email address.
