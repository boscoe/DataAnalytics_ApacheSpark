AdvertisementSupported byBy The Associated PressThe United States stock market capped a four-day losing streak with its biggest drop of the week.Disappointing quarterly results and outlooks from several companies pulled the major stock indexes sharply lower on Friday. New signs pointing to a slowing of China’s economy also added to investor jitters, bringing down the price of oil and other commodities.While corporate profits have mostly exceeded Wall Street’s expectations so far, investors have grown uneasy as many companies provided cautious outlooks or weak sales.“The revenue numbers have been very shaky,” said JJ Kinahan, TD Ameritrade’s chief strategist. “After next week, we’ll have a much better picture over all how the earnings season was.”The mixed company earnings increasingly weighed on stocks as the week wore on. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index has now lost ground four out of the last five weeks.The S.&P. 500 ended the day down 22.50 points, or 1.1 percent, to 2,079.65, while the Dow Jones industrial average slid 163.39 points, or 0.9 percent, to 17,568.53. The Nasdaq lost 57.78 points, or 1.1 percent, to 5,088.63.Stocks kicked off the week on a strong note, driving the Nasdaq to another nominal high and bringing the S.&P. 500 close to a milestone of its own. But it has been downhill since then. As of Friday, the Dow was down 1.4 percent for 2015.Trading started unevenly on Friday. The major indexes were all down by midmorning as traders examined the latest corporate earnings.The biotechnology company Biogen and the pharmaceutical company AbbVie both reported better-than-expected second-quarter profits, but their revenue fell short of Wall Street forecasts. Biogen plunged $85.02, or 22.1 percent, to $300.03. AbbVie declined $2.44, or 3.5 percent, to $68.08.Shares of American Airlines, the largest carrier in the world, fell nearly 7 percent after it said that revenue for each seat flown one mile, a crucial measure, would drop by between 6 and 8 percent in the third quarter. Still, American said cheaper fuel costs helped it nearly double its second-quarter profit to $1.7 billion. Even merger news failed to impress. Anthem agreed to buy rival Cigna for $48 billion in a deal that would create the nation’s largest health insurer by enrollment. Anthem fell $4.35, or 2.8 percent, to $150.86, while Cigna lost $8.64, or 5.6 percent, to $145.72.Investors did welcome Amazon’s surprise profit late on Thursday. The stock vaulted $47.24, or 9.8 percent, to $529.42. Nine of the 10 sectors in the S.&P. 500 ended lower. Health care stocks fell the most, dropping 2.5 percent. Utilities rose higher.Another 163 companies, or a third of the S.&P. 500, are due to report earnings next week, including Facebook, Twitter and Exxon Mobil. The price of oil continued to slide Friday. Benchmark United States crude fell 31 cents to close at $48.14 a barrel in New York. Crude fell 5 percent for the week, and is down 19 percent for the month. Brent crude fell 65 cents Friday to close at $54.62 a barrel in London. In other futures trading, wholesale gasoline fell 2.4 cents to close at $1.828 a gallon, while heating oil fell 2.5 cents to close at $1.630 a gallon. Natural gas fell 4 cents to close at $2.776 per 1,000 cubic feet.Gold lost $8.40, to $1,085.60 an ounce; silver fell 21 cents to finish at $14.48 an ounce; and copper fell less than a penny, to $2.38 a pound.The yield on the 10-year Treasury note fell to 2.26 percent from 2.27 percent late on Thursday.Advertisement
