By CHAD BRAYAUG. 3, 2015
LONDON — A group of investors led by the private equity arm of German insurer Allianz agreed to acquire Autobahn Tank & Rast Holding, the German motorway service area operator, its owners said on Monday.The investment consortium will pay more than 3.5 billion euros, or about $3.8 billion, according to a person familiar with the discussions.The acquiring investors include Allianz Capital Partners; Borealis Infrastructure Management; Infinity Investments, a wholly owned subsidiary of the Abu Dhabi Investment Authority; and MEAG, Munich Re’s asset manager.“The acquisition is an important step in our ambition to substantially grow our portfolio of high-quality infrastructure companies and to diversify across sectors and countries,” said Christian Fingerle, the chief investment officer overseeing Allianz Capital’s infrastructure investments.Advertisement“We are committed to support management in sustainably growing Tank & Rast in close cooperation with all stakeholders and to further improve its essential services for all customers,” Mr. Fingerle said.Please verify you're not a robot by clicking the box.Invalid email address.
