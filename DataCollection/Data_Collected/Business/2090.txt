AdvertisementSupported byBy The Associated PressStocks posted moderate gains in a quiet trading day on Monday, recovering part of their losses sustained late last week. Energy stocks continued to be a drag on the market, as the price of oil fell once again.The Dow Jones industrial average added 123.07 points, or 0.7 percent, to 17,251.62. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index rose 15.60 points, or 0.8 percent, to 2,021.15, and the Nasdaq composite rose 45.84 points, or 0.9 percent, to 4,968.92.Investors are still assessing the effects of the Federal Reserve’s decision last week to raise interest rates for the first time in nearly a decade. Markets rose last Wednesday after the Fed made its decision, but then fell sharply later in the week as traders sold shares to book profits. The Dow dropped 2.1 percent on Friday.The buying on Monday was partly a recovery from last week’s drop, a common response to a sell-off.Crude oil dropped again, however, nearing levels not seen since the financial crisis. Crude oil fell 25 cents, or 0.7 percent, to $35.81 a barrel. Brent crude, which is used to price international oils, was down 53 cents, or 1.4 percent, to $36.35 a barrel.Energy stocks struggled as a result, making it the worst-performing sector in the S.&P. 500. Shares of Newfield Exploration fell $1.19, or nearly 4 percent, to $30.41. ConocoPhillips shares fell 83 cents, or about 2 percent, to $45.10.Trading volume tends to decrease on Wall Street during the last two weeks of the year, as many investors begin to close their books for the holidays. Markets are closed for the next two Fridays for holidays, and trading closes early on Thursday for Christmas Eve. There is also little in the way of market-moving economic data or company news expected.“Today’s calendar is relatively light, and with liquidity getting thinner over the course of the week, the potential for exaggerated moves is very real,” said Chris Beauchamp, senior market analyst at IG. “The default direction is still likely to be up, given past performance.”In other energy trading, heating oil fell a penny to $1.13 a gallon, wholesale gasoline fell 5 cents to $1.22 a gallon and natural gas jumped 14 cents to $1.91 per 1,000 cubic feet.The yield on the 10-year United States Treasury note fell to 2.19 percent from 2.21 percent on Friday. The dollar fell against the euro to $1.09, but was mostly unchanged against the yen at $121.10.In metals, gold rose $15.70 to $1,081.90 an ounce, silver rose 22 cents to $14.31 an ounce and copper rose 3 cents to $2.14 a pound.Advertisement
