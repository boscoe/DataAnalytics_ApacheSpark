Chico’s FAS, the women’s clothing retailer, is nearing a sale to the private equity firm Sycamore Partners for upward of $3 billion, according to a person briefed on the matter. If completed, the deal would be the largest buyout of the year.Chico’s, with a market value of more than $2.65 billion, is traded on the New York Stock Exchange. Shares in the company jumped more than 10 percent in after-hours trading after news of the potential sale was reported.Sycamore, a relatively new firm that opened its doors only in 2011, focuses on buying out smaller retailers like Chico’s.The firm also has stakes in or controls brands including Aéropostale, Talbots and Stuart Weitzman.  In 2013, it agreed to buy the Jones Group for $2.2 billion including debt, giving it control of brands including Nine West, Anne Klein and Easy Spirit.Last month, Sycamore was trying to acquire Express, but walked away from the deal after failing to secure financing. The firm had already bought nearly 10 percent of the company’s stock and pursued a buyout for nearly seven months before ending its pursuit.The person briefed on the matter cautioned that a deal to take Chico’s private was still in the works and could yet fall apart.Chico’s also owns brands including White House Black Market, Soma Intimates and Boston Proper. Sales at the company topped $665 million in the most recent quarter, with profits of more than $26 million. Chico’s has been the subject of speculation as a takeover target for some time now.Sycamore declined to comment. Chico’s did not immediately reply to a request for comment. The Wall Street Journal earlier reported that Chico’s was nearing a sale to Sycamore.A version of this article appears in print on 02/11/2015, on page B5 of the NewYork edition with the headline: Chico's Nears Sale.
