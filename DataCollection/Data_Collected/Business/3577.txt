By REUTERSFEB. 13, 2015
J. M. Smucker says it expects its coffee business to recover this year as bean prices stabilize and it introduces smaller Folgers cans to recover customers it lost after raising prices. Smucker expects a modest decline in net sales in the quarter ending in April, saying demand for its coffee products is likely to remain low because of price increases. Smucker, along with Kraft Foods, raised prices last year after a drought in Brazil caused green coffee bean prices to soar.A version of this brief appears in print on February 14, 2015, on Page B2 of the New York edition with the headline: Smucker Predicts Rebound in Coffee Business.
