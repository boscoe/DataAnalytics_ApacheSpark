AdvertisementSupported byBusiness BriefingBy The Associated PressRhapsody International, the operator of the Rhapsody and Napster streaming music services, says it has surpassed three million paying subscribers globally, as more consumers realize the benefits of offline listening. The gain from about two million a year ago represents the fastest growth in the 13-year history of Rhapsody. The company cited competitors like Apple and Spotify for helping raise awareness of the category. Spotify, the global leader, said in June it had doubled its paying user base from a year ago to 20 million. Apple started a streaming plan last month. Advertisement
