By CHAD BRAYJULY 7, 2015
LONDON — Goldman Sachs has named Karen Cook, one of its top bankers in London, as chairwoman of its investment banking division.According to an internal memorandum, Ms. Cook will continue to serve her most important clients in Britain in her new role, while helping enhance the firm’s investment banking business.“She is a trusted adviser to many corporate leaders and has led numerous franchise-defining transactions,” according to the memo, which was written by Richard Gnodde, David Solomon and John Waldron, the co-heads of Goldman’s investment banking division.Ms. Cook joined Goldman in 1999 as a managing director and head of its investment banking business in Britain. She was named a partner in 2000 and is a member of the investment banking division’s executive committee and its global operating committee.A version of this article appears in print on July 8, 2015, on Page B5 of the New York edition with the headline: Goldman Promotion.
