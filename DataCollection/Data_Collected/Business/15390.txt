By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSAUG. 15, 2016
Stocks on Wall Street closed at a record high Monday. Energy companies rose as the price of oil continued its recent recovery.Makers of chemicals and mining companies made the biggest gains, and machinery companies and banks followed. Investors sold government bonds and utility and phone companies. Those stocks had climbed earlier in the year as investors sought safety.The Dow Jones industrial average climbed 59.58 points, or 0.3 percent, to 18,636.05. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index rose 6.10 points, or 0.3 percent, to 2,190.15. The Nasdaq composite added 29.12 points, or 0.6 percent, to 5,262.02.Stocks have seesawed between small gains and losses for more than a week as investors consider mixed reports on the health of the economy and a decline in corporate earnings. That has not stopped them from setting records, but investors remain wary.Advertisement“The market has run up in anticipation of better earnings ahead,” said Brian Nick, chief investment strategist for TIAA Investments. “If those earnings don’t come, we have the Wile E. Coyote moment where we’re off the cliff,” Mr. Nick said, adding, “and we’re going to fall.”

Second-quarter earnings are nearly all in the books, with this week’s releases from Home Depot, Walmart and Target among the last to appear. Corporate earnings are down once again this quarter. Investors don’t expect much growth in the third quarter either, but they are starting to expect improvement after that.AdvertisementBenchmark crude oil jumped $1.25, or 2.8 percent, to $45.74 a barrel in New York. Brent crude, a benchmark used to price international oils, rose $1.38, or 2.9 percent, to $48.35 a barrel in London. After a steep slide for most of June and July, the price of United States crude oil gained 6.4 percent last week.The drilling rig operator Transocean added 53 cents, or 5.4 percent, to $10.43. National Oilwell Varco picked up $1.04, or 3.1 percent, to $34.85, and ConocoPhillips rose 81 cents, or 2 percent, to $42.18.The chemicals company LyondellBasell Industries rose $2.16, or 2.9 percent, to $77.49. The mining and energy company Freeport-McMoRan climbed 36 cents, or 3 percent, to $12.17. The aluminum producer Alcoa gained 35 cents, or 3.4 percent, to $10.52.Utility companies took the largest losses: Southern Company declined 86 cents, or 1.6 percent, to $51.49. Consolidated Edison sank $1.88, or 2.4 percent, to $76.24. Phone companies and household goods makers also slipped.
