By CHAD BRAYOCT. 13, 2015
LONDON — Shares of Worldpay Group, a British payment processor, jumped as high as 6 percent in early trading on Tuesday after the company’s initial public offering valued it at more than $7 billion.The company, which is owned by the private equity firms Advent International and Bain Capital, priced its offering at 240 pence, or about $3.68 a share. That valued the company at £4.8 billion.Worldpay had expected to price its offering between 235 pence and 250 pence a share. The company announced plans for an offering in London last month.The company’s shares were trading 5.4 percent higher at 253 pence in London on Tuesday morning.The offering consisted of 900 million shares. If an overallotment of shares is fully exercised, the offering would represent about 51.8 percent of the company’s outstanding shares.AdvertisementWorldpay was carved out of the Royal Bank of Scotland to become a stand-alone business in 2010.The company provides payment services to a variety of merchants around the world, processing about 31 million mobile, online and in-store transactions a day. It posted revenue of £863.4 million in 2014 and has about 4,500 employees.The offering raised gross proceeds of £2.16 billion, with about £947.8 million going to the company. After fees, the remainder of the proceeds will go to funds advised by Advent International and Bain Capital, and other selling shareholders.AdvertisementThe offering was led by Bank of America Merrill Lynch, Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley.
