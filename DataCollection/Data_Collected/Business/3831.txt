AdvertisementSupported byBy Michael J. de la MercedGrupo FerroAtlántica of Spain and Globe Specialty Metals, which is based in Miami, agreed on Monday to merge in an all-stock deal, creating a big international producer of specialty alloys valued at about $3.1 billion.The deal is a bet on the rising demand for silicon metal and alloys used in products like solar panels, consumer products and materials for the construction and energy industries. It will also give FerroAtlántica a significant toehold in North America.The Spanish company is owned by Grupo Villar Mir, an industrial conglomerate founded by the billionaire Juan-Miguel Villar Mir.Under the terms of the deal, Grupo Villar Mir will own 57 percent of the combined business; existing Globe shareholders will own the rest.Together, the combined company will report about $2.3 billion in pro forma revenue and $325 million in earnings before interest, tax, depreciation and amortization. The deal is expected to generate about $65 million in cost savings, $30 million in savings by refinancing existing debt and $100 million in additional savings.Société Générale and the law firms Cravath, Swaine & Moore and Uria Menendez advised FerroAtlántica. Goldman Sachs, Nomura and the law firm Latham & Watkins advised Globe.Advertisement
