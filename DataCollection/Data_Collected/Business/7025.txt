By EMILY STEELJUNE 23, 2015
Netflix, the streaming video service, announced on Tuesday that its board had approved a seven-for-one stock split after a strong year in which its shares had doubled.A stock split makes shares more accessible to a wider group of investors, including retail investors and employees. The move had been anticipated after Netflix’s stockholders voted two weeks ago to authorize the company to increase the number of shares.On Tuesday, Netflix shares increased nearly 1 percent to close at $681.19, and soared even higher, past $700, in early after-hours trading. At the beginning of January, the shares traded around $350.Netflix beat expectations for subscriber growth both in the United States and globally during the first quarter of this year. The company has accelerated its original programming output and international expansion.For each outstanding share of common stock, shareholders will receive a stock dividend of six additional shares, the company said.
