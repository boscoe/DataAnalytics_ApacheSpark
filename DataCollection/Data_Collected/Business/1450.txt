By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSNOV. 20, 2015
The sugar and corn industries ended their billion-dollar battle over sweeteners on Friday in a secret out-of-court settlement. The rivals announced the deal, bringing to a close a trial that began nearly three weeks ago in Los Angeles federal court. Both sides said they were committed to “practices that encourage safe and healthful use of their products.” Sugar processors had been seeking $1.5 billion in a false-advertising claim against corn refiners and agribusiness giants Archer Daniels Midland and Cargill after they tried to rebrand their product as “corn sugar.” The corn bloc countersued for $530 million, saying that the sugar industry made false and misleading statements that included a comment that high fructose corn syrup was as addictive as crack cocaine. Corn’s fortunes have been slipping since the mid-2000s when studies began connecting the product to health problems like obesity. But science has shown that the products are nearly identical and are metabolized by the body in the same way, said Roger A.
