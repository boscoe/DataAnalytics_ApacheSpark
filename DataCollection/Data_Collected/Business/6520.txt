AdvertisementSupported byBusiness BriefingBy The Associated PressBetter Homes and Gardens said its longtime chief editor, Gayle Butler, who helped expand the magazine’s scope and digital reach, was retiring. Meredith, which owns the magazine, released a statement Wednesday saying Ms. Butler will complete her 30-year career with the company at the end of June. Stephen Orr, the executive editor at Condé Nast Traveler and former editorial director for Martha Stewart Living, will succeed Ms. Butler, Meredith said. Mr. Orr will take over July 6. Ms. Butler has directed editorial content since 2006 for Better Homes and Gardens, which has 7.6 million readers. Meredith said she expanded the magazine to include beauty, fashion and home technology, and led it into digital, mobile and social media platforms. Advertisement
