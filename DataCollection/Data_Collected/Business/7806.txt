By CHAD BRAYJULY 23, 2015
LONDON — LeasePlan Corporation, one of Europe’s biggest automobile leasing and fleet managers, said on Thursday that its owner, Global Mobility Holding, had agreed to sell the Dutch company to a group of investors for 3.7 billion euros, or about $4.04 billion.The acquiring investors include the Dutch pension fund manager PGGM; the Danish pension fund ATP; the Singapore sovereign wealth fund GIC; a subsidiary of the Abu Dhabi Investment Authority; Goldman Sachs; and investment funds managed by the private equity firm TDR Capital.Global Mobility Holding is 50 percent owned by the German carmaker Volkswagen and 50 percent owned by the German private banker Friedrich von Metzler. LeasePlan was acquired by a consortium led by Volkswagen in 2004, for about €2 billion.LeasePlan first announced that its owners were in discussions about a sale in March.“The change of ownership announced today marks a new era for our company and will enable LeasePlan to continue our successful journey and focus on executing our long-term strategy and growth ambitions,” Vahid Daemi, the chairman of the managing board and chief executive of LeasePlan, said in a news release. “We remain fully committed to providing high quality and innovative fleet management and driver mobility services to our clients worldwide.”AdvertisementThe transaction is subject to regulatory approval, in part because the company holds a banking license in the Netherlands.Please verify you're not a robot by clicking the box.Invalid email address.
