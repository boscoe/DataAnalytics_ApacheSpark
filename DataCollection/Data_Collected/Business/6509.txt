AdvertisementSupported byBy Chad BrayFitbit, the maker of wearable health-tracking devices, expects to price its initial public offering at $14 a share to $16 a share, according to filing on Tuesday with the Securities and Exchange Commission.The company intends to sell 22.4 million shares and its existing shareholders expect to sell 7.5 million shares, according to the filing. At the midpoint of the price range, Fitbit would be valued at nearly $3.1 billion.Fitbit could raise up to $358.4 million from its sale of shares in the offering, but it will not receive any proceeds from the sale of the 7.5 million shares belonging to existing investors, according to the filing.Fitbit unveiled its plans for an I.P.O. last month.Founded in 2007 and based in San Francisco, Fitbit sells wearable devices that allow consumers to track their physical activity, heart rate and calories burned. Their products range from a simple activity tracker to a more complex sports watch.Through the end of March, the company said it had sold more than 20.8 million devices since its inception eight years ago. The company reported revenue of $745.4 million in 2014.The company is looking to go public while it faces increasing competition in the wearable technology sector from the likes of Apple, Microsoft and Samsung.Jawbone, a privately held competitor, sued Fitbit in California last month, accusing its rival of “systematically plundering” confidential information by hiring Jawbone employees who improperly downloaded sensitive materials shortly before leaving the company.Fitbit will have two classes of stock when it is listed. Class B shares will have 10 times the voting power of the Class A shares that will be sold in the offering. As a result, insiders will effectively maintain control of the company following the I.P.O.Advertisement
