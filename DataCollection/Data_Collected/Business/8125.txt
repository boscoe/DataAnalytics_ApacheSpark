AdvertisementSupported byBusiness BriefingBy The Associated PressLumber Liquidators reported a second-quarter loss as its struggles over imported Chinese-made products persist. The flooring retailer said it was unable to provide an outlook for the full year and its shares tumbled 28 percent Wednesday to a multiyear low.The company suspended the sale of all laminate flooring from China this spring after disclosing that the Justice Department was seeking criminal charges against it. The television newsmagazine “60 Minutes” reported in March that the flooring contained high levels of formaldehyde, a carcinogen.The publicity from the episode was damaging. Shares have fallen 80 percent this year. On Wednesday, Lumber Liquidators, based in Toano, Va., said it lost $20.3 million, or 75 cents a share. That is in contrast to a profit of $16.6 million, or 60 cents a share, a year ago. Advertisement
