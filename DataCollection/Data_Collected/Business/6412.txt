AdvertisementSupported byBy The Associated PressStocks sank Friday after news that the United States economy shrank in the first three months of the year.The revised data showed that gross domestic product contracted 0.7 percent in the first quarter. That was worse than the government’s initial growth estimate of 0.2 percent. The Dow Jones industrial average lost 115.44 points, or 0.6 percent, to close at 18,010.68. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index lost 13.4 points, or 0.6 percent, to 2,107.39. The Nasdaq composite index lost 27.95 points, or 0.6 percent, to 5,070.03. All three indexes ended the week lower, and it was the first weekly loss for the S.&P. 500 after three weeks of gains.Investors had two other disappointing pieces of economic news to contend with: A Chicago manufacturing survey fell to 46.2, well below the 53 that economists were anticipating; and a measure of consumer sentiment fell to a six-month low in May.Along with the discouraging economic data, investors continued to watch developments out of Greece. Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras has said a deal with the country’s creditors could be ready by the weekend, but other key officials seem less confident.“With it being a Friday and the uncertainty around the Greece situation and the disappointing economic data, naturally investors are taking some of their positions off the table,” said JJ Kinahan, a strategist at TDAmeritrade. Christine Lagarde, the head of the International Monetary Fund, said a Greek exit from the euro remained a possibility, and Finance Minister Wolfgang Schäuble of Germany also appeared cautious in comments after a meeting of top finance officials in Berlin. Without a deal to acquire its remaining bailout cash soon, Greece faces the grim possibilities of defaulting on its debt or ditching the euro. Figures from the European Central Bank, which revealed that Greek bank deposits were at their lowest in more than a decade, only added to the gloom.Among individual stocks, shares of Humana rose $36.24, or 20.31 percent, to $214.65 after The Wall Street Journal reported that the health insurance company had hired investment bankers to potentially sell the company.GameStop rose $2.49, or 6.09 percent, to $43.41, after the video game retailer posted results that exceeded analysts’ estimates, helped by the sale of recently released titles.In energy, the price of oil rose nearly 5 percent Friday because of an increase in demand and a surprisingly large decline in the number of oil rigs drilling in the United States. Benchmark crude rose $2.62 to settle at $60.30 a barrel in New York. Oil finished the week up 1 percent. Brent crude, a benchmark for international oil used by many American refineries, rose $2.92 to close at $65.50 in London.In other futures trading on the Nymex, wholesale gasoline rose 10.1 cents to close at $2.086 a gallon; heating oil rose 8.5 cents to close at $1.955 a gallon; and natural gas fell 6.4 cents — its fifth decline in a row — to close at $2.642 per 1,000 cubic feet.United States government bond prices rose slightly. The yield on the 10-year Treasury note fell to 2.12 percent. The euro rose to $1.0984 to the dollar, while the dollar rose to 124.13 yen against the Japanese currency.Precious and industrial metals futures ended mixed. Gold rose $1.30 to $1,189.40 an ounce; silver rose three cents to $16.70 an ounce; and copper fell four cents to $2.73 a pound.Advertisement
