By REUTERSNOV. 27, 2015
Stock indexes ended little changed in light trading on Friday. Consumer stocks fell as investors worried about early reports on the holiday shopping season, and Disney’s subscriber losses weighed on the market.Wall Street closed three hours earlier after the Thanksgiving holiday on Thursday, and many traders took the day off.Trading volume was modest as 2.79 billion shares changed hands compared with the seven billion average for the previous seven sessions.The Walt Disney Company was the biggest drag on the Dow Jones industrial average and the Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index, falling 3 percent to close at $115.11, after saying late on Wednesday that its ESPN sports network lost three million subscribers this year.Advertisement“We’re going to get today over with and hit the ground running next week,” said Brian J. Battle, director of trading at Performance Trust Capital Partners in Chicago.AdvertisementMr. Battle said he expected a busy start to next week as investors prepared for a Dec. 4 report on nonfarm payrolls that may cause volatility before a widely expected decision by the Federal Reserve to raise interest rates at its mid-December meeting.

The Dow fell 14.9 points, or 0.08 percent, to 17,798.49, the S.&P. gained 1.24 points, or 0.06 percent, to 2,090.11 and the Nasdaq composite index added 11.38 points, or 0.22 percent, to 5,127.53.Seven of the 10 major S.&P. sectors rose slightly. The energy index fell 0.7 percent as oil prices fell. Media and retailer stocks led the consumer discretionary sector’s 0.4 percent decline.The top retail percentage decliner was Urban Outfitters with a 2.7 percent drop, followed by a 2.5 percent drop for Gap.
