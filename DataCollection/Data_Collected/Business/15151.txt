JULY 30, 2016
To the Editor:Re “They Promised Us Jet Packs,” by Conor Dougherty (July 24), about Alphabet’s X research lab, formerly called Google X:The tension, and sometimes the synergy, in a corporate research lab between doing world-class, often basic work, evidenced by recognition such as Nobel Prizes, and being a vital element of a company’s ongoing success, is clearly manageable.In contrast to the cases cited, Bell Labs and Xerox PARC, there are the likes of IBM Research and the research labs at Corning, which have been succeeding on both fronts for many decades. Let’s hope that Alphabet finds the magic balance, and contributes mightily to future innovation.Jim McGroddyBriarcliff Manor, N.Y., July 24The writer was vice president for research at IBM from 1989 to 1996.Letters for Sunday Business may be sent to sunbiz@nytimes.com.A version of this article appears in print on July 31, 2016, on Page BU6 of the New York edition with the headline: Letters.
