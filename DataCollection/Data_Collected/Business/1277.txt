By CHAD BRAYNOV. 12, 2015
LONDON — Shares of Amundi, one of the largest asset managers in Europe, rose by about 4 percent in their debut on the Euronext stock exchange in Paris on Thursday after its initial public offering.The French company was created in 2010 by a merger of the asset management operations of two French banks, Crédit Agricole and Société Générale.Société Générale sold a 20 percent stake in the offering, which raised 1.5 billion euros, or about $1.6 billion. The offering was priced at €45 a share on Wednesday.Amundi’s debut was a positive sign for initial public offerings in Europe.Several companies, including the French digital security company Oberthur Technologies and the French music-streaming service Deezer, have delayed their listings in recent weeks as uncertainty in the stock market has weighed on valuations.Advertisement“The success of Amundi’s initial public offering proves the success of its business strategy since it has been created in 2010,” Yves Perrier, the Amundi chief executive, said in a news release. “This profitable growth strategy will be pursued to the benefit of its clients, its employees and its shareholders.”AdvertisementEuronext said the offering, which valued Amundi at around €7.5 billion, was its largest listing in Paris in a decade. Shares of Amundi rose as much as 6 percent in early trading on Thursday.
