By MICHAEL J. de la MERCEDJULY 28, 2015
The American International Group said on Tuesday that it had hired Douglas A. Dachille as its chief investment officer, succeeding a longtime executive in the role.Mr. Dachille will join the insurance giant after it buys First Principles Capital Management, his investment firm with about $10 billion in assets under management, for an undisclosed price.In becoming A.I.G.’s chief investment officer, overseeing the company’s huge swath of holdings, he will succeed William N. Dooley, who is retiring after spending 37 years at the insurer.Before founding First Principles, Mr. Dachille worked at Zurich Capital Markets, and before that at JPMorgan Chase, where he once served as global head of proprietary trading and co-treasurer.“Doug is a leader in financial services and investments and has an extensive track record in all aspects of asset management, structured finance and risk management at global companies,” Peter D. Hancock, A.I.G.’s chief executive, said in a statement. “The First Principles investment team is a uniquely talented group, and their customized asset management services align with A.I.G.’s commitment to providing the highest level of solutions-oriented services to clients.”The acquisition of First Principles is expected to close by Sept. 30.A version of this article appears in print on July 29, 2015, on Page B7 of the New York edition with the headline: New A.I.G.
