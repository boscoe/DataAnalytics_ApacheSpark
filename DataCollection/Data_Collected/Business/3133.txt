AdvertisementSupported byBusiness BriefingBy ReutersAT&T spent close to half the total in the record-setting American auction of airwaves for mobile data. AT&T bid a total of $18.2 billion to win licenses of the airwaves, known as AWS-3 spectrum. Verizon bid $10.4 billion, and T-Mobile bid $1.8 billion, according to the results released by the Federal Communications Commission. The record $44.9 billion auction ended on Thursday. Dish Network did not win any licenses, but was in a joint bidding agreements with SNR Wireless LicenseCo and Northstar Wireless, which had a net bid amount of $10 billion. Advertisement
