By CHAD BRAYAUG. 13, 2015
LONDON — Nomad Holdings Limited said on Thursday that it had agreed to acquire the Continental Europe business of Findus Group, the British frozen food and seafood supplier, for 500 million pounds, or about $780 million.The deal continues a series of acquisitions by Nomad, a so-called special purpose acquisition company, founded by Martin E. Franklin and Noam Gottesman last year to acquire food businesses.In June, Nomad, which is based in the British Virgin Islands, completed its acquisition of Iglo Foods Holdings Limited for 2.6 billion euros, or about $2.9 billion.Iglo, which sells fish sticks and frozen foods under Birds Eye, Iglo and other brands in Europe, already sells some products under the Findus brand in Italy.Advertisement“This transaction is in line with our growth strategy, an exciting addition to the Nomad portfolio, and a significant milestone in reaching our goal of building a global consumer foods company,” Stéfan Descheemaeker, the chief executive of Nomad, said in a news release.AdvertisementNomad first confirmed that it was in discussions to buy the Continental Europe operations of Findus in June.Nomad said it expected the owners of Findus to exercise an option to sell the business in the fourth quarter. The owners include Highbridge Capital and Lion Capital.Nomad would acquire the Findus business in Belgium, Denmark, Finland, France, Norway, Spain and Sweden, including the rights to the Findus, Lutosa and La Cocinera brands in those markets.Those operations employ about 1,500 people at six manufacturing facilities in France, Norway, Spain and Sweden and have annual revenue of about €600 million.The transaction would not include Young’s Seafood in Britain.Nomad said that it planned to finance the deal through cash and debt. Nomad would have to pay the Findus owners up to £34.2 million if the deal does not go through.Pershing Square Capital Management, the New York hedge fund run by the billionaire investor William A.
