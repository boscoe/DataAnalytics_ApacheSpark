By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 11, 2015
L. L. Bean, known for its century-old catalog, plans to make a bigger push into bricks-and-mortar retail by more than tripling its number of stores over the next five years, officials said on Wednesday. The company, which is coming off five years of increasing revenue, will open four locations this year before accelerating growth with a goal of “at least 100” by 2020, the chief executive, Christopher J.
