By REUTERSMARCH 16, 2015
Describing the eurozone economy as “steadily recovering,” Mario Draghi, the president of the European Central Bank, called on Monday for a “quantum leap” in European integration so countries share more sovereignty. A week after the central bank began printing money to buy sovereign bonds, Mr. Draghi said the bank’s stimulus, lower oil prices and structural reforms in eurozone economies were helping support growth in the 19-country currency bloc. “Most indicators suggest a sustained recovery is taking hold,” he said in a speech for delivery at a finance conference in Frankfurt. Earlier Monday, the central bank said it settled 9.751 billion euros ($10.3 billion) of public-sector bond purchases in the first week of its bond-buying program to pump more than €1 trillion into the eurozone economy.
