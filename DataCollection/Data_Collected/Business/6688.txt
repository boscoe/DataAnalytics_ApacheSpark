By MICHAEL J. de la MERCEDJUNE 9, 2015
Shares of Etsy on Tuesday received a healthy boost from a newly disclosed investment by Tiger Global Management — but it was barely enough to bring the stock back to its initial public offering levels.Etsy’s shares were up roughly 8 percent after Tiger Global, a prominent backer of Internet technology companies, disclosed that it had raised its stake in the online bazaar to 8.9 percent as of late May.That put the stock at about $16.10 as of midafternoon, just above the company’s initial public offering price of $16. Yet it was still far below the $31 at which Etsy opened for trading in mid-April.The company’s stock has tumbled since then, spurred on by a bigger-than-expected quarterly loss last month. The shares finally fell below the initial offering price last week.It is unclear why the stock rose on a relatively modest move by Tiger Global.
