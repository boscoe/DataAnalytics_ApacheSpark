AdvertisementSupported byBy Liz MoyerThe holding company of the family of the French billionaire Marc Ladreit de Lacharrière has acquired a 5 percent passive stake in Warburg Pincus, where Mr. Ladreit de Lacharrière will act as a senior strategic partner, the private equity firm said on Wednesday.Warburg Pincus, with $35 billion of assets under management, is based and New York and run by Charles R. Kaye and Joseph P. Landy as co-chief executives. Timothy F. Geithner, the former Treasury secretary, joined the firm as president last year.Mr. Ladreit de Lacharrière is the chairman and chief executive of his holding company, Fimalac, which is based in Paris and holds a 20 percent stake in the American ratings firm the Fitch Group, as well as other interests in the leisure and luxury hotel markets, according to its website. His family holding company is Groupe Marc de Lacharrière.Warburg said in a statement that Mr. Ladreit de Lacharrière would invest in future funds as a limited partner. The proceeds from the investment will be used to invest in Warburg’s funds and “create additional financial flexibility” for the firm, Warburg said.A Warburg spokesman would not comment on the dollar amount of the investment.“We are very pleased to have the opportunity to invest in Warburg Pincus,” Mr. Ladreit de Lacharrière said in the statement.A person briefed on the matter who spoke on condition of anonymity said Mr. Ladreit de Lacharrière had been looking for a long-term investment in a private equity firm and approached Warburg about the transaction.Advertisement
