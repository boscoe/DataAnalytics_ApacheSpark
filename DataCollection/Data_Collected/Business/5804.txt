By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMAY 5, 2015
A combination of concerns knocked the stock market lower on Tuesday, ending a two-day run.Crude oil climbed above $60 a barrel for the first time this year, raising expectations that inflation will go up and take interest rates along with it. Greece’s government remained in a standoff with its European creditors.“There are some jitters,” said Bill Stone, chief investment strategist at PNC Asset Management Group. “Greece is definitely part of it. The other part is oil prices going up. That implies more inflation.”Major indexes wavered at the outset of trading, drifted lower throughout the morning, then spent the afternoon slowly ceding ground.The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index lost 25.03 points, or 1.2 percent, to 2,089.46. The Dow Jones industrial average dropped 142.2 points, or 0.8 percent, to 17,928.20. The Nasdaq composite index fell 77.6 points, or 1.6 percent, to 4,939.33.AdvertisementThe price of oil jumped $1.47 to close at $60.40 a barrel, the first time crude has traded that high since early December, after reports that a Libyan oil terminal had closed. Brent crude rose $1.07, to $67.52, in London.AdvertisementTalks between Greece and its lenders reached an impasse, raising concerns about the country’s ability to handle a coming debt payment. Greece will have to scrounge for cash to make a payment of 750 million euros ($838 million) to the International Monetary Fund on May 12.Among companies turning in results on Tuesday, Kellogg, the maker of Frosted Flakes and Pop-Tarts, reported that its first-quarter earnings fell 44 percent as a rising United States dollar offset sales. The company fell 95 cents, or 1 percent, to $63.18.Walt Disney’s stock hit a record high after it delivered quarterly results that beat Wall Street’s estimates, thanks, in part, to rising revenue from its Walt Disney World Resort and other theme parks. France’s CAC-40 sank 2.1 percent, while Germany’s DAX fell 2.5 percent. Britain’s FTSE 100 slipped 0.8 percent. European government bond prices also fell, shooting government borrowing costs up.The Shanghai composite index in mainland China had its worst day in months, losing 4.1 percent, in turn dragging down Hong Kong’s Hang Seng 1.3 percent.
