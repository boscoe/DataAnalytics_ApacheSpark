By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSOCT. 27, 2015
Stocks slipped on Tuesday after several American companies delivered disappointing results and forecasts. Energy companies fell more than the rest of the market as the price of crude oil hit two-month lows.The market made only small moves as the Federal Reserve opened a two-day meeting to discuss interest rates, marking a pause after a rally this month that has wiped out much of a steep loss from August.Investors, still concerned about the slow pace of the economic recovery in the United States, were wondering how the economy and the market will react when interest rates eventually start to rise.Few now expect the Fed to raise interest rates this year. But while very low rates tend to be favorable for stocks and bonds, many investors would like to see the uncertainty over interest rate policy resolved.Advertisement“We don’t think there’s going to be enough evidence between now and the end of the year to support a move by the Fed,” said Patrick Maldari, a senior fixed income investment specialist at Aberdeen Asset Management. “They just have not had the ammunition. The economic data continues to be weak.”AdvertisementThe Dow Jones industrial average fell 41.62 points, or 0.2 percent, to 17,581.43. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index lost 5.29 points, or 0.3 percent, to 2,065.89. The Nasdaq fell 4.56 points, or 0.1 percent, to 5,030.15.The price of United States oil fell almost 2 percent and reached its lowest level since late August’s market turmoil. Oil prices had recovered some of their losses from the summer as companies cut back on production.Jim Ritterbusch, an energy analyst, said a recent increase in oil stockpiles, the result of decreased refining production, sent oil prices back down. He said he thought crude would slip back to its August low of around $38 a barrel and stay there through the end of 2015.Energy stocks declined along with the price of oil. Chesapeake Energy shares fell 41 cents, or 5.8 percent, to $6.72, while stock in Marathon Oil was down 48 cents, or 2.7 percent, to $17.11, and Anadarko Petroleum shares lost $3.71, or 5.4 percent, to $65.29.

Health care was the only sector to rise after some of the world’s largest drug companies announced strong results. Pfizer raised its profit forecast after its third-quarter results came in better than expected, while Bristol-Myers said sales of new medicines like the cancer drugs Opdivo and Yervoy improved. Pfizer stock rose 83 cents, or 2.4 percent, to $34.99, and Bristol-Myers shares added $2.25, or 3.5 percent, to $66.80.Airline stocks fell after Spirit and JetBlue fanned investor fears about lower fares. Spirit said that revenue from the average ticket tumbled by one-fifth in the third quarter compared with a year ago and that pricing was “volatile.” Its shares were down more than 9 percent. JetBlue said its passenger-carrying capacity would grow slightly faster than previously expected for 2015. Its shares fell more than 4 percent.Among other stocks making big moves, Ford reported improved sales of its new F-150 pickup truck, but its net income fell short of Wall Street estimates.
