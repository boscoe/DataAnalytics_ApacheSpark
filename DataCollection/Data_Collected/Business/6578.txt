By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJUNE 4, 2015
Uncertainty surrounding Greece’s debts helped knock stocks in the United States lower Thursday, and put major indexes on track for a weekly loss. Oil and gas stocks had some of the biggest losses as the price of crude sank 3 percent.Major indexes headed lower at the outset of trading, briefly returned to the starting line, then lost ground throughout the afternoon.Hank Smith, chief investment officer at Haverford Trust, said he thought Greece and its creditors would eventually reach an agreement that allowed the country to receive new loans. But just the possibility that Greece could default, and become the first country to drop the euro currency, makes investors nervous.“It creates a tremendous amount of anxiety because we don’t have a playbook,” he said. “It just hasn’t happened before.”
