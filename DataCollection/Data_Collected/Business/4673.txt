By REUTERSMARCH 25, 2015
WASHINGTON —  The Justice Department said on Wednesday that a subsidiary of Schlumberger Oilfield Holdings had pleaded guilty to violating U.S. sanctions related to Iran and Sudan and would pay a $237.2 million fine.The company also agreed to a three-year period of corporate probation, during which it will cease all operations in Iran and Syria and hire an independent consultant to review its policies on complying with sanctions.“For years, in a variety of ways, this foreign company facilitated trade with Iran and Sudan from Sugar Land, Texas,” Eric Hirschhorn, a Justice Department undersecretary, said in a statement. “Today’s announcement should send a clear message to all global companies with a U.S. presence.”In a statement, Schlumberger said it voluntarily ceased oil field operation in Iran as of the second quarter of 2013 and said it has ceased oil field operations in Sudan as of the plea agreement. “We are satisfied that this matter is finally resolved,” it said.Schlumberger has principal offices in Paris, Houston, London and The Hague.A version of this article appears in print on March 26, 2015, on Page B2 of the New York edition with the headline: Business Briefing; Unit of Schlumberger Fined for Violating U.S. Sanctions.
