AdvertisementSupported byBy The Associated PressThe United States stock market rebounded on Thursday after a two-day slump, pushing the Dow Jones industrial average up more than 300 points as it notched its biggest gain in over two weeks.The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index is now nearly back to break-even for the year after steep declines in August and September. Industrials stocks were among the index’s biggest gainers.Encouraging earnings from McDonald’s, eBay and other companies helped fuel the rally. Alphabet, Microsoft and Amazon also delivered better-than-expected results shortly after the close of regular trading.News that the European Central Bank could consider expanding its stimulus program in December also spurred investors. Such a move could help stimulate spending in the region, a plus for American companies struggling with declining overseas revenue, said Bob Doll, chief equity strategist at Nuveen Asset Management.“We’ve had some pretty good earnings in a season that so far has been mixed,” Mr. Doll said. “Then you layer some chatter out from the E.C.B., and all the uncertainty and skepticism and negativism, and the mass amount of cash on the sidelines, and it doesn’t take much to get a rally going.”The Dow climbed 320.55 points, or 1.9 percent, to 17,489.16. The S.&P. 500 rose 33.57 points, or 1.7 percent, to 2,052.51. The Nasdaq added 79.93 points, or 1.7 percent, to 4,920.05. A surge in European stocks set the stage for the three major American stock indexes to rise early on Thursday.Mario Draghi, head of the European Central Bank, signaled that the bank could increase monetary stimulus at its meeting in December. That raised expectations that the central bank might extend its $1.2 trillion bond purchase program. Mr. Draghi added that policy makers were considering other measures, such as further cutting one of the bank’s key interest rates.Beyond Europe, investors pored over the latest company earnings, which helped put them in a buying mood.Traders bid up eBay, which reported earnings late Wednesday that substantially topped forecasts. The company’s shares jumped $3.37, or 13.9 percent, to $27.58. Stock in McDonald’s climbed 8.1 percent after the company handily beat Wall Street estimates and said its sales increased in the third quarter. McDonald’s shares added $8.32 to $110.86. Texas Instruments beat earnings projections and at the same time gave an upbeat outlook for the current quarter. The company’s stock jumped $6.19, or 11.9 percent, to $58.09. Some companies turned in disappointing results, sending their shares into a slide. Shares in the PulteGroup, the home builder, fell 6.6 percent, to $18.16. American Express shares slid 5.2 percent, to $72.46, a day after the company reported a 16 percent drop in profits and cut its full-year forecast. American companies have been struggling to drum up sales overseas as the dollar has strengthened, which makes their products less competitive, and as global demand has weakened.All told, nearly 150 companies in the S.&P. 500 index have reported third-quarter earnings so far. Some 68 percent of those have reported results that beat Wall Street’s expectations. That’s better than the historical average of 66 percent, according to S&P Capital IQ.Nine of the 10 sectors in the S.&P. 500 index rose, led by industrials, up 2.8 percent. The sector is down 2.7 percent this year. Health care stocks fell 0.5 percent. The sector, which was among the biggest risers in the index for much of the year, is down 0.9 percent.In energy futures trading, benchmark United States crude oil rose 18 cents to $45.38 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange. Brent crude, used to price international oils, added 23 cents, to $48.08 a barrel in London.Wholesale gasoline rose 2.6 cents, to $1.307 a gallon on the New York Mercantile Exchange, while heating oil rose 1.5 cents, to $1.465 a gallon. Natural gas fell 1.8 cents, to $2.386 per 1,000 cubic feet.Precious and industrial metals futures were mixed. Gold fell $1, to $1,166.10 an ounce; silver rose 13 cents, to $15.84 an ounce; and copper gained 2 cents, to $2.38 a pound.United States government bond prices rose. The yield on the 10-year Treasury note fell to 2.02 percent, from 2.03 percent the day before. The euro fell to $1.1109, and the dollar rose to 120.72 yen.Advertisement
