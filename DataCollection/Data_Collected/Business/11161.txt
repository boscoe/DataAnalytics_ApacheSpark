By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJAN. 14, 2016
Energy stocks led a broad rally in United States shares on Thursday, giving the market its biggest gain in over a month.A recovery in crude oil prices helped stocks rebound a day after the market had its worst drop since September. Investors also welcomed some encouraging company earnings.“That all led to a little bit of confidence in the markets and some buyers coming in,” said Sean Lynch, co-head of global equity for Wells Fargo Investment Institute.

The Dow Jones industrial average rose 227.64 points, or 1.4 percent, to 16,379.05. The Dow had risen as much as 330 points. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index gained 31.56 points, or 1.7 percent, to close at 1,921.84. The Nasdaq composite added 88.94 points, or 2 percent, to close at 4,615. It was the best gain for each index since Dec. 4.AdvertisementEven with the big rebound, the three major United States stock indexes remain down for the year. The Dow and S.&P. 500 are both off about 6 percent, while the Nasdaq is down nearly 8 percent. The first eight trading days of 2016 represent the worst start to a year in the history of both the S.&P. 500 and the Dow.AdvertisementThat slump worsened on Wednesday, pushing the S.&P. 500 into what is known as a correction, or a drop of 10 percent or more from a peak. On Thursday, after wavering in the first hour of trading, the market shifted higher and remained on an upward track the rest of the day.

Investors welcomed a pickup in the price of crude oil, which the day before had briefly fallen below $30 a barrel for the first time since late 2003. It ended up rising 72 cents, or 2.3 percent, to settle at $32.11 a barrel in New York. Brent crude, a benchmark for international oils, also gained 72 cents, or 2.4 percent, to $31.03 a barrel in London.The rise in crude oil led traders to pile into several big-name energy companies. Exxon Mobil shares added $3.47, or 4.6 percent, closing at $79.12, while Chevron stock rose $4.14, or 5.1 percent, to $85.47.“The markets in general needed a little dose of confidence and they got it through a firming of oil prices,” Mr. Lynch said.

The energy company Williams vaulted 34.4 percent, to lead the gainers in the S.&P. 500. The stock, which fell sharply a day earlier, rose $4.68 to $18.29. It is still down 29 percent for the year.The S.&P. 500’s energy stocks jumped 4.5 percent. The sector is down 6.1 percent for the year.The start of the latest corporate earnings season also helped lift the market Thursday.JPMorgan Chase shares rose 1.5 percent after the bank’s earnings topped forecasts. The stock added 86 cents to close at $58.20.

Some companies provided less encouraging updates.Shares of Best Buy, the electronics store operator, slid 9.7 percent after the company reported a sales drop during the holiday season. The company also said it expected a wider drop in fourth-quarter revenue, partly on weak mobile phone and personal device sales. The stock was the S.&P. 500’s biggest decliner.
