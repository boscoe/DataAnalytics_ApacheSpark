By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSOCT. 20, 2016
A day of mostly listless trading on Wall Street ended on Thursday with stocks giving back modest gains from the day before.Telecom sector stocks declined the most, weighed down by a slide in shares of Verizon, Sprint and other phone companies. Only health care stocks eked out a gain. The broad slide snapped a two-day winning streak for the market. Energy futures and precious metals also closed lower.The Dow Jones industrial average slipped 40.27 points, or 0.2 percent, to 18,162.35. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index lost 2.95 points, or 0.1 percent, to 2,141.34. The Nasdaq composite index slid 4.58 points, or 0.1 percent, to 5,241.83.Investors mostly waded through another round of earnings reports, looking to glean insights into the health of corporate America and the United States economy.AdvertisementOn Thursday, investors bid up shares in American Express, which reported better-than-anticipated earnings late Wednesday and also raised its annual outlook. The stock was the biggest gainer in the S.&P. 500, climbing $5.53, or 9 percent, to $66.78.

The toy maker Mattel also rose a day after it reported strong earnings. The stock rose $1.84, or 6 percent, to $32.46.AdvertisementSnap-on, a tool and diagnostic equipment maker, climbed 6.7 percent after it posted a larger profit than analysts had forecast. Its shares rose $9.95, to $159.06.Shares of Time Warner spiked 9 percent in afternoon trading after Bloomberg News reported the company had held merger discussions with AT&T. Time Warner shares later closed 4.7 percent higher, at $82.99. AT&T shares fell nearly 2 percent.Several other companies posted results that failed to impress investors.EBay slumped 10.8 percent after the e-commerce giant reported disappointing third-quarter results. The stock was the biggest decliner in the S.&P. 500, sliding $3.50, or 10.8 percent, to $29.02.Union Pacific fell 6.7 percent, after it said weak demand for consumer goods had lowered its freight and coal shipments volume. The railroad operator lost $6.48, to $90.64.Verizon fell 2.5 percent after the company posted weak quarterly revenue; it added far fewer wireless and internet service subscribers than a year ago. Shares in the company, which is in the process of a potential acquisition of Yahoo’s digital operations, shed $1.24 to close at $49.14.
