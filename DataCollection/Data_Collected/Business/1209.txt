By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSNOV. 10, 2015
Stocks reversed course and moved mostly higher on Tuesday, ending a four-day losing streak.The gainers included retailers and media companies. Strong quarterly results from D. R. Horton lifted shares of homebuilders.Health care stocks including drug companies also made gains, while Apple dragged down the technology sector. Analysts at Credit Suisse said a slowdown in orders for components suggested demand for the new iPhone 6 was weak.The Dow Jones industrial average rose 27.73 points, or 0.2 percent, to 17,758.21. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index added 3.14 points, or 0.2 percent, to 2,081.72. The tech-heavy Nasdaq gave up 12.06 points, or 0.2 percent, to 5,083.24.Stocks spent most of Tuesday in the red, then turned positive in the last hour and a half of trading. The S.&P. 500 had fallen 1.5 percent over the previous four trading days. Most of that loss came on Monday, when the index took its biggest dive in six weeks.AdvertisementThe Botox maker Allergan was one of the biggest gainers in health care. Shares in the company, which has held talks with its competitor Pfizer about a potential sale, rose $9.88, or 3.3 percent, to $306.70. The drug makers Perrigo, Endo and Mallinckrodt all traded higher.

Apple shares lost $3.80, or 3.2 percent, to $116.77. Apple, the world’s most valuable company, reported record earnings just two weeks ago, lifted by surging sales in China. Apple counts more than two-thirds of its revenue from iPhone sales, and some investors are worried that Apple will not be able to maintain its growth as worldwide smartphone sales slow.AdvertisementApple’s loss weighed down tech stocks. The tech sector has had a strong year, with its stocks rising more than 6 percent, the second-best industry gain in the S.&P. 500.While tech and health care stocks have performed well this year, some companies could have a rougher road ahead. With the Federal Reserve poised to raise interest rates, the dollar could grow stronger. That would be a source of pain for semiconductor makers and drug companies, said Jack Ablin of BMO Private Bank.“The types of companies that could get hurt are the ones that rely on exports for their growth and profits,” he said.Chipotle Mexican Grill’s restaurants in Washington State and Oregon could reopen in a few days. A total of 43 locations were closed because of an E. coli outbreak, and tests for the bacteria at Chipotle restaurants came back negative. Shares of Chipotle rose $19.59, or 3.2 percent, to $628.88.
