By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSSEPT. 21, 2015
Stocks rose broadly on Monday, recouping some losses from a sell-off last week as investors tried to look beyond uncertainty about interest rates.The major American indexes dipped into losses in the afternoon on sharp declines for drug companies. The drop was set off by a Twitter post from the Democratic presidential front-runner, Hillary Rodham Clinton, about plans to unveil policy proposals that would stop “price gouging” in the industry. But rising technology and banking shares helped lift the market by the end of the day.Jack Ablin, chief investment officer at BMO Private Bank, said investors had been rewarded for buying after sell-offs in the current bull market that has run for more than six years, and so they were inclined to buy again.“Investors who have been buying the dips in recent years have benefited,” Mr. Ablin said. “So, perhaps they’ll use that strategy until it doesn’t work anymore.”AdvertisementThe Dow Jones industrial average closed up 125.61 points, or 0.8 percent, to 16,510.19. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index rose 8.94 points, or 0.5 percent, to 1,966.97. Nine of the 10 industry sectors in the index rose. The Nasdaq composite index gained 1.73 points, or 0.04 percent, to 4,828.96.

The broad slump last week was ignited by a decision by the Federal Reserve not to raise interest rates. Low borrowing rates have helped stock prices triple since 2009, and typically a decision to keep them low would encourage investors to buy shares.AdvertisementBut the central bank cited concerns over a global economic slowdown, and that alarmed investors already on edge after weeks of second-guessing growth in China and the impact of struggling emerging markets with plunging currencies.Financial stocks rose Monday after comments over the weekend by Federal Reserve officials suggested that some still see a rate increase as likely this year. Dennis Lockhart, the president of the Federal Reserve Bank of Atlanta, said on Monday that he was “confident” of a rate increase this year.Citigroup shares rose 42 cents, or 0.8 percent, to $50.71. Stocks of financial companies dropped sharply on Friday over fears that lenders would have to delay increasing borrowing rates on their customers because the Fed had decided against raising rates now.Biogen, a maker of specialty drugs, was among the big losers after Mrs. Clinton’s comments on drug companies. The stock dropped $17.51, or 5.6 percent, to $297.16. Dow members Pfizer, Merck and Johnson & Johnson also fell.Atmel, a California-based semiconductor company, was one of the day’s big winners. Its stock surged after the company accepted a purchase offer worth about $4.6 billion in cash and stock from Britain’s Dialog Semiconductor. Atmel provides electronics products used in the industrial, automotive, consumer, communications, and computing markets. Its stock jumped 92 cents, or 13 percent, to $8.19.

In Europe, stocks stabilized from losses on Friday. Britain’s FTSE 100 index rose 0.1 percent while the CAC-40 in France climbed 1.1 percent. Germany’s DAX rose 0.3 percent.AdvertisementThe price of oil rose on expectations that production in the United States would continue to slip, helping to eventually ease the supply glut. Investors are also betting that a higher price for wholesale gasoline could spur refiners to process more crude.United States crude rose $2 to settle at $46.96 a barrel in New York.
