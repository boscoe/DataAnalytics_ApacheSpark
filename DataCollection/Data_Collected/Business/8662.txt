By BRETT COLESEPT. 1, 2015
SYDNEY, Australia — GE Capital has three final participants in the final round of an auction of its Australian commercial lending and leasing businesses, said a person with direct knowledge of the matter.General Electric, which for years has been moving away from banking as it has become less profitable and riskier, announced plans in the spring to shed GE Capital and sell off most of that division over the next two years.GE Capital was said to be having trouble finding buyers for its Australian units. In the next five to six weeks, though, this person said, final bids are expected for businesses in private aircraft financing, equipment rental, lending for working capital and leveraged loans.This person, who spoke only on the condition of anonymity and was not authorized to speak publicly because the sale process was private, said the final participants were the American private investment funds Sankaty Advisors and Lone Star and HNA Group of China.AdvertisementGE Capital’s Australian businesses have a book value of about $1.8 billion.
