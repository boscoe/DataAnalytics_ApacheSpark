By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 27, 2015
A tough week on the stock market ended quietly on Friday.Major indexes notched modest gains, but not nearly enough to make up for the four previous days of losses. And it wound up being the second-worst week for the market so far this year.The Dow Jones industrial average remains down slightly for 2015, and the Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index is essentially flat.There was no one major catalyst to move the market one way or another Friday. Biotechnology stocks, battered over the last week, were among the top gainers, while energy stocks lagged as the price of oil fell.The Dow Jones industrial average rose 34.43 points, or 0.2 percent, to 17,712.66. The S.&P. 500 rose 4.87 points, or 0.2 percent, to 2,061.02, and the Nasdaq rose 27.86 points, or 0.6 percent, to 4,891.22.AdvertisementStocks fell most of the week because of a combination of weaker-than-expected economic data and concerns that the rapid rise of the dollar might crimp corporate earnings in the United States. Companies start releasing their first-quarter results next month.AdvertisementThe biggest sell-off came on Wednesday, when a report showed orders at American factories for long-lasting manufactured goods fell in February, the latest disappointing data suggesting the United States economy has hit a soft patch. The Dow plunged nearly 300 points that day.
