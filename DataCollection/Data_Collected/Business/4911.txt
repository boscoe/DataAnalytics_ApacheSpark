By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSAPRIL 3, 2015
Cyprus’s last remaining restrictions on transferring money abroad will be gone next week, the country’s president said on Friday. President Nicos Anastasiades said the lifting of the restrictions on Monday would represent the full restoration of confidence in the country’s banking sector. Cyprus, one of the 19 countries that uses the euro, introduced the limits to prevent a run in March 2013 when it received a multibillion-euro international rescue in a bid to halt a slide to bankruptcy.
