By MICHAEL CIEPLYJULY 2, 2015
The Comic-Con International fan convention will remain in San Diego through at least 2018 under a deal reached with the event’s operators, the city’s mayor announced on Thursday.“Comic-Con is here to stay in San Diego for another two years,” Mayor Kevin L. Faulconer said in announcing an agreement that had been under negotiation for months.The 46th annual convention is set to begin with a preview night on Wednesday at the San Diego Convention Center. But an existing deal for use of the site was set to expire next year, and negotiators have been wrestling with capacity constraint, convention costs and rising hotel rates.In a joint statement on Thursday, neither the mayor nor representatives of the San Diego Comic Convention, which operates Comic-Con International, addressed details of the new agreement, though David Glanzer, Comic-Con’s marketing and communications director, said “hoteliers and the tourism industry” joined in reaching the deal.A version of this article appears in print on July 3, 2015, on Page B2 of the New York edition with the headline: BUSINESS BRIEFING; Comic-Con Convention Renews San Diego Deal.
