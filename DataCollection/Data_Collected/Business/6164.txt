By REUTERSMAY 19, 2015
Shareholders of JPMorgan Chase narrowly approved on Tuesday the 2014 compensation packages for the chief executive, Jamie Dimon, and other top executives in a rebuke from investors over the bank’s pay practices. Only 61 percent of votes cast at a shareholders meeting were in favor of the compensation, which came under criticism by the influential proxy adviser Institutional Shareholder Services. It said the board did not sufficiently link pay to performance and shareholder interests. Mr. Dimon, 59, was paid $20 million for 2014, the same as for 2013. But 40 percent of his latest incentive bonus was in cash.
