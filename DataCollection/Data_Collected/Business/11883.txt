By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSFEB. 16, 2016
Britain’s annual rate of consumer price inflation rose to 0.3 percent in January from 0.2 percent in December — the highest level in a year, according to the Office of National Statistics. But inflation is well short of the Bank of England’s 2 percent target, which is keeping pressure off policy makers in little hurry to raise interest rates from a record low 0.5 percent.A version of this article appears in print on February 17, 2016, on Page B2 of the New York edition with the headline: British Consumer Inflation Inched Up to 0.3% in January.
