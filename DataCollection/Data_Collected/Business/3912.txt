By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSFEB. 25, 2015
The Dow Jones industrial average notched its third nominal high close in a row Wednesday, even as other market indexes ended lower.Trading was relatively subdued as investors reviewed the latest corporate earnings news. Stocks of utilities were among the biggest decliners. Energy stocks rebounded as oil prices ended a five-day slide and climbed back above $50 a barrel.Wall Street also kept an eye on the second day of congressional testimony from the chairwoman of the Federal Reserve, Janet L. Yellen, though her remarks did not generate any major market-moving news. On Tuesday, Ms. Yellen suggested that the Fed was not in a hurry to raise interest rates.“The market is just trying to figure out whether the next move is up or down,” said David M. Lebovitz, a global market strategist at J. P. Morgan Asset Management.AdvertisementThe Dow ended up 15.38 points, or 0.1 percent, at 18,224.57. McDonald’s was the biggest gainer in the 30-company index, climbing 3.9 percent.
