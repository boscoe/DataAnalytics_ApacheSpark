By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSOCT. 19, 2016
Oil and gas exploration companies led Wall Street stocks modestly higher on Wednesday, giving the market its second gain in two days.Energy stocks, already the best-performing category this year, got help from the price of United States benchmark crude oil, which climbed to $51.60 a barrel, the highest level in 15 months. Consumer products makers were the biggest laggards.The Dow Jones industrial average gained 40.68 points, or 0.2 percent, to 18,202.62. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index rose 4.69 points, or 0.2 percent, to 2,144.29. The Nasdaq composite index added 2.58 points, or 0.1 percent, to 5,246.41.Investors brushed off data showing that residential construction slowed last month. Instead, the focus remained on the latest companies reporting quarterly results.AdvertisementOverall earnings for companies in the S.&P. 500 have been down on an annual basis the last five quarters. But the results suggest the start of a turnaround, said Paul Christopher, head global market strategist for Wells Fargo Investment Institute.Advertisement“We do think the earnings recession is ending,” Mr. Christopher said. “We’re still early in the reporting season, but we see, so far, the trends that we’re looking for.”Morgan Stanley, the investment bank, rose 1.9 percent after announcing that its earnings soared 62 percent in the third quarter because of big gains in bond trading. Goldman Sachs disclosed similar results on Tuesday. Morgan Stanley added 61 cents, to $32.93.Quarterly results from other companies did not impress traders.Intel, which issued a downbeat earnings outlook late Wednesday, slumped $2.24, or 5.9 percent, to $35.51.

Manhattan Associates, a business software company, tumbled 10.4 percent after reporting weak quarterly sales and cutting its revenue outlook. The stock fell $6.16, to $52.85.Cree, a lighting maker, also reported weak sales and an earnings outlook that fell short of Wall Street’s expectations. The stock tumbled $2.79, or 11.1 percent, to $22.41.About 80 of the companies in the S.&P. 500 were scheduled to report quarterly results this week.A report of a drawdown in oil inventories helped lift crude prices. United States benchmark crude oil gained $1.31, or 2.6 percent, to close at $51.60 a barrel in New York.
