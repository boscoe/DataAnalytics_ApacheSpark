By CHAD BRAYMAY 18, 2015
LONDON — The suitors are lining up for the online gambling company Bwin.Party Digital Entertainment.On Monday, 888 Holdings became the latest online gambling company to confirm that it had made an offer for Bwin.Party.GVC Holdings, based on the Isle of Man, unveiled its own takeover approach for Bwin.Party on Friday. Bwin.Party had announced in November that it had entered “preliminary discussions with a number of interested parties regarding a variety of potential business combinations.”The Gibraltar-based 888 Holdings said in a news release on Monday that its board of directors believed there was “significant industrial logic” in combining with Bwin.Party and that it had submitted a cash-and-share offer for the company.AdvertisementDetails of the 888 Holdings offer were not released, but the company said that shareholders representing about 59 percent of company stock had committed to vote in favor of the proposed transaction.AdvertisementShares of Bwin.Party rose 6.9 percent to 106 pence, or about $1.67, in early trading in London on Monday.
