By REUTERSAPRIL 6, 2015
Less than a week after McDonald’s said it planned to raise wages for some United States workers, the chief executive of Domino’s Pizza, Patrick Doyle, told CNBC that it would need to raise its base pay to get the “right people” to stay competitive. Labor groups and allies have been calling for a “living wage” at retailers and fast-food companies across the country as companies face increased competition for labor. The higher wages would only affect restaurants owned by the pizza chain, about 376 as of Sept. 7.
