AdvertisementSupported byBy The Associated PressIt’s an adage that investors hate uncertainty. Unfortunately for them, the stock market on Friday harbored more of the same volatility.American markets have been erratic for weeks because of concerns that China’s economy is slowing more rapidly than previously thought, but investors have also had to contend with uncertainty about the outlook for interest rates in the United States.Investors had been hoping that the government’s August jobs report would give them more clarity before a pivotal Federal Reserve Board meeting later this month. However, a mixed report left many guessing as to whether policy makers would feel confident enough about the strength of the United States economy to raise interest rates from record lows.The report showed that the American unemployment rate fell to a seven-year low in August, but also that employers added fewer jobs than expected.“It’s interesting and disappointing that today’s data didn’t provide us with that ‘ah-ha!’ clarity that everyone is seeking,” said Michael Arone, chief investment strategist at State Street Global Advisors.The Dow Jones industrial average fell 272.38 points, or 1.7 percent, to close at 16,102.38. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index lost 29.91 points, or 1.5 percent, to 1,921.22. The Nasdaq slipped 49.58 points, or 1.1 percent, to 4,683.92. Fed policy makers have kept their benchmark interest rate close to zero since late 2008 to help revive the economy. Those low rates have also been good for the market, supporting a bull run that has lasted over six years.On Friday, the S.&P. 500 ended the week down 3.4 percent, its second-worst weekly drop of the year. The index was down nearly 10 percent from a peak of 2,130.82 that was reached May 21.Trading volume was lighter than usual ahead of the Labor Day holiday. American markets will be closed on Monday in observance of the holiday; however, the Chinese stock market, which was closed for a two-day holiday, will reopen.Among individual stocks, Netflix continued its slide on Friday. The company’s stock had slumped for six straight days and closed the week down 16 percent on speculation that competition from rivals, including Amazon and Hulu, was intensifying. Variety also reported on Monday that Apple was exploring a move into original programming.Bond prices edged up after the jobs report, reducing the yield on the benchmark 10-year Treasury note to 2.13 percent from 2.16 percent on Thursday.In Europe, the FTSE 100 index of leading British shares was down 2.4 percent. The DAX in Germany fell 2.7 percent. The CAC-40 in France was 2.8 percent lower.The euro edged up to $1.12. The dollar fell 1 percent against the Japanese currency, to 119 yen.In metals trading, the price of gold fell $3.10 to settle at $1,121.60 an ounce. Silver fell 16 cents to $14.54 an ounce. Copper declined seven cents to $2.32 a pound.The price of oil fell along with stocks, but pared its losses after a closely watched count of active drilling rigs in the United States fell. Crude declined 70 cents to settle at $46.05 a barrel in New York. Brent crude, a benchmark for international oils used by many United States refineries, fell $1.07 to $49.61 a barrel in London.In other futures trading on the Nymex, wholesale gasoline fell 1.9 cents to settle at $1.42 a gallon; heating oil fell 2.3 cent to settle at $1.60 a gallon; and natural gas fell 7 cents to settle at $2.66 per 1,000 cubic feet.Advertisement
