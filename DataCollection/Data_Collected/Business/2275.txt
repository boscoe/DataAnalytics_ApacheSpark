By REUTERSJAN. 1, 2015
South Korean exports grew more than expected in December, but protracted weakness in Europe and China is clouding the prospects for 2015. Shipments in December grew 3.7 percent over a year earlier, the strongest growth since September, the trade ministry said on Thursday. South Korea, the world’s seventh-largest exporter, is the first major exporting economy in the world to report trade figures every month, thus providing a quick indication of the state of global trade. The trade ministry cautioned that exports in 2015 would post growth of only 3.7 percent after a 2.4 percent gain in 2014.
