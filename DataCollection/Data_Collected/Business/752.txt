By LESLIE PICKEROCT. 26, 2015
Duke Energy, which provides electric power for 7.3 million customers in the Southeast and Midwest, agreed to acquire Piedmont Natural Gas for $4.9 billion.Piedmont shareholders will receive $60 in cash a share, representing a 40 percent premium to the closing price on Oct. 23, according to a statement Monday. Duke Energy will also assume about $1.8 billion in Piedmont’s existing debt.By acquiring the natural gas distributor, Duke Energy will add about one million customers in North Carolina, South Carolina and Tennessee. The companies are aiming to close the transaction by the end of 2016.Shares of both Duke Energy and Piedmont gained in early trading after the deal was announced.As Duke Energy shifts from coal to gas generation, the acquisition of Piedmont could help provide needed growth opportunities, according to Andrew Bischof, an analyst with Morningstar. Those benefits did not come cheap, as Duke Energy’s offer was twice what Morningstar estimated to be the fair value for the stock, and higher than other local gas distribution companies’ valuations, Mr. Bischof said.Duke Energy and Piedmont had been part of a group of energy companies working to build and operate the 550-mile Atlantic Coast Pipeline to serve Virginia and North Carolina for about $5 billion. Both companies are based in Charlotte, N.C.Barclays served as the financial adviser to Duke Energy, while Goldman Sachs represented Piedmont.
