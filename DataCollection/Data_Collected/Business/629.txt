By EMILY STEELOCT. 21, 2015
Walt Disney plans to start a subscription streaming service next month in Britain called DisneyLife that includes movies, books, television series and music.The service, which will cost £9.99 a month, about $15.45, will include movies from the Pixar catalog, such as the “Toy Story” series, as well as Disney classics like “Snow White” and “The Jungle Book,” according to a person familiar with the plan, who spoke on condition of anonymity. It also will offer thousands of Disney Channel episodes.Disney plans to make the streaming service available across Europe next year in France, Spain, Italy and Germany, but it does not have any plans to introduce it in the United States for now because of current agreements with cable and satellite companies.News of Disney’s plans for the streaming service was reported earlier by The Financial Times.“This is the future, in many respects,” Bob Iger, Disney’s chief executive, told The Financial Times.AdvertisementA slew of media companies in the United States and around the world have created stand-alone streaming services that do not require a traditional cable or satellite subscription. At the same time, digital incumbents like Netflix, Amazon and Hulu are rapidly expanding their offerings.DisneyLife will include new material as it becomes available but will not include movies produced by Marvel or the Lucasfilm “Star Wars” franchise. Separate “Star Wars” and Marvel offerings could be a possibility.AdvertisementDisney’s service in Europe creates new competition for Netflix as it pushes ahead with its international expansion. This week, Netflix is adding Spain, Portugal and Italy to its current European lineup of 13 countries, including Britain and Germany.A version of this article appears in print on October 22, 2015, on Page B2 of the New York edition with the headline: Business Briefing; In Britain, Disney Plans Subscription Streaming.
