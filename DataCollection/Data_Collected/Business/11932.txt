A contract for deed is a type of alternative financing that is commonly used by people who do not qualify for a mortgage. While this type of contract has been around for decades, it has been in a revival since the financial crisis. Sarah Edelman at the Center for American Progress warned that these seller-financed deals had the potential to create a two-tier system in the housing market, “where low- and moderate-income borrowers don’t have the same protections.”
