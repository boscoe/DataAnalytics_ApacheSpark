By REUTERSSEPT. 24, 2015
A gauge of United States business investment plans fell slightly in August, while the number of Americans filing new applications for jobless benefits barely rose last week. Both reports were taken as signs that global headwinds were doing little to impede American economic growth.Other economic data released on Thursday showed that new home sales rose in August to the highest level since early 2008, further evidence that the domestic economy was on the upswing.The Commerce Department said orders of capital goods excluding military wares and aircraft, a closely watched proxy for business spending plans, dropped 0.2 percent last month, a slightly bigger decline than analysts polled by Reuters had expected.

Durable goods over all fell 2 percent in August, in line with forecasts and held back by a sharp decline in orders for civilian aircraft. Excluding the transportation industry, orders were flat.Some business sectors are being hit by falling commodity prices, though. Caterpillar said on Thursday that it might close or consolidate more than 20 plants around the world across its three large businesses — construction, resources, and energy and transportation.Caterpillar also cut its 2015 revenue forecast and said it could cut up to 10,000 jobs through 2018, joining a list of big industrial companies grappling with the shock waves from a mining and energy downturn.

A separate report from the Labor Department showed that initial claims for state unemployment benefits rose by 3,000 to a seasonally adjusted 267,000 for the week that ended Sept. 19. Analysts expected new claims to rise more.The four-week moving average of jobless claims fell by 750, to 271,750, which was close to its lowest level since 2000 and suggested that the job market continued to strengthen.Robust job growth over the last year has helped the country’s housing market.Sales of new homes surged 5.7 percent last month to a seasonally adjusted annual rate of 552,000, the Commerce Department said.
