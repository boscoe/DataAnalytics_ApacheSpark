By CHAD BRAYSEPT. 13, 2016
LONDON — Nets, the Danish payment processor, said on Tuesday that it expected to be valued at up to 32 billion Danish kroner, or $4.8 billion, in its initial public offering this month.The offering comes about two and a half years after a consortium of investors lead by the private equity firms Advent International, ATP Private Equity Partners and Bain Capital agreed to acquire Nets for 17 billion kroner from a group of Nordic banks.Nets announced its intention to pursue an I.P.O. this month.“I am delighted with the response we have received to the announcement of our intention to float, both from potential investors and other stakeholders,” Bo Nilsson, the Nets chief executive, said in a news release.Nets, which is based in Copenhagen, said it expected to price the offering in the range of 130 kroner to 160 kroner. That would give the company a market capitalization of 32 billion kroner at the top end of the range.Trading is expected to begin on Nasdaq in Copenhagen on Sept. 27.Founded in 1968, Nets provides payment processing services for bank and card issuers in the Nordic and Baltic regions, as well as corporate customers in Denmark and Norway and merchants. The company generated revenue of 6.84 billion kroner in 2015, and it employs about 2,400 people in six countries.Deutsche Bank, Morgan Stanley and Nordea are acting as joint global coordinators on the offering.
