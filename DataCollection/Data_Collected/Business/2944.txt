LONDON – GrandVision, the Dutch operator of the optical retailers Vision Express in Britain and Générale d’Optique in France, plans to raise as much as 1.1 billion euros, or about $1.2 billion, in an initial public offering on Euronext in Amsterdam next month.An indirect subsidiary of the Dutch investment group HAL Holding plans to sell up to 51 million shares, or about a 20 percent stake in GrandVision, in the offering. As much as 23 percent of the company could be sold if an overallotment of shares is fully exercised.In a news release, GrandVision said it expected to price its stock at €17.50 to €21.50 a share, valuing it at up to €5.4 billion. The company expects trading to begin in its shares on Feb. 6. GrandVision announced plans to pursue an I.P.O. this month.Institutional investors and retail investors in the Netherlands will be able to participate in the I.P.O., as well as certain other institutional investors in other countries.GrandVision also plans to purchase 2.5 million shares from HAL “to hedge the price risk of grants made by GrandVision under its long-term incentive plans.”If the overallotment is fully exercised, HAL expects to hold 75.60 percent of GrandVision’s shares after the I.P.O.GrandVision operates more than 5,600 stores in 43 countries in Europe, Latin America and Asia, with about 80 percent of the stores directly owned by the company. In addition to Vision Express and Générale d’Optique, its brands include Apollo-Optik in Germany, Pearle Opticiens in the Netherlands and Ópticas Lux in Mexico.The company, which sells prescription glasses, contact lenses and sunglasses online and in retail stores, posted revenue of €2.6 billion in 2013 and revenue of €2.1 billion in the first nine months of 2014. It had 24,500 full-time employees as of the end of September.ABN Amro and JPMorgan Chase are acting as joint global coordinators on the offering.
