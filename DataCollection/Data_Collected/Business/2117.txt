Chipotle Mexican Grill fell on more concern about the safety of its food.The Dow Jones industrial average rose 165.65 points, or 1 percent, to 17,417.27. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index rose 17.82 points, or 0.9 percent, to 2,038.97, and the Nasdaq composite index rose 32.19 points, or 0.7 percent, to 5,001.11. Trading was light. Many investors have closed their portfolios for the year or are on vacation for the Christmas and New Year’s holidays. However, a modest recovery in oil prices helped lift energy and materials stocks.“Investors are either done for the year or are setting up their portfolios for 2016, buying this year’s winners or doing reallocation to their portfolios,” said JJ Kinahan, chief strategist at TD Ameritrade.Chipotle Mexican Grill’s shares fell $27.40, or 5 percent, to $494.61 after it disclosed additional cases of E. coli at its restaurants.United States crude oil futures closed up 33 cents to $36.14 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange. Brent crude, used to price international oils, was down 24 cents to $36.11 a barrel in London. Energy stocks were among the bigger gainers, with the energy sector of the S.&P. 500 up 1.2 percent. Mining and materials stocks also rose.Stock in Diamond Offshore Drilling rose $1.13, or 5.6 percent, to $21.43. The drilling rig operator Transocean’s shares rose 43 cents, or 4 percent, to $12.55. Markets are searching for direction as the flow of economic data slows greatly until after Jan. 1. Investors have already had plenty of time to digest the recent major decisions by the Federal Reserve to raise rates and by the European Central Bank to increase its stimulus efforts.Many investors expect stocks and bonds to trade in a narrow range until January.“We need a catalyst, and look to the week of the employment report to be that catalyst, but that is still two weeks away,” John Briggs, head of American fixed income strategy at Royal Bank of Scotland, wrote to investors.In other energy trading, heating oil fell 1.3 cents to $1.09 a gallon, wholesale gasoline fell 3.5 cents to $1.175 a gallon, and natural gas fell 1.3 cents to $1.89 per thousand cubic feet.The euro rose to $1.0952 from $1.0926 on Monday. The yield on the 10-year United States Treasury note rose to 2.24 percent from 2.19 percent on Monday.Gold fell $6.50 to $1,074.10 a troy ounce, silver was unchanged at $14.31 an ounce, and copper fell three cents to $2.109 a pound.Advertisement
