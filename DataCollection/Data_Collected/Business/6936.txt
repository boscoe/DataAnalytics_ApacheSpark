By CHAD BRAYJUNE 19, 2015
LONDON — A consortium of private equity firms that includes Bain Capital agreed on Friday to acquire the Italian payment and clearing services company Istituto Centrale delle Banche Popolari Italiane for 2.15 billion euros, or about $2.4 billion.A group of cooperative banks in Italy, including Credito Valtellinese, put the firm up for sale as they faced pressure from regulators to increase their capital and entered into exclusive talks with the private equity firms in May.The consortium includes Bain, Advent International and the Italian private equity firm Clessidra.“We are delighted to have the opportunity to contribute to the future success of this market leader in Italy,” Luca Bassi, a managing director at Bain Capital Europe, said in a news release.“All of the investors in the consortium have a strong track record of investing in the transformation and growth of similar businesses and together we will support the management team’s implementation of its ambitious plans for the company,” he said.The transaction is subject to regulatory approval.Founded in 1939, Istituto Centrale delle Banche Popolari Italiane, based in Milan, provides payment, interbank clearing and securities services. It generated revenue of €670 million in 2014 and has more than 1,900 employees.The selling banks will retain an 8 percent stake in the company after the transaction.Rothschild, HSBC and Vitale & Associate and the law firms Bonelli Erede Pappalardo; Studio Carbonetti; Carlo Pavesi; and Weil, Gotshal & Manages advised the consortium.We’re interested in your feedback on this page.
