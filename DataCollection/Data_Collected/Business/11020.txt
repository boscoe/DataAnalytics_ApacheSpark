By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJAN. 8, 2016
Toys “R” Us reported stronger holiday sales despite intense competition from online retailers such as Amazon.com. The company said on Friday that sales at stores open at least a year rose 2 percent from Nov. 1 to Jan. 2, a crucial period for retailers, compared with last year. From Nov. 29 to Jan. 2, sales at stores open at least a year rose 3.7 percent. The results are an illustration of advances made under the new chief, David Brandon. He put a priority on ensuring that the hottest toys were in stock in the final stretch before Christmas.
