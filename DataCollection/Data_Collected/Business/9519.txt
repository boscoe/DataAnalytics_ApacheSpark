By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSOCT. 12, 2016
Stocks on Wall Street finished slightly higher on Wednesday as investors looked toward shares that pay big dividends, like real estate and phone companies. Energy companies fell with the price of oil, and health care stocks continued to slide.Stocks barely budged for most of the day. The top gains went to the biggest dividend payers, which are seen as relatively safe investments. The price of oil, which reached a 15-month high on Monday, inched lower for the second day in a row. Health care stocks continued to lose ground after the health insurer Humana said its bonus payments from Medicare could be reduced.

The Dow Jones industrial average edged up 15.54 points, or 0.1 percent, to 18,144.20. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index added 2.45 points, or 0.1 percent, to 2,139.18. The Nasdaq composite index slipped 7.77 points, or 0.1 percent, to 5,239.02.The real estate investment trust Crown Castle International gained $2.45, or 2.8 percent, to $91.45, and American Tower picked up $2.60, or 2.4 percent, to close at $111.54. Verizon Communications led phone companies higher with a rise of 40 cents, or 0.8 percent, to $50.30, and the utility group WEC Energy went up 83 cents, or 1.5 percent, to $57.42.AdvertisementStanley Black & Decker agreed to buy the tools business of the consumer products maker Newell Brands for $1.95 billion. Stanley Black & Decker gained $3.39, or 2.9 percent, to $121.05, and Newell Brands rose $1.08, or 2.2 percent, to $51.42.

The health insurer Humana said its plans received lower ratings from the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services, and weaker ratings could reduce the bonus payments the company gets from the government. For 2018, the company said 37 percent of its members were in plans rated four stars or higher. That is down from 78 percent a year ago. The stock fell $9.09, or 5.1 percent, to $168.44.AdvertisementCompanies that make and sell household products joined in the gains. The supermarket chain Kroger jumped $1.08, or 3.6 percent, to $31.19, and CVS Health rose $1.07, or 1.2 percent, to $87.47.The wireless equipment maker LM Ericsson plunged after it forecast disappointing quarterly results and said it did not expect improvement anytime soon. Its stock dropped $1.46, or 20.8 percent, to $5.55.

Benchmark crude oil slid 61 cents, or 1.2 percent, to close at $50.18 a barrel in New York. Brent crude, the international standard, fell 60 cents, or 1.1 percent, to close at $51.81 a barrel in London.On the bond markets, the yield on the 10-year Treasury note remained steady at 1.77 percent. The price fell by 1/32, to 9718/32.The dollar rose to 104.24 Japanese yen from 103.41 yen. The euro dipped to $1.1014 from $1.1053. The British pound inched up to $1.2208 from $1.2133 but remains at its lowest levels in decades.In the metals market, gold fell $1.90, to $1,251.10 an ounce. Silver remained at $17.51 an ounce.In France, the CAC 40 slipped 0.4 percent and the DAX in Germany fell 0.5 percent. In London, the FTSE 100 was down 0.7 percent.
