By CHAD BRAYOCT. 2, 2015
LONDON — The Equiniti Group, a British provider of employee benefits administration and payments services, said on Friday that it expected to raise 390 million pounds, or about $590 million, in its initial public offering this month.The company said it intended to seek a listing on the London Stock Exchange and would use a portion of the proceeds from the offering to pay down its debt.The private equity firm Advent International acquired Equiniti from Lloyds TSB, now part of the Lloyds Banking Group, for £550 million in 2007.“The I.P.O. will enable Equiniti’s management team to continue its proven growth strategy, whilst benefiting from a growing market and favorable trends towards outsourcing, greater regulation and digitalization,” Guy Wakeley, the Equiniti chief executive, said in a news release.AdvertisementEquiniti provides administration, processing and payments services to businesses, processing more than £90 billion in payments a year.AdvertisementThe company says it works with about 70 percent of the companies listed in the FTSE 100 index of leading London stocks, providing share registration and associated investor services, as well as administering employee share plans, pensions and employee benefit programs.Please verify you're not a robot by clicking the box.Invalid email address.
