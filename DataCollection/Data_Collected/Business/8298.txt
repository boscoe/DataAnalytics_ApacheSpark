AdvertisementSupported byBy Chad BrayLONDON — The British bank HSBC has promoted two bankers as the lender continues to reshape its business globally.Natalie Blyth, the co-head of banking for Britain in HSBC’s investment bank, will become the global head of large corporations in its commercial banking business beginning on Sept. 1, according to an internal memorandum reviewed by The New York Times. She will succeed Joel Van Dusen.Ms. Blyth has more than 20 years’ experience in investment banking and has been with HSBC since 2007, when she left Deutsche Bank. She was named co-head of banking in Britain in June 2014.Philip Noblet will replace Ms. Blyth and serve as co-head of banking for Britain in the investment bank alongside Alan Thomas, who was named to the role last year.Mr. Noblet joined HSBC in March from Bank of America Merrill Lynch, where he most recently served as chairman of mergers and acquisitions for Europe, the Middle East and Africa.The Wall Street Journal reported the promotions earlier on Wednesday.HSBC, which is Europe’s largest bank and generates more than half of its earnings in Asia, has suffered damage to its reputation and bottom line amid a series of legal and regulatory settlements.The lender announced plans in June to shed 50,000 jobs, sell several underperforming businesses and shrink its global investment banking business. HSBC has targeted up to $5 billion in annual cost savings by the end of 2017.Advertisement
