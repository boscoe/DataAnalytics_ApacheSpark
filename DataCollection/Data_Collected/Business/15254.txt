By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSAUG. 4, 2016
United States stocks wavered on Thursday and finished barely higher. An interest rate cut by the Bank of England, a move intended to shore up the British economy, was not enough to get investors out of their recent cautious mode.Technology companies made the biggest gains, as they have done over the last few months. Oil prices rose for the second day in a row, something that had not happened for almost three weeks. Bank stocks fell the most, as the interest rate cut suggested that they would not be able to make as much money on lending. While the Bank of England’s moves sent European stocks higher, investors on Wall Street moved carefully, waiting for Friday’s jobs report.The Dow Jones industrial average slipped 2.95 points to 18,352.05. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index inched up 0.46 points to 2,164.25. The Nasdaq composite index rose 6.51 points, or 0.1 percent, to 5,166.25.Quincy Krosby, a market strategist at Prudential Financial, said investors were playing it safe as they waited for the Labor Department’s July employment report. Hiring in June was stronger than expected, but that followed shockingly weak job growth in May.

“The market has been surprised before and wants to make sure that they’re prepared in either direction,” Ms. Krosby said. “To get the market to move higher you need an underpinning of stronger economic growth.”AdvertisementThe Labor Department said applications for unemployment aid rose to 269,000 last week, a level close to historical lows and a positive sign for the job market. The number of Americans collecting unemployment benefits has fallen more than 5 percent in the last year, but the pace of hiring and economic growth slowed in the first half of 2016.AdvertisementThe Bank of England cut interest rates to new lows and unveiled a raft of stimulus measures that include resuming a bond-buying program to pump money into the economy and offering cheap loans to banks. The measures seemed to exceed investors’ expectations.The Bank of England is trying to counter the shock of Britain’s vote in late June to leave the European Union. Britain’s central bank had not cut interest rates since the financial crisis.The pound fell to $1.3127 from $1.3312 on Wednesday.Tech stocks were led higher by Facebook and the communications chip maker Broadcom. Facebook rose $1.85, or 1.5 percent, to $124.36, and Broadcom gained $2.88, or 1.8 percent, to $166.99.
