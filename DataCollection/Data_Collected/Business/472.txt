By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSOCT. 15, 2015
Financial stocks surged on Thursday, helping push the market to its biggest gain in almost two weeks.Citigroup jumped after reporting that its earnings rose sharply in the third quarter as the bank continued to cut expenses and clean up its books in the wake of the financial crisis. KeyCorp also climbed after posting solid earnings.The reports cheered investors, who have been looking for good news since a summer sell-off roiled the markets. However, the optimism may be short-lived. Third-quarter earnings are forecast to contract over all as falling energy prices and weak global demand start to eat into profits.“Financials could be the bright spot of the whole earnings season, so let the market have its fun,” said Karyn Cavanaugh, senior market strategist at Voya Investment Management. “We’ll take any bright spot we can get in this earnings season, because things really don’t look good.”AdvertisementThe Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index rose 29.62 points, or 1.5 percent, to 2,023.86. That was the biggest one-day gain for the index since Oct. 5. The Dow Jones industrial average climbed 217 points, or 1.3 percent, to 17,141.75. The Nasdaq rose 87.25 points, or 1.8 percent, to 4,870.10.AdvertisementEarnings for companies in the S.&P. 500 are forecast to drop 5 percent in the third quarter, according to S&P Capital IQ.

On Thursday, financial stocks climbed 2.3 percent, the most among the 10 sectors that make up the S.&P. 500. Citigroup climbed $2.25, or 4.4 percent, to $52.97. KeyCorp rose 60 cents, or 4.7 percent, to $13.31.Stocks have started October with a strong rally after closing out September with their worst quarterly performance in four years. Worries about the Chinese economy and the possibility of a Federal Reserve rate increase shook the market in August and September.After a run of weak economic reports in the last month, many investors now think the Fed will keep interest rates unchanged for the rest of the year. Fed policy makers have kept the bank’s benchmark lending rate close to zero for almost seven years, supporting a bull market in stocks.The outlook for interest rates will probably hold the key for how stocks perform for the rest of the year, said Sean Lynch, co-head of global equity strategy at Wells Fargo Investment Institute.“Last year, we had a pretty good rally into year-end,” Mr. Lynch said. “I wouldn’t be surprised, should people get a clear indication of what’s going to happen with the Fed, if we see that happen again.”Netflix was among the day’s biggest losers. The stock slumped $9.14, or 8.3 percent, to $101.09 after the company reported late Wednesday that it was holding onto fewer United States viewers than it hoped.
