By JOHN KOBLINMARCH 19, 2015
After last week’s episode of “Empire,” a Fox teaser warned viewers: Don’t miss the two-hour finale, or you’ll almost certainly risk being spoiled.They listened. “Empire,” Fox’s first-year hip-hop drama, brought in a season-high 16.7 million viewers for Wednesday’s finale, according to Nielsen data provided by the network. That caps off an improbable run of 10 consecutive weeks of viewership growth.“Empire” also brought in 17.6 million viewers from 9 to 10 p.m., its normal time slot. The show posted a 6.5 rating among the key 18-to-49-year-old demographic, and Fox said that it had its highest-rated Wednesday night among that age group in three years. The finale also prompted 2.4 million Twitter posts, an enormous increase over the 750,258 sent last week (the premiere in January generated slightly fewer than 300,000). The show has the highest average of tweets per episode of any show, according to Nielsen Social.The total viewership for the show will increase in the coming days and months as people catch up after two months of nonstop buzz. Hulu announced on Thursday that it had exclusive streaming rights for current and future episodes of “Empire.”The show’s success has given a badly needed bump to Fox, which is the fourth-rated network. Last week, Fox executives hosted a party for the show’s cast and producers at Nobu in West Hollywood.
