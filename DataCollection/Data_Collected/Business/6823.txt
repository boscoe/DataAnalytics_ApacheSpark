By CHAD BRAYJUNE 15, 2015
Two big California homebuilders, the Standard Pacific Corporation and the Ryland Group, have agreed to combine in a so-called merger of equals.The deal, announced late Sunday, would combine two of the nation’s largest builders of homes, creating a company with a combined market value of about $5.2 billion that would own or control 74,000 home sites. In the 12 months ended March 31, the two companies combined to build more than 12,600 homes and had combined revenue of about $5.1 billion.“Combining two industry leaders with nearly 100 years of homebuilding experience between them puts us in a strong position to benefit from the continued housing market recovery,” Scott Stowell, Standard Pacific’s president and chief executive, said in a news release.The transaction is subject to approval by shareholders of both companies and is expected to be completed in the early fall.AdvertisementAfter the deal closes, Mr. Stowell will serve as executive chairman and Larry Nicholson, Ryland’s chief executive, will serve as president and chief executive.AdvertisementThe combined company plans to operate under one brand and will announce its new name before the deal closes.Please verify you're not a robot by clicking the box.Invalid email address. The companies said that they would seek to develop a corporate presence on the East Coast in the next two years while continuing to maintain a corporate presence in California.“With similar cultures and long histories of crafting quality homes and providing superior customer service, we are each proud of where we’ve been and look to the future confident that we will be better together,” Mr. Nicholson said in a news release.As part of the deal, Standard Pacific will first undertake a 1-for-5 reverse stock split.
