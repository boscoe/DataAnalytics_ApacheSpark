AdvertisementSupported byBy The Associated PressNews about corporate deals gave the stock market a lift on Wednesday. Health care stocks rose after the generic-drug maker Mylan bid $29 billion for the rival Perrigo. In Europe, the oil company Royal Dutch Shell agreed to buy the BG Group for $69.7 billion in cash and stock. That offset a slump in energy stocks prompted by a big drop in oil prices. Corporations worldwide are seeking to increase growth through acquisitions and have announced almost $1 trillion worth of deals so far this year, according to the data provider Dealogic. That is elevating stock markets as the acquiring companies typically offer a premium for their targets.Daniel Morris, global investment strategist at the investment company TIAA-CREF, said a debate was occurring about whether deals “are value-creating or value-destroying,” but he added that “in the short term, it is generally good for markets.”The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index rose 5.57 points, or 0.3 percent, to 2,081.90. The Dow Jones industrial average was up 27.09 points, to 17,902.51. The Nasdaq gained 40.59 points, or 0.8 percent, to 4,950.82.High yield in monthly refunding auction.Investors were also parsing the minutes from the latest Federal Reserve meeting for clues about the timing of a possible interest rate increase, and waiting for companies to start reporting their first-quarter earnings.Alcoa, a metals maker, said after the market closed that its revenue fell short of analysts’ expectations. Its stock slid 46 cents, or 3.4 percent, to $13.21 in after-hours trading.Over all, earnings per share are projected to decline by about 3 percent for S.&P. 500 companies, according to data from S&P Capital IQ. That would be the first contraction since the third quarter of 2009, when the economy was emerging from the recession.On Wednesday, Perrigo was the biggest gainer in the S.&P. 500. The stock surged $30.29, or 18.4 percent, to $195, after Mylan announced that it had made a cash-and-stock offer for the company. Both Mylan and Perrigo recently left the United States for Europe. If they combine they would form one of the world’s largest makers of generic and over-the-counter medicines. Mylan’s stock also rose, gaining $8.79, or 14.8 percent, to $68.36.Position of the Dow Jones industrial average at 1-minute intervals.Energy stocks were the biggest decliners on the day, falling as the price of oil plunged nearly 7 percent, its biggest drop in two months. This was after the Energy Department reported an increase in oil storage that was about three times what analysts had expected.Crude stocks rose by 11 million barrels during the week ending April 3, reaching a new high for this time of year. Benchmark United States crude fell $3.56, to settle at $50.42 a barrel in New York. Brent crude, a benchmark for international oils used by many refineries in the United States, fell $3.55, to $55.55, in London.Government bond prices were little changed. The yield on the 10-year Treasury note was 1.91 percent, up from 1.89 percent. The dollar fell to 120.06 yen from 120.31 yen on Tuesday. The euro slipped to $1.0782 from $1.0814.In metals trading, gold fell $7.50 to $1,203.10 an ounce, silver fell 39 cents to $16.45 an ounce, and copper fell three cents to $16.45 a pound.In other energy futures trading on the Nymex, wholesale gasoline fell 12.2 cents to $1.739 a gallon; heating oil fell 8.6 cents to close at $1.698 a gallon; and natural gas fell 6.1 cents to close at $2.619 per 1,000 cubic feet.
