By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMAY 21, 2015
Gap reported an 8 percent decline in its first-quarter profit, hurt by currency fluctuations and persistent sluggish sales at its Gap and Banana Republic stores. But it stuck with its annual profit outlook. Gap is among the companies struggling with the impact of a stronger dollar, but it is also dealing with the uneven performance of its brands. The company’s Old Navy brand has been a bright spot while it is trying to turn around weak business at Banana Republic and Gap. The retailer reported earnings of $239 million, or 56 cents a share, for the three-month period that ended May 2. That is down from $260 million, or 58 cents a share, for the same period last year. Revenue slipped 3.1 percent to $3.66 billion.
