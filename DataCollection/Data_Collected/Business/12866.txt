By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 30, 2016
Stocks rose Wednesday as technology companies traded higher for the second day in a row and consumer companies gained steam as cruise lines rose. The financial sector recovered some losses from early this year.Stocks started the day sharply higher after a jump in European markets, although they returned some gains. Oil prices followed the same pattern and ended a few cents higher. Shares in cruise lines rose after Carnival posted strong first-quarter results, and the dollar weakened further.Stocks are up about 7 percent this month, part of a rally that has canceled their losses from a bad start to the year.“The Fed really cured a lot of global ills” by deciding to go slower in raising interest rates, said John Canally, chief economic strategist for LPL Financial. The Federal Reserve announced that decision two weeks ago, and its chairwoman, Janet L. Yellen, emphasized it again in a speech Tuesday.AdvertisementThe Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index gained 8.94 points, or 0.4 percent, to 2,063.95. The Nasdaq composite index added 22.67 points, or 0.5 percent, to 4,869.29.AdvertisementThe Dow Jones industrial average rose 83.55 points, or 0.5 percent, to 17,716.66.Banks and insurance companies made the biggest gains. MetLife gained $2.27, or 5.4 percent, to $44.73 after it successfully challenged its “too big to fail” designation.

The Financial Stability Oversight Council had said MetLife needed more government oversight because of its size and importance to the financial system, but the company took the council to court. On Wednesday a judge ruled in its favor.Stock in a fellow insurer, the American International Group, advanced $1.13, or 2.1 percent, to $54.52, and Prudential shares rose $1.43, or 2 percent, to $72.95. Banks also traded higher.Apple led the gains among technology companies. Its stock rose $1.88, or 1.8 percent, to $109.56. Visa shares gained $1.40, or 1.9 percent, to $76.78.After two weeks of mixed trading, stocks started rising late Tuesday when Ms. Yellen said the central bank expected to proceed slowly in raising interest rates. The market is now on pace for its best month since October, and the Dow and S.&P. 500 are higher for the year. In February, each was down 10 percent.Carnival shares rose after the cruise line posted first-quarter results that were better than analysts had expected, and the company raised its profit projections for the year. The stock gained $2.73, or 5.5 percent, to $52.37, and Royal Caribbean Cruises jumped $4.30, or 5.7 percent, to $80.35.The railroad operator Norfolk Southern added $1.95, or 2.4 percent, to $84.75 after it said it was open to a possible sale to Canadian Pacific, but only if Canadian Pacific offers a better price and regulators approve the structure of the deal.AdvertisementAmerican companies added 200,000 jobs in March, according to a survey of private employers by ADP, a payroll processing company. The survey showed that companies in construction, retailing and shipping continued to hire. The federal government will release its monthly jobs report Friday.
