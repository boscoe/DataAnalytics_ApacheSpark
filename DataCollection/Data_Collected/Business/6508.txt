By CHAD BRAYJUNE 2, 2015
LONDON — The British bank HSBC said on Tuesday that it was adding two independent directors to its board.Irene Yun Lien Lee, the executive chairwoman of the Hong Kong property investment company Hysan Development Company, will join the lender’s board of directors on July 1, while Pauline van der Meer Mohr, the president of the executive board of Erasmus University Rotterdam, will join HSBC on Sept. 1.“I am delighted to welcome both Irene and Pauline to the HSBC Holdings Board,” Douglas Flint, the HSBC chairman, said in a news release.Ms. Lee, 61, has worked more than 30 years in finance, including stints at the Commonwealth Bank of Australia and Citigroup. She currently serves as a director on the boards of two of HSBC’s subsidiaries.Ms. van der Meer Mohr, 55, began her career in the legal profession and has previously worked as an executive at Royal Dutch Shell Group and ABN Amro Bank.The appointments are for three-year terms.We’re interested in your feedback on this page.
