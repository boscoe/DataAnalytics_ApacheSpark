By CHAD BRAYJULY 30, 2015
LONDON — The European private equity firm PAI Partners said on Thursday that it had signed an agreement to sell the air cargo services company Swissport International to the owner of Hainan Airlines of China for 2.7 billion Swiss francs, or about $2.8 billion.Swissport International, based in Zurich, provides cargo and ground services at airports in 48 countries. It employs about 60,000 people and has annual revenue of 3 billion francs. The company said it handled 4.1 million tons of cargo and baggage for 224 million passengers annually.PAI Partners agreed in 2010 to acquire Swissport International from Ferrovial of Spain for 654 million euros, or about $722 million.PAI Partners, based in Paris, manages about €7.5 billion in private equity assets.The parent of Hainan Airlines, HNA Group of China, operates in a variety of sectors, including airlines, hospitality, retail and financial services. It posted revenue of 170 billion renminbi, or about $28 billion, in 2014 and employs more than 110,000 people worldwide.“The acquisition by HNA will enable the company to grow in the underpenetrated Asian markets and in China, in particular, thanks to HNA’s strong roots in the region,” Ricardo de Serdio, a partner at PAI Partners, said in a news release.The transaction is subject to regulatory approval, and it is expected to close by the end of the year.A version of this article appears in print on July 31, 2015, on Page B5 of the New York edition with the headline: Air Cargo.
