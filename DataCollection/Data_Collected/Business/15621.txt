By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSAUG. 30, 2016
Stocks fell slightly on Tuesday in another quiet late-summer day on Wall Street as hesitant investors remained on the sidelines.Shares of the candy company Hershey plunged after a merger proposal fell apart, and Apple slipped after the company was hit with a large tax bill in Europe.Investors continue to wait to see whether the Federal Reserve will raise interest rates later this year. The August jobs report, the next important piece of data, is coming out on Friday.The Dow Jones industrial average fell 48.69 points, or 0.3 percent, to 18,454.30. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index fell 4.26 points, or 0.2 percent, to 2,176.12 and the Nasdaq composite fell 9.34 points, or 0.2 percent, to 5,222.99.AdvertisementTrading was extremely light once again, with about 2.95 billion shares changing hands on the New York Stock Exchange, the seventh-slowest day of the year. Monday was the slowest trading day of 2016.AdvertisementBank stocks were among the few gainers as investors continued to interpret comments from Janet L. Yellen, the Federal Reserve chairwoman, and Stanley Fisher, the vice chairman, at a conference in Wyoming last week as signs the Fed is ready to raise interest rates later this year. In her comments, Ms. Yellen said the case for raising rates “has strengthened in recent months.”Banks are a major beneficiary of rising interest rates because they can charge more for loans when interest rates rise.Bank of America rose 35 cents, or 2 percent, to $16.19, Wells Fargo rose $1.06, or 2 percent, to $50.62, and Morgan Stanley rose 78 cents, or 2.5 percent, to $32.19.
