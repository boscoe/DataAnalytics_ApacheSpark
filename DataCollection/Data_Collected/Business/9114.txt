AdvertisementSupported byBy Chad BrayStarwood Waypoint Residential Trust, an investment vehicle that owns thousands of single-family rental homes in the United States, said on Monday that it would buy its rival Colony American Homes in an all-stock deal that values Colony American at $1.5 billion.The deal will create a company that owns and manages more than 30,000 rental homes in the United States and has an asset value of $7.7. billion.Barry Sternlicht, the chairman and chief executive of the private equity firm Starwood Capital Group, said the deal was a “transformative event” for Starwood Waypoint Residential Trust and the industry.An affiliate of Starwood Capital had acted as the investment manager for Starwood Waypoint Residential Trust and will become part of the combined company.“Combining two best-in-class teams, with a superior portfolio of homes in carefully selected geographic markets, positions us to deliver long-term capital appreciation for our shareholders while earning compelling current yields at or above those currently achievable in other major real estate asset classes,” Mr. Sternlicht said in a news release.Not included in the deal is Colony American Finance, a relatively new operation started by Colony that provides financing to small and midsize landlords that invest in single family homes to operate them as rental properties, said a person who had been briefed on the matter. In the last two years, several private equity firms have begun providing so-called landlord loans to smaller real estate investors.The transaction has been approved by the board of Starwood Waypoint Residential Trust and that of Colony American Homes.It is subject to approval by Starwood Waypoint Residential Trust shareholders and is expected to close in the first quarter of 2016.Colony American Homes, based in Scottsdale, Ariz., owns and manages about 19,000 homes. It was formed in 2012 by Colony Capital, a real estate and investment management firm based in Los Angeles.Mr. Sternlicht and Thomas J. Barrack Jr., the executive chairman of Colony Capital, will be nonexecutive co-chairmen of the combined company’s board of trustees.Under the terms of the transaction, investors in Colony American Homes will receive 64.9 million shares of Starwood Waypoint Residential Trust for their Colony American Homes stock.Based on Starwood Waypoint Residential Trust’s closing price on Friday, the deal values Colony American Homes at $1.5 billion.After the transaction, shareholders of Starwood Waypoint Residential Trust will own about 41 percent of the combined company. Investors in Colony American Homes will own the remaining 59 percent.The companies said they expected to achieve annual cost savings of $40 million to $50 million after the transaction.Starwood Waypoint Residential Trust, based in Oakland, Calif., owns about 12,500 rental homes in eight states and invests in portfolios of so-called nonperforming loans. It posted revenue of $142.9 million in 2014.Moelis & Company and the law firm Sidley Austin advised Starwood Waypoint. Morgan Stanley and the law firms Skadden, Arps, Slate, Meagher & Flom and Clifford Chance advised Colony American. A special committee of the board of trustees of Starwood Waypoint was advised by Wells Fargo and the law firm Wachtell, Lipton, Rosen & Katz.Matthew Goldstein contributed reporting.Advertisement
