By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJULY 8, 2015
It was already a tough day in the market when the New York Stock Exchange halted trading because of unexpected technical trouble.Concerns about China’s plunging stock market and a logjam in talks between Greece and its creditors were weighing on traders Wednesday.Major indexes were already falling before the shutdown, which occurred shortly after 11:30 a.m. Eastern time. The exchange resumed trading at 3:10 p.m.By the end of the day, the Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index had fallen 34.66 points, or 1.7 percent, to close at 2,046.68.AdvertisementThe Dow Jones industrial average dipped 261.49 points, or 1.5 percent, to 17,515.42, and the Nasdaq slid 87.70 points, or 1.8 percent, to 4,909.76.AdvertisementIn China, the Shanghai composite sank 6 percent despite new attempts by the Chinese government to stop the selling. Hong Kong’s Hang Seng, a victim of the turmoil in mainland Chinese markets, also lost 6 percent. Beijing ordered state-owned companies to buy shares and promised more credit to finance trading.The Shanghai index has lost almost a third of its value in the last month. It is still up 70 percent over the past year.In Europe, Greece applied for a new three-year loan and said it would have a new proposal for creditors in coming days. The deeply indebted country needs a financial lifeline from its lenders before its banks collapse. The region’s major markets finished with gains.
