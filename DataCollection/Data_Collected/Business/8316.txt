By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSAUG. 13, 2015
More people sought unemployment aid last week, but the average for the past month fell to the lowest level in 15 years, a sign that fewer employers are cutting jobs. The Labor Department said on Thursday that applications for jobless benefits rose 5,000 to a seasonally adjusted 274,000 last week. Yet the four-week average, a less volatile measure, dropped 1,750 to 266,250, the lowest since April 15, 2000. The decline in applications for unemployment aid echoes other data that points to a steadily improving job market.A version of this brief appears in print on August 14, 2015, on Page B2 of the New York edition with the headline: Jobless Benefits Applications Fall to a 15-Year Low.
