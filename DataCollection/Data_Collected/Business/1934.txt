By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSDEC. 11, 2015
Americans will have to wait a bit longer to find out who will be the first woman on their paper currency in more than a century. Treasury Secretary Jacob J. Lew had said that, by the end of this year, he would name the woman whose portrait will replace Alexander Hamilton’s on the $10 bill. But late Friday, the Treasury issued a statement saying the announcement was being delayed until 2016. The Treasury said it wanted to “carefully review and consider a range of options” in response to an outpouring of suggestions, more than 1.5 million responses, including Twitter messages and handwritten notes.
