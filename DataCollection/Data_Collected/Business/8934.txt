By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSSEPT. 14, 2015
More signs of slowing economic growth in China weighed on companies that produce raw materials, pulling the stock market to a slight loss on Monday.Trading was light as investors looked ahead to a much-anticipated Federal Reserve meeting later this week.Brad McMillan, chief investment officer for the Commonwealth Financial Network, says the market is most likely to drift until the Fed wraps up its two-day meeting on Thursday. “Everybody is waiting to see what happens when and if the Fed raises rates,” he said.Until recently, many on Wall Street thought the Fed would raise its benchmark interest rate at the end of this week’s meeting. Now, opinions are split.AdvertisementSome analysts suggest that China’s slower economy, and turbulence in financial markets, might prompt the Fed to postpone its first rate increase since 2006.

But the Fed’s deputy chairman, Stanley Fischer, recently said he saw a “pretty strong case” for raising rates.AdvertisementMajor United States indexes opened higher, then sank slowly throughout the morning and remained lower through the rest of the day.Miners and other materials companies had some of the biggest losses. Shares of the metals companies Alcoa and Nucor dropped about 3 percent, and Freeport-McMoRan lost 2 percent.The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index lost 8.02 points, or 0.4 percent, to close at 1,953.03. The Dow Jones industrial average lost 62.13 points, or 0.4 percent, to 16,370.96, and the Nasdaq fell 16.58 points, or 0.3 percent, to 4,805.76.Apple reported strong demand for its latest iPhones, driving its stock up. The company said that initial sales of the iPhone 6s and iPhone 6s Plus were on track to beat the tally from last year, when it sold a record 10 million large-screen iPhones during the first weekend. Apple’s stock climbed $1.10, or 1 percent, to $115.31.In Europe, the DAX in Germany closed with a gain of 0.1 percent, the CAC-40 in France lost 0.7 percent, and the FTSE 100 index of leading British shares slipped 0.5 percent.

Two economic reports out Sunday rekindled concerns over China’s economic slowdown. Factory output and investment grew at a slower pace than expected. China’s main stock index, the Shanghai composite, took another tumble on Monday, dropping 2.7 percentAdvertisementElsewhere, the Hang Seng in Hong Kong added 0.3 percent. The Nikkei 225 in Japan lost 1.6 percent, and the Kospi in South Korea lost 0.5 percent.Back in the United States, Raptor Pharmaceuticals lost more than one-third of its market value after it said that it might scrap development of a liver disease treatment because the treatment failed to pass a pivotal test.
