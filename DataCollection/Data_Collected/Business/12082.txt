Orszag, a former cabinet official in the Obama administration turned investment banker, has joined Lazard as a senior deal maker, the company announced on Wednesday.Mr. Orszag, who will hold the roles of managing director and vice chairman of investment banking, will begin on May 16 and will be based in New York.Though working as an investment banker for the last five years, Mr. Orszag is best known for his government service. He worked as President Obama’s first director of the Office of Management and Budget, holding that position from January 2009 until July 2010.Before that, he worked as the director of the Congressional Budget Office. He also served in the Clinton administration as a special assistant for economic policy.Mr. Orszag has also worked as a senior fellow at the Brookings Institution.He was most recently at Citigroup, which he joined months after leaving the Obama administration and where he served as vice chairman of corporate and investment banking and chairman of that bank’s financial strategy and solutions group.At Lazard, Mr. Orszag is expected to focus on advising companies on mergers and other financial matters.“Peter is one of the most respected economic advisers on the world stage,” Kenneth M. Jacobs, Lazard’s chairman and chief executive, said in a statement. “His expertise in finance, capital markets and public policy across industry sectors will be invaluable.”Advertisement
