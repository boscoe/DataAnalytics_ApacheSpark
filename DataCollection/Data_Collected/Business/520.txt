AdvertisementSupported byBy ReutersPARIS — The French police have carried out searches at Volkswagen’s offices in France, the Paris prosecutor’s office said on Sunday, as part of an investigation into the automaker’s cheating on diesel vehicle emissions tests.The police searched Volkswagen’s main French office in Villers-Cotterêts northeast of Paris and another office at Roissy near the main Paris airport, seizing computer material, an official at the prosecutor’s office said. The searches were conducted on Friday, but were not reported until Sunday.A Volkswagen spokesman confirmed that the company’s offices had been searched by the French police and said the carmaker was cooperating with the authorities.The Paris prosecutor is conducting a preliminary inquiry into suspected “aggravated deception” by Volkswagen.The German auto group has said 946,092 vehicles in France were equipped with the EA 189 engines potentially affected by the emissions data manipulation.Volkswagen has said up to 11 million vehicles worldwide could contain banned software, and on Thursday the company announced that it would recall around 8.5 million vehicles in the European Union.News of the searches came a day after Martin Winterkorn resigned as chief executive of Porsche Automobil Holding, the company that holds a majority of Volkswagen shares. Mr. Winterkorn had quit as chief executive of Volkswagen on Sept. 23.Advertisement
