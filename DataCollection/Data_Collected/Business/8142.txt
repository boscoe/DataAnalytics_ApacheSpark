By MICHAEL CIEPLYAUG. 5, 2015
LOS ANGELES — Cheryl Boone Isaacs was elected to a third one-year term as president of the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences on Tuesday, the Academy said. Academy presidents may serve up to four consecutive years under the group’s rules, and have typically been re-elected by its governors if they choose to return.Ms. Isaacs, a member of the Academy’s public relations branch, is joined by a slate of newly elected or re-elected officers that includes Jeffrey Kurland as first vice-president; John Bailey, Kathleen Kennedy and Bill Kroyer as vice presidents; Jim Gianopulos as treasurer; and Phil Robinson as secretary.The group’s annual Academy Awards are granted by its approximately 6,000 voting members, drawn from the ranks of film professionals both in the United States and abroad.A version of this article appears in print on August 6, 2015, on Page C3 of the New York edition with the headline: Motion Picture Academy Re-elects Its President.
