By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSOCT. 9, 2015
Air France sent an unusual appeal Friday to its passengers worldwide, insisting that the violent, clothes-ripping protests against executives earlier should not scar the airline’s reputation. Its appeal came on the day unions and Air France management resumed negotiations over hotly contested job cuts for the first time since Monday, when activists assaulted airline executives and left two of them shirtless and clambering over a fence to safety. “I am sure that like all of us, you were shocked by the events,” Air France said in an email to customers. “We are doing all we can to earn your trust.” Air France heaped blame for the “violent” acts on “isolated individuals” and said it needed to take “brave steps” to stay competitive.
