By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSAPRIL 30, 2015
Stocks logged their biggest drop in two weeks on Thursday as investors assessed some disappointing earnings news.The social network LinkedIn; Yelp, an online listings company; and Harman International, which makes audio systems for cars, were among the companies that fell sharply after posting disappointing results.The stock market has sagged this week after climbing to new peaks on Friday.While many investors remain optimistic that growth will resume later in the year, that belief has not yet been backed up by stronger economic data or healthy corporate earnings reports. In addition, a surge in the dollar in the first three months of the year is hurting company earnings for those that sell overseas, and a plunge in oil prices that lasted into January is hurting the energy sector.Gina Martin Adams, an equity strategist at Wells Fargo Securities, said that until there was an improvement in earnings numbers, “investors are kind of caught.”AdvertisementThe Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index dropped 21.34 points, or 1 percent, 2,085.51. That was its biggest drop since April 17.

That compares with a growth rate of 7.8 percent in the fourth quarter of 2014.Gains for stocks have slowed this year as the market entered the seventh year of a bull run. After Thursday’s losses, the S.& P. 500 index was up just 1.3 percent for the year and the Dow just 0.1 percent. The Nasdaq is doing slightly better, up 4.3 percent for the year.The market is struggling to push higher even as valuations have climbed. The price-to-earnings ratio, a measure of how much investors are willing to pay for every dollar of earnings, is now just above 17. Three years ago it was 12.5.“We’re treading water,” said Ronald Sanchez, chief investment officer at the Fiduciary Trust Company International. He said his expectations for returns were “much more modest” than at any time in the last three years.
