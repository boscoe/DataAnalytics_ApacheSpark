Anheuser-Busch InBev has completed a $46 billion, seven-part bond deal to help finance its acquisition of a fellow brewing company, SABMiller, in the second-largest corporate debt offering ever in the United States, according to Thomson Reuters.Investors had put in orders for $117 billion — the biggest backlog of orders ever, though the size of the deal fell just shy of breaking the record to date, $49 billion of debt raised in a deal by Verizon in 2013 to finance the acquisition of a 45 percent stake in its wireless unit that was held by Vodafone of Britain.Investors have rushed for a piece of the investment-grade Anheuser-Busch InBev deal as global stock markets have been turbulent at the start of the year and sinking commodity markets have left investors reeling.Brendan Hanley, Bank of America Merrill Lynch’s co-head of investment-grade capital markets in the Americas, said the Anheuser-Busch InBev bond offering was attractive to investors because of the “strength of its credit profile, global brands and strong track record with bondholders.”AdvertisementA merger boom last year is setting up a string of big bond transactions in 2016 as companies finance those purchases.Please verify you're not a robot by clicking the box.Invalid email address.
