The company is expected to spend more than $450 million on original programming this year, up 88 percent from $243 million in 2014, according to the MoffettNathanson research firm.Also this week, Netflix announced a new original drama series called “Montauk,” written and directed by Matt and Ross Duffer, who are brothers. Set in 1980, the series of eight hourlong episodes explores a mystery surrounding the disappearance of a young boy in Montauk, L.I.The company also announced that it had ordered a sketch comedy show, “With Bob and David,” from Bob Odenkirk of “Better Call Saul” and David Cross of “Arrested Development.” The series, of four half-hour episodes, follows the story of two characters who were dishonorably discharged from the Navy Seals and are making sketch comedy.
