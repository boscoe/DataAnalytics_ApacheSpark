By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSFEB. 23, 2015
The slump in crude oil prices and disappointing home sales data nudged stocks mostly lower on Monday, pulling the market back from last week’s closing highs.The Dow Jones industrial average and Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index spent much of the day hovering slightly below their recent highs. But the Nasdaq mounted a late-afternoon comeback that extended its win streak to nine days.Oil drilling companies and homebuilders notched broad declines, while traders bid up shares in utilities stocks.Investors were looking ahead to the start of a two-day round of congressional testimony by the Federal Reserve’s chairwoman, Janet L. Yellen. The remarks could provide insight into when the central bank will begin raising its key interest rate from near zero.Advertisement“The markets are in a holding pattern,” said Erik Davidson, chief investment officer of Wells Fargo Private Bank. “We’ll have some very interesting information coming up from Janet Yellen tomorrow and Wednesday, so the markets are looking at that very closely.”AdvertisementThe Dow ended down 23.60 points, or 0.1 percent, to 18,116.84; and the S.&P. 500 fell 0.64 point, or 0.03 percent, to 2,109.66.The Nasdaq gained 5.01 points, or 0.1 percent, to 4,960.97. The index is now within 87 points of its dot-com-era closing high, not adjusting for inflation, from March 2000.
