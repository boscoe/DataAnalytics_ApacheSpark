By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSAUG. 9, 2016
Stocks on Wall Street recovered from a late-afternoon slide to eke out small gains on Tuesday, nudging the Nasdaq composite to its second record close in less than a week.The three major stock indexes, all of which set new highs last Friday, rebounded from a slight decline on Monday.Investors focused on the latest batch of company earnings, bidding up health care, telecommunications and consumer-focused companies most.

The Dow Jones industrial average added 3.76 points, or 0.02 percent, to 18,533.05. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index rose 0.85 points, or 0.04 percent, to 2,181.74. The Nasdaq composite index gained 12.34 points, or 0.2 percent, to 5,225.48. That’s up less than 0.1 percent from its most recent high last Friday.AdvertisementEnergy was the biggest laggard, as crude oil prices closed lower, backtracking after an early rally. Traders also sold off shares in department store chains after Gap reported lower sales figures.AdvertisementFor the year, the Dow is now up 6.4 percent, while the S.&P. is up 6.7 percent and the Nasdaq is up 4.4 percent.The stock market has bounced back in recent weeks, buoyed by strong job growth in the United States, improved company earnings and persistently low inflation and interest rates.

On Tuesday, stocks in the United States got some help early on from a rally in European markets and in Asia, where most of the indexes closed higher.Data out of China showing that consumer price growth declined in July for the third month in a row helped fuel expectations among investors that Beijing would pump out more stimulus in a bid to soften the slowdown in its economy, the world’s second-largest after the United States.On Wall Street, traders bid up shares in several companies that posted strong quarterly results.Valeant Pharmaceuticals surged 25.4 percent after the Canadian drug maker reaffirmed its earnings outlook for the year, despite reporting a wider second-quarter loss. The company said it was undergoing a restructuring.Retailers didn’t fare as well.Gap sank 6.2 percent after reporting lower sales in the second quarter and in July. Kohl’s also fell, shedding 4.2 percent, and Target slid 3.2 percent.
                Over the last two and a half years, the oil industry experienced its deepest downturn since at least the 1990s.            The home furnishings store Wayfair slumped 19.6 percent after posting a wider-than-expected loss in the second quarter.AdvertisementAn early rally in United States crude oil prices evaporated. Benchmark crude fell 25 cents, or 0.6 percent, to close at $42.77 a barrel in New York. Brent crude, used to price international oils, slid 39 cents, or 0.9 percent, to close at $45 a barrel in London.The slide in oil weighed on several oil and gas companies. NRG Energy fell 5.1 percent, while Southwestern Energy shed 3.6 percent. Chesapeake Energy also declined 4 percent.The DAX in Germany rose 2.5 percent, while the CAC 40 in France gained 1.2 percent. The British FTSE 100 added 0.6 percent.
                A glut of crude oil on the markets is pushing the price of oil down to levels not seen since the global financial crisis.            Markets in Asia were mostly higher. Japan’s Nikkei 225 index rose 0.7 percent, while the South Korean Kospi climbed 0.6 percent. The Hang Seng in Hong Kong fell 0.1 percent, while China’s Shanghai composite index gained 0.7 percent. Stocks in Australia, Taiwan and the Philippines were also higher.In metals trading, the price of gold rose $5.60, or 0.4 percent, to $1,339 an ounce. Silver added 6 cents, or 0.3 percent, to $19.91 an ounce. Copper slipped 2 cents, or 0.7 percent, to $2.15 a pound.Bond prices rose.
