By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 14, 2016
Stocks barely budged on Monday on Wall Street, finishing mixed as lower oil prices pulled energy companies down while hotels and travel-related companies rose.Trading was quiet ahead of the Federal Reserve’s meeting this week, which is expected to shed some light on the possibility of a future increase in benchmark interest rates.The market “seems to quiet down ahead of the Fed,” said Peter Stournaras, a BlackRock portfolio manager.American stocks missed out on a global rally that lifted those in Europe and Asia. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index fell for just the second time this month.AdvertisementThe Dow Jones industrial average rose 15.82 points, or 0.1 percent, to 17,229.13. The S.&P. 500 lost 2.55 points, or 0.1 percent, to 2,019.64. The Nasdaq composite index gained 1.81 points to 4,750.28.AdvertisementStarwood Hotels shares jumped after a consortium led by the Anbang Insurance Group of China offered to buy the hotel chain, aiming to outflank an existing bid by Marriott International.Starwood said it would examine the new offer and its stock gained $5.51, or 7.8 percent, to $75.93.Marriott stock rose $2.04, or 3 percent, to $70.93. It will get a $400 million payment if Starwood backs out of their agreement.

The news lifted other travel-based companies. TripAdvisor shares gained $2.84, or 4.5 percent, to $66.54, and Expedia stock rose $1.46, or 1.3 percent, to $116.39.Energy and materials stocks fell with the prices of oil, gas and precious metals.Over the weekend, Iran’s oil minister dismissed the idea of a production freeze. United States benchmark crude fell $1.32, or 3.4 percent, to settle at $37.18 a barrel. Investors do not expect the Fed to raise interest rates, but they will look closely at its comments on the state of the domestic and global economies to discern clues about possible moves to come. In December, the Fed raised interest rates for the first time in almost a decade, but it left them unchanged in January.Big gainers on Monday included the Fresh Market grocery store chain, whose shares jumped $5.41, or 23.5 percent, to $28.39 after the private equity firm Apollo Global said it would buy the company for $1.36 billion, or $28.50 a share.Stock in GW Pharmaceuticals more than doubled after the company reported positive results from a late-stage study of its drug Epidiolex, an experimental seizure disorder treatment derived from a marijuana extract. The stock surged $46.25, or 120 percent, to $84.71. Shares of Zynerba Pharmaceuticals, which is also studying a drug based on synthetic compounds derived from cannabis, climbed $12.59, or 149 percent, to $21.03.Stocks in Europe rose after the eurozone had its biggest monthly increase in industrial production since 2009. The DAX in Germany rose 1.6 percent. The CAC 40 in France added 0.3 percent while the British FTSE 100 gained 0.6 percent.Chinese stocks rose after the chief of the China Securities Regulatory Commission said at a news conference over the weekend that it was too early to talk about reducing official support measures for the markets, according to the official news agency Xinhua. That suggests the government will continue to support Chinese equities.AdvertisementBond prices rose. The yield on the 10-year Treasury note slipped to 1.96 percent from 1.98 percent. The euro declined to $1.1096 from $1.1157 late Friday.
