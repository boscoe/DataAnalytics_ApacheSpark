AdvertisementSupported byBusiness BriefingBy The Associated PressThe Swiss mining giant Glencore has announced a $10.2 billion plan to cut debt and issue shares to adapt to plunging market prices. The plan involves issuing $2.5 billion of shares, as well as suspending its dividend, further reducing its capital expenditures and selling $2 billion in assets through the end of next year. Glencore said in a statement on Monday that the moves were “prudent” in the face of market volatility and speculation. The moves come on top of cuts to capital expenditures and costs announced last month. Advertisement
