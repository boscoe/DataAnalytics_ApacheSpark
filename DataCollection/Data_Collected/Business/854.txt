AdvertisementSupported byBusiness BriefingBy Leslie PickerKeyCorp is close to buying the First Niagara Financial Group, people briefed on the matter said, the latest in a string of transactions that may signal bank deals are back. KeyCorp, based in Cleveland, is looking to pay slightly more than the almost $4 billion market value of First Niagara, which is based in Buffalo, said the people, who spoke on condition of anonymity. The deal could be announced within the next few days, they said. Bank mergers have increased recently as midsize companies seek to contend with lower interest rates that are cutting into their margins from loans. Earlier Thursday, New York Community Bancorp agreed to buy the Astoria Financial Corporation for about $2 billion. In August, BB&T said it planned to acquire National Penn Bancshares for $1.8 billion. Shares of First Niagara rose 3.5 percent in after-hours trading after The Wall Street Journal earlier reported the talks. Advertisement
