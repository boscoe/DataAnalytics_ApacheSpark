To lead its business development efforts, the Ford Motor Company is turning to a longtime Wall Street banker and analyst with a specialty in the auto industry. Fordsaid on Tuesday that it had hired John Casesa as group vice president for global strategy. Mr. Casesa, 52, will help oversee Ford’s business strategy, potentially including acquisitions and other partnerships. He will report directly to Mark Fields, the company’s chief executive.Mr. Casesa, who will start on March 1, was most recently the head of automotive investment banking at Guggenheim Partners, the banking and investment firm. He previously worked as a research analyst at Merrill Lynch and Schroders, though not all of his experience lies on Wall Street: He also worked at General Motors as a product planning analyst.“John knows business and the auto industry inside and out,” Mr. Fields said in a statement. “His deep experience and relationships will help guide and shape our global strategies — particularly as we challenge today’s business model and push to innovate to make us even stronger tomorrow.”A version of this article appears in print on 02/18/2015, on page B5 of the NewYork edition with the headline: Ford Hires Casesa.
