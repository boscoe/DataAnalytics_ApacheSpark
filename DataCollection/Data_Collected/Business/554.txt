By LIZ MOYEROCT. 19, 2015
Geoffrey Graber, a former Justice Department lawyer who oversaw the government’s investigation of the packaging and sale of mortgage-backed securities by banks in the years leading up to the 2008 financial crisis, has joined the law firm Cohen Milstein Sellers & Toll as a partner.Mr. Graber, 42, left the department five weeks ago. He was deputy associate attorney general and director of its working group on mortgage securities matters. The investigation of the mortgage securities industry led to a $16.65 billion settlement by Bank of America last year, the largest to date. There was also a $13 billion settlement with JPMorgan Chase & Company and a $7 billion settlement with Citigroup.While at the Justice Department, Mr. Graber also led the government’s investigation of the ratings agency Standard & Poor’s over its assessments of asset-backed securities. That case settled this year, with S.&P. agreeing to pay more than $1 billion.Before joining the Justice Department, he was a securities litigation lawyer at the San Francisco law firm Morrison & Foerster LLP. Cohen Milstein represents consumers and investors in class-action lawsuits.
