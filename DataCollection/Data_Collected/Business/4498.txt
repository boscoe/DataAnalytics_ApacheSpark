By DEALBOOKMARCH 18, 2015
The Microsemi Corporation announced on Wednesday that it had reached an agreement to acquire Vitesse Semiconductor for $389 million, furthering a drive toward consolidation in the chip-making inudstry.The bid of $5.28 a share, through a cash tender offer, represents a premium of 36 percent over Vitesse’s closing price on Tuesday and a premium of 32 percent over the 30-day average closing price. Under the terms of the deal, Vitesse has until April 8 to solicit a superior counterbid, in what is known as a go-shop provision.Based in Aliso Viejo, Calif., Microsemi is known largely as a supplier to the military. In 2014, some 27 percent of its sales were in defense and security.Vitesse, which has headquarters in Camarillo, Calif., supplies products to a number of network equipment makers, including Alcatel-Lucent, Cisco Systems, Ericsson and Hewlett-Packard.Bank of America Merrill Lynch is providing debt financing for the acquisition. Deutsche Bank is assisting Vitesse on its go-shop process.Microsemi is being advised by Bank of America Merrill Lynch and the law firm O’Melveny & Myers.
