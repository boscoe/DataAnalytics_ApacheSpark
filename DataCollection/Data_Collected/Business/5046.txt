By CHAD BRAYAPRIL 8, 2015
LONDON — Labco, a French medical diagnostics firm, said on Wednesday that it had filed to pursue an initial public offering in Paris in the first half of this year.The company said it would seek to list its shares on the Euronext exchange in Paris, subject to market conditions and approval by the Financial Markets Authority of France.Labco, based in Paris, said it expected to raise at least 320 million euros, or almost $350 million, in the offering, according to documents filed with the French market regulator.“We are delighted to reach this important milestone today,” Philippe Charrier, the Labco chief executive, said in a news release. “With this planned I.P.O., our aim is to continue the group’s growth dynamic and the rollout of our medical project based on technological innovation and operational excellence.”AdvertisementLabco said that it would use proceeds from the offering to repay part of its debt and to invest in growth opportunities.Please verify you're not a robot by clicking the box.Invalid email address.
