By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 18, 2015
Gov. Chris Christie of New Jersey signed a measure Wednesday that would allow the sale of Tesla and other zero-emission cars in the state. The new law comes about a year after the New Jersey Motor Vehicle Commission issued regulations requiring cars to be sold through franchises. Tesla markets its vehicles directly to consumers, and the company’s chief, Elon Musk, complained about the New Jersey regulations last year, saying franchises that get most of their revenue from gasoline-powered cars would have little incentive to sell electric vehicles.A version of this brief appears in print on March 19, 2015, on Page B2 of the New York edition with the headline: New Jersey to Allow Sale of Tesla Electric Cars.
