By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSNOV. 16, 2015
Stocks rallied broadly on Monday, ending a three-day losing streak as investors moved past concerns that the terrorist attacks in Paris could hurt the global economy.Oil and gas stocks were among the biggest gainers. The price of crude rose. Traders also bid up shares in military contractors, while travel-related stocks slumped.The gains followed an uneven day in European markets, which shook off an early loss and ended mixed.“It all comes down to, are consumers going to be staying at home and not out spending money because they’re afraid that if they go anywhere they’re going to be victims of a terrorist attack?” said Scott Wren, senior global equity strategist for Wells Fargo Investment Institute. “That might be the case if you saw a series of these things, but hopefully that’s not what’s going to happen, and the economy is not going to be affected.”The Dow Jones industrial average rose 237.77 points, or 1.4 percent, to 17,483.01. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index gained 30.15 points, or 1.5 percent, to 2,053.19. The Nasdaq composite index added 56.73 points, or 1.2 percent, to 4,984.62.AdvertisementThe rally helped lift the market after its biggest weekly loss since August.The New York Stock Exchange paused for a moment of silence before regular trading. The major market indexes started lower but rebounded in the first 10 minutes as investors piled into shares of consumer staples, a category that includes Coca-Cola and Procter & Gamble, the household products maker.

Stocks wavered, then began to climb steadily by midday as crude oil prices rose, pushing up energy stocks. The energy sector in the S.&P. 500 surged 3.3 percent.AdvertisementConsol Energy, a producer of natural gas and coal, added 56 cents, or 7.6 percent, to close at $7.96. Cabot Oil & Gas gained $1.82, or 8.8 percent, to $22.56, while Range Resources rose $2.90, or 9.1 percent, to $34.61.The energy sector has been the biggest laggard among industries this year, down 14.9 percent, as the decline in oil prices has deepened.Benchmark United States crude oil climbed $1, or 2.5 percent, to close at $41.74 a barrel in New York. Brent crude, used to price international oils, rose 9 cents, or 0.2 percent, to close at $44.56 a barrel in London.News that Marriott International had agreed to acquire its rival Starwood Hotels and Resorts Worldwide for $12.2 billion received a mixed response. The prospects of more military action to counteract terror threats helped lift military contractors. Northrop Grumman gained $7.87, or 4.4 percent, to $186.69, while Lockheed Martin added $7.51, or 3.5 percent, to $220.67. Raytheon rose $4.84, or 4.1 percent, to $121.11.Traders unloaded some travel-related stocks. Priceline Group slid $30.88, or 2.4 percent, to $1,266.87, and Expedia declined $2.67, or 2.1 percent, to $122.53. Carnival dipped 79 cents, or 1.5 percent, to $50.77.
