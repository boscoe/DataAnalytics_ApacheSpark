By CHAD BRAYSEPT. 12, 2016
LONDON — HarbourVest Partners, a Boston private equity firm, said on Monday that it had offered to acquire SVG Capital, a much smaller British rival, for 1.02 billion pounds, or about $1.35 billion, in cash.Under the terms of the offer, SVG Capital shareholders would receive 650 pence a share, a 14.7 percent premium to the firm’s closing price on Friday.“While our offer does not currently have the recommendation of the board of SVG Capital, we look forward to a constructive dialogue with them in order to crystallize the certainty of value, today and in cash, to its shareholders,” David Atterbury, a HarbourVest managing director in London, said in a news release.HarbourVest said that it had support for the deal from investors holding about 43 percent of SVG Capital’s shares, including an irrevocable agreement to acquire about 20 percent of the company’s stock.AdvertisementThe deal to acquire 20 percent would remain binding even if SVG were to receive a higher offer from a competing bidder, HarbourVest said.Please verify you're not a robot by clicking the box.Invalid email address.
