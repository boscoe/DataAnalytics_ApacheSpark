By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJAN. 13, 2015
Stocks swung from gains to losses and almost back again.Wall Street opened Tuesday higher, encouraged by news on hiring and small-business confidence. Then it swooned in the afternoon as oil prices fell once more. The pendulum swung back late in the day as oil gained in after-hours trading, and stocks ended with small losses.From peak to trough, the Dow Jones industrial average swung 425 points.Stocks are having a jumpy start to the year as investors assess the effects of the collapse in the price of oil. The outlook for global growth also remains fuzzy as the recovery continues in the United States but economies in Europe and Asia struggle.Even though market volatility has picked up at the start of the year, investors should stay calm, said Janet Dougherty, a global investment specialist in Chicago at JPMorgan Private Bank. “You have to remember that we’ve been through an extended period where there wasn’t a lot of volatility in the equity markets, and now we’re just getting back to normalized levels,” she said.The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index fell 5.23 points, or 0.3 percent, to 2,023.03. The Dow slipped 27.16 points, or 0.2 percent, to 17,613.68. The Nasdaq fell 3.21 points, or less than 0.1 percent, to 4,661.50.
