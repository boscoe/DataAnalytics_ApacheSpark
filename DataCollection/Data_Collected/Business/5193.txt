By EMILY STEELAPRIL 14, 2015
Sunday’s premiere of the fifth season of HBO’s “Game of Thrones” drew about 8 million viewers live and on the same day, according to Nielsen.The audience is likely to grow in the coming days as viewers tune in to watch the program, recorded and on demand.“Game of Thrones” is HBO’s most-watched original series ever.The fourth season of the series, which ended in June, averaged 19 million cumulative viewers.The start of the new season was an important development for the network, which introduced its new streaming service to coincide with the debut.Two other popular HBO series also returned for new seasons on Sunday. The comedy “Silicon Valley” attracted about 2.1 million viewers watching live and on the same day, according to Nielsen. “Veep” drew about 1.1 million viewers.HBO announced Monday that it has renewed “Veep” for a fifth season and “Silicon Valley” for a third season.A version of this article appears in print on April 15, 2015, on Page C3 of the New York edition with the headline: Game of Thrones’ Draws Eight Million.
