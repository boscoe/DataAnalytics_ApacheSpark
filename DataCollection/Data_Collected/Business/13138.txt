By REUTERSAPRIL 12, 2016
The Labor Department said on Tuesday that import prices gained 0.2 percent last month after a downwardly revised 0.4 percent drop in February, the first time since June that import prices rose and the largest increase since May. Prices of imported products were down 6.2 percent in the 12 months through March, the smallest gain since December 2014, suggesting that import deflation was ebbing. Export prices were unchanged in March after declining 0.5 percent in February.
