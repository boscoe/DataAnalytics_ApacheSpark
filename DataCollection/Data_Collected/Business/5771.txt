By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMAY 4, 2015
The stock market rose on Monday as investors assessed some positive earnings reports.Cognizant Technology Solutions, a technology consulting business, was the biggest gainer in the Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index after it reported earnings that beat the expectations of Wall Street analysts and raised its outlook for earnings and sales for the year. The stock climbed $3.64, or 6.2 percent, to $62.78. Tyson Foods, the maker of Jimmy Dean sausage, was another company that gained after posting strong earnings.Corporate earnings for the first quarter have surpassed analysts’ expectations, growing slightly rather than contracting, as had been forecast. That’s helping increase demand for stocks over all, keeping alive a bull market that is now in its seventh year.“The picture isn’t too bad,” said Sean Lynch, co-head of global equity strategy for Wells Fargo Investment Institute.The S.&P. 500 rose 6.20 points on Monday, or 0.3 percent, to 2,114.49. The Dow Jones industrial average climbed 46.34 points, or 0.3 percent, to 18,070.40. The Nasdaq gained 11.54 points, or 0.2 percent, to 5,016.93.

Investors follow the survey closely in hopes of gaining insight into when the Federal Reserve may raise interest rates.Fed policy makers have held their benchmark interest rate close to zero since 2008. Most investors expect that they will refrain from raising rates until the second half of the year at the earliest to allow the economy more time to strengthen.Although the economy slowed in the first three months of the year, earnings are going up. Average earnings per share for S.&P. 500 companies are forecast to rise by 1.7 percent in the first quarter, according to data from S&P Capital IQ. While the pace of growth has slowed from the final quarter of 2014, it is a much better performance than analysts were expecting.
