By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJULY 28, 2016
Stocks on Wall Street had another day of meager gains on Thursday as investors worked through a new batch of mixed company earnings, including results from Facebook, Ford and Whole Foods.Cautious investors are looking ahead to a meeting of the Bank of Japan on Friday, which is expected to result in an announcement of more stimulus for that country, the world’s third-largest economy.The Dow Jones industrial average lost 15.82 points, or 0.1 percent, to 18,456.35. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index rose 3.48 points, or 0.2 percent, to 2,170.06 and the Nasdaq composite rose 15.17 points, or 0.3 percent, to 5,154.98.

After the market’s run-up this month, investors have mostly been in wait-and-see mode this week. While it has been a busy week for corporate earnings, and individual stocks have moved a lot, the overall market has been relatively quiet. Market strategists have said that stocks have gotten expensive in recent days, and many investors are waiting for earnings to play out before making any major moves.AdvertisementOnce again, technology companies were front and center. Facebook shares rose $1.66, or 1.4 percent, to $125. The social networking company reported earnings that more than doubled from a year earlier, topping analysts’ views, as well as a 15 percent rise in monthly users. However, the shares had been trading much higher earlier in the session.AdvertisementNetSuite jumped $16.84, or 18.4 percent, to $108.41 after the computer software giant Oracle announced it was buying the company for $9.3 billion. NetSuite and Oracle both provide high-end software, but NetSuite specializes more in cloud computing while Oracle is heavy on mainframe database software. Oracle rose 26 cents, or 0.6 percent, to $41.19 on the news.Investors got results from Google’s parent company, Alphabet, and Amazon after the market closed on Thursday. Both companies’ results beat analysts’ expectations, and stocks of both were higher in after-hours trading.

Investors are now hoping for new stimulus efforts from the Bank of Japan, which is expected to vote Friday on expanding monetary policy measures aimed at reviving sputtering growth. Japan’s prime minister, Shinzo Abe, has announced 28 trillion yen (about $266 billion) in extra government spending to jump-start growth, but details are uncertain.Bond prices fell slightly, having jumped a day earlier after a decision by the Federal Reserve to keep interest rates steady. Investors are now looking to the September meeting.“We think September has a good chance of a rate hike, and this week’s announcement confirmed that,” said Kristina Hooper, head of United States investment strategies at Allianz Global Investors.The yield on the benchmark United States 10-year note was 1.51 percent, up from 1.50 percent the day before.

In other trading, Whole Foods Market, the high-end supermarket chain, fell $3.03, or 9 percent, to $30.61 after reporting that sales declined in the quarter. The company is facing more competition from other supermarkets that have been increasing their organic produce options.AdvertisementFord lost $1.13, or 8.2 percent, to $12.71 after the company reported a 9 percent drop in profit as sales slowed in the United States and struggled in China. The company warned that its full-year guidance might need to be cut.In currencies, the euro rose to $1.1074 from $1.1051 and the dollar rose to 105.45 yen from 1.0520 yen.In the energy market, benchmark crude fell 78 cents to close at $41.14 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange. Brent crude, used to price international oils, lost 77 cents to close at $42.70 a barrel in London.In the metals markets, gold rose $5.60, to $1,332.30 an ounce.
