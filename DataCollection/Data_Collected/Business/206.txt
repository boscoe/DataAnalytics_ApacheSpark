AdvertisementSupported byBy Leslie PickerFreeport-McMoRan, the mining and energy company, said on Tuesday that it might separate its oil and gas unit. The announcement comes six weeks after the activist investor Carl C. Icahn disclosed an 8.5 percent stake in the company.Freeport-McMoRan said that “following constructive discussions with many of its largest shareholders,” it was considering selling a minority interest in the oil and gas business through a public offering or spinning off the unit to shareholders. The company is also looking into joint ventures and ways to cut spending for the unit, it said.Additionally, Freeport-McMoRan said it had reduced the board to nine members from 16.Freeport-McMoRan’s stock price has tumbled nearly 52 percent so far this year as commodity prices have slumped. The company said in August that it would reduce production levels and expenditures to help weather the weakness in the markets.The company, which says it is the world’s largest publicly traded copper producer, plans to also focus on that commodity and reducing its debt load.In early morning trading, shares of Freeport-McMoRan were up nearly 4 percent.Advertisement
