AdvertisementSupported byBusiness BriefingBy The Associated PressThe chairman of Yum Brands, David C. Novak, will retire in May, ending a three-decade career with the restaurant giant. The company said on Tuesday that it planned to name one of its board members to succeed Mr. Novak, 63, at its annual meeting. Mr. Novak began his tenure in 1986 as marketing chief for Pizza Hut when the chain was still owned by PepsiCo. When PepsiCo spun off KFC, Pizza Hut and Taco Bell in 1997, he became vice chairman and president of the new company. He was named chief of Yum Brands in 1999, and chairman in 2000. Advertisement
