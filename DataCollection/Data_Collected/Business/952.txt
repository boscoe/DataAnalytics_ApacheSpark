AdvertisementSupported byBy The Associated PressStocks posted solid gains on Monday, adding to last month’s big advances and pushing the Dow Jones industrial average into positive territory for 2015. Health care stocks were among the winners as the drug companies Pfizer and AbbVie climbed, while energy stocks rose sharply in a late rally.The Dow Jones industrial average rose 165.22 points, or 0.9 percent, to 17,828.76. The gain made the Dow the last of the three major American market indexes to return to positive territory for 2015.The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index rose 24.69 points, or 1.2 percent, to 2,104.05, and the Nasdaq composite index rose 73.40 points, or 1.5 percent, to 5,127.15. Health care stocks continued to rally. Pfizer rose $1.24, or 3.7 percent, to $35.06, the second-biggest gain in the Dow, behind Chevron, which rose $4.08, or 4.5 percent, to $94.96. Pfizer’s shares slumped late last week on word that it was in talks to buy Allergan, a competitor. AbbVie, which surged 10 percent Friday after announcing strong third-quarter results, climbed $3.83, or 6.4 percent, to $63.38 Monday after analysts at Morgan Stanley upgraded the stock.Health care stocks gained 2 percent, nearly twice as much as the S.&P. 500 index. Energy stocks rose even more, 2.4 percent. Exxon Mobil, Chevron, Chesapeake Energy and others rose 3 percent or more.Visa, the payments processor, was among the biggest decliners. It fell $2.36, or 3 percent, to close at $75.22 after saying it would buy its sister company Visa Europe in a deal that could be worth more than $23 billion. Visa warned that 2016 earnings could be hurt as it finances the debt and stock to pay for the deal.Investors are focusing on quarterly earnings reports, which continue this week. Earnings season is nearly done, and the stock market has recovered significantly as results have been somewhat better than expected.With 340 companies out of the S.&P. 500 reporting, third-quarter earnings are down 2.2 percent from 2014. When earnings season began, analysts predicted earnings would be down 5.2 percent.In other company news, Hewlett-Packard officially split in two over the weekend. HP, which will sell personal computers and printers, rose $1.59, or 13 percent, to $13.83. Hewlett-Packard Enterprise will sell commercial computer systems, software and technology services. Its stock edged down 23 cents, or 1.6 percent, to $14.49. Chipotle Mexican Grill stumbled as an E. coli outbreak linked to restaurants in Oregon and Washington spread. Chipotle lost $16.23, or 2.5 percent, to $624. In other markets, United States crude oil fell 45 cents, or 1 percent, to close at $46.14 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange. Brent crude, which is used to price international oils, slid 77 cents, or 1.6 percent, to $48.79 a barrel in London.In other energy trading, wholesale gasoline edged up 0.4 cent, to $1.375 a gallon in New York; heating oil declined a penny, to $1.507 a gallon; and natural gas fell 6.5 cents, to $2.256 per 1,000 cubic feet.Government bond prices fell.
