By STEPHANIE STROMJUNE 1, 2015
Stepping into line with many of its competitors, Foster Farms, one of the nation’s largest poultry producers, is moving to stop using antibiotics that are also administered to humans.Its compliance with the new policy will be independently audited, the privately held company said on Monday.Foster is also introducing a line of organic poultry products.Poultry companies like Perdue and Tyson Foods have previously announced plans to eliminate human antibiotics from their processes, as major customers like McDonald’s and Chick-fil-A have demanded antibiotic-free meat.
