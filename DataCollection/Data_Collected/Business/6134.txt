By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMAY 18, 2015
The National Association of Home Builders/Wells Fargo builder-sentiment index released on Monday fell to 54 this month, down from 56 in April. Readings above 50 indicate more builders view sales conditions as good. The latest reading is up nine points from a year ago. Measures of current sales conditions and traffic by prospective buyers also fell. Builders’ outlook for sales of single-family homes over the next six months edged higher. The index of future sales expectations has been tracking upward all year.A version of this brief appears in print on May 19, 2015, on Page B2 of the New York edition with the headline: Home Builder Optimism Dips, but Outlook for Future Is Rosier.
