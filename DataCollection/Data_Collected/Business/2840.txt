LONDON — Suzlon Group, the Indian wind turbine maker, said on Thursday that it had agreed to sell its German business to the private equity firm Centerbridge Partners for 1 billion euros, or about $1.2 billion.Suzlon said the all-cash sale of a 100 percent stake in Senvion SE is in line with its strategy to reduce its debt and focus on domestic opportunities, high-growth regions like the United States and emerging markets.Senvion, based in Hamburg, Germany, manufactures onshore and offshore wind turbines and employs more than 3,400 people. Following the deal, Suzlon will retain Senvion’s license for offshore technologies in the Indian market.“Senvion is a company with impressive technology and leading market positions,” Stefan Kowski, a Centerbridge managing director, said in a news release. “The global market environment for renewable energies is promising for a wind turbine manufacturer.”The transaction is subject to regulatory approval and is expected to close before the end of Suzlon’s current fiscal year, which ends in March.Based in Pune, India, Suzlon is one of the world’s largest manufacturers of wind turbines, with operations in more than 31 countries and more than 10,000 employees.
