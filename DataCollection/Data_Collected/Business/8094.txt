AdvertisementSupported byBusiness BriefingBy ReutersKellogg, the 109-year-old company whose cereals have been a popular breakfast choice for decades, said on Tuesday it was aiming to stop using artificial colors and flavors in its cereals and snack bars by the end of 2018. The world’s biggest breakfast-cereal maker is the latest in a string of American food companies to bow to growing pressure to remove synthetic ingredients from products because of health concerns. Kellogg reported second-quarter revenue on Tuesday that topped analysts’ expectations, but the 5.1 percent decline in sales was the seventh time in eight quarters that sales had fallen. Advertisement
