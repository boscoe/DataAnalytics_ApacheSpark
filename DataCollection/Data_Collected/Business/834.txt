By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSOCT. 28, 2015
The stock market ended solidly higher on Wednesday, recovering from an afternoon wobble after the Federal Reserve indicated that it would consider raising its benchmark interest rate at its December meeting.Bond yields rose and the dollar climbed against other currencies as traders anticipated that higher rates were on the way.Fed policy makers decided to leave interest rates unchanged after a two-day meeting, but they also said they would consider raising rates at the last meeting of 2015 if the economy looked as if it were still in good shape.Bob Doll, chief equity strategist at Nuveen Investments, said October and November employment data would feature prominently in the Fed’s deliberations.

“If they’re O.K., I think the Fed will finally, finally, finally go,” Mr. Doll said.The Dow Jones industrial average had been up 130 points just before the Fed released its policy statement at 2 p.m., Eastern time, then briefly retreated into the red after the statement came out.AdvertisementBy late afternoon, however, the Dow had more than recovered its earlier gain, closing up 198.09 points, or 1.1 percent, to 17,779.52. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index gained 24.46 points, or 1.2 percent, to 2,090.35. The Nasdaq picked up 65.55 points, or 1.3 percent, to 5,095.69.AdvertisementThe dollar rose against the yen and the euro. If the Fed does begin to raise rates in December, it will do so as central banks in Europe and Japan continue to pursue stimulus programs. The euro fell sharply against the dollar, to $1.0917, from $1.1040 late Tuesday. The dollar rose to 121.15 yen, from 120.40 yen.In bond trading, the yield on the 10-year Treasury note rose to 2.10 percent, from 2.04 percent the day before. Yields on Treasury notes affect rates on mortgages and many other kinds of loans.Oil prices soared after Pemex, the national oil company of Mexico, said it received permission to swap crude oil with the United States. That could represent a step toward ending the American ban on exporting crude. The price of oil had been sliding since early October and reached its lowest level in two months on Tuesday. The 6.3 percent gain on Wednesday was the largest increase since Aug. 31.

United States crude climbed $2.74, to $45.94 a barrel in New York. Brent crude, which is used to price international oils, rose $2.24, or 4.8 percent, to $49.05 a barrel in London.While the Fed news heavily influenced the broader market, earnings reports continued to drive many individual stocks. The military contractor Northrop Grumman surged $9.90, or 5.5 percent, to $190.50 after it said it received a contract worth up to $80 billion to build new bombers for the Air Force. Northrop Grumman stock has been trading at record highs.The tech giant Apple gained $4.73, or 4.1 percent, to $119.28 after the company reported another quarter of record earnings late Tuesday, bolstered by surging sales in China. Apple also forecast healthy iPhone sales during the holidays.The American International Group advanced $2.97, or 4.9 percent, to $63.89 after the billionaire investor Carl C. Icahn said he had taken a “large” stake in the company. Mr. Icahn said A.I.G., an insurance conglomerate, should split into three separate businesses. Mr. Icahn said his view had support from other major shareholders.Metal prices increased. Gold rose $10.30, or 0.9 percent, to $1,177.10 an ounce and silver added 43 cents, or 2.7 percent, to $16.29 an ounce.
