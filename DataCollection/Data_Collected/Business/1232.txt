By CHAD BRAYNOV. 11, 2015
LONDON — Amundi, one of the largest asset managers in Europe, said on Wednesday that it had raised 1.5 billion euros, or about $1.6 billion, in its initial public offering of stock in Paris.The company, a French asset manager, was created in 2010 by a merger of the asset management operations of two French banks, Crédit Agricole and Société Générale.In a news release on Wednesday, Amundi said it had priced an offering of 33.4 million shares at €45 a share. The asset manager said this month that it expected to price its shares in a range of €42 to €52.50 a share.The offering valued Amundi at €7.5 billion.
