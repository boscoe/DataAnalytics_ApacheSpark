By MICHAEL J. de la MERCEDMAY 21, 2015
SAN FRANCISCO — Hewlett-Packard announced on Thursday that it had sold a majority stake in its Chinese networking business to Tsinghua University, in a move to deal with Chinese government restrictions on foreign technology companies.By selling to Tsinghua, one of China’s top universities, HP will place the division in the hands of local owners and maintain its ability to sell to state-controlled businesses and government offices.Under the terms of the deal, HP will sell a 51 percent stake in its Chinese enterprise technology to Tsinghua Holdings for about $2.3 billion.The division, which sells networking equipment like servers and storage, reported about $3.1 billion in adjusted revenue and $400 million in adjusted operating profit last year.“Partnering with Tsinghua, one of China’s most respected institutions, the new H3C will be able to drive even greater innovation for China, in China,” Meg Whitman, HP’s chairwoman and chief executive, said in a statement. “The combined company will build upon an extensive and valuable patent portfolio, best-in-class products and customer focus, and Tsinghua’s world-class research capability.”HP will hold onto other operations in China, including enterprise software and printing businesses.The deal is expected to close by year’s end.We’re interested in your feedback on this page.
