By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSFEB. 8, 2016
India says its economy grew 7.3 percent in the last three months of 2015, a rate of expansion that puts the South Asian nation ahead of China’s 6.8 percent growth during the same period. The data, released on Monday by the Indian Central Statistics Office, shows a slight downturn for the country from the previous quarter, when India registered growth of 7.4 percent.A version of this article appears in print on February 9, 2016, on Page B2 of the New York edition with the headline: Business Briefing; India’s Economy Up 7.3 Percent for Quarter, Surpassing China’s.
