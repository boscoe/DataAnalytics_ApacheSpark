AdvertisementSupported byBy The Associated PressStocks fell broadly on Tuesday as investors around the world fled risky bets, alarmed by worrisome trade data in China and a slump in crude oil prices.Investors dumped stocks from the start of trading, then picked up their selling near the close.Seven of the 10 industry sectors of the Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index dropped, putting an end to a five-day winning streak.As investors sought safety, Treasury bonds rose sharply.Oil prices, which have been rising from 13-year lows in recent weeks, reversed course on Tuesday with a nearly 4 percent drop in United States crude. Energy stocks were hit hard. Stock in Murphy Oil plunged 15 percent, and shares of the oil rig operator Transocean lost 10 percent.A report overnight from China showing that exports and imports dropped last month helped revive fears that a slowdown in China could hurt the slowly strengthening American economy.“Where is the global growth going to come from?” asked Steven Ricchiuto, chief economist at Mizuho Securities. “There is no acceleration in growth.”The S.&P.’s 500-stock index fell 22.5 points, or 1.1 percent, to 1,979.26. The Nasdaq composite gave up 59.43 points, or 1.3 percent, to 4,648.83. The Dow Jones industrial average fell 109.85 points, or 0.6 percent, to 16,964.10.After dropping sharply earlier this year, stocks have been climbing as a series of reports on the United States economy — hiring, construction spending and manufacturing — suggested that the country might be able to buck a slowdown abroad.The S.&P. 500 is up 10 percent from mid-February.But E. William Stone, chief investment officer of PNC Asset Management Group, said investors had been worried that the climb was not sustainable, given the continuing weakness in overseas markets. The prolonged drop in oil prices and disappointing Chinese trade data also pushed many of them to sell.“We’re overbought,” Mr. Stone said. “People are taking some profits off of the larger run-up from the low.”The China data showed exports plunged 25 percent in February from a year earlier as weak global demand and a business shutdown during the Lunar New Year holiday depressed sales. Customs data also showed imports fell 14 percent.In overseas trading, nearly every major market fell. Japan’s Nikkei 225 dropped 0.8 percent and South Korea’s Kospi lost 0.6 percent.In Europe, France’s CAC 40, Germany’s DAX and Britain’s FTSE 100 each fell 0.9 percent.Investors are looking ahead to a policy announcement from the European Central Bank on Thursday. Further stimulus moves are expected, but the bank’s program of buying bonds and driving interest rates into negative territory has had mixed results so far.Among American stocks making big moves on Tuesday, stock in the hamburger chain Shake Shack plunged $5, or 12 percent, to $37.23 after the company delivered quarterly results and an outlook that disappointed investors.Urban Outfitters shares jumped $4.53, or 16 percent, to $32.69. Late on Monday, the retailer reported strong earnings during the holiday season.The euro fell to $1.1002 from $1.1011. The dollar edged down to 112.58 yen from 113.36 yen.Yields on United States government bonds fell sharply. The yield on the 10-year Treasury note fell to 1.83 percent from 1.90 percent late Monday.Benchmark United States crude fell $1.40 to $36.50 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange. It had jumped $1.98 on Monday. Brent crude, which is used to price international oils, fell $1.19, or 3 percent, to $39.65 a barrel.In other energy markets, wholesale gasoline fell less than a penny, to $1.388 a gallon; heating oil dropped 2.3 cents, to $1.20 a gallon; and natural gas rose 2.2 cents, to $1.712 per 1,000 cubic feet.Industrial and precious metals ended broadly lower. Gold slipped $1.10, to $1,262.90 an ounce; silver fell 24 cents, to $15.39 an ounce; and copper lost six cents, to $2.22 a pound.Advertisement
