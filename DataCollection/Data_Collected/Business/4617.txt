By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 23, 2015
A late turn pulled the stock market to a loss on Monday, as major indexes wavered after a strong run last week.The railroad Kansas City Southern slumped 8 percent, the largest fall in the Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index, after it trimmed its revenue estimates, citing declining fuel prices and the strengthening dollar. Its stock lost $9.21 to close at $106.48.Major indexes started higher in morning trading, settled into an afternoon lull, then dipped in the last 10 minutes of trading.The S.&P. 500 declined 3.68 points, or 0.2 percent, to close at 2,104.42. The Dow Jones industrial average lost 11.61 points, or 0.1 percent, to 18,116.04, and the Nasdaq composite index fell 15.44 points, or 0.3 percent, to 5,010.97.AdvertisementTraders watched a meeting in Europe between the leaders of Greece and Germany for signs of progress in Greece’s debt negotiations. Greece, facing a cash squeeze within weeks, is talking with its European lenders about what it must do to receive more loans.

Investors have pushed the S.&P. 500 to new highs despite concerns over Europe’s sluggish economy and slumping oil prices.“Greece hasn’t pulled it down; deflation hasn’t pulled it down,” Mr. McMillan said. “Unless the Federal Reserve says it’s going to raise interest rates in June, I just can’t see what’s going to pull it down.”Last week, the S.&P. 500 jumped nearly 3 percent, its largest weekly gain since early February. Investors cheered Wednesday when the Federal Reserve said that it was in no hurry to raise interest rates with inflation low.Among other companies making moves, the pharmaceutical company Gilead Sciences dropped after reports that it had told physicians that nine patients taking its hepatitis C treatments developed slow heartbeats and that one died. Gilead slid $2.03, or nearly 2 percent, to $100.26.Tenet Healthcare surged 5 percent after news that it planned a hospital venture with a private equity firm. Tenet’s stock gained $2.45, or nearly 5 percent, to $52.07.
