

      By KEVIN GRANVILLE

JUNE 18, 2016


State licensing rules foster a bizarre maze of obstacles for employment. Nearly 30 percent of the American work force requires a license to work, up from about 10 percent in the 1970s. Getting certifications can require months or years of expensive education and testing. Economists on the left and right agree that excessive licensing rules harm economic growth but the standards are fiercely defended by the groups that benefit from them.
