AdvertisementSupported byBy Emily SteelBuzzFeed, the digital media hub known for its shareable lists and a relatively recent push into more ambitious journalism, has landed a $200 million strategic investment from the entertainment conglomerate NBCUniversal, the two companies confirmed on Tuesday.The deal, which values BuzzFeed at about $1.5 billion, includes plans for the new and old media companies to work together on television, movies, the Olympics and partnerships with marketers.“It’s a fascinating time for the media industry; social, mobile, digital, and broadcast platforms are converging to create new opportunities to connect with global audiences,” Jonah Peretti, BuzzFeed’s founder and chief executive, said in a statement. “We’re excited to partner with NBCUniversal to combine our respective strengths to build the future of news and entertainment.”NBCUniversal, which is owned by Comcast, recently announced that it was investing $200 million in Vox Media, a publishing house for the digital age, putting the valuation of that company at about $1 billion.The pair of deals represent the latest effort by a traditional media company to invest in and collaborate with fast-growing digital media players as the industry tries to adapt to changing media habits, especially among younger audiences.Advertisement
