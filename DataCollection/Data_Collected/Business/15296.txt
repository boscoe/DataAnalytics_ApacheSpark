AdvertisementSupported byBy The Associated PressA mostly listless day of trading left United States stock indexes little changed on Monday, hovering just below the record highs they set late last week.Drug company and consumer-focused stocks weighed on the market, while energy companies surged, getting a lift from a pickup in crude oil prices.The slight pullback came as investors took advantage of the milestones reached on Friday by the Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index and Nasdaq composite to pocket some gains. Strong jobs data left traders feeling more confident about the United States economy heading into this week.“It’s a little bit of profit-taking,” said Joe Kinahan, chief strategist at TD Ameritrade. “We’re coming off a good employment number and we know the consumer’s been strong.”The Dow Jones industrial average slipped 14.24 points, or 0.1 percent, to 18,529.29. The S.&P. 500 index dipped 1.98 points, or 0.1 percent, to 2,180.89. The Nasdaq shed 7.98 points, or 0.2 percent, to 5,213.14.The stock market hit record highs on Friday after the Labor Department said employers added 255,000 jobs in July — far more than investors expected. The hiring spree, which followed an even bigger surge in June, gave investors more confidence that the economy is still growing.The major stock indexes appeared headed for another day of gains early on, as markets in Asia and Europe shrugged off new data showing China’s exports and imports declined again last month. A report indicating industrial production in Germany grew at a better-than-expected rate in June helped lift markets overseas.Two drug makers weighed on the market early on.Shares in Bristol-Myers Squibb were hammered after plunging on Friday following news that the company’s cancer treatment Opdivo failed in a study aimed at extending its use for lung cancer patients. The stock lost $2.98, or 4.7 percent, to close at $60.30.Allergan, the maker of Botox, slid 2.2 percent after second-quarter revenue fell short of Wall Street’s forecasts. The stock fell $5.54 to $248.31.Walmart, the retailing giant, slipped 0.6 percent after the company agreed to buy the fast-growing online retailing newcomer Jet.com for $3 billion in cash and another $300 million in stock. The deal underscores how serious Walmart is about challenging the online leader, Amazon. Shares in Walmart shed 42 cents to close at $73.34.A pickup in oil prices drove several oil and natural gas companies higher.Marathon Oil added 38 cents, or 2.7 percent, rising to $14.25, while Tesoro rose $2.76, or 3.7 percent, to $77.47. Chesapeake Energy gained 12 cents, or 2.5 percent, closing at $5.01.The benchmark United States crude rose $1.22, or 2.9 percent, to close at $43.02 a barrel in New York. Brent crude, used to price international oils, gained $1.12, or 2.5 percent, to close at $45.39 a barrel in London.Major stock indexes in Europe eked out gains. The German DAX rose 0.6 percent, while the CAC 40 in France gained 0.1 percent. In Britain, the FTSE 100 index added 0.2 percent.Earlier, markets in Asia moved mostly higher despite the discouraging economic data out of China. The Hang Seng in Hong Kong gained 1.6 percent, while the Shanghai composite index rose 0.9 percent. Tokyo’s Nikkei 225 index surged 2.4 percent.In metals trading, the price of gold fell $3.10, or 0.2 percent, to $1,341.30 an ounce. Silver dipped 1 cent, or 0.1 percent, to $19.81 an ounce. Copper added a penny, or 0.5 percent, to $2.17 a pound.Bond prices were little changed. The yield on the 10-year Treasury note held steady at 1.59 percent. In currency markets, the dollar rose to 102.43 yen from Friday’s 101.74 yen. The euro edged down to $1.1080 from $1.1086.Advertisement
