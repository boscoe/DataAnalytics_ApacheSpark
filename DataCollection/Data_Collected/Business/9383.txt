By MICHAEL J. de la MERCEDOCT. 4, 2016
A senior deal maker at Goldman Sachs, John Vaske, will retire from the investment bank after 28 years, the company announced on Tuesday.Mr. Vaske is a co-chairman of Goldman’s mergers and acquisitions team, a position he has held alongside Tim Ingrassia and Paul G. Parker since December. He was previously co-chairman of the company’s natural resources investment banking group.He joined Goldman in 1988 as an analyst in the mergers team in New York and later worked for the bank in Tokyo and London. He was promoted to managing director in 1998 and partner in 2000.Among Mr. Vaske’s transactions was DuPont’s merger with Dow Chemical.“Over the course of his career, John has enhanced our client franchise by building meaningful and longstanding relationships with numerous clients, has led a number of significant transactions and has played a central role in growing our natural resources business across regions,” Richard J. Gnodde, David Solomon and John Waldron — Goldman’s three heads of investment banking — wrote in an internal memorandum that was reviewed by The New York Times.A version of this article appears in print on October 5, 2016, on Page B5 of the New York edition with the headline: Goldman Deal Maker to Retire.
