AdvertisementSupported byBy Chad BrayLONDON — The London Stock Exchange Group said on Thursday that Mary L. Schapiro, the former chairwoman of the Securities and Exchange Commission, would join its board as it continued to expand its business in the United States.Ms. Schapiro, who served as chairwoman of the S.E.C. from 2009 to 2012, will become a nonexecutive director of the stock exchange and index operator on July 1.“Mary’s extensive knowledge of the U.S. financial and regulatory landscape, her broad global markets experience, and her understanding of complex financial infrastructure will be of great value to the group as we continue to grow our global footprint,” Chris Gibson-Smith, chairman of the London Stock Exchange Group, said in a news release.Ms. Schapiro, the first woman to serve as the S.E.C.’s permanent chairwoman, has also served as chief executive of the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority and chairwoman of the Commodity Futures Trading Commission.The appointment of Ms. Schapiro to the stock exchange operator’s board comes as it makes a major push into the United States.Last year, the London Stock Exchange acquired Russell Investments, the owner of the Russell 2000-stock market index, for $2.7 billion. In February, it put the investment management arm of Russell Investments up for sale in a widely expected move to focus on increasing the size of its index business.On Thursday, the stock exchange operator also said that David Weisbrod had been appointed its country head for the United States. Mr. Weisbrod will continue to serve as chief executive of LCH.Clearnet, a clearing house owned by the London Stock Exchange.Advertisement
