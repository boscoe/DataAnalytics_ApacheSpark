AdvertisementSupported byBy Chad BrayLONDON — The Swedish private equity firm EQT Partners and funds advised by Goldman Sachs have sold their remaining stake in the Danish outsourcing company ISS for about $1 billion a decade after acquiring the company.At the same time, the Kirk Kristiansen family, the owners of the Lego brand, increased their stake in the company and are now the third-largest shareholder behind the Ontario Teachers’ Pension Plan and the investment manager Artisan Partners, ISS said in a news release on Friday.Kirkbi Invest, which makes investments on behalf of the Kristiansen family, now owns about 10.3 percent of ISS, which provides services like cleaning, catering and security.The sale by EQT and the Goldman funds signals the end of a decadelong relationship with the Danish firm.EQT and the Goldman funds acquired ISS for about $3.8 billion in 2005 and took the company public on its third try last year. Two previous offerings were delayed in 2007 and in 2011.In a news release late Thursday, ISS said that EQT and the Goldman funds sold their remaining 19.3 percent stake in the company for 7.3 billion Danish kroner, or about $1.01 billion. EQT and the Goldman funds sold 35.8 million shares for 204 kroner a share.After the initial public offering last March, EQT and the Goldman funds had reduced their holdings through two sales, the most recent in December.ISS is one of the world’s largest private employers, with more than 510,000 people worldwide. The company posted revenue of 74.1 billion kroner in 2014.Shares of ISS rose about 4.7 percent to 213.80 kroner in midafternoon trading in Copenhagen on Friday.Advertisement
