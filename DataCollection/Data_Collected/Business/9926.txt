By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSNOV. 3, 2016
Stocks on Wall Street retreated for an eighth consecutive day on Thursday as nervous investors remained transfixed on the potential outcome of next week’s United States presidential election, which polls show has tightened.The stock market is now on its longest losing streak since the depths of the 2008 financial crisis.The Dow Jones industrial average dropped 28.97 points, or 0.2 percent, to 17,930.67. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index lost 9.28 points, or 0.4 percent, to 2,088.66 and the Nasdaq composite index fell 47.16 points, or 0.9 percent, to 5,058.41.Investors like certainty, which means they generally favor a Hillary Clinton victory as she is seen as maintaining the status quo. Donald J. Trump’s policies are less clear, and the uncertainty has caused anxiety in financial markets. The last time the S.&P. 500 fell for eight straight days was early October 2008, the depths of the financial crisis. However, the losses over this period have been modest, unlike the losses seen in 2008.“It’s a pretty simple equation: Uncertainty goes up, stock market goes down,” said David Kelly, chief global strategist with J.P. Morgan Funds.

The Mexican peso, which has become an indirect proxy among investors for Mr. Trump’s chances of winning the White House, advanced 1 percent against the dollar on Thursday. Investors have speculated that a Trump administration would be bad for the Mexican economy, and would cause the Mexican peso’s value to fall.AdvertisementThe VIX, a measure of volatility that is called Wall Street’s “fear gauge,” jumped 16 percent this week to its highest level since June.AdvertisementIn economic news, the Institute for Supply Management reported that services companies in the United States grew at a slower pace in October, although the economy appears poised to continue expanding. The institute’s services index fell to 54.8 last month, down from 57.1 in September. Still, any reading above 50 signals growth. New orders, production and employment were positive in October, although each of these categories decelerated.In other parts of the market, share prices of generic drug makers plunged after news reports that the Justice Department was investigating possible price-fixing among the companies. Mylan lost $2.53, or 7 percent, to $34.14; Teva Pharmaceuticals fell $4.13, or 9.5 percent, to $39.20; and Endo International plunged $3.54, or 19.5 percent, to $14.63.Facebook fell $7.22, or 6 percent, to $119.95. While the company reported third-quarter results that easily exceeded analysts’ estimates, it also acknowledged that growth in advertising revenue was slowing.

Fitbit, the maker of wearable fitness trackers and other devices, plunged $4.30, or 34 percent, to $8.51 after the company slashed its outlook for the year, citing weak demand for its products.The price of crude oil extended a losing streak into a fifth day. Benchmark crude slipped 68 cents, to $44.66 a barrel in New York. Brent crude, the international standard, fell 51 cents, to $46.35 a barrel in London.United States government bond prices fell. The yield on the 10-year Treasury note rose to 1.81 percent from 1.80 percent a day earlier.In currency trading, the dollar fell to 102.90 yen from 103.38 yen, while the euro edged up to $1.1106 from $1.1090 the day before.Gold fell $4.70, to $1,302.10 an ounce.A version of this article appears in print on November 4, 2016, on Page B4 of the New York edition with the headline: Election Looming, Wall Street Hits Longest Losing Streak Since Crisis.
