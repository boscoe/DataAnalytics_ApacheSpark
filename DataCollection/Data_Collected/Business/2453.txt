By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJAN. 9, 2015
Parties involved in a proposed $200 million settlement fund for victims of a fiery train disaster in 2013 that killed 47 people in Lac-Mégantic, Quebec, asked a Canadian judge on Friday to give them until September to iron out the details. “This is the first step in implementing the settlement fund,” said Robert Keach, a court-appointed trustee. Much of downtown Lac-Mégantic was destroyed when an unattended train with 72 oil tankers derailed in the middle of the night. Mr. Keach said he hoped to eventually have a fund of $500 million, but several corporations with potential liability have not yet agreed to participate.A version of this brief appears in print on January 10, 2015, on Page B2 of the New York edition with the headline: Draft Settlement in Place for Victims of Derailment.
