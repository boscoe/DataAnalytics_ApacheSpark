By REUTERSMARCH 12, 2015
Prime Minister Narendra Modi of India passed his first major economic changes almost a year after coming to power, as Parliament voted Thursday to allow more foreign investment in the insurance sector. Lawmakers in the upper house agreed to increase the foreign investment limit in local insurers to 49 percent from 26 percent. The passage of the bill represents a rare victory for Mr. Modi, who has seen his initiatives stymied by clashes with the opposition in Parliament. The legislation will shake up a crowded sector, allowing global insurers like Britain’s Prudential and others to increase their stakes in Indian companies.A version of this brief appears in print on March 13, 2015, on Page B2 of the New York edition with the headline: India Approves Changes on Foreign Investment.
