By CHAD BRAYMARCH 16, 2015
LONDON — GE Capital, the financing arm of General Electric, has agreed to sell its consumer lending business in Australia and New Zealand in a deal that values the business at 8.2 billion Australian dollars, or about $6.3 billion.The business will be acquired by a consortium of investors that includes Deutsche Bank and the private equity firms Värde Partners and Kohlberg Kravis Roberts.“We are delighted to have signed this agreement with a well-respected consortium of businesses,” Duncan Berry, the chief executive of GE Capital’s Australia and New Zealand business, said in a news release on Sunday. “Consumer finance has been a great business for G.E. and is well positioned for further growth.”The business has more than three million customers in Australia and New Zealand.
