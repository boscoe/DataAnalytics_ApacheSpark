AdvertisementSupported byBy The Associated PressStocks in the United States bucked a global market slump on Thursday. Investors pushed major indexes in the United States lower in the morning after drops in Asia and Europe, then reversed course as oil prices rose. That helped lift shares of energy companies, which have been battered in recent weeks.Trading was relatively light, with little news moving prices one way or the other.A report on Thursday showing a decline in applications for unemployment claims was the latest bullish sign on the job market, which could prompt Federal Reserve policy makers to begin raising interest rates. “The U.S. economy is in significantly better shape than in the past,” said Mike Ryan, chief investment strategist at UBS Wealth Management Americas. “We’re not dependent on Fed largess and stimulus to support growth.”The Dow Jones industrial average rose 76.83 points, or 0.5 percent, to close at 16,330.40. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index gained 10.25 points, or 0.5 percent, to 1,952.29. The Nasdaq climbed 39.72 points, or 0.8 percent, to 4,796.25.Apple shares jumped $2.42, or 2.2 percent, to $112.57, on Thursday, a day after updated versions of the iPhone, Apple TV and iPad were introduced. Technology stocks rose 1 percent overall, the biggest gain among the 10 industry sectors of the S.&P. 500.In Asia, Japan’s Nikkei 225 slumped 2.5 percent after surging 7.7 percent on Wednesday in its biggest gain since October 2008. Hong Kong’s Hang Seng Index dropped 2.6 percent, and China’s Shanghai Composite Index finished 1.4 percent lower.European markets were mostly lower. France’s CAC-40 lost 1.5 percent.Among American stocks making big moves, stock in Krispy Kreme Doughnuts plunged $2.08, or 12 percent, to $15.65 after the company lowered its outlook after disappointing second-quarter results.Lululemon Athletica shares sank $10.51, or 16 percent, to $53.54 after the company predicted profits for the current quarter that were lower than Wall Street forecasts.Shares in the freight company Con-way soared $12.01, or 34 percent, to $47.54 after it agreed to be acquired by XPO Logistics.United States crude rose $1.77 to settle at $45.92 a barrel in New York. Brent crude, a benchmark for international oils used by many American refineries, rose $1.31 to $48.89 a barrel in London.In other futures trading on the Nymex, wholesale gasoline rose 3.4 cents to $1.394 a gallon; heating oil rose 3.6 cents to $1.575 a gallon; and natural gas rose 3.2 cents to $2.683 per 1,000 cubic feet.Bond prices fell slightly. The yield on the 10-year Treasury note rose to 2.22 percent, from 2.20 percent late Wednesday. The United States dollar rose to 120.60 yen from 120.48 yen. The euro rose to $1.1278 from $1.1206.The price of gold rose $7.30 to $1,109.30 an ounce. Silver rose 7 cents to $14.65 an ounce, and copper gained a penny to $2.45 a pound.Advertisement
