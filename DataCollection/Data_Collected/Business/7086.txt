By REUTERSJUNE 25, 2015
Barnes & Noble reported declining sales for the fourth consecutive quarter as demand for the company’s Nook tablets continued to tumble and customers made fewer purchases at its brick-and-mortar bookstores. Shares of Barnes & Noble were down 1.5 percent. Nook sales fell 40 percent to $52.4 million in the fourth quarter. To return to growth, Barnes & Noble has been prominently featuring popular books and movies, organizing group reading activities and integrating its online and retail platforms. The company has also added gifts, toys, craft-beer kits, portable turntables and a wide selection of signed books to its stores. Revenue fell 10.4 percent to $1.18 billion, in line with the average analyst estimate.
