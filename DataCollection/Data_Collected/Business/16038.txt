AdvertisementSupported byBy Sydney EmberThe turmoil at Viacom is not letting up.In the latest changeover at the beleaguered entertainment company, Rob Moore, the vice chairman of Paramount Pictures, will leave the studio, a spokesman for the studio confirmed on Friday. Just two days earlier, Viacom, which also includes the cable networks MTV and Nickelodeon, said that its interim chief executive, Thomas E. Dooley, would step down in November.The fate of Paramount, whose film franchises include “Transformers,” “Star Trek” and “Mission: Impossible,” has been one of the central issues in a fierce fight for control of Sumner M. Redstone’s $40 billion media empire. Philippe P. Dauman, who was recently ousted as Viacom’s chief executive, wanted to sell a minority stake in Paramount; Mr. Redstone and his daughter, Shari Redstone, were against a deal.War has spread across the empire of Sumner Redstone, one of the entertainment industry’s most tenacious titans. At stake are the fortunes of his family and confidants, as well as the fate of Viacom and CBS.When Viacom announced on Wednesday that Mr. Dooley was stepping down, the company said it was no longer exploring a deal for Paramount.Mr. Moore, who joined Paramount in 2005 and was a vocal supporter of selling part of the studio, has been a principal architect of its underperforming film slate. “Ben-Hur,” which cost about $100 million to make, was the studio’s latest flop, bringing in only $11 million in domestic ticket sales during its opening weekend in August. “Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles: Out of the Shadows,” “Whiskey Tango Foxtrot” and “Zoolander 2” also performed poorly.News of Mr. Moore’s departure was reported earlier by The Hollywood Reporter.Brooks Barnes contributed reporting from Los AngelesAdvertisement
