Oil has fallen sharply since the end of June on evidence that a global supply glut is building at the same time demand appears to be slowing.The energy sector is down 15 percent this year, making it easily the worst-performing industry group in the Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index. Earnings at energy companies have dropped almost 60 percent in the second quarter.The S.&P. 500 fell 5.80 points, or 0.3 percent, to 2,098.04. The Dow Jones industrial average fell 91.66 points, or 0.5 percent, to 17,598.20. The Nasdaq composite index slipped 12.90 points, or 0.3 percent, to 5,115.38.Benchmark United States crude oil fell $1.95, or 4.1 percent, to close at $45.17 a barrel in New York. American crude has been sliding since reaching a high this year of $61.43 a barrel on June 10.The price of the 10-year Treasury note rose 10/32 to 99 25/32. The yield fell to 2.15 from 2.19 on Friday. Over all, stocks have been in the doldrums since the S.&P. 500 closed at a record high of 2,130 on May 21. Short sell-offs have been followed by short rallies as investors have weighed signs of an improving economy against signs of weakening growth overseas.Among individual stocks, Tyson Foods was the biggest loser in the S.&P. 500 on Monday. The meat producer slumped $4.39, or 9.9 percent, to $39.96 after cutting its outlook for fiscal 2015 earnings.The company, which owns the Jimmy Dean breakfast sausage brand, blamed conditions in the beef market for its trouble, citing high cattle costs and “export issues” as factors that were hurting its profits.Michael Kors was a big loser, dropping $3.28, or 7.8 percent, to $38.71 on concern that demand for the luxury retailer’s handbags was dropping off. Analysts at the investment bank Canaccord cut their price target on the stock before the company’s earnings report due Thursday.Investors also got an update on how the American economy was doing. Factories were a little less busy last month. The Institute of Purchasing Managers’ manufacturing index slipped to 52.7 from 53.5 in June. Economists had expected the index to remain unchanged. Any reading above 50 indicates growth.Investors are following this month’s economic reports to see if the economy is strengthening enough for the Federal Reserve to raise interest rates this year.Advertisement
