By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJAN. 23, 2015
A batch of mixed earnings reports on Friday helped push the stock market to its first day of losses this week.Shares of the tissue and diaper maker Kimberly-Clark dropped after the company’s earnings fell short of expectations and it gave a disappointing outlook. The package-delivery service UPS plunged after it cut its earnings forecast.Strong growth in company earnings have underpinned a bull run in stocks that has stretched for nearly six years. Earnings are still expected to keep rising, but the pace of growth is slowing, and investors are looking for signs that sales are up.“Our view is that the market is poised to have a reset to reflect what we think is a lower growth environment,” said James Abate, chief investment officer of Centre Funds, an asset management company.AdvertisementThe Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index fell 11.33 points, or 0.6 percent, to 2,051.82. The Dow Jones industrial average slipped 141.38 points, or 0.8 percent, to 17,672.60. The Nasdaq bucked the trend, gaining 7.48 points, or 0.2 percent, to 4,757.88.AdvertisementDespite the fall, stocks ended with their first weekly gain of the year.Global stocks rose sharply on Thursday after the European Central Bank announced that it would buy 60 billion euros, or $67 billion, of government and corporate bonds each month at least through September 2016. The €1.1 trillion program signals the willingness of the bank to bolster the economies in the euro currency alliance.The stimulus “was a big positive not just for Europe, but also the U.S.,” said Jerry Braakman, chief investment officer of First American Trust, in Santa Ana, Calif.Stocks have wavered since the start of the year on signs that growth outside of the United States was slowing. Many investors worried that a pronounced slowdown would eventually curb the American economic recovery.
