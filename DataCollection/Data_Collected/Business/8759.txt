By REUTERSSEPT. 7, 2015
European stocks rose on Monday, countering a fall in Asian markets led by weakness in China after a four-day break there. Trading was lighter than usual with United States markets closed in observance of Labor Day. The FTSEurofirst index of 300 leading shares closed up 0.48 percent at 1,399.34. MSCI’s broadest index of Asia-Pacific shares outside Japan fell 1 percent, driven by stocks in China. Shanghai shares initially rose as much as 1.8 percent after weekend remarks by regulators aimed at calming the market, but closed down 2.5 percent.A version of this brief appears in print on September 8, 2015, on Page B2 of the New York edition with the headline: European Shares Rise, as China Stocks Seesaw Lower .
