By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSFEB. 10, 2015
Net income for United States farmers is expected to fall nearly 32 percent this year because of low crop prices and increasing expenses. The Agriculture Department on Tuesday estimated that 2015 would be the second consecutive year that farmers’ incomes would fall. Income declined 16 percent in 2014 from 2013. The report estimates that net farm income will be $73.6 billion in 2015, compared with $108 billion in 2014. It was at a high of $129 billion in 2013.A version of this brief appears in print on February 11, 2015, on Page B2 of the New York edition with the headline: Farmers’ Profit Is Expected to Decline 32% in 2015.
