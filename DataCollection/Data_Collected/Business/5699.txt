By REUTERSMAY 1, 2015
OMAHA — Berkshire Hathaway, Warren E. Buffett’s holding company reported on Friday that first-quarter profit rose 10 percent and operating results easily beat forecasts, helped by its railroad and insurance businesses and gains from derivatives.Results were released one day before Berkshire’s annual meeting in Omaha, where shareholders are expected to celebrate Mr. Buffett’s 50th anniversary at Berkshire’s helm.Mr. Buffett and Charlie Munger, Berkshire’s vice chairman, will answer questions for five hours at the meeting, which is expected to draw more than 40,000 shareholders. It is part of a weekend of events throughout Omaha that Mr. Buffett calls Woodstock for Capitalists.Profit from the Burlington Northern Santa Fe railroad totaled $1.05 billion, up 44 percent from a year earlier, when bad weather and congestion led to what Berkshire called “substandard” service that left many customers unhappy. Berkshire attributed the better performance this year to increased capacity, new equipment and other upgrades made as part of a $6 billion capital improvement program that will continue this year.AdvertisementBerkshire has more than 80 operating companies in insurance, energy, food, railroad and other sectors, and owns more than $115 billion of stocks.AdvertisementMr. Buffett took control of Berkshire 50 years ago and began reshaping it into a conglomerate. Many of those subsidiaries are offering some special trinkets for shareholders to buy.Fruit of the Loom has “Berky” boxers and bras. The Richline Group offers a gold Warren Buffett watch, bracelets and a commemorative coin. And Berkshire’s Brooks Running subsidiary shows a limited-edition shoe with a cartoon depiction of Mr. Buffett inside.Net income rose to $5.16 billion, or $3,143 a share, from $4.71 billion, or $2,862 a share, a year earlier.
