By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSFEB. 19, 2015
Freddie Mac, the government-controlled mortgage finance giant, posted net income of $227 million for the fourth quarter, down sharply from the same period of 2013, as it sustained a $3.4 billion loss on derivatives used to hedge against swings in interest rates. Nonetheless, the results reported Thursday were the company’s 13th straight profitable quarter. Freddie also said it would pay a dividend of $900 million to the United States Treasury next month. Freddie has paid $91.8 billion in dividends, exceeding its government bailout of $71.3 billion. For all of 2014, the agency reported net income of $7.7 billion, down from $48.7 billion in 2013. It was the company’s third straight profitable year. Freddie improved its operations “through innovation and efficiency,” the chief executive, Donald H.
