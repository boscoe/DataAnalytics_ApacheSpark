By CHAD BRAYSEPT. 23, 2016
LONDON — Nets, a Danish payments processor, said on Friday that it was valued at 30 billion Danish kroner, or about $4.5 billion, in its initial public stock offering in Copenhagen.The offering came about two and a half years after a group of Nordic banks sold the company for 17 billion kroner to a consortium of investors that included the private equity firms Advent International, ATP Private Equity Partners and Bain Capital.Nets priced its offering of 105 million shares on Nasdaq in Copenhagen at 150 kroner apiece, within the company’s anticipated range of 130 to 160 kroner. It announced its intention to proceed with an initial public offering this month.Shares of Nets were up slightly, at 151.4 kroner, in early trading in Copenhagen.Founded in 1968, Nets provides payment processing services for bank and card issuers in the Nordic and Baltic regions, as well as corporate customers in Denmark and Norway, and merchants. The company generated revenue of 6.84 billion kroner in 2015 and employs about 2,400 people in six countries.Deutsche Bank, Morgan Stanley and Nordea acted as joint global coordinators on the offering.
