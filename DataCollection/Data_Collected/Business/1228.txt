The Internet conglomerate IAC/InterActiveCorp offered on Wednesday to buy Angie’s List, the repository of online reviews for home repairs and other services, for about $512 million, taking its offer public after being rebuffed.The move could potentially unite the reviews site, which has struggled since going public four years ago, with IAC’s stable of Internet brands including About.com and the video site Vimeo, all run under the eye of the company’s founder, Barry Diller.It also comes after TCS Capital Management, one of Angie’s List’s biggest shareholders, with a stake of roughly 9.5 percent, publicly urged the company to consider selling itself — potentially to IAC’s HomeAdvisor.The reviews site, named for its founder, Angie Hicks Bowman, has lost money every year since going public and has grappled with how to cut the costs of expanding.AdvertisementIn a letter to the directors of Angie’s List that it made public, IAC disclosed that it had approached its target on Oct. 23, but was disappointed that it garnered no interest in a deal.Please verify you're not a robot by clicking the box.Invalid email address.
