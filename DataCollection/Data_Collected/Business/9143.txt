By REUTERSSEPT. 22, 2015
United States stocks dropped on Tuesday as a sell-off in commodities dragged down materials shares, and the shares of Volkswagen suppliers dropped on news about the company’s emissions scandal.The sell-off was broad; all 10 major sectors in the Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index were lower.More worries about slower growth in China pushed commodities to two-week lows, with copper prices and industrial metals leading losses. United States crude oil also settled lower.Shares of the suppliers BorgWarner, Honeywell and Delphi Automotive fell after Volkswagen, the German carmaker, admitted to cheating on vehicle emission tests. BorgWarner shares were down 7.6 percent, while Honeywell fell 1.7 percent, and Delphi lost 3.6 percent.Goldman Sachs fell 2 percent to $179.72 and was the biggest drag on the Dow after its chief executive, Lloyd C. Blankfein, said he had a “highly curable” form of lymphoma.Advertisement“It’s China. It’s the Fed. It’s slowing global growth,” said Timothy M. Ghriskey, the chief investment officer of Solaris Group in Bedford Hills, N.Y. “The news on Volkswagen is overhanging the auto industry. There is a bit of a bubble in the health care area.”AdvertisementThe Dow Jones industrial average fell 179.72 points, or 1.09 percent, to 16,330.47, the S.&P. 500 lost 24.23 points, or 1.23 percent, to 1,942.74 and the Nasdaq composite index dropped 72.23 points, or 1.5 percent, to 4,756.72.
