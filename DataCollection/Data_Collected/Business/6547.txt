By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJUNE 3, 2015
Encouraging economic news pushed stocks higher Wednesday, although a slump in energy companies and utilities kept broader gains in check.Stocks climbed from the start of trading on news that American exports rose in April and that hiring picked up in May. The buying followed a rise in overseas markets in the hope of a breakthrough in Greece’s talks with its creditors.By the end of the day, seven of 10 industry groups in the Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index rose. Utility stocks dropped 1.4 percent and energy companies fell 0.7 percent, along with the price of oil.The S.&P. 500-stock index edged up 4.47 points, or 0.2 percent, to 2,114.07. The Dow Jones industrial average rose 64.33 points, or 0.4 percent, to end at 18,076.27. The Nasdaq composite index climbed 22.71 points, or 0.5 percent, to 5,099.23.AdvertisementColleen S. Supran, principal at the investment firm Bingham, Osborn & Scarborough, said Wednesday’s reports seemed to strike a sort of Goldilocks balance of hot, but not too hot.Advertisement“It’s not so robust, that anyone can come out and say, ‘The Fed has got to raise rates,’ ” she said. “Everything is just good enough.”Utility companies were driven down by a sharp rise in bond yields. Investors like utility stocks for their big dividends, but bonds are becoming more attractive as a source of income. The price of the 10-year Treasury note fell 26/32 to 97 24/32, and its yield rose to 2.37 percent, the highest since November.
