AdvertisementSupported byBy Chad BrayLONDON — General Electric said on Monday that it had agreed to sell its fleet-financing businesses in the United States, Mexico, Australia and New Zealand to Element Financial Corporation of Canada for $6.9 billion.On Monday, G.E. also announced that it had separately signed a memorandum of understanding to possibly sell its fleet businesses in Europe to Arval, a unit of the French bank BNP Paribas and an alliance partner of Element’s.The sale of the bulk of GE Capital Fleet Services represents the latest move in G.E.’s effort to retreat from banking and refocus on its industrial roots.The company has been pursuing sales of a variety of assets in its GE Capital finance arm and expects to sell most of GE Capital by the end of next year. This month, G.E. agreed to sell a division that finances leveraged buyouts to the Canada Pension Plan Investment Board in a $12 billion deal.“We continue to demonstrate speed and execution on our strategy to sell most of the assets of GE Capital,” Keith S. Sherin, the chairman and chief executive of GE Capital, said in a news release. “We are on track to execute sales of $100 billion by the end of 2015 and expect to be substantially done by the end of 2016.”The fleet-financing sales are subject to regulatory approval.The sale of the United States and Mexico business is expected to close in the third quarter, and the Australia and New Zealand transaction is expected to close in the fourth quarter.G.E. said that it aimed to close the Arval sale in the fourth quarter, if the deal went ahead.The sales did not include GE Capital’s fleet business in Japan.GE Capital Fleet Services provides car and truck financing for commercial vehicles and fleet management services, including more than 1.5 million vehicles leased, serviced and managed worldwide.Element, which is based in Toronto, provides equipment financing and fleet management services for a variety of businesses, including commercial vehicle, aircraft and rail financing. Element acquired GE Capital’s fleet business in Canada in 2013 and has about $12.5 billion in assets.“Adding these very high quality businesses to our existing fleet operations firmly establishes Element as a leader in the North American fleet management industry,” Steven Hudson, the chief executive of Element, said in a news release.Arval specializes in vehicle leasing in 25 countries and operates in an additional 14 countries through partners, including Element, with which it has a global alliance.JPMorgan Chase and the law firm Weil, Gotshal & Manges advised G.E., while Element was advised by BMO Capital Markets, Barclays, Infor Financial and CIBC and the law firms Blake, Cassels & Graydon and Cravath, Swaine & Moore.Advertisement
