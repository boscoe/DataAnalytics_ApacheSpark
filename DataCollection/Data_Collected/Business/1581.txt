AdvertisementSupported byBy Chad BrayLONDON — Dometic Group, a Swedish maker of appliances for boats and recreational vehicles, said on Wednesday that it had raised 4.6 billion Swedish kronor, or about $528 million, in its initial public offering in Stockholm.The company is being taken public by the Swedish private equity firm EQT Partners, which acquired Dometic in 2011. EQT previously invested in Dometic between 2001 and 2005.Dometic said that it priced its offering of 97.8 million shares at 48 kronor apiece. The company said this month that it expected to price its stock at 43 kronor to 52 kronor a share.The offering on Wednesday valued Dometic at 14.2 billion kronor, or about $1.6 billion.“The listing is a key milestone for the company, and I look forward to continuing to develop Dometic as a listed company,” Roger Johansson, the Dometic president and chief executive, said in a news release.Shares of Dometic rose nearly 7 percent to 51.2 kronor in early trading in Stockholm on Wednesday.If an overallotment of shares is fully exercised, Dometic expects to sell as much as 38 percent of its shares in the offering.EQT would remain Dometic’s largest shareholder and would hold a 57 percent stake in the company after the offering, Dometic said.“The strong interest from both Swedish and international investors is a seal of approval from the market and a validation of the management team’s hard work over the last few years,” Harry Klagsbrun, a partner at EQT, said in a news release. “It shows confidence in Dometic’s ability to continue executing on its strategy and to generate value for its shareholders.”AMF, Handelsbanken Fonder and Nordea Funds have agreed to act as cornerstone investors and will own about 9.8 percent of the company after the offering.Dometic, which is based in Solna, Sweden, manufactures heating and air-conditioning systems, refrigerators and appliances for boats, recreational vehicles and other passenger vehicles. The company posted sales of 8.35 billion kronor in 2014 and employs about 5,900 people.Jefferies, Skandinaviska Enskilda Banken and Morgan Stanley are leading the offering, and Lazard is acting as a financial adviser to Dometic and EQT.Advertisement
