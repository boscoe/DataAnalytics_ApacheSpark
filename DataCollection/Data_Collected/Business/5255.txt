AdvertisementSupported byBusiness BriefingBy The Associated PressUnitedHealth, the nation’s largest health insurer, raised its 2015 forecast after soaring past Wall Street’s first-quarter expectations with a performance fueled partly by growth outside health insurance. The company said on Thursday that earnings from its Optum pharmacy benefits management segment, combined with strong growth in its core insurance business, helped push net income up about 29 percent to $1.41 billion in the quarter. Operating earnings from Optum climbed 14 percent to $742 million, even though it also booked $42 million in costs tied to the acquisition of another pharmacy benefits manager, Catamaran. Over all, UnitedHealth earned $1.46 a share in the quarter that ended March 31, up from $1.10 a share, or $1.1 billion, in last year’s quarter. Revenue jumped 13 percent to $35.76 billion. Analysts expected, on average, earnings of $1.33 a share on $34.73 billion in revenue, according to Zacks Investment Research. Shares climbed 3.7 percent, or $4.28, to $121.60. Advertisement
