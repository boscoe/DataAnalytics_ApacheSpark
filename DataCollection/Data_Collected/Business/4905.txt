By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSAPRIL 4, 2015
United States government bond prices rose and the dollar fell on Friday after news that American employers added the fewest jobs in a month since December 2013. The stock market was closed in observance of Good Friday. The rise in bond prices sent the yield on the 10-year Treasury down to 1.84 percent, its lowest level in two months. Major European stock markets were closed, while in Asia, Tokyo’s Nikkei 225 gained 0.6 percent. Seoul’s Kospi rose 0.8 percent and the Shanghai composite added 1 percent.A version of this article appears in print on April 4, 2015, on Page B2 of the New York edition with the headline: Jobs Report Sends Government Bond Yields Down.
