By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSNOV. 11, 2015
Stock indexes ended lower on Wednesday after a day of wavering between small gains and losses.A weak report from Macy’s pushed retail stocks lower, and energy shares retreated as the price of oil fell.Macy’s plunged 14 percent, its largest one-day drop in seven years. Other retailers slumped as investors took its results as a warning about third-quarter sales and the coming holiday shopping season.October’s big rally is fading into the distance, but most of the market’s recent losses have been small. Stocks have lost ground for five of the last six trading days, leaving the market with a meager gain for the year to date.The Dow Jones industrial average lost 55.99 points, or 0.3 percent, to 17,702.22. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index fell 6.72 points, or 0.3 percent, to 2,075. The Nasdaq composite index gave up 16.22 points, or 0.3 percent, to close at 5,067.02.AdvertisementMacy’s third-quarter sales were lower than analysts had expected, prompting the company, which also owns Bloomingdale’s, to cut its profit forecast for the year. Macy’s also said it would close more stores. Its shares sank $6.58, to $40.44.AdvertisementPaul Lejuez, a Citi Research investment analyst, said fewer people bought winter clothes like boots and coats in the third quarter because the weather was warm. That meant they were less likely to go shopping for clothes at all, so inventories built up and discounts increased — a troubling sign less than three weeks before Black Friday.“That’s bad for anybody that competes against Macy’s,” Mr. Lejuez said.Investors dumped shares of other retailers.Kohl’s fell $2.44, or 5.4 percent, to $43.16, and Nordstrom dropped $2.41, or 3.7 percent, to $62.32. Urban Outfitters fell $2.12, or 7.4 percent, to $26.59. PVH, the company behind the Calvin Klein and Tommy Hilfiger brands, lost $4.67, or 5 percent, to close at $88.26.

In energy trading, the price of oil dropped 3 percent after a report showed a big buildup in United States crude stockpiles. The American Petroleum Institute said supplies grew by 6.3 million barrels, far more than had analysts expected, according to Phil Flynn, an analyst for the Price Futures Group. United States crude slid $1.28, to $42.93 a barrel in New York, and Brent crude, which is used to price international oils, lost $1.63, to close at $45.81 a barrel in London.The two largest beer makers in the world said they had hammered out the terms of a merger. The $106 billion joining of Anheuser-Busch InBev and SABMiller brings together brands including Budweiser, Corona, Grolsch and Stella Artois. The combined company will make about a third of the world’s beer. Shares of AB InBev rose $3.28, or 2.8 percent, to $121.63.As part of the agreement, SABMiller will sell its majority stake in a United States joint venture to Molson Coors Brewing for $12 billion.
