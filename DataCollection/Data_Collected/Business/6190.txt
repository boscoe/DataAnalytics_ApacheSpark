By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMAY 19, 2015
The stock market slipped back from its latest high in a listless day of trading on Tuesday.Oil companies and drillers tugged major indexes down as the price of crude oil dropped for a fifth day in a row.Walmart fell after reporting weak sales and a drop in quarterly earnings, a result of raises for workers and a rising dollar squeezing its profits. Walmart’s stock sank $3.49, or 4.4 percent, to $76.43.The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index has gained 2 percent this month, rising to new heights along the way.But it has not felt like it, said Hank Smith, chief investment officer at Haverford Trust. “The market pulls back slightly one day, then moves ahead. It has been a grind,” he said.AdvertisementThe S.&P. 500 fell 1.37 points, or 0.1 percent, to 2,127.83. The Nasdaq composite index dipped 8.41 points, or 0.2 percent, to 5,070.03.AdvertisementThe Dow Jones industrial average managed a slight gain, edging up 13.51 points, or 0.1 percent, to 18,312.39. That was a fourth straight daily gain for the Dow.The government reported that builders started work on new houses at the fastest pace in seven years. Housing starts jumped 20.2 percent to an annual rate of 1.1 million homes, the fastest clip since November 2007. The news helped lift shares in D. R. Horton, PulteGroup and other builders.

A rising dollar made oil, which is priced in dollars, more expensive for overseas buyers. Benchmark United States crude fell $2.25 to close at $57.99 a barrel in New York.
