AdvertisementSupported byBy The Associated PressA late turn gave the market a meager gain to start the month on Thursday, a day after it finished its worst quarter in four years.Investors were looking ahead to Friday when the government releases its monthly jobs report. Strong hiring would most likely raise expectations that the Federal Reserve will increase its benchmark interest rate at its next meeting later this month.Without any big developments to drive the action on Thursday, trading appeared aimless. The S.&P. 500 added 3.79 points, or 0.2 percent, to close at 1,923.82. The Dow Jones industrial average fell 12.69 points, or 0.1 percent, to 16,272.01. The Nasdaq gained 6.92 points, or 0.2 percent, to 4,627.08.Among companies in the news, Dunkin’ Brands plunged 12 percent after the company’s revenue estimates fell short of analysts’ forecasts. The company also said Thursday that it planned to close 100 stores in Speedway gas stations this year and next. In Europe, Germany’s DAX fell 1.6 percent, and France’s CAC-40 fell 0.7 percent. The FTSE 100 index of leading British shares edged up 0.2 percent.In Asia, Japan’s Nikkei 225 jumped 1.9 percent, South Korea’s Kospi rose 0.8 percent, and Australia’s S&P/ASX 200 advanced 1.8 percent.Prices for United States government bonds edged up, nudging the yield down to 2.04 percent from 2.05 percent late Wednesday. The euro rose to $1.1188; the dollar dipped to 119.90 yen.Gold dropped $1.30 to finish at $1,114.20 an ounce, and silver slipped a penny to $14.51 an ounce. Copper lost 4 cents, to $2.30 a pound.The price of crude oil fell 35 cents to close at $44.74 a barrel in New York. Brent crude, a benchmark for international oils used by many American refineries, fell 68 cents, to $47.69 in London.Wholesale gasoline remained unchanged at $1.367 a gallon on the New York Mercantile Exchange; heating oil fell 1.7 cents, to close at $1.520 a gallon; and natural gas fell 9.1 cents, to close at $2.433 per 1,000 cubic feet.Advertisement
