By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSFEB. 17, 2015
Stocks closed slightly higher Tuesday as investors continued to monitor talks involving Greece and its creditors in hopes that a deal will be reached to keep the country from falling out of the eurozone.Investments typically considered less risky, like bonds, gold and utilities stocks, declined.The Dow Jones industrial average rose 28.23 points, or 0.2 percent, to 18,047.58. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index added 3.35 points, or 0.2 percent, to 2,100.34 and the Nasdaq composite index rose 5.43 points, or 0.1 percent, to 4,899.27.With Tuesday’s gains, the S.&P. 500 is at another nominal high. United States markets were closed Monday for Presidents’ Day.Stock indexes had been lower most of the day, but recovered their losses on reports that progress was being made between Greece and its creditors.AdvertisementGreece wants an extension of its loan agreement, government officials said Tuesday. The talks are aimed toward a last-minute deal with Greece’s European lenders and to keep Greece from leaving the eurozone. Without a deal, investors expect that Greece may have little option but to default. But many analysts expect a deal will be reached.Advertisement“Greece’s new government wants more independence, but it doesn’t want that independence at all costs,” said Anastasia Amoroso, a global market strategist at JPMorgan Funds. An extension would be good news because it would give Greece and the eurozone time to reach a long-term resolution. While the major stock indexes moved little Tuesday, investors made noticeable moves out of the tradition havens: bonds, gold and dividend-paying stocks like utilities.The price of the 10-year Treasury note fell 26/32 to 98 23/32 and its yield rose to 2.14 percent from 2.05 percent on Friday.
