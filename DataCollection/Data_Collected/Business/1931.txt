
DEC. 12, 2015

Documents show that as early as June 2011, the Treasury Department had been told that Fannie and Freddie were on the road to recovery and that their tax assets would most likely generate significant value. That discussion occurred in a private meeting with a group of Wall Street restructuring experts. Four executives from Blackstone, the financial services giant, and three restructuring specialists from Skadden Arps, the New York law firm, made a presentation to Treasury.
