By THE NEW YORK TIMESJAN. 5, 2016
James Dao, a veteran editor and reporter currently serving as deputy national editor, will become the Op-Ed editor, The New York Times said on Tuesday.Mr. Dao, 58, succeeds Trish Hall, who will move to the newsroom after almost five years in the Op-Ed job, to take on a role involving special projects.After joining The Times from The Daily News in 1992, Mr. Dao worked on the metropolitan desk, served as a national correspondent and also worked as a Washington correspondent covering Congress, the State Department, the 2000 presidential campaign and the military. He also served as the paper’s military and veterans affairs writer in 2010 and 2011.Andrew Rosenthal, the Editorial Page editor, said in a statement: “He brings with him a richness of journalistic experience, great writing skill, editing chops and the right style of management to a team that is already flourishing and innovating.”A version of this article appears in print on January 6, 2016, on Page B5 of the New York edition with the headline: Times Names James Dao Editor of the Op-Ed Page .
