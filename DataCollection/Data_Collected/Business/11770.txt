AdvertisementSupported byBy The Associated PressStocks extended their three-day losing streak Tuesday, closing slightly lower after spending most of the day wavering between gains and losses.Energy companies led the decline as the price of United States crude oil sank nearly 6 percent to about $28 a barrel.The market’s bumpy ride followed a slide in European stock indexes and steep losses in Japan, reflecting rising anxiety among investors that the global economy is slowing.“The market continues to price in worst-case scenarios, a recessionary China and an energy sector that’s looking basically like it should go bankrupt at this level,” said Jeffrey A. Carbone, managing director of Cornerstone Financial Partners.The Dow Jones industrial average fell 12.67 points, or 0.1 percent, to 16,014.38. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index slipped 1.23 points, or 0.1 percent, to 1,852.21. The Nasdaq composite index lost 14.99 points, or 0.4 percent, to 4,268.76.The latest losses pulled the three indexes further down for the year. The Dow is off 8.1 percent, while the S.&P. 500 is down 9.4 percent. The Nasdaq is off 14.8 percent.Investors have fretted over several issues, including the fall in the price of oil, a slowdown in China and whether many parts of the global economy will fall into recession.The market veered lower early on Tuesday after a wave of selling in Europe and Japan, where the Nikkei index closed 5.4 percent lower. The interest rate on Japan’s benchmark bond also dropped into negative territory for the first time.Major American stock indexes rebounded early as oil prices briefly rose, but the rally did not last. A late-afternoon rebound also did not hold as oil prices settled lower for the second day in a row.“The market’s correlation to oil has not subsided at this time,” Mr. Carbone said. “There seems to be no end in sight.”Benchmark United States crude oil dropped $1.75 to settle at $27.94 a barrel in New York. Brent crude, a benchmark for international oils, fell $2.56 to $30.32 a barrel in London.The S.&P. 500 index’s energy sector companies lost 2.5 percent, the worst performer in the index.Consol Energy lost $1.02, or 11.9 percent, to $7.53, while Southwestern Energy fell 97 cents, or 10.4 percent, to $8.37. Murphy Oil slid $1.37, or 7.1 percent, to $17.86.Investors also had their sights on the latest batch of company earnings news. The entertainment conglomerate Viacom plunged 21.5 percent after missing revenue estimates for the fifth quarter in a row. The stock fell $8.99 to $32.86.Traders were also looking toward Wednesday, when Janet L. Yellen, chairwoman of the Federal Reserve, will begin two days of testimony before Congress.Ms. Yellen is scheduled to outline the central bank’s outlook on the economy. Traders will be watching for hints about when the Fed will make its next move to raise its key interest rate. Many analysts and investors predict the Fed will raise rates fewer than four times this year, if at all.Overseas, the FTSE 100 index of leading British shares lost 1 percent, while Germany’s DAX fell 1.1 percent. The CAC-40 in France dropped 1.7 percent.Precious metals prices were mixed. Gold rose 70 cents, or 0.1 percent, to $1,198.60 an ounce, and silver inched up 2 cents, or 0.1 percent, to $15.44 an ounce. Copper, an industrial metal that will often rise and fall with investor’s optimism about the global economy, fell 5 cents, or 2.4 percent, to $2.04 a pound.Bond prices rose. The yield on the 10-year Treasury note fell to 1.73 percent from 1.75 percent late Monday. The dollar was down at 115.12 yen from 115.58 yen.Advertisement
