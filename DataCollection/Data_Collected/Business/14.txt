AdvertisementSupported byBy Leslie PickerSurgery Partners, braving a sell-off in health care stocks, completed a smaller-than-expected initial public offering on Wednesday night.The company, backed by the private equity firm H.I.G. Capital, priced its shares at $19 each, at least 17 percent less than it had hoped. At the I.P.O. price, Surgery Partners has a market value of $915 million.When Surgery Partners was speaking with investors last week about its initial offering, the health care sector posted its biggest loss in the stock market in four years. Surgery Partners had hoped to price its offering at $23 to $26 a share.Surgery Partners, which owns surgical centers across the country, started with one outlet in Florida in 2004 and has grown to own 99, mostly through acquisitions of two other operators, NovaMed and Symbion, according to Surgery Partners’ filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission. The company said it generated more than $400 million in revenue in the first six months of the year – largely from private insurance and government. Surgery Partners performs operations for a variety of conditions, including gastrointestinal and orthopedic ailments and pain, the filing showed.Health care stocks have declined recently during a broader sell-off in equities, fed in part by fears of an economic slowdown in China. The Standard & Poor’s health care index dropped 5.8 percent last week, the biggest weekly decline since 2011. On Wednesday, it rose 2.1 percent.H.I.G. Capital acquired Surgery Partners in January 2010, although the terms at the time were not disclosed. The private equity firm still owns a majority of the shares even after the initial offering.The 11-year-old company will use the money from the offering to help repay more than $1 billion in debt.The stock, listed on the Nasdaq exchange under the symbol SGRY, will begin trading on Thursday. Bank of America, Goldman Sachs and Jefferies managed the offering.Advertisement
