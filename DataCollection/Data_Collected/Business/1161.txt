By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSNOV. 9, 2015
A slowdown in international trade could be a harbinger of a new recession for the world’s leading economies, the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development said on Monday. In its world economic outlook, the O.E.C.D. says declining rates of trade seen this year had “in the past, been associated with global recession.” It projected global trade growth at 2 percent this year, improving to 3.6 percent next year.
