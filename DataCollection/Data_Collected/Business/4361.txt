By CHAD BRAYMARCH 13, 2015
LONDON — Fortum, the state-controlled Finnish utility, said on Friday that it had agreed to sell its Swedish electricity distribution network to a consortium of Swedish funds and Borealis Infrastructure Management of Canada for 6.6 billion euros, or about $6.9 billion, including debt.Fortum said that it would book a one-time gain on the sale of €4.4 billion in the second quarter.The sale signals the exit of Fortum from the electricity distribution business after earlier sales of its electricity grids in Norway and Finland.Since December 2013, the company has sold its Norwegian, Finnish and Swedish distribution businesses for a total of €9.3 billion, including debt.“The business is financially and operationally in a very good shape,” Timo Karttinen, Fortum’s interim president and chief executive, said in a news release. “It will now have a new owner that will develop the business as a core business.”AdvertisementFortum will focus on power generation and electricity sales going forward.The Swedish distribution business includes 906,000 customers and accounts for about 17 percent of local electricity distribution in Sweden.
