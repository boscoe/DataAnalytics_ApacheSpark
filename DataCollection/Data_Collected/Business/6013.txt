By CHAD BRAYMAY 12, 2015
LONDON — The Dutch supermarket operator Ahold and Delhaize Group of Belgium said on Tuesday that they had entered early-stage discussions about a potential merger.The deal would combine Delhaize, the owner of the Food Lion grocery store chain, with Ahold, which owns the Stop & Shop and Giant chains in the United States, creating a company worth about 25.6 billion euros, or about $28.6 billion, based on market capitalization.If completed, the merger would create a company with €54 billion in combined sales and more than 6,600 stores, with the United States accounting for about 60 percent of its revenue.“These discussions may or may not result in a future transaction,” Ahold said in a news release on Tuesday.AdvertisementThe companies did not disclose financial details of any potential transaction.Shares of Ahold rose 2.8 percent to €18.70 in early trading in Amsterdam on Tuesday, while shares of Delhaize rose 1.8 percent to €84.41 in early trading in Brussels.Please verify you're not a robot by clicking the box.Invalid email address.
