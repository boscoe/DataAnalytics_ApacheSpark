COVENTRY, England — As troops were being rushed to this stricken city, which suffered on Thursday night the worst raid to date in the German aerial war on Great Britain, King George VI and other government leaders visited today [Nov. 16] the injured and homeless and inspected the vast damage to Coventry, a Midlands industrial center, where 200 persons were killed and 800 injured by the Nazi air attack. — New York Herald Tribune, European Edition, November 17, 1940
