By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJAN. 1, 2015
The Interior Ministry said Thursday that 940 cars were set on fire by vandals on New Year’s Eve, fewer than the 1,067 set aflame the previous year. A ministry spokesman, Pierre-Henry Brandet, said on BFM-TV that cars had been burned around the country, particularly in the east, as well as in suburban Paris, notably the Seine-Saint-Denis region — where riots that spread throughout France started in 2005.A version of this brief appears in print on January 2, 2015, on Page A10 of the New York edition with the headline: France: Hundreds of Cars Set on Fire.
