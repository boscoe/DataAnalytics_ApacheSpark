By REUTERSJAN. 13, 2015
The director of the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention said Tuesday that the Ebola outbreak in West Africa could be brought under control, but that “we are by no means out of the woods.” The director, Dr. Thomas R. Frieden, said he was “very confident we can get to zero cases in this epidemic if we continue the way we’re going and nothing unexpected happens.” But he said that the recovery was patchy and fragile. Liberia and Sierra Leone have made progress, said Jeremy Konyndyk, an official with the Agency for International Development.
