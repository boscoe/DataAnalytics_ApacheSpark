By REUTERSJAN. 5, 2015
United Nations and Congolese troops launched airstrikes and a ground offensive on Monday against remnants of a Burundian rebel group based in an eastern region of the Democratic Republic of Congo, a United Nations military spokesman said. The strikes targeted the National Liberation Forces, but a United Nations diplomat said they were mainly aimed at clearing the way for a later offensive against another group, Democratic Forces for the Liberation of Rwanda. Earlier Monday, Burundi’s army said that it had killed 95 gunmen from an unidentified rebel group based in Congo that the army said wanted to carry out attacks to disrupt elections this year.A version of this brief appears in print on January 6, 2015, on Page A8 of the New York edition with the headline: Congo: Rebels Under Attack.
