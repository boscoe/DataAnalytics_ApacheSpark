By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJAN. 31, 2015
A senior Kurdish commander died in clashes with Islamic State militants in northern Iraq, Kurdish officials said Saturday. The commander, Brig. Gen. Hussein Mansour, was killed Friday night by a sniper near Kirkuk during clashes between Islamic State fighters and Kurdish security forces, known also as pesh merga, two Kurdish security officers said. The officers said that General Mansour had just replaced Brig. Gen. Shirko Fatih, who was killed in an earlier clash. In and around Baghdad, a series of bombings killed nine people on Saturday, Iraqi officials said.A version of this brief appears in print on February 1, 2015, on Page A9 of the New York edition with the headline: Iraq: Militants Kill a Kurdish Commander Near Kirkuk.
