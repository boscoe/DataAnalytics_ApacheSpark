By HARI KUMARJAN. 27, 2015
NEW DELHI — A colonel in India’s army, a police officer and two militants died Tuesday in a gun battle in Kashmir, the police said. Indian forces had surrounded a house where militants were thought to be visiting, in the Pulwama district of India-administered Kashmir, when the troops were attacked with grenades and Kalashnikov fire. Col. Munindra Nath Rai, a highly decorated officer and commander of the local Indian Army unit, led the operation and was killed. He had been awarded a gallantry medal the day before, Indian news media reported.A version of this article appears in print on January 28, 2015, on Page A11 of the New York edition with the headline: India: Kashmir Firefight Kills Four.
