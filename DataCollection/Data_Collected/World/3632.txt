France launched its first satellite into space on Nov. 26, 1965, from Hammaguir, Algeria. British Pathé.PARIS — France today [Nov. 26] launched a 92.4-pound satellite into orbit, thereby becoming the world’s third full-fledged space power after the United States and the Soviet Union. The satellite, launched from the French Sahara base of Hammaguir, will orbit the earth for the next two weeks, sending back radio messages to tracking stations near Paris and around the world. President Charles de Gaulle hailed the launching as ‘‘an important accomplishment.’’ — New York Herald Tribune, European Edition, November 27, 1965
