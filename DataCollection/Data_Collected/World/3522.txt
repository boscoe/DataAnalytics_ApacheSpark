AdvertisementSupported byWorld BriefingBy Kareem Fahim Egyptian security forces on Monday shot and killed at least five Sudanese migrants who were trying to cross the border from Egypt into Israel, the military said in a statement.   The deaths came eight days after Egyptian border guards were accused in the fatal shootings of at least 15 Sudanese migrants trying to cross the frontier.  Human rights groups have for years criticized the Egyptian authorities as resorting too quickly to lethal force against migrants and asylum seekers, mostly from East Africa, who travel through the Sinai Peninsula to reach Israel.  In the latest episode, the military said the border guards fired warning shots and were moving to arrest the migrants when the Egyptian guards came under fire.  In addition to the five migrants who were killed, six others were injured, the statement said.
