By STEPHEN CASTLEJAN. 26, 2015
LONDON — Centuries of tradition were overturned in the Church of England on Monday when it consecrated the first woman to serve as one of its bishops at a service in York Minister.Last year a long dispute within the church was won by those arguing that women should be permitted to serve as bishops. There was an echo of that divisive debate on Monday when the congregation was asked to agree to the consecration of the Rev. Libby Lane as the bishop of Stockport. One man called out: “No. Not in my name!”Archbishop John Sentamu of York proceeded with the ceremony, which was followed by loud and prolonged applause.A version of this article appears in print on January 27, 2015, on Page A7 of the New York edition with the headline: Britain: Church of England Consecrates First Woman to Be One of Its Bishops.
