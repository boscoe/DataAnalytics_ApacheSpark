By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSOCT. 2, 2015
Spain granted citizenship on Friday to 4,302 people whose Jewish ancestors fled the country after being told in 1492 to convert to Catholicism or go into exile. The action on Friday came a day after Spain adopted a new citizenship law for descendants of Sephardic Jews, Justice Minister Rafael Catala said. Those granted citizenship will be permitted to maintain their original citizenship.A version of this brief appears in print on October 3, 2015, on Page A5 of the New York edition with the headline: Europe; Spain: Descendants of Exiled Jews Granted Citizenship.
