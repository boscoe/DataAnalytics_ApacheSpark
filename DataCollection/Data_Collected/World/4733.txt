American military authorities are losing no time in pushing ahead the development of the new United States base at Bermuda. Already United States Army engineers are in the field, making a thorough survey so that construction may start within three months. Bermuda, long recognized as a paradise for American tourists, a blissful haven for wedding trips and a peaceful place never touched by actual warfare, suddenly has become one of the most important links in a chain of military bases reaching from Newfoundland to South America. — New York Herald Tribune, European Edition, December 16, 1940
