By SAEED AL-BATATIDEC. 18, 2015
AL MUKALLA, Yemen — Days after Yemen’s warring parties agreed to a cease-fire, fierce fighting in at least three provinces on Friday threatened negotiations underway in Switzerland to end the country’s nine-month civil war.Military forces allied with Yemen’s government advanced in several parts of northern Yemen bordering Saudi Arabia on Friday, capturing the capital of Jawf Province and a major town in Hajja Province, according to a senior pro-government military official. There were also reports of fighting in Marib Province.The government forces, backed by a military coalition led by Saudi Arabia, are fighting against the Houthi rebels, a northern group. The Houthis and the government have been accusing each other of breaking the cease-fire since soon after it officially started on Tuesday.On Friday, Houthi negotiators said that they had complained to the United Nations about the latest military advances by the pro-government forces, but that they had decided not to leave the talks brokered by the United Nations.A version of this article appears in print on December 19, 2015, on Page A10 of the New York edition with the headline: World Briefing | Middle East; Yemen: Fighting Follows Truce.
