AdvertisementSupported byBy The Associated PressBEIJING — A late-night fire at a coal mine killed 21 people and left one person missing in the northeastern Chinese province of Heilongjiang, the local authorities and state news media said on Saturday.The fire at the Xinghua mine in the city of Jixi was brought under control on Saturday, and 21 bodies were recovered at the mine, owned by the Heilongjiang Longmay Mining Holding Group, the official Xinhua News Agency said.The provincial work safety administration confirmed the fire and the death toll on Saturday.Rescuers were searching for the missing person, said a work safety employee, who gave only his family name, Xing, as is customary with low-ranking Chinese officials.The news agency said that 38 miners were working underground when an angle belt caught fire on Friday night, and that 16 people had been pulled out to safety.Xinhua did not say what caused the fire.This is the deadliest mine disaster since April, when a water leak at a coal mine killed 21 people in the northern city of Datong in Shanxi Province.China’s mines have long been the world’s deadliest, but safety improvements have reduced deaths in recent years. Last year, 931 people were killed in mine accidents throughout China. In 2002, nearly 7,000 miners were killed.Advertisement
