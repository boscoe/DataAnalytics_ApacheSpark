By HARI KUMAR and GARDINER HARRISFEB. 13, 2015
NEW DELHI — Nine passengers were killed and 10 were seriously injured when a train in southern India derailed Friday morning near Bangalore after hitting a boulder that had rolled onto the tracks, officials said.Nine cars of the Bangalore-Ernakulam Intercity Express train derailed after the train plowed into the boulder, said Anil Kumar Saxena, a spokesman for India’s railways. Suresh Prabhu, the country’s railway minister, posted on Twitter, “Boulder fell on track in Ghat section, train hit it.”Some of the dead had to be cut out of the damaged train cars by rescue workers, Mr. Saxena said.Deadly train accidents are common in India, which has one of the world’s largest railroad networks.A version of this article appears in print on February 14, 2015, on Page A6 of the New York edition with the headline: India: 9 Killed as Train Hits a Boulder.
