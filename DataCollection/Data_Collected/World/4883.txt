By ELISABETH MALKINDEC. 16, 2015
The United States Department of the Treasury said Wednesday that the vice president of a Mexico City newspaper, Unomásuno, had used the publication to support a drug trafficking gang. The executive, Naim Libien Tella, has had a long relationship with the leader of the gang, Abigael González Valencia, who was arrested in February, according to the Treasury’s Office of Foreign Assets Control. The Treasury designated Mr. Libien Tella and several businesses connected to him, including Unomásuno, as drug traffickers, barring American citizens from doing business with them.A version of this brief appears in print on December 17, 2015, on Page A18 of the New York edition with the headline: The Americas; Mexico: U.S. Penalizes Newspaperman.
