AdvertisementSupported byWorld BriefingBy Reuters Members of Venezuela’s governing Socialist Party have filed Supreme Court appeals to challenge the election of eight opposition legislators, spurring accusations that the party is trying to undermine the opposition’s landslide victory this month. Losing candidates from the Socialist Party and allies in six legislative districts also asked the court for injunctions that could block the winners from taking office, potentially stripping the opposition of the two-thirds majority it won on Dec. 6. The situation has fueled concerns of a looming power struggle between President Nicolás Maduro and a legislature controlled by the opposition, which says the move is part of a broader ploy to undermine its new advantage. A Supreme Court statement did not say on what grounds the appeals were based. Winning the appeals could lead to the elections being repeated.  Advertisement
