By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSNOV. 11, 2015
A deal reached at a United Nations meeting on Wednesday opens the way for satellite tracking of airliners, a major breakthrough motivated by the disappearance of a Malaysia Airlines jetliner last year. The agreement allows nations to set aside radio frequencies so that airplanes can be tracked by satellite, not just from the ground. Under radar-based systems, the movement of planes is monitored by land-based systems, leaving around 70 percent of the world’s surface uncovered, said the International Telecommunications Union, a United Nations agency.
