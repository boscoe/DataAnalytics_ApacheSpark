By BEN HUBBARDMAY 5, 2015
Rebels in Yemen struck a border town in Saudi Arabia with mortar and rocket fire on Tuesday, damaging buildings and leading the national airline to cancel flights to the area. The attack on the town of Najran near the Saudi southern border was the first direct assault on an inhabited area in the kingdom since Saudi Arabia and its Arab allies began bombing the rebels, known as Houthis, in Yemen in March. A version of this brief appears in print on May 6, 2015, on Page A11 of the New York edition with the headline: Middle East; Saudi Arabia: Yemeni Rebels Attack.
