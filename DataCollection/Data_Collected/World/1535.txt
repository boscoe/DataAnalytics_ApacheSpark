By SALMAN MASOODOCT. 23, 2015
ISLAMABAD, Pakistan —  A suicide bomber attacked a procession of Shiite Muslims in southern Pakistan on Friday, killing at least 22 people and wounding at least 20 others, officials said.There was no immediate claim of responsibility, but hard-line Sunni extremists are believed to be behind the attack, the officials said. Shiites in Pakistan have been repeatedly attacked by militants with close links to the Taliban.At least 22 people were killed when a suicide bombing hit a celebration for the Shiite Muslim holiday Ashura in Sindh Province, Pakistan.The attack took place in Jacobabad, a city in the southern province of Sindh, as Shiite Muslims were taking part in a Muharram procession, to commemorate the anniversary of the death of Imam Hussein, a grandson of the Prophet Muhammad who was killed in the battle of Karbala in 680.Bisharat Haider, a local Shiite leader, said that 22 people, including five children, were killed in the suicide attack. The wounded were taken to a hospital in the city but because of a shortage of medicine, many had to be moved to a hospital in nearby Larkana. Soon after the attack, dozens of angry protesters took to the streets in Jacobabad, throwing stones at police officers and setting several vehicles on fire.Additional security measures have been put into effect across Pakistan amid fears of sectarian attacks. Thousands of police officers and paramilitary troops have been mobilized to provide security during Shiite processions.Zia ur-Rehman contributed reporting from Karachi, Pakistan.A version of this article appears in print on October 24, 2015, on Page A7 of the New York edition with the headline: 22 Dead in Pakistan Bomb Attack.
