By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSDEC. 3, 2015
A Cambodian court sentenced an unlicensed medical practitioner to 25 years in prison on Thursday after finding him responsible for infecting more than 100 villagers with H.I.V., the virus that causes AIDS, by reusing unsanitized syringes. At least 10 of the infected people have died. The man, Yem Chrin, was found guilty of cruel behavior resulting in death, intentionally spreading H.I.V. and practicing medicine without a license, said a spokesman for the court in the northwestern province of Battambang.
