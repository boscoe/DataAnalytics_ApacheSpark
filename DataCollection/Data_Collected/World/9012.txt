By REUTERSMARCH 5, 2015
Japanese and Chinese officials will hold their first security talks in four years in Tokyo this month, Japan’s Foreign Ministry said Thursday, the latest sign of a possible improvement in ties strained by a territorial dispute. Relations have been damaged by conflicting claims to a group of East China Sea islands and the legacy of Japan’s wartime occupation of China. The March 19 meeting will involve top officials from the countries’ foreign and defense ministries, the Foreign Ministry said.
