By REUTERSDEC. 23, 2015
Prime Minister Irakli Garibashvili resigned Wednesday after two years in office in a surprise move less than a year before parliamentary elections. In a televised address, Mr. Garibashvili gave no specific reason for his decision. But opposition politicians, who blame Mr. Garibashvili’s government for mounting economic woes, linked it to a decline in the popularity of the governing Georgia Dream coalition, which faces an election in October.
