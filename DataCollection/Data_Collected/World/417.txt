

      By THE NEW YORK TIMES

UPDATED October 8, 2015

The contenders for the 2015 Nobel Peace Prize included statesmen, priests, an obstetrician and a newspaper, but the surprise winner was the National Dialogue Quartet in Tunisia, “for its decisive contribution to the building of a pluralistic democracy in Tunisia in the wake of the Jasmine Revolution of 2011.” The prizes have honored hundreds of brilliant minds since they were first awarded in 1901. But they have also led to controversy, intrigue and, at times, serious second​-​guessing.
