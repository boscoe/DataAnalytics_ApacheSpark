By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSDEC. 15, 2015
Brazil’s federal police raided the homes and offices of top political figures on Tuesday, including the home of the speaker of the lower house of Congress, in a sweeping graft investigation. The speaker, Eduardo Cunha, is suspected of accepting at least $5 million in bribes linked to a sprawling kickback scandal at the state-run oil company, Petrobras. He told reporters Tuesday that he was “totally innocent.” Mr. Cunha introduced impeachment proceedings on Dec. 2 against President Dilma Rousseff, whose government is accused of fiscal irregularities relating to the use of funds from state-run banks to cover budget gaps.
