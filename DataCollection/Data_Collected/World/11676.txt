By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSAPRIL 20, 2015
Russia’s leading environmentalist, Yevgenia Chirikova, has moved to Estonia under increasing government pressure. Ms. Chirikova, 38, is best known for a 2010 campaign opposing the construction of an $8 billion toll highway, a project that is partly owned by a childhood friend of President Vladimir V. Putin. The project, now under construction, has come under fire because it will destroy a federally protected forest even though there were routes less disruptive to the environment. “It is difficult to work in Russia because they can come for you at any moment,” she said in Tallinn, the Estonian capital.A version of this brief appears in print on April 21, 2015, on Page A9 of the New York edition with the headline: Russia: Activist Leaves the Country.
