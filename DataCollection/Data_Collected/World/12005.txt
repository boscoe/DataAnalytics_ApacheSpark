AdvertisementSupported byWorld BriefingBy The Associated Press A string of car bombings in busy commercial areas of Baghdad killed at least 20 civilians on Monday, officials said. The deadliest attack, on Monday night, occurred in the western district of Mansour, killing 10 people and wounding 25, the police said. Security forces sealed off the area, which is often packed with nighttime shoppers. At least 20 cars were set on fire by the blast. No one claimed responsibility. Baghdad has seen near-daily attacks as security forces struggle to dislodge Islamic State militants from vast areas in northern and western Iraq which they seized last summer. Advertisement
