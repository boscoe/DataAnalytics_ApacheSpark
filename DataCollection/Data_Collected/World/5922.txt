By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJAN. 6, 2015
Aleksei A. Navalny, the Russian opposition leader, said Tuesday on his blog that he had defied his house arrest by walking to his local store to buy milk, and that he had been quickly intercepted by three men who escorted him home. Mr. Navalny, a political foe of President Vladimir V. Putin, was convicted last week of fraud and given a suspended sentence.
