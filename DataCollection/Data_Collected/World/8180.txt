By REUTERSFEB. 17, 2015
President Thein Sein declared a state of emergency in the eastern region of Kokang and imposed a three-month period of martial law there on Tuesday night. Fighting broke out on Feb. 9 between the army and an ethnic Kokang force called the Myanmar National Democratic Alliance Army. At least 47 soldiers and 26 rebels have been killed since then, a state-backed newspaper reported, and thousands of civilians have fled, either to other areas in Myanmar or over the border into China. The clashes have alarmed China, which fears that the influx of refugees will increase.A version of this brief appears in print on February 18, 2015, on Page A5 of the New York edition with the headline: Myanmar: Martial Law Imposed in Area Near China.
