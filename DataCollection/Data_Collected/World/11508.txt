The bones of victims of the 1915 Armenian genocide on display at a small memorial chapel in Antelias, Lebanon. Amid the upheaval of World War I, the desperate leadership of the Ottoman Empire, worried that its Christian Armenian population was planning to align with Russia, a primary enemy, embarked on what historians have called the first genocide of the 20th century.
