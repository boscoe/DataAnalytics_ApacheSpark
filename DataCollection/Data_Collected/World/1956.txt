By SEWELL CHANOCT. 29, 2015
A three-year-old memorial in Berlin to Gypsy victims of the Holocaust was defaced on Thursday morning with a spray-painted swastika and a neo-Nazi message, “Gas them,” according to the foundation that runs the memorial, which is in the Tiergarten, across from the Reichstag. The paint was quickly removed, security was tightened, and the German federal police have opened a criminal investigation, according to Uwe Neumärker, the director of the foundation that runs the memorial, which honors hundreds of thousands of people in the Sinti and Roma communities (often referred to as Gypsies) who were killed during World War II.
