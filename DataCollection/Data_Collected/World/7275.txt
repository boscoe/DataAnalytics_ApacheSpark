By MICHAEL R. GORDONJAN. 30, 2015
WASHINGTON — Secretary of State John Kerry plans to travel to Ukraine next week and will meet with President Petro O. Poroshenko and other senior Ukrainian leaders, the State Department announced Friday.Mr. Kerry last visited Kiev in March. Since then, pro-Russian separatists have gained ground in eastern Ukraine and the Kremlin has tightened its hold on Crimea. Facing a major budget shortfall, the Ukrainian government is coming under considerable economic pressure.Jen Psaki, the State Department spokeswoman, said that Mr. Kerry, who will visit Kiev on Thursday, “will highlight the United States’ steadfast support for Ukraine and its people.”Mr. Kerry will also meet with Prime Minister Arseniy P. Yatsenyuk, Foreign Minister Pavlo Klimkin and members of Ukraine’s legislature. After Kiev, Mr. Kerry will travel to Munich for an annual security conference.
