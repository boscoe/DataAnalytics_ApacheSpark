By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSFEB. 18, 2015
A special tribunal dealing with war crimes related to Bangladesh’s 1971 independence war convicted and sentenced a leading political figure to death on Wednesday for crimes committed during the war, including mass killings. The defendant, Abdus Subhan, 79, of Jamaat-e-Islami, Bangladesh’s largest Islamic political party, is the ninth senior leader of the party to be convicted of such crimes since Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina initiated the long-stalled war crimes trials in 2010. Bangladesh blames Pakistani soldiers and Bangladeshis who aided them for the deaths of three million people during the 1971 war.A version of this brief appears in print on February 19, 2015, on Page A6 of the New York edition with the headline: Bangladesh: War Crimes Tribunal Sentences a Party Leader to Death.
