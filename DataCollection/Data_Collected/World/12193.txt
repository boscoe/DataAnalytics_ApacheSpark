AdvertisementSupported byWorld BriefingBy The Associated Press Burundi’s national university was abruptly closed Thursday amid protests in the capital as barricades burned in some neighborhoods. The capital, Bujumbura, has been the scene of street protests since Sunday, with the security forces confronting demonstrators who say a third term for President Pierre Nkurunziza, left, is unconstitutional and threatens to ignite ethnic tensions years after a civil war. Mr. Nkurunziza was nominated on Saturday to represent his party in the presidential election on June 26. At least six people have been killed in clashes with the police, according to the Burundi Red Cross. Social media platforms like Twitter and Facebook having been blocked by the authorities, according to the United Nations Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs. A visiting American diplomat, Tom Malinowski, the assistant secretary for democracy, human rights and labor, said Thursday that the country’s government had been warned of “real consequences” if the political crisis escalated. “We hope that cool heads will prevail,” Mr. Malinowski said.
