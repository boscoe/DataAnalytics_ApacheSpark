AdvertisementSupported byWorld BriefingBy The Associated Press A Japanese court on Thursday ruled that two nuclear reactors could be restarted after the operator said in an appeal that they were safe. The Fukui District Court in western Japan lifted an April injunction that was filed by a group of residents who said that an earthquake exceeding the reactors’ quake resistance could cause a disaster similar to the Fukushima crisis set off by a March 2011 quake and tsunami. The order paves the way for a resumption of the Takahama No. 3 and No. 4 reactors, operated by the Kansai Electric Power Company. The operator had already obtained the approval of the safety regulators, and local leaders expressed their support for restarting the reactors this month. Dozens of reactors in Japan were taken offline after the Fukushima disaster; two of Japan’s 43 reactors are currently back online. The utility plans to conduct the final safety checks before putting one of the Takahama reactors back online in late January.
