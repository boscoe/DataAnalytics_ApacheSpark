AdvertisementSupported byWorld BriefingBy The Associated Press The United States Treasury on Thursday removed a Burmese businessman from a blacklist that had barred him from doing business in the United States. The man, Win Aung, is president of a business association. The Treasury also removed two companies that he heads from the list. The Obama administration rolled back trade and investment sanctions against Myanmar in 2012 to reward its shift from direct military rule, but the United States still forbids business dealings with military corporations and certain other individuals and companies. Myanmar wants Washington to lift sanctions entirely, but its government has been criticized for stalling on reforms and detaining peaceful protesters. Advertisement
