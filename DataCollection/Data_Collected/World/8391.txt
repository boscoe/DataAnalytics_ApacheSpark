AdvertisementSupported byWorld BriefingBy The Associated Press Lee Kuan Yew, Singapore’s first prime minister, has been hospitalized for severe pneumonia, the prime minister’s office said Saturday. Mr. Lee, 91, was admitted to Singapore General Hospital on Feb. 5, the office said in a statement. His condition has stabilized and he remains on mechanical ventilation in the hospital’s intensive care unit, the statement said. A founding member of the governing People’s Action Party, which transformed Singapore from a slow port city to a bustling metropolis, Mr. Lee became prime minister in 1959 and held power for 31 years. One of his sons, Lee Hsien Loong, is the current prime minister. Advertisement
