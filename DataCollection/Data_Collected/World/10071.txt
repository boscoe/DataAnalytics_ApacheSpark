By KATRIN BENNHOLDMARCH 22, 2015
LONDON —  Six medical students and three recent graduates are the latest Britons believed to have traveled to Syria to work in the territory controlled by Islamic State militants.The four women and five men, who were all born in Britain and had been studying medicine in Sudan, are thought to have entered Syria a week ago, according to reports Sunday in the British news media.They are in their late teens and early 20s, and they apparently kept their travel plans hidden from their families until just before entering Syria when at least one of them sent a message to her sister, The Observer newspaper reported.Some of the medical students’ family members have traveled to the Turkish-Syrian border to look for them.
