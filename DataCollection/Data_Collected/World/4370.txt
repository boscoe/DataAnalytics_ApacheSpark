The German chancellor has personified her country's leadership role in the most pressing issues of the year.
Angela Merkel, the chancellor of Germany, has personified her country’s leadership role in the most pressing issues facing Europe this year. Ms. Merkel confronted Russia over its involvement in Ukraine, navigated the potentially perilous financial showdown between Greece and its European creditors, and has led the effort to absorb more than a million refugees and migrants. On Wednesday, she was singled out as “Person of the Year” by Time magazine in its annual promotional issue. Here is a look at how Ms. Merkel asserted herself — and Germany — in a tumultuous year.Europe’s indispensable nation, at the center of crises over Ukraine and Greece and often uncomfortable with leadership, has an assertive steward in its chancellor, Angela Merkel.The German chancellor had to find a way to maintain European unity and the euro as a currency while also devising an acceptable resolution to Greece’s financial crisis.
