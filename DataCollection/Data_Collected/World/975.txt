AdvertisementSupported byWorld BriefingBy The Associated Press The government is calling for international assistance to help feed 8.2 million people after erratic rains devastated crop harvests. The government has allocated $192 million for food and other aid and is appealing for $596 million in aid from international donors for the rest of 2015, said Mitiku Kassa, secretary of the Ethiopian Disaster Prevention and Preparedness Committee. More than 300,000 children are in need of specialized nutritious food, and a projected 48,000 more children under 5 are suffering from severe malnutrition, according to a government assessment conducted in September. The situation is “incredibly serious,” said John Aylieff, an official with the World Food Program. Advertisement
