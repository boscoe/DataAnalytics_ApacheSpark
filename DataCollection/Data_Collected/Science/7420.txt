Researchers only recently discovered a path, known as the Red Desert-to-Hoback migration, which is as long as any known land migration in the lower 48 states. It is a twice-yearly, 150-mile journey that has inspired numerous conservation groups to work together to protect the mule deer’s route from encroaching development. About 500 deer travel the full 150 miles from their winter range here in the Red Desert to spring and summer grounds in the Hoback River basin near Jackson, Wyo.  Here, the Red Desert in Wyoming.CreditMichael Kirby Smith for The New York Times1 of 16
