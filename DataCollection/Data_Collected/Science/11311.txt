SINDYA N. BHANOO

APRIL 13, 2015
Sea turtles six to 18 months old are active swimmers that work hard to find favorable ocean habitats, according to a new study in Current Biology. Scientists know little about the so-called lost years that young turtles spend at sea before returning to coastal areas to forage and reproduce. Juvenile sea turtles were thought to passively drift with ocean currents. Researchers discovered otherwise after using satellite telemetry to track 44 young turtles in the Gulf of Mexico.A version of this article appears in print on April 14, 2015, on Page D4 of the New York edition with the headline: Marine Biology: End of a Sea Turtle Stereotype.
