Twenty miles north of Stonehenge is Avebury, a larger henge of stones consisting of the same type of large blocks of sandstone known as sarsens. The stones at Avebury were moved and stood up in a circular pattern, but unlike at Stonehenge, they were not shaped and stacked together.CreditTim Ireland/Press Association, via Associated Press1 of 10

To many visitors, Stonehenge is not much more than some big stones and not as impressive as they imagined. But the story is not just Stonehenge. The early Britons erected many monuments in this era around 2500 B.C., and modern technologies are revealing more about the landscape and the people.
