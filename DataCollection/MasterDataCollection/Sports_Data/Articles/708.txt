By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 26, 2016
The Indiana Pacers’ playoff hopes took a big hit with a loss to the lowly Nets.Brook Lopez scored 23 points, Bojan Bogdanovic added 18, and the Nets beat the Pacers, 120-110, on Saturday night at Barclays Center.Paul George missed a 3-pointer with 25 seconds remaining, sealing the win for the Nets. George was asked if the loss concerned him, with the team fighting for a playoff spot.“Yeah, it does, because we know, we understand every game counts,” said George, who scored 27 points. “This is a game that we should win, and we lost it.”Indiana is tied with Detroit for the final two playoff spots, two games ahead of Chicago.Sergey Karasev added a career-high 17 points and 7 rebounds for the Nets, who took a 104-102 lead on Bogdanovic’s 3-pointer with 5 minutes 41 seconds remaining in the game.Advertisement“Bojan hit a big 3 and Brook played a great fourth quarter,” Karasev said. “Everyone was touching the ball on the court.”AdvertisementLopez then scored 9 consecutive points before Bogdanovic nailed a contested 3-pointer to put the Nets ahead, 116-110, with 39 seconds remaining.HAWKS 112, PISTONS 95  Paul Millsap had 23 points and 9 rebounds, and Atlanta ended Detroit’s five-game winning streak with a road victory.The Hawks moved a game ahead of Miami atop the Southeast Division.
