By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 5, 2016
David Perron scored the go-ahead power-play goal in the second period as the surging Anaheim Ducks earned their 11th consecutive victory, beating the Kings, 3-2, on Saturday in Los Angeles and taking over sole possession of the Pacific Division lead from their Southern California rivals.The Ducks, trying to win a fourth straight division title, lead the season series by three games to one and have won 11 of the past 14 regular-season meetings over all, including a 4-2 victory last Sunday at Honda Center.Ryan Kesler and Jakob Silfverberg also scored for Anaheim, helping Bruce Boudreau earn his 400th regular-season coaching victory.PREDATORS 5, AVALANCHE 2 Mike Fisher broke a tie with 9 minutes 44 seconds remaining in a four-goal third period, Pekka Rinne stopped 34 shots, and visiting Nashville ran its streak to 12 straight games with at least a point by beating Colorado.
