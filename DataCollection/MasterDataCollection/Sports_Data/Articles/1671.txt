By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 19, 2016
Top-ranked Connecticut made an early statement about its focus in the quest for a fourth consecutive national title.The freshman Katie Lou Samuelson scored 22 points, the two-time national player of the year Breanna Stewart added 18, and the Huskies (33-0) routed Robert Morris, 101-49, on Saturday in Storrs, Conn., in the first round of the N.C.A.A. tournament.“When it comes to N.C.A.A. tournament time, you don’t have to get anyone up for a game,” said Stewart, who also had eight steals and three blocked shots.Anna Niki Stamolamprou had 11 points for 16th-seeded Robert Morris (20-13) in the final game for Coach Sal Buscaglia, who is retiring after a 38-year career.AdvertisementThe Huskies, who are seeking an 11th championship, have won 70 consecutive games, which matches the second-longest winning streaks in team and N.C.A.A. history.S.
