By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJUNE 1, 2016
Tom Lysiak, a Canadian-born former National Hockey League All-Star who played 13 seasons with the Atlanta Flames and the Chicago Blackhawks, died on Monday in Atlanta. He was 63.The cause was leukemia, his daughter, Jessie Lysiak Braun, wrote on Twitter.Lysiak, a center, put up 292 goals and 551 assists in 919 career games. He made the All-Star Game in 1975, 1976 and 1977.He was the runner-up for rookie of the year in 1973-74 with the Flames (who moved from Atlanta to Calgary, Alberta, in 1980).
