By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 26, 2016
Imani Boyette scored 18 points and grabbed 10 rebounds to lead second-seeded Texas to a come-from-behind 72-64 win over U.C.L.A. on Saturday in a regional semifinal in Bridgeport, Conn.Brooke McCarty added 15 points for the Longhorns (31-4), who will play Connecticut in their first regional final since 2003.Monique Billings scored 20 points and grabbed 12 rebounds for U.C.L.A., which finished its season at 26-9. Jordin Canada also had 20 points for the third-seeded Bruins.U.C.L.A. led by 10 points in the second quarter, by 5 points at the half and by 54-48 after three quarters.AdvertisementBut Texas scored the first 10 points of the fourth to take a 58-54 lead, and it did not trail again. Texas’s Ariel Atkins made a 3-point play, hitting a shot as she fell down in the lane while being fouled, to push the lead to 63-56.AdvertisementThe Longhorns hit their free throws down the stretch, and U.C.L.A. missed its last nine shots.Texas won the game underneath, outrebounding U.C.L.A. by 46-40 and outscoring the Bruins in the paint, 40-22. U.C.L.A. had the early advantage in part because Texas’s two big women, the 6-foot-7 Boyette and 6-5 Kelsey Lang, each picked up two fouls in the first half.Boyette, who averages 11 points and 9 rebounds a game, scored 7 points in the first half, but she had just one rebound at the break, two fewer than her 5-4 teammate McCarty.
