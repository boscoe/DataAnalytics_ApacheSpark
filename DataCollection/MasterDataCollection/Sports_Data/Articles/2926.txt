By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 10, 2016
A bloodied Marshall Plumlee’s tiebreaking 3-point play with 1 minute 14 seconds left helped 19th-ranked Duke overcome a second-half deficit against North Carolina State (16-17), and the Blue Devils, the reigning N.C.A.A. champions, edged the Wolfpack, 92-89, in the Atlantic Coast Conference tournament’s second round Wednesday in Washington.Duke (23-9) will face Notre Dame, the defending A.C.C. tournament champion, in the quarterfinals.In an earlier second-round game, Cameron Johnson scored a career-high 24 points off the bench to lead Pittsburgh (21-10) over Syracuse (19-13). Pitt will meet North Carolina in the quarterfinals Thursday.GEORGETOWN 70, DEPAUL 53 D’Vauntes Smith-Rivera had 20 points, and Georgetown (15-17) subdued DePaul (9-22) in the first round of the Big East tournament at Madison Square Garden. The Hoyas will face the top seed, third-ranked Villanova, in Thursday’s quarterfinals.NEBRASKA 89, RUTGERS 72 Shavon Shields had 20 points and 11 rebounds to lead No. 11 seed Nebraska (15-17) over 14th-seeded Rutgers (7-25) in the first round of the Big Ten tournament in Indianapolis.HALL OF FAMER DIES Clyde Lovellette, the Hall of Fame forward who led Kansas to the 1952 national championship before helping the United States win gold at the Helsinki Olympics, died at his home in North Manchester, Ind., after a battle with cancer. He was 86.A two-time all-American, Lovellette had 33 points and 17 rebounds in the Jayhawks’ victory over St. John’s in the N.C.A.A. title game and earned tournament most valuable player honors.
