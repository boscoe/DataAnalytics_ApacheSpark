By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 8, 2016
Carter Capps, who was expected to contend for the Miami Marlins’ closer’s job, has a torn ligament in his throwing elbow and was to have Tommy John surgery.■ A July 3 game between the Braves and the Marlins originally scheduled to be played at Turner Field in Atlanta will instead be played on the military base at Fort Bragg, N.C., at a 12,500-capacity ballpark to be built with funding from Major League Baseball and the players’ union. The ballpark will be converted to a softball field and multipurpose facility after the game.  (AP)■ The Yomiuri Giants of Japan said three executives would resign in the wake of an announcement that a player was found to have bet on baseball games. Three other Yomiuri players were barred in November as part of the scandal. One of the resigning officials, Tsuneo Watanabe, was for decades considered the most powerful individual in Nippon Professional Baseball.A version of this brief appears in print on March 9, 2016, on Page B14 of the New York edition with the headline: Marlins Lose a Closer Candidate.
