Tampa Bay Buccaneers running back Doug Martin was suspended for four games for violating the N.F.L.’s policy on performance-enhancing substances and said Wednesday that he would enter a treatment center rather than appeal the decision.Martin, a two-time Pro Bowl selection who has been slowed by a hamstring injury, said in a statement that his “initial instinct” had been to “appeal the suspension and finish the season with my teammates.”But Martin said that after discussing the matter with people close to him, including Buccaneers Coach Dirk Koetter, he had decided to enter a treatment center “and receive the help I truly need.”Martin, 27, averaged just 2.9 yards per carry this season, rushing for 421 yards on 144 attempts.MARIOTA HAS SURGERY Tennessee Titans quarterback Marcus Mariota had surgery for his broken right leg, and team officials said the procedure was “considered a success.”AdvertisementMariota broke his right fibula on Saturday in a 38-17 loss to Jacksonville. Ware had four sacks in 10 games to bring his career total to 138 ½, sixth in N.F.L. history.RAVENS’ SMITH TALKS RETIREMENT Baltimore receiver Steve Smith said he was “89 percent sure” that Sunday’s game against Cincinnati would be his last. Minnesota’s win total was its best since 2003, but the support Claeys expressed on Twitter for his players during their boycott hinted at conflict with Coyle.
