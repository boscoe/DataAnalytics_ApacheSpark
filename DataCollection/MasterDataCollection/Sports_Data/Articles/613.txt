By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSAPRIL 25, 2017
BOSTON — Rain washed out series openers for both the Yankees and the Mets on Tuesday.The Yankees were set to open a three-game series against the Boston Red Sox at Fenway Park, but the game was postponed after hours of steady rain. That game has been rescheduled as part of a day-night doubleheader on July 16.
            Today's game has been postponed due to inclement weather & rescheduled for July 16th as game 1 of a day-night doubleheader.#FenwayWeather pic.twitter.com/hbhP5HmD1w Earlier Tuesday, Boston placed third baseman Pablo Sandoval on the 10-day disabled list with a sprained right knee, a move retroactive to Monday.There was also heavy rain in Queens on Tuesday, which caused the Mets’ series opener against the Atlanta Braves at Citi Field to be postponed about 90 minutes before the scheduled first pitch.The game was rescheduled as part of a single-admission doubleheader on Sept. 25 at 4:10 p.m. That doubleheader will start the Mets’ final home series of the regular season, followed by a three-game weekend series against the Phillies in Philadelphia to conclude the schedule.A version of this article appears in print on April 26, 2017, on Page B14 of the New York edition with the headline: Yankees and Mets Are Rained Out.
