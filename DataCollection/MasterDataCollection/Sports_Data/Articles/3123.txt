Karl-Anthony Towns had 28 points, 7 assists and 6 rebounds and the Minnesota Timberwolves shot a franchise-record 68.4 percent to beat the visiting Nets, 132-118, on Saturday night.No Timberwolves player missed more than four shots in the game.Markel Brown scored 23 points and Thomas Robinson added 18 points and 17 rebounds for the Nets, who rested the starters Brook Lopez and Thaddeus Young.The Nets are on a nine-game trip, and the interim coach Tony Brown decided to rest Lopez and Young on the second night of back-to-back games after they played heavy minutes Friday in Denver. The Nets entered the night just one victory ahead of the Timberwolves at the bottom of the league. The team with the worse record will have a better chance at the No. 1 pick in the draft lottery.A team that rested its two best players in a game like this might normally be accused of tanking, but not the Nets.
