Mike Weber ran for 111 yards and a touchdown on Saturday, and No. 2 Ohio State held off host Michigan State, 17-16, after the Spartans missed a 2-point conversion with 4 minutes 41 seconds remaining that would have given them the lead.The Buckeyes (10-1, 7-1 Big Ten) can now turn their attention to next weekend’s showdown with Michigan, but only after withstanding a Michigan State team that had derailed their national title bids in two of the previous three seasons.The Spartans (3-8, 1-7) were down, 17-10, when L. J. Scott scored on a 1-yard run. Michigan State opted to try to take the lead, but Tyler O’Connor’s pass on the 2-point conversion attempt was intercepted in the end zone.“I think they’re tough, and they shot everything at us today,” Ohio State Coach Urban Meyer said.AdvertisementMichigan State Coach Mark Dantonio said he had planned all week for the Spartans to go for 2 if the situation arose.Advertisement“They had us covered up on that particular play,” Dantonio said. “So good job by them.”The Spartans got the ball back a final time, at their own 20-yard line, with 2:04 left. Tyquan Lewis sacked O’Connor on the first play, and Gareon Conley intercepted his pass on the second.MICHIGAN 20, INDIANA 10 De’Veon Smith ran for two touchdowns late in the third quarter and finished with a career-high 158 yards rushing, and No. 4 Michigan (10-1, 7-1 Big Ten) shook off visiting Indiana (5-6, 3-5) in a game that ended amid a snowstorm.John O’Korn made his first start at quarterback for the Wolverines — replacing Wilton Speight, who was out with an undisclosed injury — and struggled to lead an offense that was averaging 45 points and 232 passing yards a game.O’Korn completed 7 of 16 passes for 59 yards. On his biggest play, a 30-yard run, he escaped a collapsing pocket on a third down to set up a 34-yard run by Smith that put the Wolverines up, 13-10, with 4:02 left in the third quarter. A 39-yard run by Smith later in the quarter accounted for the final margin.ALABAMA 31, CHATTANOOGA 3 Jalen Hurts passed for three touchdowns and rushed for 68 yards to help No. 1 Alabama (11-0) overcome a slow start and thump visiting Chattanooga (8-3) of the Football Championship Subdivision.With their offense struggling, the Crimson Tide fell behind the Mocs, 3-0, and did not shake them until late in the third quarter.
