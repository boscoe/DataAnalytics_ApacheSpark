By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 10, 2016
PHOENIX —  Sasha Vujacic scored a season-high 23 points in a rare start, Carmelo Anthony also scored 23, and the Knicks beat the Suns, 128-97, on Wednesday night.Vujacic was 7 of 8 from the field and tied a career high by going 6 of 7 from 3-point range. He made his first start since last November and had not played in two of the Knicks’ last three games.The Knicks hit a season-high 16 3-pointers. Derrick Williams scored 18 points and Jose Calderon added 10 points and 12 assists as the Knicks won for the second time in six games.The rookie Devin Booker led Phoenix with 32 points. Tyson Chandler’s nine rebounds gave him 9,005 for his career, making him the 50th player in N.B.A. history to reach that milestone.AdvertisementThe Suns tried to mount a comeback in the third quarter and trimmed a 20-point halftime deficit to 13 on a jumper by Alex Len with 3 minutes 35 seconds to go.
