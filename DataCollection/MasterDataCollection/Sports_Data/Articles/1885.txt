By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 18, 2016
Kyrie Irving scored 26 points and LeBron James had 18, including a layup and a pair of free throws down the stretch, to help the visiting Cleveland Cavaliers hold off the Orlando Magic, 109-103, on Friday night and clinch a playoff spot.Channing Frye came off the bench to make four 3-pointers and score 14 against Orlando, his former team, as the Cavaliers beat the Magic for the 14th consecutive time. A rested James returned to the lineup after sitting out Wednesday’s home victory over Dallas and finished 6 of 15 from the field, with eight assists and seven rebounds.Victor Oladipo had a career-high 45 points for Orlando but scored only 6 in the fourth quarter. The last of his six 3-pointers trimmed Cleveland’s lead to 107-103 before James made two free throws to put it away.Cleveland led by 63-54 at the half, using a 31-9 run to wipe out an 11-point deficit. Kevin Love had two 3-pointers and a dunk during an 11-0 burst that built Cleveland’s lead to double digits.AdvertisementOladipo scored 24 before halftime and 15 more in the third quarter, when the Magic opened with an 18-7 run to regain the lead and cling to an 86-83 edge going into the fourth.AdvertisementBut in the end, Oladipo did not get enough offensive help to keep the Cavaliers from improving the best record in the Eastern Conference to 49-19 with a fifth victory in six games.WARRIORS 130, MAVERICKS 112 Stephen Curry had several dazzling plays on his way to 31 points, Klay Thompson scored 39, and Golden State tuned up for a showdown in San Antonio with a victory on the road over Dallas.In one of many displays that drew roars from the typical swarm of Curry’s fans on the road, Curry stole the ball from Dirk Nowitzki near midcourt before hitting a 35-footer over him at the first-quarter buzzer. In the second quarter, he glanced a 3-pointer off the glass from the corner with Nowitzki again in his face.The Warriors made 14 of their first 17 from 3-point range, finishing 22 of 38, and won their seventh straight game.
