By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSAPRIL 12, 2016
The San Antonio Spurs had to rally to beat the Oklahoma City Thunder, 102-98, in overtime to finish 40-1 at home, a mark that matches the home record set by the Boston Celtics in 1985-86.Coach Billy Donovan rested Kevin Durant, Russell Westbrook and Serge Ibaka for the game, the Thunder’s regular-season finale. San Antonio Coach Gregg Popovich started his regular lineup.■ Dwyane Wade did not score until only 4 minutes 14 seconds remained in the first half — his slowest start offensively since March 2006, according to the agency Stats — but he finished with 14 points to help the Miami Heat beat the host Detroit Pistons, 99-93. Miami will win the Southeast Division and earn home-court advantage in the first round of the playoffs if it closes the regular season Wednesday night by winning at Boston.The Pistons’ loss sealed their spot as the eighth seed in the Eastern Conference, setting up a matchup with top-seeded Cleveland.
