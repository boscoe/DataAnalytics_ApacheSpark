By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSDEC. 2, 2016
Rob Gronkowski’s season is most likely over, with the New England Patriots tight end scheduled to have surgery for a herniated disk in his lower back, the team said in a statement.He is likely to be out for eight weeks or more. The Super Bowl is scheduled for Houston on Feb. 5, should the Patriots get that far.Gronkowski sat out practice Wednesday with what was listed as a back injury. He was absent from practice again on Thursday.INJURY ENDS CUTLER’S SEASON Bears Coach John Fox announced that quarterback Jay Cutler will have season-ending surgery on his right shoulder, the latest blow in a lost year for the team. The operation will take place Saturday and could mark the end of Cutler’s time in Chicago.Cutler injured his shoulder during a loss to the Giants on Nov. 20 but finished the game.A version of this article appears in print on December 2, 2016, on Page B11 of the New York edition with the headline: Gronkowski Season in Doubt.
