By DAVE CALDWELLMARCH 24, 2016
The Rangers tightened their grip on second place in the Metropolitan Division and moved closer to a playoff berth and home-ice advantage in the first round by rolling past the stumbling Boston Bruins, 5-2, on Wednesday at Madison Square Garden.The Rangers (42-24-8) have a 4-point lead over third-place Pittsburgh (40-24-8), which has played two fewer games. The Rangers also moved to 9 points ahead of the Detroit Red Wings (36-26-11), who are in ninth place in the Eastern Conference. Detroit has one more game left than the Rangers.Defenseman Keith Yandle fed Mats Zuccarello and Derek Stepan for first-period power-play goals to chase Boston goaltender Tuukka Rask.
