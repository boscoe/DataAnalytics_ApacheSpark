Jalen Hurts broke loose for a 21-yard touchdown run to snap a scoreless tie early in the fourth quarter, and Alabama’s defense stifled Leonard Fournette in a 10-0 victory at No. 15 Louisiana State on Saturday night.The top-ranked Crimson Tide (9-0, 6-0 Southeastern Conference) won their sixth straight meeting with L.S.U. (5-3, 3-2) and remained in first place in the SEC West, one game ahead of Auburn.Hurts, a freshman, was the game’s leading rusher, finishing with 114 yards on 20 carries. He also completed 10 of 19 passes for 107 yards.The Tigers finished with just 125 total yards.
