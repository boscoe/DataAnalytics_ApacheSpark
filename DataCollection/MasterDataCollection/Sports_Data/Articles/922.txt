By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSNOV. 8, 2016
Washington moved into fourth in the College Football Playoff rankings, putting the four remaining unbeaten teams from the Power 5 conferences at the top of the selection committee’s second Top 25.Alabama, Clemson and Michigan still hold the top three spots.The committee’s first ranking of the season, released last week, caused a bit of a stir because the unbeaten Huskies were behind Texas A&M. The Aggies then lost at Mississippi State, clearing up the miniature controversy.Ohio State is behind Washington, but the Buckeyes are still in fine shape. Ohio State plays Michigan on Nov. 26 in a game that could decide the Big Ten’s East Division.Louisville moved up a spot to sixth, followed by Wisconsin and Texas A&M.A version of this article appears in print on November 9, 2016, on Page B10 of the New York edition with the headline: Washington Up to 4th in Playoff Rankings.
