TORONTO — DeMar DeRozan scored 33 points, Norman Powell added 19, and the Raptors defeated the Knicks, 118-107, on Saturday night, Toronto’s fourth straight victory in meetings between the teams.DeRozan, the N.B.A.’s leading scorer, had his eighth game of 30 points or more in nine starts this season, putting him in an exclusive club. In the last 50 years, only Michael Jordan, World B. Free and Tiny Archibald have started a season by registering 30 or more points in eight of the first nine games.Carmelo Anthony had 31 points for the Knicks, while Kristaps Porzingis and Derrick Rose had 21 each.
