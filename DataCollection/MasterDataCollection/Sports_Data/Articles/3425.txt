Malcolm Brogdon scored 26 points, and after mounting a 16-5 second-half run to break open a tie game, No. 3 Virginia held off No. 7 North Carolina to win, 79-74, on Saturday night for the Cavaliers’ 19th straight home victory.Anthony Gill added 15 points, and London Perrantes had 12 for Virginia (22-6, 11-5 Atlantic Coast Conference), which is one game behind the Tar Heels and Miami in the conference standings with two regular-season games to play.Joel Berry II led North Carolina (23-6, 12-4) with 21 points, making five 3-pointers. Marcus Paige added 13 points while Brice Johnson and Justin Jackson each had 12.The Tar Heels trailed by 11 after Virginia’s big run, but they closed the gap to 77-74 with 14 seconds left on a 3-pointer by Paige.
