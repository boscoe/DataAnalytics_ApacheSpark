By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 10, 2016
The N.H.L. ruled that an off-season rape allegation made against the Chicago Blackhawks star forward Patrick Kane was unfounded and that he would not face any disciplinary action from the league.The league released a one-paragraph statement Wednesday announcing it had completed its independent review of the allegations against Kane.“Based on its review, including the determination made by the Erie County district attorney not to pursue charges, the N.H.L. has concluded that the allegations made against Kane were unfounded,” the N.H.L. said. “The league considers the matter closed and will have no further comment.”In November, Frank Sedita, the district attorney in Erie County, N.Y., ruled against pursuing charges against Kane, citing a lack of credible evidence in a case he found “rife with reasonable doubt.”The inquiry began in August when Kane was accused of sexually assaulting a woman at his off-season home outside Buffalo.A version of this article appears in print on March 10, 2016, on Page B15 of the New York edition with the headline: League Clears Kane Over a Rape Allegation.
