By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 8, 2016
OAKLAND, Calif. — Stephen Curry scored 41 points and became the first player in N.B.A. history to make 300 3-pointers in a season, and the Golden State Warriors set another record in a season full of them by holding off the Orlando Magic, 119-113, on Monday night for their 45th straight home victory.Curry didn’t match his 51-point performance against the Magic on Feb. 25, in which he hit 10 of 15 3-pointers, but he shot 7 for 13 from long range and pulled down 13 rebounds. He has 301 3s with 20 games to go.Klay Thompson added 27 points for the Warriors (56-6), who overcame a season-high 24 turnovers to bounce back from an ugly 112-95 road loss to the Lakers on Sunday.Golden State surpassed the 1995-96 Chicago Bulls’ 44 straight regular-season home victories and improved to 26-0 at Oracle Arena this season with its franchise-best sixth straight win against Orlando.The Warriors need to win 17 of their remaining 20 games to surpass the 1995-96 Bulls’ record 72 regular-season victories.Aaron Gordon of the Magic had 20 points and 16 rebounds in his Bay Area homecoming.
