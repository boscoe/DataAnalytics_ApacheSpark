By VICTOR MATHERAPRIL 24, 2017
A judge ordered on Monday that the family of the former N.F.L. player and convicted murderer Aaron Hernandez be given his suicide notes.His fiancée, Shayanna Jenkins, had asked for three notes found in Mr. Hernandez’s cell, where he hanged himself last week.Judge Thomas F. McGuire Jr. of Bristol Superior Court ordered the notes released to the family, The Boston Globe reported. The notes would be redacted if any information in them might affect the investigation into the case.A lawyer for Mr. Hernandez, George Leontire, had written in legal papers that “the family has the right, during this grieving process, to know their loved one’s final thoughts.”AdvertisementA private funeral was held for Mr. Hernandez on Monday afternoon in Bristol, Conn., attended by Ms. Jenkins and close relatives.Mr. Hernandez, a former star tight end with the New England Patriots, was serving a term of life without parole for the killing of Odin L. Lloyd, who was dating Ms. Jenkins’s sister. Mr. Hernandez’s motive in that case, prosecutors said, was that Mr. Lloyd spoke with people Mr. Hernandez did not like at a bar in Boston.AdvertisementThe verdict was under appeal at the time of Mr. Hernandez’s death.Mr. Hernandez was found not guilty on April 14 in a second murder case, a drive-by shooting of two people in Boston in 2012.A version of this article appears in print on April 25, 2017, on Page B10 of the New York edition with the headline: Pro Football; Hernandez’s Family to Get Suicide Notes.
