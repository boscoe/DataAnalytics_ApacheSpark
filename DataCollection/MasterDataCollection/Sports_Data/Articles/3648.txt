By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSDEC. 24, 2016
Oakland Raiders quarterback Derek Carr sustained a broken right fibula when he was sacked on Saturday, and earlier in the day, the Tennessee Titans’ Marcus Mariota went down with a broken right leg.Raiders Coach Jack Del Rio announced after Oakland beat Indianapolis, 33-25, that Carr would have surgery Sunday and would be out indefinitely.Carr was taken off the sideline on a cart after being hurt in the fourth quarter. Carr was twisted to the ground by Indianapolis linebacker Trent Cole with the Raiders leading, 33-14. Carr stayed on the ground for several minutes in pain as trainers came out to treat him.With the crowd chanting “M-V-P! M-V-P!” Carr limped off the field without putting any pressure on his right leg.After Tennessee lost, 38-17, at Jacksonville, Titans Coach Mike Mularkey said Mariota would need at least six weeks to recover, meaning that his season had ended early for a second straight year. Mariota missed the final two games of last season with a sprained knee.Mariota was hurt when he was sacked late in the third quarter by the rookie defensive tackle Sheldon Day while Mariota was scrambling to throw on first down. Day grabbed Mariota around the ankles, and Mariota stayed down after the play.A version of this article appears in print on December 25, 2016, on Page SP3 of the New York edition with the headline: 2 Quarterbacks Lost to Broken Legs.
