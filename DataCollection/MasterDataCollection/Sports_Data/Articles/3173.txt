By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 3, 2016
Melo Trimble and Jake Layman scored 18 points each, and No. 14 Maryland closed out its home schedule with its best game in weeks, an 81-55 rout of visiting Illinois on Thursday night.Jared Nickens and Robert Carter Jr. had 14 points apiece for the Terrapins, who never trailed. Maryland (24-6, 12-5 Big Ten) had lost three of four since climbing to No. 2 in the Associated Press poll in early February.Trimble, a standout sophomore guard, made half of his 14 shots, collected eight rebounds and added five assists. Over the previous four games, he was 11 for 37 from the field.Maverick Morgan reached career highs with 21 points and 10 rebounds for Illinois (13-17, 5-12).S.M.U. 80, UCONN 54 Sterling Brown scored 20 points and No. 24 Southern Methodist beat Connecticut in the final home game for the seniors Nic Moore and Markus Kennedy, the only players with Coach Larry Brown for all four of his seasons during an incredible turnaround for the Mustangs.AdvertisementMoore had 16 points and 4 assists in his 99th career game for the Mustangs, while Kennedy had only 4 points but a career-high 9 assists. Quinnipiac roared back, though, and trimmed the deficit to 1 point, 58-57, on a 3-pointer by James Ford Jr. with 18 seconds to go.The teams traded turnovers, and Rider’s Okereafor ended up at the line after a steal and made both free throws with eight seconds remaining. They led by at least 7 points from there and advanced to Sunday’s semifinals to host ninth-seeded Holy Cross.
