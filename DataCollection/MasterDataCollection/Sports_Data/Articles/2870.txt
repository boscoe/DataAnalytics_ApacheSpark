The Yankees released him in June 1974.A good hitter but an undistinguished third baseman, Hart, who also played in the outfield, had a career batting average of .278, with 170 home runs and 578 R.B.I. in 1,125 games. In 1966, he batted .285 with 33 home runs, a career high, and 93 R.B.I. in 156 games. He was a member that year of the National League All-Star team.Hart was born on Oct. 30, 1941, in Hookerton, N.C. His marriage to Janet Hart-Ayala ended in divorce.
