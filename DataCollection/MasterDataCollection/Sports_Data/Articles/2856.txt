By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 12, 2016
DETROIT —  For nearly an hour Saturday, the Detroit Red Wings were on the wrong end of one of Henrik Lundqvist’s superb performances.Then the puck bounced right to Brad Richards, with the net open.“It was fortunate to get him when he was out of position there in the end,” Richards said.Richards tied the score with 31.8 seconds left in the third period, and Darren Helm added a goal in overtime to give the Red Wings a 3-2 win over the Rangers. Lundqvist made 40 saves in his first game since March 3.Helm also scored in the third period for the Red Wings. Derek Stepan and Chris Kreider scored for the Rangers, who also got Rick Nash back after an extended absence.AdvertisementThe Red Wings tied it in the final minute of regulation when Richards scored on a power play.
