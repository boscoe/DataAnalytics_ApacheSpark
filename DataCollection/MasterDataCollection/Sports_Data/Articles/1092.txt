By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 23, 2016
The rookie Kristaps Porzingis tied a season high with 29 points and grabbed 10 rebounds, Carmelo Anthony added 24 points, and the Knicks beat the Bulls, 115-107, on Wednesday night in Chicago.In the front end of back-to-back games with the Knicks, the Bulls failed to complete a sweep of their four-game homestand and dropped behind the Detroit Pistons for the eighth and final playoff spot in the Eastern Conference.Nikola Mirotic scored a career-high 35 points for the Bulls, and Derrick Rose added 21.The Knicks led by as many as 22 in the second half. Rose and Mirotic tried to start a late rally, but each was answered by Porzingis and Anthony.Rose cut the deficit to 10 late in the fourth, but Porzingis hit a jumper. Mirotic made a 3-pointer to make the score 109-101 with 3 minutes 7 seconds left, but Anthony followed with a 3-pointer from the corner.AdvertisementThe Knicks led, 57-55, at the half. Porzingis scored 17 points on 7-of-9 shooting, including 3 of 4 from 3-point range. Rose led the Bulls with 12 points.AdvertisementAnthony, who missed Sunday’s loss to Sacramento with a migraine, scored 10 points with 5 assists in the first half.SPURS 112, HEAT 88 Kawhi Leonard matched his career high with 32 points before leaving with a bruised quadriceps, and San Antonio beat Miami to remain perfect at home.San Antonio earned its 36th straight home victory to open the season, leaving the Spurs a game shy of matching the record set by the Bulls in 1995-96.Leonard’s injury did not appear serious. The extent of the injury was not immediately known, but the Spurs’ doctors remained in the team’s box suite — possibly indicating that the injury did not require urgent attention.Josh Richardson led Miami with 17 points, and Dwyane Wade added 16.JAZZ 89, ROCKETS 87 A dunk by Derrick Favors with 1.6 seconds left lifted Utah to a victory at Houston.Gordon Hayward had 22 points as Utah moved a half-game ahead of Houston into the eighth and final playoff spot in the Western Conference standings.The Jazz trailed by as many as 18 in the second half before rallying for their first lead of the game with about four minutes left. Afterward, the teams exchanged leads several times before Dwight Howard tied the score with a dunk with 22.3 seconds remaining.AdvertisementRodney Hood then found Favors, who finished with 17 points, under the basket to put Utah on top.
