By LIZ MOYERJAN. 20, 2016
Two prominent lawyers at Paul, Weiss, Rifkind, Wharton & Garrison are breaking away and forming their own litigation boutique with lawyers from other firms.Beth A. Wilkinson and Alexandra M. Walsh of Paul, Weiss are forming their firm, Wilkinson Walsh & Eskovitz, with Sean Eskovitz from the Los Angeles firm Munger, Tolles & Olson. The firm will have offices in Washington and Los Angeles.Also joining them are Brian L. Stekloff from Covington & Burling, Brant W. Bishop from Kirkland & Ellis and Eric C. Liebeler, a former Kirkland partner who currently heads litigation at Siemens.Ms. Wilkinson and Ms. Walsh defended Pfizer in a lawsuit involving the antidepressant drug Zoloft, as well as Major League Baseball in a recently settled antitrust case.Advertisement“We are excited for Beth and Alex and wish them all the best in their new venture,” said Brad S.
