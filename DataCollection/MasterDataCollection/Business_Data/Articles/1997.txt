By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSNOV. 18, 2016
Wall Street stock indexes slipped on Friday as drug companies dragged the market lower. Small-company stocks bucked the downward trend and continued to climb, and bond yields rose to their highest level in a year.Drug makers like Merck and the biotech giant Amgen experienced some of the biggest losses. Weak results from Gap and Abercrombie & Fitch hurt retailers as investors kept a close eye on the coming holiday season.The Dow Jones industrial average slid 35.89 points, or 0.2 percent, to close at 18,867.93. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index lost 5.22 points, or 0.2 percent, to 2,181.90. The Nasdaq composite reached a record high early on, but turned lower and gave up 12.46 points, or 0.2 percent, to 5,321.51.Small companies including regional banks continued to make large gains. Those stocks have risen sharply since the presidential election last week and are now at record highs. Among small-company stocks, the mortgage lending service company LendingTree added $6.80, or 7.3 percent, to close at $100, and the coal mining company Cloud Peak Energy rose 77 cents, or 15.3 percent, to $5.79.AdvertisementLosses for drug companies weighed down health care stocks. The Botox maker Allergan retreated $8.20, or 4.1 percent, to close at $191.78, and Amgen fell $2.13, or 1.5 percent, to $145.23. Gilead Sciences, which makes drugs used to treat hepatitis C, lost 96 cents, or 1.3 percent, to $74.62.AdvertisementDrug company stocks are coming off their biggest weekly gain in two years. The stocks had been falling in the months leading up to the election because investors worried that under a Hillary Clinton presidency, the federal government would take steps to rein in drug prices. Those kinds of steps are less likely under a Donald J. Trump presidency and a Republican-controlled Congress.

The dollar continued to climb. The dollar rose to 110.8 yen from 109.95 yen. The euro fell to $1.0591 from $1.0623. The dollar has not been this strong since early 2003.Investors continued to sell United States government bonds at a rapid clip, and bond prices wobbled and turned lower. The yield on the 10-year Treasury note rose to 2.35 percent from 2.30 percent. Bond prices have fallen hard since the election, and yields are now at their highest level in a year.Abercrombie & Fitch plunged $2.33, or 13.8 percent, to close at $14.60 after it reported weaker sales than analysts had expected. Shoppers are buying fewer clothes and moving toward discount chains. Procter & Gamble lost $1.07, or 1.3 percent, to close at $82, and Walgreens Boots Alliance slumped 72 cents, or 0.9 percent, to $83.27. Those companies have fallen since the election as investors buy stock that could benefit from faster economic growth.Benchmark crude rose 27 cents to settle at $45.69 a barrel in New York, while Brent crude, which is used to price international oils, added 37 cents to $46.86 a barrel in London. Energy companies traded higher. Chevron rose $1.09, or 1 percent, to close at $109.20, and ConocoPhillips jumped $1.15, or 2.6 percent, to $44.76.Gold fell $8, to $1,208.50 an ounce. Silver lost 15 cents, to $16.62 an ounce. Copper dipped 2 cents, to $2.47 a pound.The CAC 40 in France fell 0.5 percent, and the FTSE 100 in Britain dipped 0.3 percent. The DAX in Germany lost 0.2 percent. Japan’s benchmark Nikkei 225 index added 0.6 percent as the yen hit a six-month low, helping shares of Japan’s big exporters.
