By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSAPRIL 12, 2016
Stocks posted solid gains on Tuesday, led by energy companies, after news reports said Saudi Arabia and Russia were working toward an agreement to cut oil production.Investors also worked through the initial batch of earnings from the first quarter of the year.The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index climbed 19.73 points, or 1 percent, to 2,061.72 and the Nasdaq composite increased 38.69 points, or 0.8 percent, to 4,872.09.The Dow Jones industrial average rose 164.84 points, or 0.9 percent, to 17,721.25.Corporate earnings got underway on a weak note after Alcoa, the aluminum mining company, reported a 15 percent decline in revenue late Monday. Alcoa also had a huge drop in first-quarter profit from a year earlier as aluminum prices fell. Shares of Alcoa fell 26 cents, or 2.7 percent, to $9.48 on Tuesday.

Banks are often seen as a proxy for how the economy is doing.Advertisement“It’s not going to be a clean earnings season for financials at all,” said Peter Stournaras, a portfolio manager at BlackRock. “The banks have suffered from fears about oil loans, but those fears are overblown.”AdvertisementOil prices moved sharply higher after Russian officials told Interfax, the Russian news agency, that they planned to reach a deal with Saudi Arabia to cut oil production. OPEC ministers are set to meet on Sunday in Doha, Qatar.Benchmark American crude oil climbed $1.81, or 4.5 percent, at $42.17 a barrel in New York. Brent crude, the international standard, rose $1.86 to $44.69 a barrel in London.

Energy stocks, which have been beaten down in recent months, followed the price of crude oil higher. The energy component of the S.&P. 500 rose almost 3 percentGovernment bond prices fell. The yield on the 10-year Treasury note rose to 1.78 percent, from 1.73 percent on Monday.The euro fell to $1.1397 from $1.1412 while the dollar rose to 108.53 yen from 107.94 yen.In other energy commodities, heating oil rose 6 cents to $1.276 a gallon, wholesale gasoline rose 3 cents to $1.534 a gallon and natural gas rose 9 cents to $2.004 per thousand cubic feet.Precious and industrial metals prices closed broadly higher.
