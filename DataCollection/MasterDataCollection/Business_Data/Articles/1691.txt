By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSFEB. 10, 2016
The seafood restaurant chain Red Lobster said sales surged 33 percent on Sunday, compared with last year’s Super Bowl Sunday, after Beyoncé released a song, “Formation,” in which she sings about taking a romantic partner to the restaurant.“It’s clear that Beyoncé has helped create some Red Lobster fans,” Red Lobster’s chief, Kim Lopdrup, said in a statement. More conventional contributing factors for the rise in sales included relatively mild weather and a “Lobsterfest” promotion.The spotlight is a welcome change for Red Lobster, which was cast off by Darden Restaurants in 2014 after a long stretch of declining sales. The chain, which is now privately held by the investment firm Golden Gate Capital, said the Beyoncé song made Red Lobster a trending topic on Twitter for the first time.A version of this article appears in print on February 10, 2016, on Page B2 of the New York edition.
