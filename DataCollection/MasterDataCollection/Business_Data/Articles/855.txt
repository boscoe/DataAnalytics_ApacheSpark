By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSFEB. 11, 2016
U.S. stocks fell for the fourth day in a row as concerns about global economic weakness intensified, even as the Federal Reserve chairwoman, Janet L. Yellen, reiterated her confidence in the U.S. economy.Financial stocks fell hardest as investors worried that interest rates in the U.S. and elsewhere would remain low and sap bank profits. Oil prices sank again, this time to their lowest levels since 2003.While all three major U.S. indexes finished lower, they recovered somewhat from far steeper losses earlier in the day.The Dow Jones industrial average fell 254 points, or 1.6 percent, to close at 15,660. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index fell 22 points, or 1.2 percent, to 1,829. The Nasdaq composite fell 16 points, or 0.4 percent, to 4,266.AdvertisementGLOBAL JITTERS Investors are increasingly worried that the mounting market turmoil could put a brake on the global economy when it is already struggling with a host of issues like China’s slowdown, low inflation and plunging energy markets.

Ms. Yellen cautioned Congress on Wednesday that global weakness and falling financial markets could depress the economy’s growth. That could, in turn, slow the pace of Fed interest-rate increases.AdvertisementMs. Yellen acknowledged concern about the global economic environment but said it was too early to tell how big the effect would be on the United States.THE QUOTE Investors are losing confidence in central bank policies, said Anthony Valeri, an investment strategist at LPL Financial. Central banks “have done all they can do, and these policies may not improve economic growth or may not support financial markets,” he said.SECTOR VIEW All 10 sectors in the S.&P. 500 index were down. Financials stocks fell the most, down 3.1 percent. Energy stocks slumped 2.9 percent as oil prices continued to decline.BANK SLUMP Several major banks were trading lower. Citigroup fell 6.2 percent, while JPMorgan slid 4.3 percent. Bank of America shed 7.1 percent. Wells Fargo lost 2.3 percent.MORTGAGE HANGOVER Morgan Stanley stock fell 4.8 percent after it agreed to pay $3.2 billion to settle federal and state claims that it engaged in practices that contributed to the 2008 financial crisis, including misrepresentations about the value of mortgage-backed securities.

ENERGY Benchmark U.S. crude oil fell $1.24, or 4.5 percent, to close at $26.21 a barrel in New York. Brent crude, a benchmark for international oils, fell 78 cents, or 2.5 percent, to close at $30.06 a barrel in London. Natural gas fell 5.2 cents, or 2.5 percent, to close at $1.994 per 1,000 cubic feet.AdvertisementOIL IMPACT The drop in oil prices sent shares in several energy companies lower. Southwestern Energy lost 6.9 percent, Marathon Oil fell 6.6 percent, and NRG Energy shed 9.5 percent.STEEP DIVE Shares of the aircraft manufacturer Boeing plunged 7 percent after a report that the Securities and Exchange Commission was investigating it over accounting practices. The report came a day after Boeing announced plans to eliminate an unspecified number of commercial airplane jobs as part of a cost-cutting effort. In Germany, the DAX slumped 4.1 percent, and the FTSE 100 was down 2.4 percent in Britain.ASIAN MARKETS Market sentiment was hurt earlier in the day, when some Asian indexes reopened after a holiday and caught up with days of market turmoil. In Hong Kong, the Hang Seng dropped 3.9 percent. The Kospi had its biggest daily drop in four years, closing down 2.9 percent in South Korea. Both markets opened for the first time this week after Lunar New Year holidays. Markets in China and Taiwan will reopen on Monday. Japan’s market was closed Thursday for a separate public holiday.CURRENCIES AND BONDS The dollar took a dive as investors adjusted their expectations for fewer interest rate increases in the U.S. It fell to 112.01 yen from 113.35 yen. It also fell against the euro, which was up to $1.1338 from $1.1282. Bond prices rose.
