By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSDEC. 31, 2015
Stocks closed out 2015 on a downbeat note.Major U.S. indexes declined on Thursday, the last day of the year, putting a main market benchmark slightly in the red for 2015.The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index ended the year down about three-quarters of a percent. With dividends included the index was up slightly for the year. It was the worst return for the index since 2011.At the closing bell the Dow Jones industrial average was down 178 points, or 1 percent, at 17,425.The S.&P. 500 gave up 19 points, or 1 percent, to close at 2,043. The Nasdaq composite declined 58 points, or 1.2 percent, to 5,007.Bond prices rose. The yield on the 10-year Treasury note fell to 2.27 percent.LOOKING BACK With dividends included, the S.&P. 500 is on track for a total return of 2.4 percent for the year. Four years ago the index had an even more meager return of 2.1 percent. Last year it returned 13.7 percent.
