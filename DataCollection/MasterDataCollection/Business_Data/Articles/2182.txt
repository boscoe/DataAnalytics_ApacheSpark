By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSAPRIL 8, 2016
A surge in the price of oil sent energy company shares higher on Friday, but stocks over all got only a small lift as retailers suffered big losses.Stocks rose in morning trading as oil prices climbed, and the Dow Jones industrial average rose more than 150 points early on. But retailers skidded as investors assessed earnings reports from Gap and L Brands, and biotech drug makers returned some of their gains from earlier in the week.Oil prices climbed about 7 percent this week as the dollar weakened. The price of oil has seesawed as investors hope energy producing companies will cut production. Experts are not sure the gains will last.“If this fizzles out in a week or so I think you could see oil prices roll over,” said Steve Chiavarone, associated portfolio manager for Federated Investors.AdvertisementThe Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index rose 5.69 points, or 0.3 percent, to 2,047.60. The Nasdaq composite index eked out a gain of 2.32 points, or less than 0.1 percent, to 4,850.69.AdvertisementThe Dow picked up 35 points, or 0.2 percent, to 17,576.96.Benchmark United States crude oil rose $2.46, or 6.6 percent, to $39.72 a barrel in New York. Brent crude, used to price international oils, gained $2.51, or 6.4 percent, to $41.94 a barrel in London.ConocoPhillips shares rose 92 cents, or 2.3 percent, to $41.23, and stock in Murphy Oil advanced $1.25, or 5.1 percent, to $25.69.Oil prices recovered this week, and Mr. Chiavarone said that was partly because the Federal Reserve said it would raise interest rates slowly. That makes the dollar weaker and investors think that means demand for oil will go up.Gap said Thursday that its revenue at stores open at least a year fell 6 percent as its three major brands — Banana Republic, Old Navy and the Gap label — suffered sales drops. The retailer, which also owns Intermix and Athleta, also warned that it was entering April with higher levels of inventory, which it said could hurt profit margins.
