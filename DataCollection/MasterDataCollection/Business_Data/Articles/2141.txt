By REUTERSAPRIL 8, 2016
Britain’s industrial output shrank at the fastest rate in more than three years in the three months through February, and the trade deficit ballooned to its widest in eight years, data showed on Friday, adding to worries of a broader economic slowdown. The British finance minister, George Osborne, warned at the start of the year that the economy faced a “cocktail of risks” because of spillover from a slowdown in China and a slump in commodity prices. There are also jitters in Britain ahead of the June 23 referendum on whether to leave the European Union.
