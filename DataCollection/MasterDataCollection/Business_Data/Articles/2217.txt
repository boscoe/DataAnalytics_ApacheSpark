By CHAD BRAYJAN. 27, 2016
LONDON — Banco Santander of Spain said on Wednesday that its fourth-quarter profit was dragged down by one-time charges of 1.7 billion euros, or about $1.8 billion, including provisions to compensate consumers who were improperly sold a controversial insurance product in Britain.For the three months ended Dec. 31, Santander reported a profit of €25 million, down from €1.46 billion in the prior-year period.The lender said it took a €600 million charge for potential claims by customers who said they were improperly sold payment-protection insurance, a product that has cost its banking peers in Britain billions of dollars in redress.Santander, one of the largest banks in Europe, also said it took an impairment of €683 million related to the decline in value of software, and an additional goodwill impairment of €435 million during the quarter.Advertisement“We are confident that we will reach the targets we set for 2016,” Ana Botín, the Banco Santander chairwoman, said in a news release.AdvertisementNet interest income, or what a bank earns on its lending after deducting what it pays out on deposits and other liabilities, rose 2 percent to €7.89 billion, from €7.71 billion in the same period a year earlier.Please verify you're not a robot by clicking the box.Invalid email address.
