AdvertisementSupported byBy The Associated PressWall Street investors remained in a buying mood on Election Day, sending stocks broadly higher and building on big gains from a day earlier.Safe stocks like utilities and phone companies were among the biggest gainers Tuesday. Energy companies were essentially flat.The Dow Jones industrial average rose 73.14 points, or 0.4 percent, to 18,332.74. The average was briefly up as much as 140 points. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index gained 8.04 points, or 0.4 percent, to 2,139.56. The Nasdaq composite index added 27.32 points, or 0.5 percent, to 5,193.49.Investors focused on the United States presidential election, which rattled financial markets in recent weeks as polls showed a tightening race between Hillary Clinton and Donald J. Trump.Wall Street has largely seen Mrs. Clinton as more likely to maintain the status quo, while viewing Mr. Trump’s polices as less clear. The rally on Tuesday, paired with Monday’s market gains, which was the end of a nine-day losing streak and the best day for stocks since March, suggested the market anticipated a Clinton victory.“The market has been looking for the status quo result, and the polling in the last couple of days shows that result is a good likelihood,” said Mike Baele, senior portfolio manager at the Private Client Reserve at U.S. Bank. “That’s the reason why the market is bouncing here.”Investors also monitored the latest batch of company earnings.The Priceline Group climbed 6.6 percent after the online travel booking company reported quarterly earnings that easily beat analysts’ forecasts. Shares gained $97.80 to $1,578.13.Marriott International rose 2.7 percent after the hotel chain posted a big increase in earnings and revenue for the most recent quarter. The stock added $1.92 to $73.02.SeaWorld Entertainment rose 8.1 percent after the embattled parks operator said it had been able to stem falling attendance after a bruising fight with animal rights activists that led to the closure of its orca whale breeding program. The stock gained $1.16 to $15.41.Bond prices fell. The yield on the 10-year Treasury note rose to 1.86 percent from 1.83 percent late Monday.Advertisement
