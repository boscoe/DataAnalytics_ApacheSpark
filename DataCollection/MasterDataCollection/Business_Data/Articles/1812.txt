By SYDNEY EMBERAPRIL 14, 2016
Col Allan, the longtime editor in chief of The New York Post, announced on Thursday that he would retire at the end of the month after 42 years at the News Corporation, the publication’s owner.“It has been an enormous privilege to edit this great paper,” Mr. Allan, 62, said in a statement. “I know that the paper will continue to grow in scale and influence.”Mr. Allan, an Australian and the longest-serving editor at the News Corporation, joined the company in 1974. He was editor of The Daily Telegraph in Sydney before joining The Post in 2001.He will be succeeded by Stephen Lynch, 43, the Sunday editor of The Post, who will take over on May 1. Mr. Lynch will report to Jesse Angelo, The Post’s publisher and chief executive.In a statement, Rupert Murdoch, the executive chairman of the News Corporation, called Mr. Allan “one of the most outstanding editors of his generation” and a “great friend.”Mr. Allan’s move comes during an election campaign that has already provided plenty of fodder for the New York tabloids. He is, however, staying through Tuesday’s New York primary.A version of this article appears in print on April 15, 2016, on Page B6 of the New York edition with the headline: Col Allan, Longtime Editor, to Retire From The Post.
