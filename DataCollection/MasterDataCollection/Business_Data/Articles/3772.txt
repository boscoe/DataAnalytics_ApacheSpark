AdvertisementSupported byBy The Associated PressThe major Wall Street stock indexes closed mostly lower on Friday, capping a day spent wavering between small gains and losses.Phone companies were the biggest drag on the market after reports that AT&T was considering a deal to acquire the media conglomerate Time Warner. Shares of AT&T, Verizon, Sprint and T-Mobile US all fell.Health care and energy stocks also experienced some losses, and consumer staples and technology companies held on to slight gains.The Dow Jones industrial average fell 16.64 points, or 0.1 percent, to close at 18,145.71. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index slipped 0.18 points, or 0.01 percent, to 2,141.16. The Nasdaq composite index gained 15.57 points, or 0.3 percent, to 5,257.40.The three indexes ended slightly higher for the week. For the year, the Dow is now up 4.1 percent, the S.&P. 500 is up 4.8 percent and the Nasdaq is up 5 percent.Investors continued to focus on corporate America, reviewing earnings of big companies like General Electric. Several companies that reported results on Friday failed to impress investors.General Electric slipped 9 cents to $28.98 after the company said its latest quarterly sales fell more than expected because of weaker results from its lighting and transportation businesses. G.E. also trimmed its revenue forecast for the year.Earnings from banks and other financial companies have been mostly better than expected, which has helped bolster that sector.The software giant Microsoft climbed 4.2 percent a day after it posted a surprisingly high profit for its fiscal first quarter. The results help validate the company’s increased focus on software and online services. The stock gained $2.41 to $59.66, eclipsing its previous record close of $59.56 set in December 1999.News and corporate deal talks also fueled big moves on Wall Street on Friday.Reynolds American jumped 14 percent after British American Tobacco, which is based in London, offered to buy the 57.8 percent stake in Reynolds that British American does not already own. Reynolds, which was evaluating the offer, was the biggest gainer in the S.&.P 500, climbing $6.61 to $53.78.AT&T fell 3 percent after reports about the potential acquisition of Time Warner. AT&T slid $1.16 to $37.49.Benchmark United States crude rose 42 cents to close at $50.85 a barrel in New York. Brent crude, used to price international oils, gained 40 cents to close at $51.78 a barrel in London.The DAX in Germany rose 0.1 percent, while the CAC 40 in France fell 0.1 percent. The FTSE 100 in Britain also slipped 0.1 percent. In Asia, Japan’s benchmark Nikkei 225 index lost 0.3 percent, while the Kospi in South Korea lost 0.4 percent. Hong Kong’s stock market was closed because of a typhoon.In metals trading, the price of gold gained 68 cents to settle at $1,266.28 an ounce, while silver lost 6 cents to $17.49 an ounce. Copper slid a penny to $2.09 a pound.Bond prices rose. The yield on the 10-year Treasury note fell to 1.74 percent from 1.76 Thursday.In currency markets, the dollar slipped to 103.82 yen from 103.93 on Thursday.
