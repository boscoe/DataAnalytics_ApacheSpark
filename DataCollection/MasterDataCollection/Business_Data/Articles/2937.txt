AdvertisementSupported byBy The Associated PressStocks soared on Friday, the last trading day of January, and Microsoft, Visa and other tech stocks made the biggest gains in a broad market rally.Indexes finished the day with their biggest gains in about five months. Asian stocks jumped after the Bank of Japan moved to stimulate the economy, and European markets followed. In the United States, tech stocks climbed after strong fourth-quarter results from Microsoft and Visa. The price of oil rose for the fourth day in a row.The United States government said on Friday that the economy slowed in the fourth quarter, a possibility that had worried investors. But its estimate of the country’s gross domestic product was about equal to analysts’ forecasts and did not hurt stocks.The Dow Jones industrial average surged 396.66 points, or 2.5 percent, to 16,466.30. The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index rose 46.88 points, or 2.5 percent, to 1,940.24. The Nasdaq composite index jumped 107.28 points, or 2.4 percent, to 4,613.95. Despite big gains in the last two weeks, the market finished January with hefty losses. The Dow and the S.&P. 500 each fell into a correction, or a drop of at least 10 percent from a recent peak. The small-cap Russell 2000 index entered a bear market, which means a 20 percent slide.The Dow and S.&P. 500 finished January down 5 percent, while the Nasdaq lost almost 8 percent. The Russell finished January down almost 9 percent.Google’s parent, Alphabet, might soon overtake Apple as the world’s most valuable publicly traded company. Both companies are valued at more than $500 billion, and Apple is about $16 billion above Alphabet.Microsoft added $3.04, or 5.8 percent, to $55.09, a day after its results beat expectations. Visa and MasterCard both rose after reporting solid results. Visa climbed $5.16, or 7.4 percent, to $74.49, and MasterCard picked up $5.60, or 6.7 percent, to $89.03.Crude oil prices kept rising. Benchmark United States oil added 40 cents, or 1.2 percent, to $33.62 a barrel in New York. Brent crude, a benchmark for international oils, gained 85 cents, or 2.5 percent, to $34.74. The Nikkei 225 in Japan rose 2.8 percent and in Hong Kong the Hang Seng gained 2.5 percent. The Shanghai composite in mainland China jumped 3.1 percent. European indexes also rose: In Germany, the DAX climbed 1.6 percent, the British FTSE 100 added 2.6 percent and the CAC 40 in France rose 2.2 percent.The yield on the 10-year Treasury note fell to 1.92 percent from 1.98 percent. The euro weakened to $1.0831 from $1.0938.Wholesale gasoline picked up 2.4 cents, or 2.2 percent, to $1.103 a gallon. Heating oil added 2.4 cents, or 2.3 percent, to $1.055 a gallon.
