By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSAPRIL 5, 2016
United States stocks sank for a second day on Tuesday as the head of the International Monetary Fund gave a downbeat outlook for the world economy.Disney shares fell after the company’s expected successor to Robert A. Iger, its chief executive, announced he was leaving the company. Allergan stock plunged after the Treasury Department announced tax rules that would complicate the company’s planned merger.The Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index lost 20.96 points, or 1 percent, to 2,045.17. The Nasdaq composite fell 47.86 points, or 1 percent, to 4,843.93.The Dow Jones industrial average fell 133.68 points, or 0.8 percent, to 17,603.32.Shares opened lower and remained down all day. Investors moved into traditional areas of safety, including gold and government bonds. The market is coming off a multiweek rally that erased nearly all of its losses from earlier in the year.AdvertisementThe yield on the 10-year Treasury note fell to 1.72 percent, from 1.76 percent. Gold rose $10.30 to $1,229.60 an ounce.AdvertisementAfter a big rally in March, investors are waiting for companies to report quarterly results. Earnings season starts next week with Alcoa, the aluminum company, as well as the big banks like JPMorgan Chase.

Investors have been forecasting “this quarter’s earnings to be lousy, so if earnings come in better than expected, it might provide some support to the market,” said Scott Wren, senior global equity strategist at the Wells Fargo Investment Institute.Christine Lagarde, the head of the International Monetary Fund, warned in a speech on Tuesday that “the recovery remains too slow, too fragile.” Her comments weighed on the European and Asian markets.Japan’s benchmark Nikkei 225 index lost 2.4 percent, hit hard by a rise in the yen. The Kospi in South Korea fell 0.8 percent and the Hang Seng in Hong Kong sank 1.6 percent. The Treasury Department announced new tax rules to discourage corporate inversions, which are when an American company merges with a foreign company for tax purposes. Allergan was in the process of an inversion with the American drug company Pfizer.Shares of Pfizer rose 64 cents, or 2.1 percent, to $31.36.Disney shares fell $1.68, or 1.7 percent, to $97. The company said late Monday that Thomas O. Staggs, the heir apparent, would depart. The company’s current chief, Mr. Iger, has said he plans to retire in 2018.United States crude oil edged up 19 cents to settle at $35.89 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange.
